Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse renewable energy: solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, biomass, green hydrogen, grids, storage, intermittency, costs, subsidies, energy security, critical minerals, local opposition, environmental impacts, green jobs and the challenge of making the energy transition fast, fair and reliable.

A. Core renewable energy and grid vocabulary

renewable energy

IPA: /rɪˌnjuːəbəl ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: ri-NYOO-uh-buhl EN-uh-jee
French: énergie renouvelable

Definition: Energy from sources that are naturally replenished, such as sun, wind, water and biomass.

Example: Renewable energy is central to decarbonisation.

renewables

IPA: /rɪˈnjuːəbəlz/
Pronunciation: ri-NYOO-uh-buhlz
French: énergies renouvelables

Definition: Short form for renewable energy sources or technologies.

Example: Renewables are expanding rapidly in many countries.

clean energy

IPA: /ˌkliːn ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: kleen EN-uh-jee
French: énergie propre

Definition: Energy that produces little pollution or greenhouse gas emissions.

Example: Clean energy can reduce air pollution and carbon emissions.

green energy

IPA: /ˌɡriːn ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: green EN-uh-jee
French: énergie verte

Definition: Energy presented as environmentally friendly.

Example: Green energy is often used in political and marketing language.

low-carbon energy

IPA: /ˌləʊ ˈkɑːbən ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: loh-KAR-buhn EN-uh-jee
French: énergie bas carbone

Definition: Energy that emits little greenhouse gas over its life cycle.

Example: Low-carbon energy includes renewables and nuclear power.

energy transition

IPA: /ˈenədʒi trænˌzɪʃən/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee tran-ZISH-uhn
French: transition énergétique

Definition: The shift from fossil-fuel energy systems to low-carbon energy systems.

Example: The energy transition requires investment and public support.

decarbonisation

IPA: /ˌdiːkɑːbənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-kar-buh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: décarbonation

Definition: Reducing or eliminating carbon emissions from energy, transport and industry.

Example: Renewables are a major tool for decarbonisation.

electrification

IPA: /ɪˌlektrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: i-lek-tri-fi-KAY-shuhn
French: électrification

Definition: Replacing fossil-fuel uses with electricity, ideally from low-carbon sources.

Example: Electrification can cut emissions from cars and heating.

energy mix

IPA: /ˈenədʒi mɪks/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee miks
French: mix énergétique

Definition: The combination of energy sources used by a country or region.

Example: A country’s energy mix reflects geography, policy and history.

power generation

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ˌdʒenəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: POW-uh jen-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: production d’électricité

Definition: The production of electricity.

Example: Renewables are changing power generation.

electricity grid

IPA: /ɪˌlekˈtrɪsəti ɡrɪd/
Pronunciation: i-lek-TRIS-uh-tee grid
French: réseau électrique

Definition: The network that transmits and distributes electricity.

Example: The electricity grid must adapt to renewable energy.

power grid

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ɡrɪd/
Pronunciation: POW-uh grid
French: réseau électrique

Definition: The system delivering electricity from producers to consumers.

Example: A modern power grid needs flexibility and storage.

grid capacity

IPA: /ˈɡrɪd kəˌpæsəti/
Pronunciation: grid kuh-PASS-uh-tee
French: capacité du réseau

Definition: The ability of the grid to carry electricity.

Example: Grid capacity can limit renewable projects.

grid connection

IPA: /ˈɡrɪd kəˌnekʃən/
Pronunciation: grid kuh-NEK-shuhn
French: raccordement au réseau

Definition: The link allowing a power project to deliver electricity to the grid.

Example: Many renewable projects wait for grid connection.

smart grid

IPA: /ˈsmɑːt ɡrɪd/
Pronunciation: smart grid
French: réseau intelligent

Definition: An electricity grid using digital technology to manage supply and demand.

Example: A smart grid can balance variable renewable energy.

energy storage

IPA: /ˈenədʒi ˌstɔːrɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee STOR-ij
French: stockage de l’énergie

Definition: Systems that store energy for later use.

Example: Energy storage helps manage solar and wind variability.

battery storage

IPA: /ˈbætəri ˌstɔːrɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: BAT-uh-ree STOR-ij
French: stockage par batterie

Definition: Storing electricity in batteries.

Example: Battery storage can supply power when the sun is not shining.

lithium-ion battery

IPA: /ˌlɪθiəm ˈaɪən ˈbætəri/
Pronunciation: LITH-ee-uhm EYE-uhn BAT-uh-ree
French: batterie lithium-ion

Definition: A rechargeable battery widely used in electronics, vehicles and grid storage.

Example: Lithium-ion batteries are essential to electric vehicles.

pumped hydro storage

IPA: /ˌpʌmpt ˈhaɪdrəʊ ˌstɔːrɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: pumpt HY-droh STOR-ij
French: stockage par pompage-turbinage

Definition: Energy storage using water pumped uphill and released to generate electricity.

Example: Pumped hydro storage can balance the grid.

intermittency

IPA: /ˌɪntəˈmɪtənsi/
Pronunciation: in-tuh-MIT-uhn-see
French: intermittence

Definition: The fact that some energy sources do not produce continuously.

Example: Intermittency is a challenge for solar and wind power.

variable renewable energy

IPA: /ˈveəriəbəl rɪˌnjuːəbəl ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: VAIR-ee-uh-buhl ri-NYOO-uh-buhl EN-uh-jee
French: énergie renouvelable variable

Definition: Renewable energy whose output changes with weather or time.

Example: Solar and wind are variable renewable energy sources.

baseload power

IPA: /ˈbeɪsləʊd ˌpaʊə/
Pronunciation: BAYSS-lohd POW-uh
French: production de base

Definition: Electricity supply that can run continuously to meet minimum demand.

Example: Debates over baseload power shape energy policy.

dispatchable power

IPA: /dɪˈspætʃəbəl ˌpaʊə/
Pronunciation: di-SPATCH-uh-buhl POW-uh
French: énergie pilotable

Definition: Power that can be turned on or adjusted when needed.

Example: Hydropower can provide dispatchable power.

peak demand

IPA: /ˌpiːk dɪˈmɑːnd/
Pronunciation: peek di-MAHND
French: pic de demande

Definition: The highest level of electricity demand in a period.

Example: Peak demand often occurs during heatwaves or cold spells.

demand response

IPA: /dɪˈmɑːnd rɪˌspɒns/
Pronunciation: di-MAHND ri-SPONS
French: effacement ; réponse à la demande

Definition: Adjusting electricity use to match supply or reduce peak demand.

Example: Demand response can reduce pressure on the grid.

solar energy

IPA: /ˈsəʊlər ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: SOH-luh EN-uh-jee
French: énergie solaire

Definition: Energy from sunlight.

Example: Solar energy can be used for electricity or heating.

French: panneau solaire

Definition: A large installation of solar panels producing electricity.

wind energy

IPA: /ˈwɪnd ˌenədʒi/

French: énergie éolienne

wind power

Definition: Electricity generated by wind turbines.

French: éolienne

Definition: A machine that converts wind into electricity.

French: éolien terrestre

offshore wind

IPA: /ˌɒfʃɔː ˈwɪnd/

French: éolien en mer

French: pale de turbine

French: hydroélectricité

Definition: Electricity generated from moving water.

Pronunciation: HY-droh-i-LEK-trik dam
French: barrage hydroélectrique

French: hydroélectricité au fil de l’eau

Definition: Hydropower using river flow with little or no reservoir.

IPA: /ˈrezəvwɑː/
Pronunciation: REZ-uh-vwar
French: réservoir

Definition: An artificial or natural lake used to store water.

French: énergie marémotrice

Definition: Electricity generated from tides.

wave energy

IPA: /ˈweɪv ˌenədʒi/
Pronunciation: wave EN-uh-jee
French: énergie houlomotrice

Definition: Energy generated from ocean waves.

Example: Wave energy remains less mature than wind or solar.

heat pump

IPA: /ˈhiːt pʌmp/

French: pompe à chaleur

Definition: A device that moves heat for heating or cooling, often using electricity.

Example: Heat pumps are important for building decarbonisation.

biomass

IPA: /ˈbaɪəʊmæs/
Pronunciation: BY-oh-mass
French: biomasse

Definition: Organic material used as fuel or raw material.

Example: Biomass can be renewable if managed sustainably.

bioenergy

IPA: /ˌbaɪəʊˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: BY-oh-EN-uh-jee
French: bioénergie

Definition: Energy produced from biological material.

Example: Bioenergy can come from wood, crops or waste.

biofuel

IPA: /ˈbaɪəʊˌfjuːəl/
Pronunciation: BY-oh-FYOO-uhl
French: biocarburant

Definition: Fuel produced from biological material.

Example: Biofuels can replace some petrol or diesel.

biogas

IPA: /ˈbaɪəʊɡæs/
Pronunciation: BY-oh-gas
French: biogaz

Definition: Gas produced from the breakdown of organic matter.

Example: Biogas can be made from agricultural waste.

Pronunciation: an-air-ROH-bik dy-JES-chuhn
French: méthanisation

Definition: A process where microorganisms break down organic matter without oxygen, producing biogas.

Example: Anaerobic digestion can turn waste into energy.

sustainable biomass

IPA: /səˌsteɪnəbəl ˈbaɪəʊmæs/
Pronunciation: suh-STAY-nuh-buhl BY-oh-mass
French: biomasse durable

Definition: Biomass produced without damaging ecosystems or food security.

Example: Sustainable biomass is controversial and hard to verify.

green hydrogen

IPA: /ˌɡriːn ˈhaɪdrədʒən/
Pronunciation: green HY-druh-juhn
French: hydrogène vert

Definition: Hydrogen produced using renewable electricity.

Example: Green hydrogen may help decarbonise heavy industry.

IPA: /ˈhaɪdrədʒən iˌkɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: HY-druh-juhn i-KON-uh-mee
French: économie de l’hydrogène

Definition: An energy system using hydrogen as a major fuel or storage medium.

electrolyser

IPA: /ɪˈlektrəlaɪzə/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-truh-ly-zuh
French: électrolyseur

Definition: A device that uses electricity to split water into hydrogen and oxygen.

Example: Electrolysers are needed to produce green hydrogen.

fuel cell

IPA: /ˈfjuːəl sel/
Pronunciation: FYOO-uhl sel
French: pile à combustible

Definition: A device that converts hydrogen or another fuel into electricity through a chemical reaction.

Example: Fuel cells can power vehicles or buildings.

renewable capacity

IPA: /rɪˌnjuːəbəl kəˈpæsəti/
Pronunciation: ri-NYOO-uh-buhl kuh-PASS-uh-tee
French: capacité renouvelable installée

Example: Renewable capacity has grown strongly.

IPA: /ɪnˌstɔːld kəˈpæsəti/

French: capacité installée

Definition: The maximum output a power plant or system can produce.

IPA: /kəˈpæsəti ˌfæktə/
Pronunciation: kuh-PASS-uh-tee FAK-tuh
French: facteur de charge

Definition: The ratio between actual energy produced and maximum possible production.

Example: Offshore wind often has a higher capacity factor than solar.

levelised cost of electricity

Pronunciation: LEV-uhl-yzd kost ov i-lek-TRIS-uh-tee
French: coût actualisé de l’électricité

LCOE

IPA: /ˌel siː əʊ ˈiː/

French: LCOE

Definition: Abbreviation for levelised cost of electricity.

IPA: /ˈsʌbsɪdi/

French: subvention

PPA

IPA: /ˌpiː piː ˈeɪ/

French: PPA

Definition: Abbreviation for power purchase agreement.

auction

IPA: /ˈɔːkʃən/
Pronunciation: AWK-shuhn
French: enchère

energy security

IPA: /ˈenədʒi sɪˌkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee si-KYOOR-uh-tee
