Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse parliaments, Congress, legislative chambers, law-making procedures, committees, scrutiny, votes, budgets, lobbying and institutional deadlock. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as bill, amendment, committee hearing, scrutiny, hung parliament, government shutdown and lobbying.
For oral exams Use this vocabulary to explain how institutions work, not just who won an election. It is especially useful for UK and US political articles.
For essays Memorise the collocations: to pass a bill, to table an amendment, to hold hearings, to scrutinise government, to push through a reform.

A. Parliaments, chambers and representatives

parliament

IPA: /ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: PAR-luh-muhnt
French: parlement

Definition: A national law-making institution made up of elected or appointed representatives.

Example: Parliament debated the bill for several days before voting on it.

Congress

IPA: /ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: KON-gress
French: Congrès américain

Definition: The national legislature of the United States, made up of the House of Representatives and the Senate.

Example: Congress has the power to pass federal laws and approve the budget.

legislature

IPA: /ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: pouvoir législatif ; assemblée législative

Definition: The institution responsible for making laws in a political system.

Example: The legislature rejected the government’s proposal.

bicameral legislature

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈkæmərəl ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: bye-KAM-uh-ruhl LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: parlement bicaméral

Definition: A legislature with two chambers or houses.

Example: The United States has a bicameral legislature.

unicameral legislature

IPA: /ˌjuːnɪˈkæmərəl ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: yoo-ni-KAM-uh-ruhl LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: parlement monocaméral

Definition: A legislature with only one chamber.

Example: Some smaller countries have a unicameral legislature.

lower house

IPA: /ˌləʊə ˈhaʊs/
Pronunciation: LOH-uh howss
French: chambre basse

Definition: The chamber of a parliament that is often more directly representative of the people.

Example: The lower house passed the bill by a narrow majority.

upper house

IPA: /ˌʌpə ˈhaʊs/
Pronunciation: UP-uh howss
French: chambre haute

Definition: The chamber of a parliament that often reviews or revises legislation.

Example: The upper house can delay or amend legislation.

House of Commons

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˈkɒmənz/
Pronunciation: howss ov KOM-uhnz
French: Chambre des communes

Definition: The elected lower house of the UK Parliament.

Example: The Prime Minister answers questions in the House of Commons every week.

House of Lords

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˈlɔːdz/
Pronunciation: howss ov lordz
French: Chambre des lords

Definition: The upper chamber of the UK Parliament, made up of appointed and hereditary members as well as bishops.

Example: The House of Lords reviewed the controversial legislation.

House of Representatives

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˌreprɪˈzentətɪvz/
Pronunciation: howss ov rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tivz
French: Chambre des représentants

Definition: The lower chamber of the United States Congress.

Example: The House of Representatives voted to pass the spending bill.

Senate

IPA: /ˈsenət/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht
French: Sénat

Definition: The upper chamber of the United States Congress or of some other legislatures.

Example: The Senate must confirm many presidential appointments.

MP

IPA: /ˌem ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: em pee
French: député ; membre du Parlement

Definition: A Member of Parliament, especially in the United Kingdom.

Example: The MP raised the issue during a parliamentary debate.

senator

IPA: /ˈsenətə/
Pronunciation: SEN-uh-tuh
French: sénateur ; sénatrice

Definition: An elected member of a senate.

Example: The senator called for stricter gun control.

representative

IPA: /ˌreprɪˈzentətɪv/
Pronunciation: rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tiv
French: représentant ; élu

Definition: An elected official who speaks and votes on behalf of citizens.

Example: Each representative serves a specific district.

lawmaker

IPA: /ˈlɔːˌmeɪkə/
Pronunciation: LAW-may-kuh
French: législateur ; élu

Definition: A person who helps make laws, especially a member of a legislature.

Example: Lawmakers are under pressure to reform the immigration system.

backbencher

IPA: /ˌbækˈbentʃə/
Pronunciation: bak-BEN-chuh
French: député de base ; parlementaire sans fonction ministérielle

Definition: An ordinary MP who does not hold a ministerial position, especially in the UK.

Example: Backbenchers criticised the government’s handling of the crisis.

frontbencher

IPA: /ˌfrʌntˈbentʃə/
Pronunciation: frunt-BEN-chuh
French: membre de premier plan ; ministre ou membre du cabinet fantôme

Definition: A senior MP who is a minister or shadow minister, especially in the UK.

Example: The frontbencher defended the party’s economic policy.

speaker

IPA: /ˈspiːkə/
Pronunciation: SPEE-kuh
French: président de séance ; Speaker

Definition: The official who presides over debates in a legislative chamber.

Example: The Speaker asked MPs to respect parliamentary rules.

whip

IPA: /wɪp/
Pronunciation: wip
French: whip ; responsable de discipline parlementaire

Definition: A party official responsible for ensuring that members vote according to the party line.

Example: The whip urged party members to support the bill.

party whip

IPA: /ˈpɑːti wɪp/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee wip
French: consigne de vote ; responsable de discipline de parti

Definition: A person or instruction used to enforce party discipline in a legislature.

Example: Several MPs defied the party whip.

constituent

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhnt
French: électeur d’une circonscription ; administré

Definition: A person represented by an elected official.

Example: The MP received hundreds of emails from constituents.

constituency

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənsi/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhn-see
French: circonscription électorale

Definition: A geographical area represented by an elected official.

Example: The candidate campaigned across the constituency.

seat

IPA: /siːt/
Pronunciation: seet
French: siège

Definition: A position in a legislature held by an elected representative.

Example: The party lost several seats in the election.

majority

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité

Definition: More than half of the votes or seats in a legislature.

Example: The government has a comfortable majority in parliament.

minority

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee
French: minorité

Definition: Less than half of the votes or seats in a legislature.

Example: The party remained in the minority after the election.

opposition

IPA: /ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən/
Pronunciation: op-uh-ZISH-uhn
French: opposition

Definition: The parties or politicians who are not in government and challenge the ruling party.

Example: The opposition accused the government of avoiding scrutiny.

ruling majority

IPA: /ˈruːlɪŋ məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: ROO-ling muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité gouvernementale

Definition: The group of lawmakers supporting the government and holding most seats.

Example: The ruling majority pushed the reform through parliament.

minority government

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement minoritaire

Definition: A government that does not control more than half the seats in parliament.

Example: A minority government often needs support from smaller parties.

coalition government

IPA: /ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: koh-uh-LISH-uhn GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement de coalition

Definition: A government formed by two or more political parties.

Example: The coalition government struggled to agree on tax reform.

hung parliament

IPA: /ˌhʌŋ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: hung PAR-luh-muhnt
French: parlement sans majorité absolue

Definition: A parliament in which no party has an overall majority.

Example: The election produced a hung parliament.

bill

IPA: /bɪl/
Pronunciation: bil
French: projet de loi ; proposition de loi

Definition: A proposed law that is discussed and voted on by a legislature.

Example: The bill would introduce tougher rules on online platforms.

B. Law-making vocabulary

law

IPA: /lɔː/
Pronunciation: law
French: loi

Definition: A rule officially adopted and enforced by the state.

Example: The new law will come into force next year.

legislation

IPA: /ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: législation

Definition: Laws considered collectively, or the process of making laws.

Example: The government introduced new legislation on data privacy.

statute

IPA: /ˈstætʃuːt/
Pronunciation: STAT-choot
French: loi écrite ; texte législatif

Definition: A written law formally passed by a legislative body.

Example: The court interpreted the statute narrowly.

amendment

IPA: /əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND-muhnt
French: amendement

Definition: A change proposed or made to a bill, law or constitution.

Example: The opposition tabled an amendment to the bill.

clause

IPA: /klɔːz/
Pronunciation: klawz
French: clause ; article

Definition: A specific section or provision in a legal document or bill.

Example: One clause of the bill caused particular controversy.

motion

IPA: /ˈməʊʃən/
Pronunciation: MOH-shuhn
French: motion

Definition: A formal proposal put forward for debate or vote.

Example: MPs voted on a motion of no confidence.

resolution

IPA: /ˌrezəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: rez-uh-LOO-shuhn
French: résolution

Definition: A formal statement or decision adopted by a legislative body.

Example: The Senate passed a resolution condemning the violence.

committee

IPA: /kəˈmɪti/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission ; comité

Definition: A small group of lawmakers appointed to study, examine or revise specific issues or bills.

Example: The committee investigated the government’s pandemic response.

select committee

IPA: /sɪˈlekt kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: si-LEKT kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission parlementaire spécialisée

Definition: A parliamentary committee that examines government work or specific policy areas, especially in the UK.

Example: The select committee questioned senior officials.

standing committee

IPA: /ˈstændɪŋ kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: STAN-ding kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission permanente

Definition: A permanent committee responsible for a particular policy area.

Example: The standing committee reviewed the education bill.

committee hearing

IPA: /kəˈmɪti ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee HEER-ing
French: audition en commission

Definition: A formal meeting where a committee hears evidence from witnesses or experts.

Example: The committee hearing revealed serious failures.

witness

IPA: /ˈwɪtnəs/
Pronunciation: WIT-nuhs
French: témoin

Definition: A person who gives evidence to a committee or court.

Example: Several witnesses testified before Congress.

testimony

IPA: /ˈtestɪməni/
Pronunciation: TES-ti-muh-nee
French: témoignage ; déposition

Definition: A formal statement given by a witness, especially before a court or committee.

Example: Her testimony contradicted the minister’s account.

hearing

IPA: /ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: HEER-ing
French: audition ; audience

Definition: A formal session where evidence or arguments are presented.

Example: The hearing focused on social media regulation.

inquiry

IPA: /ɪnˈkwaɪəri/
Pronunciation: in-KWY-uh-ree
French: enquête officielle ; commission d’enquête

Definition: An official investigation into a public issue or failure.

Example: The government launched an inquiry into the scandal.

scrutiny

IPA: /ˈskruːtəni/
Pronunciation: SKROO-tuh-nee
French: examen minutieux ; contrôle

Definition: Careful examination of decisions, policies or actions.

Example: Parliamentary scrutiny is essential for accountability.

oversight

IPA: /ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle ; surveillance institutionnelle

Definition: The monitoring of government actions by another institution.

Example: Congressional oversight can limit presidential power.

C. Scrutiny, committees and accountability

accountability

IPA: /əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: uh-kown-tuh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité ; obligation de rendre des comptes

Definition: The obligation of leaders and institutions to explain and take responsibility for their actions.

Example: Parliamentary questions are a tool of democratic accountability.

transparency

IPA: /trænsˈpærənsi/
Pronunciation: trans-PAIR-uhn-see
French: transparence

Definition: Openness that allows citizens to understand how decisions are made.

Example: Transparency helps citizens trust political institutions.

Prime Minister’s Questions

IPA: /ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstəz ˈkwestʃənz/
Pronunciation: prym MIN-is-tuhz KWES-chuhnz
French: questions au Premier ministre

Definition: A weekly session in the UK House of Commons where MPs question the Prime Minister.

Example: Prime Minister’s Questions can be theatrical but politically important.

question time

IPA: /ˈkwestʃən taɪm/
Pronunciation: KWES-chuhn tyme
French: séance de questions au gouvernement

Definition: A period in parliament when government ministers answer questions from lawmakers.

Example: Question time allows the opposition to challenge ministers.

vote

IPA: /vəʊt/
Pronunciation: voht
French: vote

Definition: A formal choice made by lawmakers or citizens.

Example: The vote was delayed until the following week.

to vote on a bill

IPA: /vəʊt ɒn ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: voht on uh bil
French: voter sur un projet de loi

Definition: To formally decide whether to approve or reject a proposed law.

Example: Lawmakers will vote on the bill tomorrow.

to pass a bill

IPA: /pɑːs ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: pass uh bil
French: adopter un projet de loi

Definition: To approve a proposed law through a vote.

Example: Parliament passed the bill by a large majority.

to reject a bill

IPA: /rɪˈdʒekt ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: ri-JEKT uh bil
French: rejeter un projet de loi

Definition: To vote against a proposed law.

Example: The Senate rejected the bill after a heated debate.

to amend a bill

IPA: /əˈmend ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND uh bil
French: amender un projet de loi

Definition: To make changes to a proposed law.

Example: MPs tried to amend the bill to protect civil liberties.

to table an amendment

IPA: /ˈteɪbəl ən əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: TAY-buhl uhn uh-MEND-muhnt
French: déposer un amendement

Definition: To formally propose a change to a bill, especially in parliamentary language.

Example: The opposition tabled an amendment on workers’ rights.

to repeal a law

IPA: /rɪˈpiːl ə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: ri-PEEL uh law
French: abroger une loi

Definition: To officially cancel or remove a law.

Example: The new government promised to repeal the controversial law.

to enact legislation

IPA: /ɪˈnækt ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: i-NAKT lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: promulguer ; adopter une loi

Definition: To formally make legislation into law.

Example: Congress enacted legislation to protect consumers.

to introduce a bill

IPA: /ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: in-truh-DYOOSS uh bil
French: présenter un projet de loi

Definition: To formally present a proposed law to a legislature.

Example: The government introduced a bill on climate targets.

D. Votes, procedures and constitutional tools

to filibuster

IPA: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstə/
Pronunciation: FIL-i-bus-tuh
French: faire de l’obstruction parlementaire

Definition: To delay or block a vote by making long speeches, especially in the US Senate.

Example: Senators threatened to filibuster the bill.

filibuster

IPA: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstə/
Pronunciation: FIL-i-bus-tuh
French: obstruction parlementaire

Definition: A tactic used to delay or block legislative action by prolonged debate.

Example: The filibuster made it difficult to pass voting rights legislation.

veto

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh
French: veto

Definition: The power to reject or block a decision or bill.

Example: The president used his veto to block the bill.

to veto

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh
French: opposer son veto

Definition: To reject or block a bill or decision officially.

Example: The president threatened to veto the legislation.

override a veto

IPA: /ˌəʊvəˈraɪd ə ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-ryd uh VEE-toh
French: passer outre un veto

Definition: To pass a law despite a veto, usually by a special majority.

Example: Congress failed to override the veto.

confidence vote

IPA: /ˈkɒnfɪdəns vəʊt/
Pronunciation: KON-fi-duhns voht
French: vote de confiance

Definition: A vote used to show whether a government still has the support of parliament.

Example: The prime minister survived a confidence vote.

vote of no confidence

IPA: /ˌvəʊt əv nəʊ ˈkɒnfɪdəns/
Pronunciation: voht ov noh KON-fi-duhns
French: motion de censure ; vote de défiance

Definition: A vote showing that parliament no longer supports the government or leader.

Example: The opposition called for a vote of no confidence.

impeachment

IPA: /ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt/
Pronunciation: im-PEECH-muhnt
French: procédure de destitution

Definition: A formal process of charging a high official, especially a president, with serious misconduct.

Example: The impeachment inquiry dominated the political agenda.

to impeach

IPA: /ɪmˈpiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: im-PEECH
French: mettre en accusation ; engager une procédure de destitution

Definition: To formally accuse a high official of serious misconduct in office.

Example: The House voted to impeach the president.

confirmation hearing

IPA: /ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn HEER-ing
French: audition de confirmation

Definition: A hearing used to assess whether a nominee should be approved for a position.

Example: The confirmation hearing focused on the nominee’s judicial record.

nominee

IPA: /ˌnɒmɪˈniː/
Pronunciation: nom-i-NEE
French: personne nommée ; candidat désigné

Definition: A person formally proposed for a position or office.

Example: The Supreme Court nominee faced difficult questions.

appointment

IPA: /əˈpɔɪntmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-POINT-muhnt
French: nomination

Definition: The act of choosing someone for an official position.

Example: The appointment sparked controversy.

budget

IPA: /ˈbʌdʒɪt/
Pronunciation: BUJ-it
French: budget

Definition: A plan for government income and spending.

Example: Parliament must approve the annual budget.

spending bill

IPA: /ˈspendɪŋ bɪl/
Pronunciation: SPEN-ding bil
French: loi de finances ; projet de dépenses

Definition: A proposed law authorising government spending.

Example: The spending bill included extra funding for hospitals.

appropriations

IPA: /əˌprəʊpriˈeɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: uh-proh-pree-AY-shuhnz
French: crédits budgétaires

Definition: Money officially allocated by a legislature for government spending.

Example: The committee oversees defence appropriations.

government shutdown

IPA: /ˈɡʌvənmənt ˈʃʌtdaʊn/
Pronunciation: GUV-uhn-muhnt SHUT-down
French: fermeture partielle de l’administration

Definition: A situation in the US when parts of the federal government stop operating because funding has not been approved.

Example: The budget deadlock led to a government shutdown.

deadlock

IPA: /ˈdedlɒk/
Pronunciation: DED-lok
French: blocage ; impasse

Definition: A situation in which no agreement can be reached.

Example: The talks ended in political deadlock.

gridlock

IPA: /ˈɡrɪdlɒk/
Pronunciation: GRID-lok
French: blocage institutionnel

Definition: A political situation in which institutions cannot make decisions because of disagreement.

Example: Partisan gridlock prevented major reforms.

bipartisan

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːtɪzən/
Pronunciation: bye-PAR-ti-zuhn
French: bipartisan ; transpartisan

Definition: Supported by two major political parties.

Example: The bill received bipartisan support.

E. Budget, deadlock, lobbying and high-value verbs

cross-party

IPA: /ˌkrɒs ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: kross PAR-tee
French: transpartisan

Definition: Involving or supported by several political parties.

Example: A cross-party group called for electoral reform.

partisan

IPA: /ˈpɑːtɪzən/
Pronunciation: PAR-ti-zuhn
French: partisan

Definition: Strongly loyal to a political party, sometimes in a biased way.

Example: The debate became increasingly partisan.

lobby

IPA: /ˈlɒbi/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee
French: lobby ; groupe de pression

Definition: A group that tries to influence politicians or policy.

Example: The energy lobby opposed the new climate law.

lobbyist

IPA: /ˈlɒbiɪst/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee-ist
French: lobbyiste

Definition: A person paid to influence politicians or government decisions.

Example: Lobbyists met lawmakers before the vote.

lobbying

IPA: /ˈlɒbiɪŋ/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee-ing
French: lobbying ; pression politique

Definition: The activity of trying to influence political decisions.

Example: Lobbying can raise concerns about transparency.

interest group

IPA: /ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: IN-truhst group
French: groupe d’intérêt

Definition: An organised group that tries to influence policy on behalf of its members or cause.

Example: Interest groups play an important role in policy debates.

pressure group

IPA: /ˈpreʃə ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: PRESH-uh group
French: groupe de pression

Definition: An organisation that tries to influence government policy without seeking office.

Example: Pressure groups campaigned against the reform.

think tank

IPA: /ˈθɪŋk tæŋk/
Pronunciation: think tank
French: groupe de réflexion ; think tank

Definition: An organisation that produces research and policy ideas.

Example: A conservative think tank published a report on tax cuts.

to lobby lawmakers

IPA: /ˈlɒbi ˈlɔːˌmeɪkəz/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee LAW-may-kuhz
French: faire pression sur les élus

Definition: To try to influence elected officials.

Example: Technology firms lobbied lawmakers over data regulation.

to hold hearings

IPA: /həʊld ˈhɪərɪŋz/
Pronunciation: hohld HEER-ingz
French: tenir des auditions

Definition: To organise formal sessions to gather evidence or question witnesses.

Example: The committee held hearings on police reform.

to scrutinise government

IPA: /ˈskruːtɪnaɪz ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: SKROO-ti-nyz GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: contrôler ; examiner l’action du gouvernement

Definition: To examine government actions carefully.

Example: Parliament has a duty to scrutinise government.

to hold to account

IPA: /həʊld tuː əˈkaʊnt/
Pronunciation: hohld too uh-KOWNT
French: demander des comptes

Definition: To make someone explain and accept responsibility for their actions.

Example: Committees can hold ministers to account.

to block legislation

IPA: /blɒk ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: blok lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: bloquer une loi

Definition: To prevent a proposed law from being passed.

Example: The upper house tried to block the legislation.

to push through a reform

IPA: /pʊʃ θruː ə rɪˈfɔːm/
Pronunciation: push throo uh ri-FORM
French: faire passer une réforme en force

Definition: To get a reform approved despite opposition.

Example: The government used its majority to push through the reform.
