Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse artificial intelligence and employment: automation, productivity, job displacement, task automation, white-collar work, reskilling, AI literacy, labour-market polarisation, algorithmic management, workplace surveillance, bias, regulation and the need for a just transition.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as generative AI, task automation, job displacement, AI literacy, algorithmic management, workplace surveillance, algorithmic bias, human oversight, robot tax and just transition.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain the tension between productivity gains and social risks: AI can augment human work, but it can also replace tasks, widen inequality and weaken worker autonomy.
For essaysMemorise the collocations: to automate tasks, to boost productivity, to reskill workers, to regulate AI, to share productivity gains.

A. Core AI and automation vocabulary

artificial intelligence

IPA: /ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃəl ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns/
Pronunciation: ar-ti-FISH-uhl in-TEL-i-juhns
French: intelligence artificielle

Definition: Computer systems able to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

Example: Artificial intelligence is transforming the labour market.

AI

IPA: /ˌeɪ ˈaɪ/
Pronunciation: ay-eye
French: IA

Definition: Abbreviation for artificial intelligence.

Example: AI can automate routine tasks and support decision-making.

IPA: /ˌdʒenərətɪv ˌeɪ ˈaɪ/
Pronunciation: JEN-uh-ruh-tiv ay-eye

machine learning

IPA: /məˌʃiːn ˈlɜːnɪŋ/
Pronunciation: muh-SHEEN LUR-ning
French: apprentissage automatique

Definition: A type of AI in which systems learn patterns from data.

Example: Machine learning is used in recruitment, finance and healthcare.

algorithm

IPA: /ˈælɡərɪðəm/
Pronunciation: AL-guh-rith-uhm
French: algorithme

Definition: A set of rules or instructions used by a computer to solve a problem.

Example: Algorithms increasingly shape hiring and management decisions.

automation

IPA: /ˌɔːtəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: aw-tuh-MAY-shuhn
French: automatisation
