Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse democracy, democratic norms, institutional safeguards, rights, freedoms, democratic erosion and authoritarian drift. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as democratic backsliding, rule of law, independent judiciary, civil liberties, executive overreach and free press.
For oral exams Use this vocabulary to move beyond “democracy is in danger” and explain exactly which institutions, rights or norms are being weakened.
For essays Memorise the collocations: to uphold the rule of law, to weaken institutions, to muzzle the press, to hold leaders accountable.

A. Democratic models and principles

democracy

IPA: /dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie

Definition: A political system in which citizens choose their leaders through free elections and enjoy political rights and freedoms.

Example: Democracy depends on elections, rights, institutions and public trust.

liberal democracy

IPA: /ˈlɪbərəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: LIB-uh-ruhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie libérale

Definition: A democracy that protects individual rights, minority rights, the rule of law and checks on government power.

Example: A liberal democracy protects citizens even when they disagree with the government.

constitutional democracy

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie constitutionnelle

Definition: A democracy in which political power is limited by a constitution and legal safeguards.

Example: In a constitutional democracy, leaders cannot simply ignore the law.

electoral democracy

IPA: /ɪˌlektərəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie électorale

Definition: A political system with regular elections, though rights and institutions may be weaker than in a liberal democracy.

Example: An electoral democracy may hold elections while still restricting civil liberties.

majoritarian democracy

IPA: /məˌdʒɒrɪˈteəriən dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: muh-jor-i-TAIR-ee-uhn di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie majoritaire

Definition: A model of democracy in which the majority has strong power to make decisions.

Example: Majoritarian democracy can be efficient, but it may overlook minority rights.

participatory democracy

IPA: /pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪtəri dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: par-tis-i-PAY-tuh-ree di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie participative

Definition: A model of democracy that encourages citizens to be actively involved in political decisions.

Example: Participatory democracy aims to involve citizens beyond election day.

deliberative democracy

IPA: /dɪˈlɪbərətɪv dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: di-LIB-uh-ruh-tiv di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie délibérative

Definition: A model of democracy based on public discussion, debate and reasoned decision-making.

Example: Citizens' assemblies are often presented as examples of deliberative democracy.

democratic values

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk ˈvæljuːz/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik VAL-yooz
French: valeurs démocratiques

Definition: Principles such as freedom, equality, fairness, tolerance and respect for the rule of law.

Example: Democratic values must be taught, defended and practised.

political pluralism

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈplʊərəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl PLOOR-uh-liz-uhm
French: pluralisme politique

Definition: The existence of different political parties, opinions and groups in society.

Example: Political pluralism allows citizens to choose between competing ideas.

free and fair elections

IPA: /ˌfriː ən ˌfeər ɪˈlekʃənz/
Pronunciation: free uhn fair i-LEK-shuhnz
French: élections libres et équitables

Definition: Elections in which voters can choose freely and results are counted honestly.

Example: Free and fair elections are a basic requirement of democracy.

universal suffrage

IPA: /ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl ˈsʌfrɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: yoo-ni-VUR-suhl SUF-rij
French: suffrage universel

Definition: The right of all adult citizens to vote, without discrimination based on wealth, gender, race or status.

Example: Universal suffrage was achieved only gradually in many countries.

political participation

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl par-tis-i-PAY-shuhn
French: participation politique

Definition: The ways citizens take part in political life, such as voting, protesting, campaigning or joining organisations.

Example: Low political participation can weaken democratic legitimacy.

civic engagement

IPA: /ˈsɪvɪk ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: SIV-ik in-GAYJ-muhnt
French: engagement citoyen

Definition: Active involvement in public life and community or political issues.

Example: Civic engagement is essential for a healthy democracy.

civil society

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl səˈsaɪəti/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl suh-SY-uh-tee
French: société civile

Definition: Organisations and groups outside the state and the market, such as charities, unions, NGOs and associations.

Example: Civil society can hold governments accountable.

public sphere

IPA: /ˈpʌblɪk sfɪə/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik sfeer
French: espace public

Definition: The space where citizens discuss public issues, through media, debate and civic life.

Example: Social media has transformed the public sphere.

public debate

IPA: /ˈpʌblɪk dɪˈbeɪt/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik di-BAYT
French: débat public

Definition: Open discussion about political or social issues.

Example: A democracy needs informed public debate.

rule of law

IPA: /ˌruːl əv ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: rool ov law
French: État de droit ; primauté du droit

Definition: The principle that everyone, including leaders and governments, must obey the law.

Example: The rule of law protects citizens against arbitrary power.

B. Institutions and safeguards

independent judiciary

IPA: /ˌɪndɪˈpendənt dʒuːˈdɪʃəri/
Pronunciation: in-di-PEN-duhnt joo-DISH-uh-ree
French: justice indépendante ; pouvoir judiciaire indépendant

Definition: A court system that is free from political pressure and can apply the law fairly.

Example: An independent judiciary can block unconstitutional measures.

constitutional safeguards

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈseɪfɡɑːdz/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl SAYF-gardz
French: garanties constitutionnelles

Definition: Legal protections designed to prevent abuses of power.

Example: Constitutional safeguards can prevent democratic erosion.

checks and balances

IPA: /ˌtʃeks ən ˈbælənsɪz/
Pronunciation: cheks uhn BAL-uhn-siz
French: contre-pouvoirs ; freins et contrepoids

Definition: A system in which different branches of government can limit one another’s power.

Example: Checks and balances make it harder for a leader to concentrate power.

separation of powers

IPA: /ˌsepəˈreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: sep-uh-RAY-shuhn ov POW-uhz
French: séparation des pouvoirs

Definition: The division of state power among the executive, legislature and judiciary.

Example: The separation of powers is a key principle of liberal democracy.

accountability

IPA: /əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: uh-kown-tuh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité ; obligation de rendre des comptes

Definition: The obligation of leaders and institutions to explain their actions and accept responsibility.

Example: Democratic accountability requires scrutiny by parliament, courts and the media.

transparency

IPA: /trænsˈpærənsi/
Pronunciation: trans-PAIR-uhn-see
French: transparence

Definition: Openness in decision-making, allowing citizens to understand how decisions are made.

Example: Transparency can reduce corruption and increase public trust.

oversight

IPA: /ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle ; surveillance institutionnelle

Definition: The process of monitoring and checking the actions of governments, institutions or officials.

Example: Parliamentary oversight is essential in a democracy.

independent media

IPA: /ˌɪndɪˈpendənt ˈmiːdiə/
Pronunciation: in-di-PEN-duhnt MEE-dee-uh
French: médias indépendants

Definition: Media organisations that are free from government control or intimidation.

Example: Independent media can expose corruption and abuses of power.

free press

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈpres/
Pronunciation: free press
French: presse libre

Definition: Media that can report, investigate and criticise power without censorship.

Example: A free press is one of the pillars of democracy.

watchdog

IPA: /ˈwɒtʃdɒɡ/
Pronunciation: WATCH-dog
French: chien de garde ; organisme de contrôle

Definition: A person, group or institution that monitors those in power and exposes wrongdoing.

Example: Journalists often act as watchdogs in democratic societies.

whistleblower

IPA: /ˈwɪsəlbləʊə/
Pronunciation: WIS-uhl-bloh-uh
French: lanceur d’alerte ; lanceuse d’alerte

Definition: A person who reveals secret or illegal activity within an organisation.

Example: The whistleblower exposed illegal surveillance practices.

civil liberties

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈlɪbətiz/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl LIB-uh-teez
French: libertés civiles

Definition: Basic freedoms protected by law, such as freedom of speech, assembly and religion.

Example: Civil liberties can be threatened during a state of emergency.

C. Rights, freedoms and participation

freedom of speech

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov speech
French: liberté d’expression

Definition: The right to express opinions and ideas without unjustified government restriction.

Example: Freedom of speech protects unpopular as well as popular opinions.

freedom of assembly

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv əˈsembli/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov uh-SEM-blee
French: liberté de réunion ; droit de rassemblement

Definition: The right to gather publicly and peacefully, often to protest or express political views.

Example: Freedom of assembly allows citizens to protest peacefully.

freedom of association

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov uh-soh-see-AY-shuhn
French: liberté d’association

Definition: The right to join groups, parties, unions or organisations.

Example: Freedom of association protects trade unions and political parties.

minority rights

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee rites
French: droits des minorités

Definition: Rights protecting groups that are smaller in number or politically less powerful.

Example: A democracy must protect minority rights, not only majority rule.

human rights

IPA: /ˌhjuːmən ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: HYOO-muhn rites
French: droits humains ; droits de l’homme

Definition: Fundamental rights believed to belong to every human being.

Example: Human rights organisations criticised the government’s crackdown.

political rights

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl rites
French: droits politiques

Definition: Rights that allow people to participate in political life, such as voting or standing for office.

Example: Political rights are meaningless if elections are not free.

to uphold rights

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD rites
French: défendre les droits ; faire respecter les droits

Definition: To defend, respect or maintain legal and political rights.

Example: Courts are expected to uphold citizens’ rights.

to safeguard freedoms

IPA: /ˈseɪfɡɑːd ˈfriːdəmz/
Pronunciation: SAYF-gard FREE-duhmz
French: préserver les libertés

Definition: To protect freedoms from threats or abuses.

Example: Independent institutions help safeguard freedoms.

democratic backsliding

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk ˈbækslaɪdɪŋ/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik BAK-sly-ding
French: recul démocratique ; régression démocratique

Definition: The gradual weakening of democratic institutions, norms, rights or practices.

Example: Attacks on courts and journalists are signs of democratic backsliding.

democratic erosion

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk ɪˈrəʊʒən/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik i-ROH-zhuhn
French: érosion démocratique

Definition: A gradual decline in the quality or strength of democracy.

Example: Democratic erosion often happens step by step rather than overnight.

D. Democratic backsliding and authoritarian drift

democratic decline

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk dɪˈklaɪn/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik di-KLYNE
French: déclin démocratique

Definition: A reduction in democratic standards, rights or institutional independence.

Example: Experts warned of democratic decline after the government weakened the courts.

authoritarian drift

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən drɪft/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uhn drift
French: dérive autoritaire

Definition: A gradual movement towards authoritarian rule.

Example: The new surveillance law was seen as part of an authoritarian drift.

illiberal democracy

IPA: /ɪˈlɪbərəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: i-LIB-uh-ruhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie illibérale

Definition: A system with elections but weak civil liberties, limited checks and balances, and pressure on independent institutions.

Example: An illiberal democracy may keep elections while restricting media freedom.

hybrid regime

IPA: /ˈhaɪbrɪd reɪˈʒiːm/
Pronunciation: HY-brid ray-ZHEEM
French: régime hybride

Definition: A political system that combines democratic features, such as elections, with authoritarian practices.

Example: Some analysts describe the country as a hybrid regime.

competitive authoritarianism

IPA: /kəmˈpetɪtɪv ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kuhm-PET-i-tiv aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: autoritarisme compétitif

Definition: A system in which elections exist but the ruling party uses state power to make competition unfair.

Example: Competitive authoritarianism keeps the appearance of democracy while weakening real competition.

executive overreach

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˌəʊvəˈriːtʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OH-vuh-reech
French: abus de pouvoir exécutif ; dépassement des pouvoirs de l’exécutif

Definition: The use of executive power beyond proper legal or constitutional limits.

Example: Critics accused the president of executive overreach.

power grab

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ɡræb/
Pronunciation: POW-uh grab
French: accaparement du pouvoir ; coup de force institutionnel

Definition: An attempt to gain more power quickly or unfairly.

Example: The reform was denounced as a power grab.

concentration of power

IPA: /ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: kon-suhn-TRAY-shuhn ov POW-uh
French: concentration du pouvoir

Definition: The accumulation of political authority in the hands of one person or institution.

Example: The concentration of power in the executive alarmed observers.

to weaken institutions

IPA: /ˈwiːkən ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənz/
Pronunciation: WEE-kuhn in-sti-TOO-shuhnz
French: affaiblir les institutions

Definition: To reduce the independence, authority or effectiveness of institutions.

Example: Authoritarian leaders often weaken institutions before attacking elections.

to pack the courts

IPA: /pæk ðə ˈkɔːts/
Pronunciation: pak thuh courts
French: remplir les tribunaux de juges acquis à sa cause

Definition: To appoint many judges for political reasons in order to influence court decisions.

Example: The government was accused of trying to pack the courts.

to muzzle the press

IPA: /ˈmʌzəl ðə ˈpres/
Pronunciation: MUZ-uhl thuh press
French: museler la presse

Definition: To silence or restrict journalists and media organisations.

Example: The new media law was seen as an attempt to muzzle the press.

to silence dissent

IPA: /ˈsaɪləns dɪˈsent/
Pronunciation: SY-luhns di-SENT
French: faire taire la dissidence

Definition: To prevent people from expressing disagreement with the authorities.

Example: The regime used arrests to silence dissent.

censorship

IPA: /ˈsensəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: SEN-suh-ship
French: censure

Definition: The control or suppression of information, speech or media by authorities.

Example: Censorship increased after the protests.

crackdown

IPA: /ˈkrækdaʊn/
Pronunciation: KRAK-down
French: répression ; durcissement

Definition: Strong action by authorities to stop or punish behaviour they oppose.

Example: The crackdown on protesters drew international criticism.

political repression

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl rɪˈpreʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl ri-PRESH-uhn
French: répression politique

Definition: The use of force, intimidation or law to prevent political opposition.

Example: Political repression often targets journalists, activists and opposition leaders.

state of emergency

IPA: /ˌsteɪt əv ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi/
Pronunciation: stayt ov i-MUR-juhn-see
French: état d’urgence

Definition: A temporary situation in which the government claims special powers because of a crisis.

Example: A state of emergency can sometimes be used to limit civil liberties.

surveillance

IPA: /səˈveɪləns/
Pronunciation: suh-VAY-luhns
French: surveillance

Definition: The monitoring of people, communications or activities, often by the state.

Example: Mass surveillance raises serious questions about privacy and democracy.

propaganda

IPA: /ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/
Pronunciation: prop-uh-GAN-duh
French: propagande

Definition: Information, often biased or misleading, used to promote a political cause or government.

Example: Propaganda can shape public opinion and discredit opponents.

E. Corruption, manipulation and resilience

disinformation

IPA: /ˌdɪsɪnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dis-in-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: désinformation

Definition: False information spread deliberately to deceive people.

Example: Disinformation can undermine trust in elections.

misinformation

IPA: /ˌmɪsɪnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: mis-in-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: fausse information ; information erronée

Definition: False or inaccurate information, whether spread deliberately or not.

Example: Misinformation spreads quickly on social media.

institutional capture

IPA: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈkæptʃə/
Pronunciation: in-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl KAP-chuh
French: capture institutionnelle

Definition: The process by which independent institutions are brought under the control of a ruling party or interest group.

Example: Institutional capture can turn courts and regulators into political tools.

clientelism

IPA: /ˌklaɪənˈtelɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kly-uhn-TEL-iz-uhm
French: clientélisme

Definition: A political system in which leaders give benefits to supporters in exchange for loyalty or votes.

Example: Clientelism weakens equal citizenship and democratic accountability.

cronyism

IPA: /ˈkrəʊniɪzəm/
Pronunciation: KROH-nee-iz-uhm
French: copinage ; favoritisme

Definition: The practice of giving jobs, contracts or advantages to friends and allies.

Example: Cronyism damaged the government’s reputation.

nepotism

IPA: /ˈnepətɪzəm/
Pronunciation: NEP-uh-tiz-uhm
French: népotisme

Definition: The practice of giving jobs or advantages to relatives.

Example: The appointment was criticised as an example of nepotism.

corruption

IPA: /kəˈrʌpʃən/
Pronunciation: kuh-RUP-shuhn
French: corruption

Definition: Dishonest or illegal behaviour by people in power, often involving bribery or personal gain.

Example: Corruption scandals can destroy trust in public institutions.

bribery

IPA: /ˈbraɪbəri/
Pronunciation: BRY-buh-ree
French: pot-de-vin ; corruption

Definition: The act of offering or receiving money or gifts to influence someone’s actions illegally.

Example: Several officials were accused of bribery.

democratic resilience

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk rɪˈzɪliəns/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik ri-ZIL-yuhns
French: résilience démocratique

Definition: The ability of democratic institutions and citizens to resist threats and recover from crises.

Example: Democratic resilience depends on strong institutions and active citizens.

institutional resilience

IPA: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl rɪˈzɪliəns/
Pronunciation: in-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl ri-ZIL-yuhns
French: résilience institutionnelle

Definition: The ability of institutions to withstand pressure, crisis or political attack.

Example: Institutional resilience helped prevent a constitutional crisis.

to defend democracy

IPA: /dɪˈfend dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: di-FEND di-MOK-ruh-see
French: défendre la démocratie

Definition: To protect democratic institutions, rights and norms.

Example: Citizens, judges and journalists all have a role to play in defending democracy.

to restore trust

IPA: /rɪˈstɔː ˈtrʌst/
Pronunciation: ri-STOR trust
French: rétablir la confiance

Definition: To rebuild confidence in institutions, leaders or democratic processes.

Example: The reform aimed to restore trust in parliament.

to hold leaders accountable

IPA: /həʊld ˈliːdəz əˈkaʊntəbəl/
Pronunciation: hohld LEE-duhz uh-KOWN-tuh-buhl
French: demander des comptes aux dirigeants

Definition: To make leaders explain their actions and accept consequences for wrongdoing.

Example: A free press helps hold leaders accountable.

to challenge abuses of power

IPA: /ˈtʃælɪndʒ əˈbjuːsɪz əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: CHAL-inj uh-BYOO-ziz ov POW-uh
French: contester les abus de pouvoir

Definition: To oppose or question the improper use of authority.

Example: Independent courts can challenge abuses of power.

to protect minority rights

IPA: /prəˈtekt maɪˈnɒrəti ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: pruh-TEKT my-NOR-uh-tee rites
French: protéger les droits des minorités

Definition: To ensure that minority groups are not oppressed or ignored by the majority.

Example: Democracy must protect minority rights as well as majority rule.
