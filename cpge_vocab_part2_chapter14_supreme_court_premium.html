Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse the US Supreme Court: justices, nominations, judicial review, constitutional interpretation, legal procedure, landmark rulings, precedent, abortion rights, gun rights, civil liberties and the Court’s legitimacy. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

A. The Court, justices and appointments

Supreme Court

IPA: /suːˌpriːm ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: soo-PREEM court
French: Cour suprême

Definition: The highest court in the United States and the final interpreter of the Constitution.

Example: The Supreme Court plays a decisive role in American political life.

SCOTUS

IPA: /ˈskəʊtəs/
Pronunciation: SKOH-tuhs
French: SCOTUS ; Cour suprême des États-Unis

Definition: An acronym for the Supreme Court of the United States.

Example: SCOTUS rulings can reshape national policy.

the Court

IPA: /ðə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: thuh court
French: la Cour

Definition: A common shortened way to refer to the Supreme Court in political and legal articles.

Example: The Court heard arguments in a major abortion case.

justice

IPA: /ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: JUS-tis
French: juge de la Cour suprême

Definition: A judge serving on the Supreme Court.

Example: Each justice has a lifetime appointment.

Chief Justice

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: cheef JUS-tis
French: président de la Cour suprême

Definition: The leading justice who presides over the Supreme Court.

Example: The Chief Justice wrote the majority opinion.

Associate Justice

IPA: /əˌsəʊsiət ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: uh-SOH-see-uht JUS-tis
French: juge assesseur de la Cour suprême

Definition: A Supreme Court justice other than the Chief Justice.

Example: The Associate Justice joined the dissenting opinion.

bench

IPA: /bentʃ/
Pronunciation: bench
French: magistrature ; juges d’une cour

Definition: The judges of a court considered collectively.

Example: The current bench has a conservative majority.

nine-member court

IPA: /ˌnaɪn ˈmembə kɔːt/
Pronunciation: nine MEM-buh court
French: Cour à neuf juges

Definition: The Supreme Court composed of nine justices, as it currently is.

Example: The nine-member Court can be deeply divided on ideological lines.

lifetime appointment

IPA: /ˌlaɪftaɪm əˈpɔɪntmənt/
Pronunciation: LYF-tyme uh-POINT-muhnt
French: nomination à vie

Definition: An appointment that lasts for the rest of a justice’s life, unless they resign, retire or are removed.

Example: Lifetime appointments make Supreme Court nominations extremely consequential.

judicial independence

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl ˌɪndɪˈpendəns/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl in-di-PEN-duhns
French: indépendance judiciaire

Definition: The principle that judges should decide cases without political pressure.

Example: Judicial independence is essential to the rule of law.

nomination

IPA: /ˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: nom-i-NAY-shuhn
French: nomination

Definition: The act of formally proposing someone for a position, such as the Supreme Court.

Example: The nomination sparked a fierce political battle.

nominee

IPA: /ˌnɒmɪˈniː/
Pronunciation: nom-i-NEE
French: personne nommée ; candidat désigné

Definition: A person formally proposed for a position.

Example: The nominee faced questions about abortion and executive power.

confirmation

IPA: /ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: confirmation ; approbation

Definition: Formal approval of a nominee, usually by the Senate.

Example: The confirmation vote was extremely close.

confirmation hearing

IPA: /ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn HEER-ing
French: audition de confirmation

Definition: A Senate hearing used to question a nominee before confirmation.

Example: The confirmation hearing became a national media event.

Senate Judiciary Committee

IPA: /ˌsenət dʒuːˈdɪʃəri kəˈmɪti/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht joo-DISH-uh-ree kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission judiciaire du Sénat

Definition: The Senate committee that reviews judicial nominations and legal issues.

Example: The Senate Judiciary Committee questioned the nominee for several days.

senatorial approval

IPA: /ˌsenəˈtɔːriəl əˈpruːvəl/
Pronunciation: sen-uh-TOR-ee-uhl uh-PROO-vuhl
French: approbation du Sénat

Definition: Approval by the Senate, especially of a presidential nominee.

Example: Supreme Court nominees require senatorial approval.

to nominate a justice

IPA: /ˈnɒmɪneɪt ə ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: NOM-i-nayt uh JUS-tis
French: nommer un juge à la Cour suprême

Definition: To formally propose someone for a seat on the Supreme Court.

Example: The President nominated a conservative justice.

to confirm a nominee

IPA: /kənˈfɜːm ə ˌnɒmɪˈniː/
Pronunciation: kuhn-FURM uh nom-i-NEE
French: confirmer une nomination

Definition: To formally approve a person proposed for a position.

Example: The Senate confirmed the nominee by a narrow margin.

to appoint a justice

IPA: /əˈpɔɪnt ə ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: uh-POINT uh JUS-tis
French: nommer officiellement un juge

Definition: To officially choose someone to serve on the Supreme Court.

Example: Presidents can shape the Court by appointing justices.

B. Judicial review and constitutional interpretation

judicial review

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl rɪˈvjuː/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl ri-VYOO
French: contrôle de constitutionnalité

Definition: The power of courts to decide whether laws or government actions are constitutional.

Example: Judicial review allows the Supreme Court to strike down laws.

constitutionality

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪtjuːʃəˈnæləti/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-too-shuh-NAL-uh-tee
French: constitutionnalité

Definition: The quality of being allowed or valid under the Constitution.

Example: The case questioned the constitutionality of the law.

unconstitutional

IPA: /ˌʌnˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl/
Pronunciation: un-kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl
French: inconstitutionnel

Definition: Not allowed by the Constitution.

Example: The Court ruled that the law was unconstitutional.

constitutional

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl
French: constitutionnel

Definition: Allowed by or related to the Constitution.

Example: The justices disagreed over the constitutional meaning of the text.

constitutional interpretation

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl in-tur-pri-TAY-shuhn
French: interprétation constitutionnelle

Definition: The process of deciding what constitutional provisions mean.

Example: Constitutional interpretation often divides liberal and conservative justices.

legal interpretation

IPA: /ˈliːɡəl ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: LEE-guhl in-tur-pri-TAY-shuhn
French: interprétation juridique

Definition: The process of deciding the meaning of laws or legal texts.

Example: The ruling depended on legal interpretation.

originalism

IPA: /əˈrɪdʒənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: uh-RIJ-uh-nuhl-iz-uhm
French: originalisme

Definition: A method of interpretation based on the original meaning of the constitutional text.

Example: Originalism is influential among conservative justices.

textualism

IPA: /ˈtekstʃuəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: TEKS-choo-uh-liz-uhm
French: textualisme

Definition: A method of interpretation focused on the ordinary meaning of the legal text.

Example: Textualism gives priority to the words of the statute.

living constitution

IPA: /ˌlɪvɪŋ ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: LIV-ing kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution vivante

Definition: The view that the Constitution should be interpreted in light of changing social conditions.

Example: Supporters of a living constitution argue that rights evolve over time.

strict construction

IPA: /ˌstrɪkt kənˈstrʌkʃən/
Pronunciation: strikt kuhn-STRUK-shuhn
French: interprétation stricte

Definition: A narrow interpretation of the Constitution or legal text.

Example: Strict construction limits judicial creativity.

broad interpretation

IPA: /ˌbrɔːd ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: broad in-tur-pri-TAY-shuhn
French: interprétation large

Definition: An interpretation that gives a legal text a wider meaning or application.

Example: A broad interpretation of rights can expand legal protections.

judicial philosophy

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl fɪˈlɒsəfi/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl fi-LOS-uh-fee
French: philosophie judiciaire

Definition: A judge’s general approach to interpreting law and the Constitution.

Example: A nominee’s judicial philosophy is heavily scrutinised.

judicial restraint

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl rɪˈstreɪnt/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl ri-STRAYNT
French: retenue judiciaire

Definition: The view that judges should avoid overturning laws or making broad policy decisions.

Example: Supporters of judicial restraint say courts should defer to elected branches.

judicial activism

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl ˈæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme judiciaire

Definition: The criticism or idea that judges use their power to shape policy rather than merely interpret law.

Example: Critics accused the Court of judicial activism.

conservative majority

IPA: /kənˈsɜːvətɪv məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SUR-vuh-tiv muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité conservatrice

Definition: A majority of justices with conservative legal or political leanings.

Example: The conservative majority changed the direction of the Court.

liberal minority

IPA: /ˈlɪbərəl maɪˈnɒrəti/
Pronunciation: LIB-uh-ruhl my-NOR-uh-tee
French: minorité libérale

Definition: A minority of justices with liberal legal or political leanings.

Example: The liberal minority dissented strongly.

ideological balance

IPA: /ˌaɪdiəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈbæləns/
Pronunciation: eye-dee-uh-LOJ-i-kuhl BAL-uhns
French: équilibre idéologique

Definition: The distribution of ideological views among justices.

Example: A single appointment can shift the Court’s ideological balance.

court packing

IPA: /ˈkɔːt ˌpækɪŋ/
Pronunciation: court PAK-ing
French: élargissement stratégique de la Cour ; court packing

Definition: The proposal or practice of adding judges to a court to change its ideological balance.

Example: Court packing is controversial because it may politicise the judiciary.

to pack the Court

IPA: /pæk ðə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: pak thuh court
French: ajouter des juges pour changer l’équilibre de la Cour

Definition: To add justices to the Supreme Court for political advantage.

Example: Some activists urged Democrats to pack the Court.

case

IPA: /keɪs/
Pronunciation: kayss
French: affaire ; dossier judiciaire

Definition: A legal dispute brought before a court.

Example: The case reached the Supreme Court.

C. Cases, procedure and legal actors

lawsuit

IPA: /ˈlɔːsuːt/
Pronunciation: LAW-soot
French: procès ; action en justice

Definition: A legal case brought by one party against another.

Example: The lawsuit challenged the state’s abortion law.

litigation

IPA: /ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: lit-i-GAY-shuhn
French: contentieux ; procédure judiciaire

Definition: The process of taking legal action in court.

Example: Years of litigation followed the new regulation.

legal challenge

IPA: /ˈliːɡəl ˈtʃælɪndʒ/
Pronunciation: LEE-guhl CHAL-inj
French: recours juridique ; contestation en justice

Definition: A formal attempt to oppose a law or decision in court.

Example: Civil rights groups filed a legal challenge.

petition

IPA: /pəˈtɪʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-TISH-uhn
French: requête ; demande à la Cour

Definition: A formal request to a court asking it to take action.

Example: The petition asked the Court to review the case.

appeal

IPA: /əˈpiːl/
Pronunciation: uh-PEEL
French: appel ; recours

Definition: A request for a higher court to review a lower court’s decision.

Example: The appeal was heard by the Supreme Court.

to hear a case

IPA: /hɪə ə ˈkeɪs/
Pronunciation: heer uh kayss
French: examiner une affaire

Definition: For a court to consider and examine a legal case.

Example: The Court agreed to hear the case.

to take up a case

IPA: /teɪk ʌp ə ˈkeɪs/
Pronunciation: tayk up uh kayss
French: se saisir d’une affaire

Definition: To agree to examine a legal case.

Example: The justices decided to take up the case.

to decline to hear a case

IPA: /dɪˈklaɪn tə hɪə ə ˈkeɪs/
Pronunciation: di-KLYNE tuh heer uh kayss
French: refuser d’examiner une affaire

Definition: To refuse to review a case.

Example: The Court declined to hear the appeal.

oral arguments

IPA: /ˌɔːrəl ˈɑːɡjəmənts/
Pronunciation: OR-uhl AR-gyuh-muhnts
French: plaidoiries orales

Definition: Spoken presentations by lawyers before a court.

Example: The justices asked sharp questions during oral arguments.

brief

IPA: /briːf/
Pronunciation: breef
French: mémoire juridique

Definition: A written legal argument submitted to a court.

Example: The lawyers filed a brief defending the law.

amicus brief

IPA: /əˈmiːkəs briːf/
Pronunciation: uh-MEE-kuhs breef
French: mémoire d’amicus curiae ; contribution extérieure

Definition: A legal brief submitted by someone who is not a party to the case but has an interest in it.

Example: Several civil rights organisations filed amicus briefs.

plaintiff

IPA: /ˈpleɪntɪf/
Pronunciation: PLAYN-tif
French: plaignant ; demandeur

Definition: The person or group bringing a legal case.

Example: The plaintiff argued that the law violated the Constitution.

defendant

IPA: /dɪˈfendənt/
Pronunciation: di-FEN-duhnt
French: défendeur ; accusé

Definition: The person, organisation or government being sued or accused.

Example: The defendant was the state government.

appellant

IPA: /əˈpelənt/
Pronunciation: uh-PEL-uhnt
French: appelant ; requérant en appel

Definition: The party that appeals a lower court decision.

Example: The appellant asked the Court to overturn the ruling.

respondent

IPA: /rɪˈspɒndənt/
Pronunciation: ri-SPON-duhnt
French: défendeur au pourvoi ; intimé

Definition: The party responding to an appeal or petition.

Example: The respondent defended the lower court’s decision.

ruling

IPA: /ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: ROO-ling
French: décision de justice

Definition: An official decision made by a court.

Example: The ruling sparked intense political debate.

decision

IPA: /dɪˈsɪʒən/
Pronunciation: di-SIZH-uhn
French: décision

Definition: A court’s formal answer to a legal question.

Example: The decision changed the legal landscape.

opinion

IPA: /əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion ; avis de la Cour

Definition: A written explanation of a court’s decision.

Example: The opinion was more than one hundred pages long.

majority opinion

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion majoritaire

Definition: The opinion joined by most justices and representing the Court’s ruling.

Example: The majority opinion struck down the law.

dissenting opinion

IPA: /dɪˈsentɪŋ əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: di-SEN-ting uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion dissidente

Definition: An opinion written by a justice who disagrees with the majority.

Example: The dissenting opinion warned that rights were being weakened.

D. Rulings, opinions and precedent

concurring opinion

IPA: /kənˈkɜːrɪŋ əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: kuhn-KUR-ing uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion concordante

Definition: An opinion agreeing with the result but giving different reasons.

Example: The justice wrote a concurring opinion.

unanimous decision

IPA: /juːˈnænɪməs dɪˈsɪʒən/
Pronunciation: yoo-NAN-i-muhs di-SIZH-uhn
French: décision unanime

Definition: A decision supported by all justices.

Example: The Court issued a unanimous decision.

split decision

IPA: /ˌsplɪt dɪˈsɪʒən/
Pronunciation: split di-SIZH-uhn
French: décision partagée

Definition: A decision in which justices are divided.

Example: The case ended in a 5–4 split decision.

5–4 ruling

IPA: /ˌfaɪv fə ˈfɔː ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: five-fuh-four ROO-ling
French: décision à cinq contre quatre

Definition: A Supreme Court ruling decided by five justices against four.

Example: The controversial 5–4 ruling divided the country.

landmark ruling

IPA: /ˈlændmɑːk ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: LAND-mark ROO-ling
French: arrêt historique ; décision majeure

Definition: A major court decision that has a lasting impact on law or society.

Example: The landmark ruling transformed abortion rights.

precedent

IPA: /ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: PRES-i-duhnt
French: précédent juridique

Definition: A previous legal decision that guides future cases.

Example: The ruling set an important precedent.

binding precedent

IPA: /ˌbaɪndɪŋ ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: BYN-ding PRES-i-duhnt
French: précédent contraignant

Definition: A precedent that lower courts must follow.

Example: Lower courts must follow binding precedent.

stare decisis

IPA: /ˌsteəri dɪˈsaɪsɪs/
Pronunciation: STAIR-ee di-SY-sis
French: stare decisis ; respect des précédents

Definition: The legal principle that courts should generally follow precedent.

Example: The justices disagreed over the meaning of stare decisis.

to set a precedent

IPA: /set ə ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: set uh PRES-i-duhnt
French: créer un précédent

Definition: To establish a legal example that may guide future cases.

Example: The case set a precedent for privacy law.

to overturn a precedent

IPA: /ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn ə ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-turn uh PRES-i-duhnt
French: renverser un précédent

Definition: To reject or reverse a previous legal rule.

Example: The Court overturned a long-standing precedent.

to uphold a law

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld ə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD uh law
French: confirmer la validité d’une loi

Definition: For a court to decide that a law is valid.

Example: The Supreme Court upheld the law.

to strike down a law

IPA: /straɪk daʊn ə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: stryke down uh law
French: annuler ; invalider une loi

Definition: For a court to declare a law invalid or unconstitutional.

Example: The Court struck down the state law.

to overturn a ruling

IPA: /ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn ə ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-turn uh ROO-ling
French: annuler ; renverser une décision

Definition: To reverse a previous court decision.

Example: The Court overturned the lower court’s ruling.

to narrow a ruling

IPA: /ˈnærəʊ ə ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: NAR-oh uh ROO-ling
French: restreindre la portée d’une décision

Definition: To limit the scope or effect of a previous decision.

Example: The Court narrowed the earlier ruling.

to broaden rights

IPA: /ˈbrɔːdən ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: BRAW-duhn rites
French: élargir les droits

Definition: To expand the scope of legal protections.

Example: The decision broadened rights for criminal defendants.

to restrict rights

IPA: /rɪˈstrɪkt ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: ri-STRIKT rites
French: restreindre les droits

Definition: To limit the scope of legal protections.

Example: Critics said the ruling restricted voting rights.

to rule in favour of

IPA: /ruːl ɪn ˈfeɪvər əv/
Pronunciation: rool in FAY-vuhr ov
French: statuer en faveur de

Definition: To decide that one side in a case is right.

Example: The Court ruled in favour of the plaintiffs.

to rule against

IPA: /ruːl əˈɡenst/
Pronunciation: rool uh-GENST
French: statuer contre

Definition: To decide against one side in a case.

Example: The Court ruled against the administration.

Roe v. Wade

IPA: /ˌrəʊ vɜːsəs ˈweɪd/
Pronunciation: roh VUR-suhs wayd
French: Roe v. Wade

Definition: The 1973 Supreme Court ruling that recognised a constitutional right to abortion before being overturned in 2022.

Example: Roe v. Wade became a central symbol in abortion-rights debates.

Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization

IPA: /dɒbz vɜːsəs ˈdʒæksən ˈwɪmɪnz helθ ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dobz VUR-suhs JAK-suhn WIM-inz health or-guh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization

Definition: The 2022 Supreme Court ruling that overturned Roe v. Wade.

Example: Dobbs returned abortion regulation largely to the states.

abortion rights

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən raɪts/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn rites
French: droits liés à l’avortement

Definition: Legal rights relating to access to abortion.

Example: Abortion rights became a major election issue after Dobbs.

E. Major issues, legitimacy and high-value verbs

gun rights

IPA: /ˈɡʌn raɪts/
Pronunciation: gun rites
French: droit de posséder ou porter des armes

Definition: Legal rights related to owning or carrying firearms.

Example: Gun rights cases often involve the Second Amendment.

voting rights

IPA: /ˈvəʊtɪŋ raɪts/
Pronunciation: VOH-ting rites
French: droit de vote ; droits électoraux

Definition: Legal rights protecting citizens’ ability to vote.

Example: Voting rights cases often reach the Supreme Court.

civil rights

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl rites
French: droits civiques

Definition: Rights protecting people from discrimination and guaranteeing equal treatment.

Example: Civil rights rulings have shaped American society.

civil liberties

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈlɪbətiz/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl LIB-uh-teez
French: libertés civiles

Definition: Basic freedoms protected by law, such as speech, religion and assembly.

Example: Civil liberties can be limited during security crises.

free speech

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: free speech
French: liberté d’expression

Definition: The right to express opinions without unjustified government restriction.

Example: Free speech cases are central to First Amendment law.

religious freedom

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈfriːdəm/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs FREE-duhm
French: liberté religieuse

Definition: The right to practise or reject a religion without unjustified state interference.

Example: Religious freedom cases often raise difficult constitutional questions.

affirmative action

IPA: /əˌfɜːmətɪv ˈækʃən/
Pronunciation: uh-FUR-muh-tiv AK-shuhn
French: discrimination positive

Definition: Policies designed to increase opportunities for groups historically discriminated against.

Example: The Court limited the use of affirmative action in university admissions.

equal protection

IPA: /ˌiːkwəl prəˈtekʃən/
Pronunciation: EE-kwuhl pruh-TEK-shuhn
French: égale protection de la loi

Definition: The constitutional principle that the law must protect people equally.

Example: Equal protection is central to civil rights litigation.

due process

IPA: /ˌdjuː ˈprəʊses/
Pronunciation: dyoo PROH-sess
French: procédure régulière ; garanties procédurales

Definition: Fair legal procedures required before the state deprives someone of rights, liberty or property.

Example: Due process protects individuals from arbitrary punishment.

privacy rights

IPA: /ˈprɪvəsi raɪts/
Pronunciation: PRIV-uh-see rites
French: droit à la vie privée

Definition: Legal protections for personal autonomy and private life.

Example: Privacy rights have been debated in abortion and surveillance cases.

federalism case

IPA: /ˈfedərəlɪzəm keɪs/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruh-liz-uhm kayss
French: affaire liée au fédéralisme

Definition: A case involving the division of power between federal and state governments.

Example: Many federalism cases concern the limits of federal authority.

states’ rights

IPA: /ˌsteɪts ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: stayts rites
French: droits des États fédérés

Definition: The idea that states should retain substantial powers against federal authority.

Example: States’ rights arguments are common in abortion and gun cases.

public confidence in the Court

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk ˈkɒnfɪdəns ɪn ðə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik KON-fi-duhns in thuh court
French: confiance du public dans la Cour

Definition: The degree to which citizens trust the Supreme Court as an institution.

Example: Public confidence in the Court declined after several controversial rulings.

legitimacy of the Court

IPA: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi əv ðə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: li-JIT-i-muh-see ov thuh court
French: légitimité de la Cour

Definition: The belief that the Supreme Court has the authority and credibility to decide major legal questions.

Example: Highly partisan rulings can weaken the legitimacy of the Court.

politicisation of the judiciary

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ðə dʒuːˈdɪʃəri/
Pronunciation: puh-lit-i-sy-ZAY-shuhn ov thuh joo-DISH-uh-ree
French: politisation du pouvoir judiciaire

Definition: The process by which courts or judicial appointments become strongly shaped by party politics.

Example: The politicisation of the judiciary worries many observers.

to erode trust in the Court

IPA: /ɪˈrəʊd ˈtrʌst ɪn ðə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: i-ROHD trust in thuh court
French: éroder la confiance dans la Cour

Definition: To gradually weaken public confidence in the Supreme Court.

Example: Ethics scandals can erode trust in the Court.

to reshape American law

IPA: /riːˈʃeɪp əˈmerɪkən ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: ree-SHAYP uh-MER-i-kuhn law
French: remodeler le droit américain

Definition: To change the direction or structure of US law in a lasting way.

Example: A conservative majority can reshape American law for decades.

to fuel political backlash

IPA: /ˈfjuːəl pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈbæklæʃ/
Pronunciation: FYOO-uhl puh-LIT-i-kuhl BAK-lash
French: alimenter une réaction politique hostile

Definition: To intensify a strong political reaction against a decision or policy.

Example: The ruling fuelled political backlash across the country.

to divide the country

IPA: /dɪˈvaɪd ðə ˈkʌntri/
Pronunciation: di-VYDE thuh KUN-tree
French: diviser le pays

Definition: To create or deepen national political disagreement.

Example: The decision divided the country along ideological lines.

to test the limits of federal power

IPA: /test ðə ˈlɪmɪts əv ˈfedərəl ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: test thuh LIM-its ov FED-uh-ruhl POW-uh
French: tester les limites du pouvoir fédéral

Definition: To raise legal questions about how far federal authority can go.

Example: The case tested the limits of federal power.
