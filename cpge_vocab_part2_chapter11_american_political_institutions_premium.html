Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse American political institutions: federalism, the Constitution, Congress, the presidency, the Supreme Court, state governments, elections and checks and balances. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

A. Federalism and levels of government

American institutions

IPA: /əˈmerɪkən ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənz/
Pronunciation: uh-MER-i-kuhn in-sti-TOO-shuhnz
French: institutions américaines

Definition: The main political bodies and rules that organise power in the United States.

Example: American institutions are based on federalism, checks and balances, and the separation of powers.

federal government

IPA: /ˈfedərəl ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement fédéral

Definition: The national government of the United States.

Example: The federal government is divided into three branches.

federalism

IPA: /ˈfedərəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruh-liz-uhm
French: fédéralisme

Definition: A system in which power is divided between the national government and the states.

Example: Federalism gives US states significant political authority.

federal system

IPA: /ˈfedərəl ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl SIS-tuhm
French: système fédéral

Definition: A political system in which powers are shared between central and regional governments.

Example: The United States has a federal system.

state government

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: stayt GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement d’un État fédéré

Definition: The government of one of the US states.

Example: State governments control many areas such as education and policing.

state legislature

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: stayt LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: assemblée législative d’un État

Definition: The law-making body of a US state.

Example: The state legislature passed new voting rules.

governor

IPA: /ˈɡʌvənə/
Pronunciation: GUV-uh-nuh
French: gouverneur ; gouverneure

Definition: The elected head of a US state government.

Example: The governor declared a state of emergency.

local government

IPA: /ˌləʊkəl ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: LOH-kuhl GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: collectivités locales ; gouvernement local

Definition: Government at city, county or local level.

Example: Local government is responsible for many everyday services.

county

IPA: /ˈkaʊnti/
Pronunciation: KOWN-tee
French: comté

Definition: A local administrative area in many US states.

Example: The county is responsible for local elections.

city council

IPA: /ˌsɪti ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: SIT-ee KOWN-suhl
French: conseil municipal

Definition: A local elected body that governs a city.

Example: The city council voted to increase public transport funding.

mayor

IPA: /meə/
Pronunciation: mair
French: maire

Definition: The elected head of a city or town government.

Example: The mayor announced new housing measures.

sovereignty

IPA: /ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté

Definition: The authority of a political body to govern itself.

Example: Federalism divides sovereignty between Washington and the states.

reserved powers

IPA: /rɪˈzɜːvd ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURVD POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs réservés aux États

Definition: Powers kept by the states rather than given to the federal government.

Example: Education is often linked to reserved powers.

delegated powers

IPA: /ˈdelɪɡeɪtɪd ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: DEL-i-gay-tid POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs délégués au gouvernement fédéral

Definition: Powers specifically granted to the federal government by the Constitution.

Example: The power to declare war is one of Congress’s delegated powers.

concurrent powers

IPA: /kənˈkʌrənt ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: kuhn-KUR-uhnt POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs concurrents

Definition: Powers shared by the federal and state governments.

Example: Taxation is an example of concurrent powers.

states’ rights

IPA: /ˌsteɪts ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: stayts rites
French: droits des États fédérés

Definition: The idea that US states should retain significant political authority against federal power.

Example: States’ rights are often invoked in debates over abortion and gun laws.

supremacy clause

IPA: /suːˈpreməsi klɔːz/
Pronunciation: soo-PREM-uh-see klawz
French: clause de suprématie

Definition: The constitutional rule that federal law takes precedence over state law when they conflict.

Example: The Supremacy Clause gives federal law priority over state law.

the Constitution

IPA: /ðə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: thuh kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: la Constitution américaine

Definition: The founding legal document that establishes the US system of government.

Example: The Constitution created a system of separated powers.

B. The Constitution and founding principles

constitutional republic

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl rɪˈpʌblɪk/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl ri-PUB-lik
French: république constitutionnelle

Definition: A republic governed according to a constitution that limits political power.

Example: The United States is a constitutional republic.

the Bill of Rights

IPA: /ðə ˌbɪl əv ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: thuh bil ov rites
French: Bill of Rights ; déclaration des droits

Definition: The first ten amendments to the US Constitution, protecting fundamental rights.

Example: The Bill of Rights protects freedom of speech and religion.

amendment

IPA: /əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND-muhnt
French: amendement

Definition: A formal change or addition to the Constitution.

Example: The Fourteenth Amendment is central to civil rights law.

First Amendment

IPA: /ˌfɜːst əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: first uh-MEND-muhnt
French: Premier amendement

Definition: The amendment protecting speech, religion, press, assembly and petition rights.

Example: The First Amendment protects freedom of speech.

Second Amendment

IPA: /ˌsekənd əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: SEK-uhnd uh-MEND-muhnt
French: Deuxième amendement

Definition: The amendment often associated with the right to keep and bear arms.

Example: The Second Amendment is central to gun control debates.

Fourteenth Amendment

IPA: /ˌfɔːˈtiːnθ əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: for-TEENTH uh-MEND-muhnt
French: Quatorzième amendement

Definition: The amendment protecting citizenship, due process and equal protection.

Example: The Fourteenth Amendment has shaped civil rights cases.

checks and balances

IPA: /ˌtʃeks ən ˈbælənsɪz/
Pronunciation: cheks uhn BAL-uhn-siz
French: freins et contrepoids ; contre-pouvoirs

Definition: A system in which each branch of government can limit the power of the others.

Example: Checks and balances prevent one branch from dominating the others.

separation of powers

IPA: /ˌsepəˈreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: sep-uh-RAY-shuhn ov POW-uhz
French: séparation des pouvoirs

Definition: The division of power between the executive, legislative and judicial branches.

Example: The separation of powers is central to US institutions.

branch of government

IPA: /ˌbrɑːntʃ əv ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: branch ov GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: branche du gouvernement

Definition: One of the main parts of government: executive, legislative or judicial.

Example: Each branch of government has different powers.

executive branch

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv brɑːntʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv branch
French: pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The branch led by the president and responsible for enforcing laws.

Example: The executive branch includes the president and federal agencies.

legislative branch

IPA: /ˈledʒɪslətɪv brɑːntʃ/
Pronunciation: LEDJ-is-luh-tiv branch
French: pouvoir législatif

Definition: The branch responsible for making laws, mainly Congress.

Example: The legislative branch can pass laws and approve budgets.

judicial branch

IPA: /dʒuːˈdɪʃəl brɑːntʃ/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl branch
French: pouvoir judiciaire

Definition: The branch made up of courts that interpret and apply the law.

Example: The judicial branch can review the constitutionality of laws.

C. The executive and legislative branches

President

IPA: /ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: PREZ-i-duhnt
French: président

Definition: The elected head of state and head of government in the United States.

Example: The President can veto bills passed by Congress.

Vice President

IPA: /ˌvaɪs ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: vyss PREZ-i-duhnt
French: vice-président

Definition: The official who supports the president and presides over the Senate.

Example: The Vice President can cast a tie-breaking vote in the Senate.

Cabinet

IPA: /ˈkæbɪnət/
Pronunciation: KAB-i-nuht
French: cabinet ; gouvernement présidentiel

Definition: The group of senior officials heading federal departments and advising the president.

Example: The Cabinet includes the Secretary of State and the Secretary of Defense.

federal agency

IPA: /ˈfedərəl ˈeɪdʒənsi/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl AY-juhn-see
French: agence fédérale

Definition: A government organisation responsible for a specific area of federal policy.

Example: Federal agencies enforce environmental and labour regulations.

executive order

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OR-duh
French: décret présidentiel

Definition: A directive issued by the president to manage federal government operations.

Example: The president signed an executive order on immigration.

veto

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh
French: veto

Definition: The president’s power to reject a bill passed by Congress.

Example: The president used his veto to block the bill.

commander-in-chief

IPA: /kəˌmɑːndər ɪn ˈtʃiːf/
Pronunciation: kuh-MAN-duhr in cheef
French: commandant en chef

Definition: The president’s role as head of the armed forces.

Example: As commander-in-chief, the president oversees the military.

Congress

IPA: /ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: KON-gress
French: Congrès américain

Definition: The national legislature of the United States.

Example: Congress is made up of the Senate and the House of Representatives.

House of Representatives

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˌreprɪˈzentətɪvz/
Pronunciation: howss ov rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tivz
French: Chambre des représentants

Definition: The lower chamber of Congress, with members elected by district.

Example: The House of Representatives has 435 voting members.

Senate

IPA: /ˈsenət/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht
French: Sénat

Definition: The upper chamber of Congress, with two senators from each state.

Example: The Senate confirms many presidential appointments.

representative

IPA: /ˌreprɪˈzentətɪv/
Pronunciation: rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tiv
French: représentant ; élu de la Chambre

Definition: An elected member of the House of Representatives.

Example: The representative introduced a bill on healthcare.

senator

IPA: /ˈsenətə/
Pronunciation: SEN-uh-tuh
French: sénateur ; sénatrice

Definition: An elected member of the Senate.

Example: Each state elects two senators.

congressional district

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl DIS-trikt
French: circonscription congressionnelle

Definition: A geographical area that elects one member of the House of Representatives.

Example: The district became more competitive after redistricting.

Speaker of the House

IPA: /ˌspiːkər əv ðə ˈhaʊs/
Pronunciation: SPEE-kuhr ov thuh howss
French: président de la Chambre des représentants

Definition: The presiding officer and political leader of the House of Representatives.

Example: The Speaker of the House controls much of the legislative agenda.

majority leader

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la majorité

Definition: A congressional leader of the party with the most seats.

Example: The majority leader negotiated the final bill.

minority leader

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la minorité

Definition: A congressional leader of the party with fewer seats.

Example: The minority leader criticised the spending plan.

committee

IPA: /kəˈmɪti/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission ; comité

Definition: A smaller group of lawmakers that examines specific issues or bills.

Example: The committee held hearings on social media regulation.

congressional hearing

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl HEER-ing
French: audition au Congrès

Definition: A formal session where Congress gathers evidence or questions witnesses.

Example: The congressional hearing exposed failures in oversight.

bill

IPA: /bɪl/
Pronunciation: bil
French: projet de loi

Definition: A proposed law debated and voted on by Congress.

Example: The bill passed the House but failed in the Senate.

federal law

IPA: /ˌfedərəl ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl law
French: loi fédérale

Definition: A law passed by the national government and applying across the United States.

Example: Federal law takes precedence over conflicting state law.

Supreme Court

IPA: /suːˌpriːm ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: soo-PREEM court
French: Cour suprême

Definition: The highest court in the United States.

Example: The Supreme Court plays a major role in constitutional interpretation.

justice

IPA: /ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: JUS-tis
French: juge de la Cour suprême

Definition: A judge on the Supreme Court.

Example: The justice wrote a dissenting opinion.

D. Courts, judicial review and appointments

Chief Justice

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: cheef JUS-tis
French: président de la Cour suprême

Definition: The presiding justice of the Supreme Court.

Example: The Chief Justice leads the Supreme Court.

federal court

IPA: /ˈfedərəl kɔːt/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl court
French: tribunal fédéral

Definition: A court that deals with cases involving federal law or constitutional questions.

Example: A federal court blocked the executive order.

district court

IPA: /ˈdɪstrɪkt kɔːt/
Pronunciation: DIS-trikt court
French: tribunal fédéral de première instance

Definition: A trial-level federal court in the United States.

Example: The case began in a district court.

court of appeals

IPA: /ˌkɔːt əv əˈpiːlz/
Pronunciation: court ov uh-PEELZ
French: cour d’appel fédérale

Definition: A federal court that reviews decisions from lower courts.

Example: The court of appeals upheld the ruling.

judicial review

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl rɪˈvjuː/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl ri-VYOO
French: contrôle de constitutionnalité

Definition: The power of courts to decide whether laws or actions are constitutional.

Example: Judicial review allows courts to limit government power.

landmark ruling

IPA: /ˈlændmɑːk ˈruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: LAND-mark ROO-ling
French: arrêt historique ; décision majeure

Definition: A highly important court decision that changes law or society.

Example: The landmark ruling reshaped abortion rights.

precedent

IPA: /ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: PRES-i-duhnt
French: précédent juridique

Definition: A previous legal decision that guides future cases.

Example: The ruling set an important precedent.

federal judge

IPA: /ˈfedərəl dʒʌdʒ/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl juj
French: juge fédéral

Definition: A judge serving in the federal court system.

Example: A federal judge ruled that the law was unconstitutional.

appointment

IPA: /əˈpɔɪntmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-POINT-muhnt
French: nomination

Definition: The act of choosing someone for an official position.

Example: Supreme Court appointments can shape law for decades.

confirmation

IPA: /ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: confirmation ; approbation

Definition: Formal approval of a presidential nominee, usually by the Senate.

Example: The Senate confirmation process was highly controversial.

nominee

IPA: /ˌnɒmɪˈniː/
Pronunciation: nom-i-NEE
French: personne nommée ; candidat désigné

Definition: A person formally proposed for a position or office.

Example: The Supreme Court nominee faced difficult questions.

electoral college

IPA: /ɪˌlektərəl ˈkɒlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruhl KOL-ij
French: collège électoral

Definition: The system used to formally elect the US president through state electors.

Example: A candidate can win the popular vote but lose the Electoral College.

popular vote

IPA: /ˌpɒpjələ ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: POP-yuh-luh voht
French: vote populaire ; suffrage exprimé

Definition: The total number of votes cast by citizens nationwide or in a state.

Example: She won the popular vote but lost the presidency.

swing state

IPA: /ˈswɪŋ steɪt/
Pronunciation: swing stayt
French: État pivot ; État indécis

Definition: A state where both major parties have a realistic chance of winning.

Example: Swing states receive intense attention during presidential elections.

red state

IPA: /ˌred ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: red stayt
French: État républicain

Definition: A US state that usually votes Republican.

Example: Texas has long been considered a red state.

blue state

IPA: /ˌbluː ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: bloo stayt
French: État démocrate

Definition: A US state that usually votes Democratic.

Example: California is a strongly blue state.

purple state

IPA: /ˌpɜːpəl ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: PUR-puhl stayt
French: État politiquement partagé

Definition: A state where Democrats and Republicans both have significant support.

Example: Georgia has become a more purple state.

E. Elections, impeachment and high-value verbs

primary election

IPA: /ˈpraɪməri ɪˌlekʃən/
Pronunciation: PRY-muh-ree i-LEK-shuhn
French: primaire

Definition: An election used to choose a party’s candidate.

Example: The primary election divided the party.

caucus

IPA: /ˈkɔːkəs/
Pronunciation: KAW-kuhs
French: caucus ; assemblée partisane

Definition: A meeting where party members discuss and choose candidates or policies.

Example: Iowa is famous for its presidential caucuses.

midterm elections

IPA: /ˈmɪdtɜːm ɪˌlekʃənz/
Pronunciation: MID-term i-LEK-shuhnz
French: élections de mi-mandat

Definition: Elections held halfway through a president’s four-year term.

Example: Midterm elections often punish the president’s party.

term limit

IPA: /ˈtɜːm ˌlɪmɪt/
Pronunciation: term LIM-it
French: limite de mandat

Definition: A legal limit on how long someone can hold an office.

Example: The president is subject to a two-term limit.

impeachment

IPA: /ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt/
Pronunciation: im-PEECH-muhnt
French: procédure de destitution

Definition: A formal process for charging a high official with serious misconduct.

Example: The impeachment process dominated the news.

to impeach

IPA: /ɪmˈpiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: im-PEECH
French: mettre en accusation ; engager une procédure de destitution

Definition: To formally accuse a high official of serious misconduct.

Example: The House voted to impeach the president.

to confirm a nominee

IPA: /kənˈfɜːm ə ˌnɒmɪˈniː/
Pronunciation: kuhn-FURM uh nom-i-NEE
French: confirmer une nomination

Definition: To formally approve a person proposed for an official post.

Example: The Senate confirmed the nominee by a narrow vote.

to veto a bill

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh uh bil
French: opposer son veto à un projet de loi

Definition: To reject a proposed law officially.

Example: The president threatened to veto the bill.

to override a veto

IPA: /ˌəʊvəˈraɪd ə ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-ryd uh VEE-toh
French: passer outre un veto

Definition: To pass a bill despite a presidential veto, usually with a special majority.

Example: Congress failed to override the veto.

to pass legislation

IPA: /pɑːs ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: pass lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: adopter une loi ; adopter une législation

Definition: To approve laws through the legislative process.

Example: Congress struggled to pass legislation on immigration.

to uphold a law

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld ə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD uh law
French: confirmer la validité d’une loi

Definition: For a court to confirm that a law is valid.

Example: The Supreme Court upheld the law.

to strike down a law

IPA: /straɪk daʊn ə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: stryke down uh law
French: annuler ; invalider une loi

Definition: For a court to declare a law invalid or unconstitutional.

Example: The Court struck down the state law.

to challenge federal authority

IPA: /ˈtʃælɪndʒ ˈfedərəl ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: CHAL-inj FED-uh-ruhl aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: contester l’autorité fédérale

Definition: To dispute the power of the national government.

Example: Several states challenged federal authority in court.

to defend states’ rights

IPA: /dɪˈfend ˌsteɪts ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: di-FEND stayts rites
French: défendre les droits des États

Definition: To argue that states should retain significant powers.

Example: Conservatives often defend states’ rights in constitutional debates.

to expand federal power

IPA: /ɪkˈspænd ˈfedərəl ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: ik-SPAND FED-uh-ruhl POW-uh
French: étendre le pouvoir fédéral

Definition: To increase the authority of the national government.

Example: The crisis led to an expansion of federal power.
