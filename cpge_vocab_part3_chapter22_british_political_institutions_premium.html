Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse British political institutions: the Westminster system, Parliament, the monarchy, the Prime Minister, Cabinet, Whitehall, the opposition, devolution, parliamentary sovereignty, elections, law-making and scrutiny.

United Kingdom Westminster Parliament Prime Minister Devolution Khôlles

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as parliamentary sovereignty, House of Commons, Prime Minister’s Questions, ministerial responsibility, devolution, first-past-the-post and hung parliament.
For oral exams Use this vocabulary to explain how power is distributed between the monarch, Parliament, the Prime Minister, Cabinet, Whitehall and devolved administrations.
For essays Memorise the collocations: to scrutinise the government, to hold ministers accountable, to command a majority, to prorogue Parliament, to restore parliamentary sovereignty.

A. The UK state, monarchy and Westminster model

British political institutions

IPA: /ˈbrɪtɪʃ pəˌlɪtɪkəl ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənz/
Pronunciation: BRIT-ish puh-LIT-i-kuhl in-sti-TOO-shuhnz
French: institutions politiques britanniques

Definition: The bodies, rules and conventions that organise political power in the United Kingdom.

Example: British political institutions combine monarchy, Parliament, government and constitutional conventions.

United Kingdom

IPA: /juːˌnaɪtɪd ˈkɪŋdəm/
Pronunciation: yoo-NY-tid KING-duhm
French: Royaume-Uni

Definition: A sovereign state made up of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Example: The United Kingdom is a union of four nations.

Great Britain

IPA: /ˌɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən/
Pronunciation: great BRIT-uhn
French: Grande-Bretagne

Definition: The island containing England, Scotland and Wales, but not Northern Ireland.

Example: Great Britain is not the same as the United Kingdom.

Westminster

IPA: /ˈwestmɪnstə/
Pronunciation: WEST-min-stuh
French: Westminster ; système politique britannique

Definition: The area where Parliament is located; also a shorthand for the UK political system.

Example: Westminster has become shorthand for British central government.

Westminster system

IPA: /ˈwestmɪnstə ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: WEST-min-stuh SIS-tuhm
French: système de Westminster

Definition: A parliamentary system based on the UK model, with government drawn from Parliament.

Example: The Westminster system relies heavily on parliamentary confidence.

parliamentary democracy

IPA: /ˌpɑːləˈmentəri dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: par-luh-MEN-tuh-ree di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie parlementaire

Definition: A system in which the government depends on support from Parliament.

Example: The UK is a parliamentary democracy.

constitutional monarchy

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie constitutionnelle

Definition: A system in which a monarch is head of state but elected institutions hold political power.

Example: The UK is a constitutional monarchy.

monarch

IPA: /ˈmɒnək/
Pronunciation: MON-uhk
French: monarque

Definition: A king or queen who serves as head of state.

Example: The monarch performs mainly ceremonial duties.

head of state

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: hed ov state
French: chef de l’État

Definition: The official representative of a country, often with symbolic duties.

Example: In Britain, the monarch is head of state.

head of government

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: hed ov GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: chef du gouvernement

Definition: The political leader who runs the government.

Example: The Prime Minister is the head of government.

the Crown

IPA: /ðə ˈkraʊn/
Pronunciation: thuh crown
French: la Couronne

Definition: The legal and symbolic authority of the state, formally represented by the monarch.

Example: Government authority is formally exercised in the name of the Crown.

royal prerogative

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl prɪˈrɒɡətɪv/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl pri-ROG-uh-tiv
French: prérogative royale

Definition: Traditional executive powers formally held by the monarch but mostly exercised by ministers.

Example: The royal prerogative is used in foreign affairs and defence.

Parliament

IPA: /ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement

Definition: The UK’s supreme legislative body, made up of the monarch, Commons and Lords.

Example: Parliament can pass, amend or repeal any law.

House of Commons

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˈkɒmənz/
Pronunciation: howss ov KOM-uhnz
French: Chambre des communes

Definition: The elected lower chamber of the UK Parliament.

Example: The government must command a majority in the House of Commons.

House of Lords

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˈlɔːdz/
Pronunciation: howss ov lordz
French: Chambre des lords

Definition: The unelected upper chamber of Parliament, which revises legislation.

Example: The House of Lords can delay and scrutinise bills.

Member of Parliament

IPA: /ˌmembər əv ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: MEM-buh ov PAR-luh-muhnt
French: député ; membre du Parlement

Definition: An elected representative in the House of Commons, usually abbreviated MP.

Example: Each MP represents a constituency.

MP

IPA: /ˌem ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: em pee
French: député ; MP

Definition: Abbreviation for Member of Parliament.

Example: The MP raised the issue during Prime Minister’s Questions.

constituency

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənsi/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhn-see
French: circonscription

Definition: A geographical area represented by one MP.

Example: Voters choose one MP for their constituency.

constituent

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhnt
French: électeur d’une circonscription ; administré

Definition: A person represented by an elected politician.

Example: MPs often hold surgeries to meet constituents.

Speaker of the House of Commons

IPA: /ˌspiːkər əv ðə ˌhaʊs əv ˈkɒmənz/
Pronunciation: SPEE-kuh ov thuh howss ov KOM-uhnz
French: président de la Chambre des communes

Definition: The official who chairs debates and maintains order in the House of Commons.

Example: The Speaker must remain politically impartial.

B. Government, Cabinet and Whitehall

Prime Minister

IPA: /ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: prime MIN-i-stuh
French: Premier ministre

Definition: The head of the UK government and leader of the ruling party or coalition.

Example: The Prime Minister appoints ministers and sets the government’s agenda.

PM

IPA: /ˌpiː ˈem/
Pronunciation: pee em
French: Premier ministre

Definition: Abbreviation for Prime Minister.

Example: The PM faced criticism over the policy.

Downing Street

IPA: /ˈdaʊnɪŋ striːt/
Pronunciation: DOWN-ing street
French: Downing Street ; bureau du Premier ministre

Definition: The Prime Minister’s official residence and office; also the Prime Minister’s team.

Example: Downing Street denied that a reshuffle was planned.

Number 10

IPA: /ˌnʌmbə ˈten/
Pronunciation: NUM-buh ten
French: 10 Downing Street

Definition: 10 Downing Street, the official residence and office of the Prime Minister.

Example: Number 10 announced the new policy.

Cabinet

IPA: /ˈkæbɪnət/
Pronunciation: KAB-i-nuht
French: cabinet ; gouvernement

Definition: The group of senior ministers who lead departments and make major policy decisions.

Example: The Cabinet met to discuss the crisis.

minister

IPA: /ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: MIN-i-stuh
French: ministre

Definition: A politician appointed to lead or assist with a government department.

Example: The minister defended the bill in Parliament.

Secretary of State

IPA: /ˌsekrətəri əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: SEK-ruh-tuh-ree ov state
French: secrétaire d’État ; ministre britannique

Definition: A senior minister in charge of a government department.

Example: The Secretary of State for Health announced reforms.

Home Secretary

IPA: /ˈhəʊm ˌsekrətəri/
Pronunciation: home SEK-ruh-tuh-ree
French: ministre de l’Intérieur britannique

Definition: The minister responsible for immigration, policing and security.

Example: The Home Secretary announced tougher immigration rules.

Foreign Secretary

IPA: /ˈfɒrən ˌsekrətəri/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn SEK-ruh-tuh-ree
French: ministre britannique des Affaires étrangères

Definition: The minister responsible for foreign affairs.

Example: The Foreign Secretary met European leaders.

Chancellor of the Exchequer

IPA: /ˈtʃɑːnsələ əv ði ɪksˈtʃekə/
Pronunciation: CHAN-suh-luh ov thee iks-CHEK-uh
French: ministre des Finances ; chancelier de l’Échiquier

Definition: The senior minister responsible for finance and the economy.

Example: The Chancellor presented the Budget.

Treasury

IPA: /ˈtreʒəri/
Pronunciation: TREZH-uh-ree
French: Trésor britannique ; ministère des Finances

Definition: The government department responsible for public finance and economic policy.

Example: The Treasury warned of spending cuts.

Home Office

IPA: /ˈhəʊm ˌɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: home OFF-is
French: ministère de l’Intérieur britannique

Definition: The department responsible for immigration, policing, security and domestic affairs.

Example: The Home Office faced criticism over asylum delays.

Foreign Office

IPA: /ˈfɒrən ˌɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn OFF-is
French: Foreign Office ; ministère des Affaires étrangères britannique

Definition: The government department responsible for foreign policy.

Example: The Foreign Office issued travel advice.

civil service

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈsɜːvɪs/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl SUR-vis
French: fonction publique

Definition: The permanent administrative staff who serve the government.

Example: The civil service is expected to be politically neutral.

civil servant

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈsɜːvənt/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl SUR-vuhnt
French: fonctionnaire

Definition: A professional employee of the state administration.

Example: Civil servants advise ministers and implement policy.

Whitehall

IPA: /ˈwaɪthɔːl/
Pronunciation: WHITE-hall
French: Whitehall ; administration centrale

Definition: The area of London with many departments; also central administration.

Example: Whitehall prepared the policy documents.

government department

IPA: /ˈɡʌvənmənt dɪˌpɑːtmənt/
Pronunciation: GUV-uhn-muhnt di-PART-muhnt
French: ministère ; département ministériel

Definition: A major administrative body responsible for a policy area.

Example: Each government department is led by a minister.

quango

IPA: /ˈkwæŋɡəʊ/
Pronunciation: KWANG-goh
French: organisme parapublic ; quango

Definition: A semi-independent public body funded by government but not directly run by ministers.

Example: Quangos are often criticised for lacking accountability.

C. Devolution and constitutional principles

devolution

IPA: /ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn
French: dévolution

Definition: The transfer of powers from central government to regional governments.

Example: Devolution has changed the UK’s constitutional structure.

devolved administration

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD ad-min-i-STRAY-shuhn
French: administration dévolue

Definition: A government in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland with powers transferred from Westminster.

Example: Devolved administrations control areas such as health and education.

Scottish Parliament

IPA: /ˌskɒtɪʃ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: SKOT-ish PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement écossais

Definition: The devolved legislature of Scotland.

Example: The Scottish Parliament has powers over health and education.

Welsh Parliament

IPA: /ˌwelʃ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: Welsh PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement gallois ; Senedd

Definition: The devolved legislature of Wales, also called the Senedd.

Example: The Welsh Parliament can pass laws in devolved areas.

Senedd

IPA: /ˈsenɛð/
Pronunciation: SEN-eth
French: Senedd ; Parlement gallois

Definition: The Welsh Parliament.

Example: The Senedd debates Welsh domestic policy.

Northern Ireland Assembly

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd əˈsembli/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd uh-SEM-blee
French: Assemblée d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The devolved legislature of Northern Ireland.

Example: The Northern Ireland Assembly depends on power-sharing.

reserved matter

IPA: /rɪˌzɜːvd ˈmætə/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURVD MAT-uh
French: compétence réservée

Definition: A policy area kept under Westminster control rather than devolved.

Example: Foreign policy is a reserved matter.

devolved matter

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ˈmætə/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD MAT-uh
French: compétence dévolue

Definition: A policy area controlled by a devolved administration.

Example: Education is a devolved matter in Scotland.

unitary state

IPA: /ˌjuːnɪtəri ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: YOO-ni-tuh-ree state
French: État unitaire

Definition: A state where ultimate legal authority rests with the central government.

Example: The UK remains a unitary state despite devolution.

parliamentary sovereignty

IPA: /ˌpɑːləˈmentəri ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: par-luh-MEN-tuh-ree SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté parlementaire

Definition: The principle that Parliament can make or unmake any law.

Example: Brexit revived debates about parliamentary sovereignty.

uncodified constitution

IPA: /ʌnˌkəʊdɪfaɪd ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: un-KOH-di-fyd kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution non codifiée

Definition: A constitution not contained in one single written document.

Example: The UK has an uncodified constitution.

constitutional convention

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl kənˈvenʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl kuhn-VEN-shuhn
French: convention constitutionnelle

Definition: An unwritten rule considered binding in political practice.

Example: By convention, the monarch acts on ministerial advice.

rule of law

IPA: /ˌruːl əv ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: rool ov law
French: État de droit ; primauté du droit

Definition: The principle that everyone, including government, must obey the law.

Example: The rule of law limits arbitrary power.

ministerial responsibility

IPA: /ˌmɪnɪˈstɪəriəl rɪˌspɒnsəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: min-i-STEER-ee-uhl ri-spon-suh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité ministérielle

Definition: The principle that ministers are accountable to Parliament.

Example: Ministerial responsibility is central to parliamentary scrutiny.

collective responsibility

IPA: /kəˌlektɪv rɪˌspɒnsəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: kuh-LEK-tiv ri-spon-suh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité collective du cabinet

Definition: The principle that Cabinet ministers must publicly support government decisions or resign.

Example: Collective responsibility helps maintain government unity.

D. Elections, opposition and party discipline

vote of confidence

IPA: /ˌvəʊt əv ˈkɒnfɪdəns/
Pronunciation: voht ov KON-fi-duhns
French: vote de confiance

Definition: A parliamentary vote showing whether the government still has support.

Example: Losing a vote of confidence can bring down a government.

no-confidence vote

IPA: /ˌnəʊ ˈkɒnfɪdəns vəʊt/
Pronunciation: noh KON-fi-duhns voht
French: motion de censure ; vote de défiance

Definition: A vote declaring that Parliament no longer supports the government.

Example: A no-confidence vote can trigger a general election.

general election

IPA: /ˌdʒenərəl ɪˈlekʃən/
Pronunciation: JEN-uh-ruhl i-LEK-shuhn
French: élections législatives britanniques

Definition: A national election to choose MPs and determine the next government.

Example: A general election must be held at least every five years.

by-election

IPA: /ˈbaɪ ɪˌlekʃən/
Pronunciation: BY i-LEK-shuhn
French: élection partielle

Definition: An election held in a single constituency to fill a vacant seat.

Example: The by-election was seen as a test for the government.

first-past-the-post

IPA: /ˌfɜːst pɑːst ðə ˈpəʊst/
Pronunciation: first past thuh post
French: scrutin uninominal majoritaire à un tour

Definition: The electoral system where the candidate with the most votes wins.

Example: First-past-the-post often favours larger parties.

hung parliament

IPA: /ˌhʌŋ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: hung PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement sans majorité absolue

Definition: A Parliament in which no party has an overall majority.

Example: A hung parliament can lead to coalition talks.

overall majority

IPA: /ˌəʊvərɔːl məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-rawl muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité absolue

Definition: A majority of seats held by one party in the House of Commons.

Example: The party won a large overall majority.

minority government

IPA: /maɪˌnɒrəti ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement minoritaire

Definition: A government formed by a party that does not hold a majority of seats.

Example: A minority government depends on support from other parties.

coalition government

IPA: /ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: koh-uh-LISH-uhn GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement de coalition

Definition: A government formed by two or more parties working together.

Example: The 2010 election produced a coalition government.

opposition

IPA: /ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən/
Pronunciation: op-uh-ZISH-uhn
French: opposition

Definition: The parties or politicians not in government who scrutinise and challenge it.

Example: The opposition accused ministers of incompetence.

Leader of the Opposition

IPA: /ˌliːdər əv ði ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən/
Pronunciation: LEE-duh ov thee op-uh-ZISH-uhn
French: chef de l’opposition

Definition: The leader of the largest party not in government.

Example: The Leader of the Opposition challenged the Prime Minister.

Shadow Cabinet

IPA: /ˈʃædəʊ ˈkæbɪnət/
Pronunciation: SHAD-oh KAB-i-nuht
French: cabinet fantôme

Definition: The senior opposition team that scrutinises corresponding government ministers.

Example: The Shadow Cabinet proposed an alternative budget.

backbencher

IPA: /ˌbækˈbentʃə/
Pronunciation: bak-BEN-chuh
French: député de base

Definition: An MP who is not a minister or party leader.

Example: Backbenchers can rebel against their own party.

frontbencher

IPA: /ˌfrʌntˈbentʃə/
Pronunciation: frunt-BEN-chuh
French: membre du gouvernement ou de l’opposition officielle

Definition: A senior MP, usually a minister or shadow minister.

Example: Frontbenchers speak for their party on policy.

whip

IPA: /wɪp/
Pronunciation: wip
French: whip ; responsable de discipline parlementaire

Definition: A party official who ensures MPs vote according to the party line.

Example: The whips tried to prevent a rebellion.

party discipline

IPA: /ˈpɑːti ˈdɪsəplɪn/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee DIS-uh-plin
French: discipline de parti

Definition: Pressure on MPs to vote with their party.

Example: Party discipline is strong in the Westminster system.

rebellion

IPA: /rɪˈbeljən/
Pronunciation: ri-BEL-yuhn
French: rébellion parlementaire

Definition: A situation where MPs vote against their party leadership.

Example: The government faced a backbench rebellion.

free vote

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: free voht
French: vote libre

Definition: A vote where MPs may vote according to conscience rather than party instructions.

Example: Moral issues are sometimes decided by a free vote.

bill

IPA: /bɪl/
Pronunciation: bil
French: projet de loi

Definition: A proposed law debated by Parliament.

Example: The bill passed its second reading.

Act of Parliament

IPA: /ˌækt əv ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: akt ov PAR-luh-muhnt
French: loi du Parlement

Definition: A law passed by Parliament.

Example: The Human Rights Act is an Act of Parliament.

E. Law-making, scrutiny and high-value verbs

legislation

IPA: /ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: législation

Definition: Laws or the process of making laws.

Example: The government introduced new legislation.

primary legislation

IPA: /ˌpraɪməri ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: PRY-muh-ree lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: législation primaire

Definition: Main laws passed directly by Parliament.

Example: Primary legislation receives detailed parliamentary scrutiny.

secondary legislation

IPA: /ˌsekəndəri ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: SEK-uhn-duh-ree lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: législation secondaire

Definition: Detailed rules made by ministers under powers granted by Parliament.

Example: Secondary legislation can be controversial because scrutiny is limited.

statutory instrument

IPA: /ˌstætʃʊtəri ˈɪnstrəmənt/
Pronunciation: STACH-oo-tuh-ree IN-struh-muhnt
French: instrument réglementaire

Definition: A form of secondary legislation used to make detailed legal changes.

Example: Statutory instruments are often used to implement policy.

reading

IPA: /ˈriːdɪŋ/
Pronunciation: REE-ding
French: lecture parlementaire

Definition: A formal stage in the passage of a bill through Parliament.

Example: The bill faced opposition at second reading.

committee stage

IPA: /kəˈmɪti steɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee stage
French: examen en commission

Definition: The stage where a bill is examined in detail and amendments can be proposed.

Example: The bill was amended during committee stage.

amendment

IPA: /əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND-muhnt
French: amendement

Definition: A proposed change to a bill or law.

Example: Peers proposed several amendments to the bill.

royal assent

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl əˈsent/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl uh-SENT
French: sanction royale

Definition: The monarch’s formal approval of a bill, making it law.

Example: After royal assent, the bill became an Act.

parliamentary scrutiny

IPA: /ˌpɑːləˈmentəri ˈskruːtəni/
Pronunciation: par-luh-MEN-tuh-ree SKROO-tuh-nee
French: contrôle parlementaire

Definition: The examination of government actions and laws by Parliament.

Example: Parliamentary scrutiny is essential for accountability.

select committee

IPA: /sɪˈlekt kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: si-LEKT kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission spéciale ; commission parlementaire

Definition: A parliamentary committee that investigates policy areas or departments.

Example: Select committees can question ministers and experts.

Prime Minister’s Questions

IPA: /ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstəz ˈkwestʃənz/
Pronunciation: prime MIN-i-stuhz KWES-chuhnz
French: questions au Premier ministre ; PMQs

Definition: A weekly session where MPs question the Prime Minister.

Example: Prime Minister’s Questions are often combative.

PMQs

IPA: /ˌpiː em ˈkjuːz/
Pronunciation: pee-em-KYOOZ
French: PMQs ; questions au Premier ministre

Definition: Abbreviation for Prime Minister’s Questions.

Example: PMQs are a highly visible part of British politics.

division

IPA: /dɪˈvɪʒən/
Pronunciation: di-VIZH-uhn
French: vote formel ; division

Definition: A formal vote in the House of Commons or Lords.

Example: MPs were called to vote in a division.

to table an amendment

IPA: /ˈteɪbəl ən əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: TAY-buhl uhn uh-MEND-muhnt
French: déposer un amendement

Definition: To formally propose a change to a bill or motion.

Example: Opposition MPs tabled amendments.

to pass a bill

IPA: /pɑːs ə bɪl/
Pronunciation: pass uh bil
French: adopter un projet de loi

Definition: To approve a proposed law through Parliament.

Example: Parliament passed the bill after months of debate.

to repeal a law

IPA: /rɪˈpiːl ə lɔː/
Pronunciation: ri-PEEL uh law
French: abroger une loi

Definition: To cancel or remove a law.

Example: Parliament can repeal any law.

to scrutinise the government

IPA: /ˈskruːtənaɪz ðə ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: SKROO-tuh-nyz thuh GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: contrôler le gouvernement

Definition: To examine and challenge government decisions.

Example: MPs are expected to scrutinise the government.

to hold ministers accountable

IPA: /həʊld ˈmɪnɪstəz əˈkaʊntəbəl/
Pronunciation: hohld MIN-i-stuhz uh-KOWN-tuh-buhl
French: demander des comptes aux ministres

Definition: To make ministers answer for decisions and failures.

Example: Parliament should hold ministers accountable.

to command a majority
