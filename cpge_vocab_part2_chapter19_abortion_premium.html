Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse abortion in the United States: Roe v. Wade, Dobbs, reproductive rights, abortion bans, medication abortion, state-by-state access, pro-choice and pro-life politics, healthcare inequalities, legal uncertainty and electoral backlash. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

A. Core vocabulary: pregnancy, rights and medical terms

abortion

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn
French: avortement

Definition: The medical ending of a pregnancy before birth.

Example: Abortion remains one of the most divisive issues in American politics.

abortion rights

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən raɪts/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn rites
French: droits liés à l’avortement

Definition: Legal rights relating to access to abortion.

Example: Abortion rights became a major election issue after Roe v. Wade was overturned.

reproductive rights

IPA: /ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv raɪts/
Pronunciation: ree-pruh-DUK-tiv rites
French: droits reproductifs

Definition: Rights relating to contraception, pregnancy, abortion and reproductive healthcare.

Example: Activists framed abortion access as part of reproductive rights.

reproductive freedom

IPA: /ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈfriːdəm/
Pronunciation: ree-pruh-DUK-tiv FREE-duhm
French: liberté reproductive

Definition: The freedom to make personal decisions about reproduction and pregnancy.

Example: Supporters argue that reproductive freedom is central to bodily autonomy.

bodily autonomy

IPA: /ˌbɒdɪli ɔːˈtɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: BOD-i-lee aw-TON-uh-mee
French: autonomie corporelle

Definition: The right to make decisions about one’s own body.

Example: The debate often turns on bodily autonomy and state power.

pregnancy

IPA: /ˈpreɡnənsi/
Pronunciation: PREG-nuhn-see
French: grossesse

Definition: The condition of carrying a developing embryo or foetus.

Example: Pregnancy can involve serious medical risks.

unwanted pregnancy

IPA: /ʌnˌwɒntɪd ˈpreɡnənsi/
Pronunciation: un-WON-tid PREG-nuhn-see
French: grossesse non désirée

Definition: A pregnancy that a person does not wish to continue.

Example: Abortion bans can force people to carry unwanted pregnancies.

unplanned pregnancy

IPA: /ʌnˌplænd ˈpreɡnənsi/
Pronunciation: un-PLAND PREG-nuhn-see
French: grossesse non planifiée

Definition: A pregnancy that was not intended or expected.

Example: Access to contraception can reduce unplanned pregnancies.

foetus

IPA: /ˈfiːtəs/
Pronunciation: FEE-tuhs
French: fœtus

Definition: A developing unborn baby, especially after the early stages of pregnancy.

Example: The legal status of the foetus is central to abortion debates.

embryo

IPA: /ˈembriəʊ/
Pronunciation: EM-bree-oh
French: embryon

Definition: A developing organism in the earliest stages of pregnancy.

Example: Some laws define personhood from the embryo stage.

viability

IPA: /ˌvaɪəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: vy-uh-BIL-uh-tee
French: viabilité

Definition: The stage at which a foetus may survive outside the womb.

Example: Viability was once a key legal threshold in abortion law.

trimester

IPA: /traɪˈmestə/
Pronunciation: try-MES-tuh
French: trimestre de grossesse

Definition: One of the three three-month stages of pregnancy.

Example: Earlier abortion law often referred to trimesters.

maternal health

IPA: /məˌtɜːnəl ˈhelθ/
Pronunciation: muh-TUR-nuhl helth
French: santé maternelle

Definition: The health of a person during pregnancy, childbirth and the period after birth.

Example: Doctors warned that strict bans could endanger maternal health.

miscarriage

IPA: /ˈmɪskærɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: MIS-kar-ij
French: fausse couche

Definition: The natural loss of a pregnancy before birth.

Example: Abortion restrictions can complicate miscarriage care.

ectopic pregnancy

IPA: /ekˌtɒpɪk ˈpreɡnənsi/
Pronunciation: ek-TOP-ik PREG-nuhn-see
French: grossesse extra-utérine

Definition: A dangerous pregnancy occurring outside the uterus, usually requiring urgent treatment.

Example: An ectopic pregnancy can be life-threatening.

abortion access

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən ˈækses/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn AK-sess
French: accès à l’avortement

Definition: The practical ability to obtain an abortion, not just the legal right.

Example: Abortion access varies dramatically from state to state.

abortion ban

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən bæn/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn ban
French: interdiction de l’avortement

Definition: A law prohibiting abortion in most or all circumstances.

Example: Several states enacted abortion bans after Dobbs.

near-total ban

IPA: /ˌnɪə ˈtəʊtəl bæn/
Pronunciation: neer TOH-tuhl ban
French: interdiction quasi totale

Definition: A ban allowing abortion only in very limited circumstances.

Example: The near-total ban included only narrow medical exceptions.

six-week ban

IPA: /ˌsɪks wiːk ˈbæn/
Pronunciation: six-week ban
French: interdiction après six semaines

Definition: A law banning most abortions after about six weeks of pregnancy.

Example: Critics say a six-week ban applies before many people know they are pregnant.

heartbeat bill

IPA: /ˈhɑːtbiːt bɪl/
Pronunciation: HART-beet bil
French: loi dite du battement de cœur

Definition: A law banning abortion once embryonic cardiac activity can be detected.

Example: Heartbeat bills have been passed in several conservative states.

trigger law

IPA: /ˈtrɪɡə lɔː/
Pronunciation: TRIG-uh law
French: loi à déclenchement automatique

Definition: A law designed to take effect automatically if Roe v. Wade was overturned.

Example: Trigger laws took effect after the Dobbs decision.

gestational limit

IPA: /dʒeˈsteɪʃənəl ˈlɪmɪt/
Pronunciation: je-STAY-shuh-nuhl LIM-it
French: limite gestationnelle

Definition: A legal limit based on how far a pregnancy has progressed.

Example: Many abortion laws set gestational limits.

medical exception

IPA: /ˈmedɪkəl ɪkˈsepʃən/
Pronunciation: MED-i-kuhl ik-SEP-shuhn
French: exception médicale

Definition: An exception allowing abortion when the pregnant person’s health or life is at risk.

Example: Doctors criticised the law’s medical exception as unclear.

rape exception

IPA: /ˈreɪp ɪkˌsepʃən/
Pronunciation: rayp ik-SEP-shuhn
French: exception en cas de viol

Definition: An exception allowing abortion when pregnancy results from rape.

Example: Some abortion bans do not include a rape exception.

incest exception

IPA: /ˈɪnsest ɪkˌsepʃən/
Pronunciation: IN-sest ik-SEP-shuhn
French: exception en cas d’inceste

Definition: An exception allowing abortion when pregnancy results from incest.

Example: The law included rape and incest exceptions.

life of the mother exception

IPA: /ˌlaɪf əv ðə ˈmʌðər ɪkˌsepʃən/
Pronunciation: life ov thuh MUTH-uh ik-SEP-shuhn
French: exception pour sauver la vie de la mère

Definition: An exception allowing abortion when the pregnant person’s life is endangered.

Example: The exception was criticised for leaving doctors uncertain.

abortion clinic

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən ˈklɪnɪk/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn KLIN-ik
French: clinique pratiquant l’avortement

Definition: A medical facility where abortions are provided.

Example: Many abortion clinics closed after state bans.

provider

IPA: /prəˈvaɪdə/
Pronunciation: pruh-VY-duh
French: prestataire de soins ; praticien

Definition: A doctor, clinic or organisation that provides healthcare services.

Example: Abortion providers faced legal uncertainty.

medication abortion

IPA: /ˌmedɪˈkeɪʃən əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: med-i-KAY-shuhn uh-BOR-shuhn
French: avortement médicamenteux

Definition: Abortion using pills rather than surgery.

Example: Medication abortion has become central to post-Roe access.

abortion pill

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən pɪl/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn pil
French: pilule abortive

Definition: A pill used as part of medication abortion.

Example: Legal battles over the abortion pill reached federal courts.

mifepristone

IPA: /mɪˈfeprɪstəʊn/
Pronunciation: mi-FEP-ri-stohn
French: mifépristone

Definition: A drug used in medication abortion, often with misoprostol.

Example: Mifepristone access became a major legal issue.

misoprostol

IPA: /ˌmaɪsəʊˈprɒstɒl/
Pronunciation: my-soh-PROS-tol
French: misoprostol

Definition: A drug used in medication abortion and miscarriage care.

Example: Misoprostol can also be used for miscarriage treatment.

surgical abortion

IPA: /ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: SUR-ji-kuhl uh-BOR-shuhn
French: avortement chirurgical

Definition: Abortion performed through a medical procedure rather than pills.

Example: Surgical abortion may be necessary later in pregnancy.

telemedicine abortion

IPA: /ˌtelɪˈmedɪsɪn əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: tel-i-MED-i-sin uh-BOR-shuhn
French: avortement par télémédecine

Definition: Abortion care provided partly through remote medical consultation.

Example: Telemedicine abortion became more important after clinic closures.

self-managed abortion

IPA: /ˌself ˈmænɪdʒd əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: self MAN-ijd uh-BOR-shuhn
French: avortement auto-géré

Definition: An abortion managed outside the formal medical system, often using pills.

Example: Self-managed abortion raises legal and safety questions.

crisis pregnancy centre

IPA: /ˌkraɪsɪs ˈpreɡnənsi ˈsentə/
Pronunciation: KRY-sis PREG-nuhn-see SEN-tuh
French: centre anti-avortement se présentant comme aide à la grossesse

Definition: An organisation often discouraging abortion while presenting itself as pregnancy support.

Example: Critics accuse crisis pregnancy centres of misleading patients.

family planning

IPA: /ˌfæməli ˈplænɪŋ/
Pronunciation: FAM-uh-lee PLAN-ing
French: planning familial

Definition: Services and information helping people control pregnancy and contraception.

Example: Family planning services reduce unintended pregnancies.

contraception

IPA: /ˌkɒntrəˈsepʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-truh-SEP-shuhn
French: contraception

Definition: Methods used to prevent pregnancy.

Example: Access to contraception is closely linked to abortion rates.

birth control

IPA: /ˈbɜːθ kənˌtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: birth kuhn-TROHL
French: contraception

Definition: Methods used to prevent pregnancy.

Example: Some conservatives have also challenged birth control access.

B. Roe, Dobbs and constitutional law

Roe v. Wade

IPA: /ˌrəʊ vɜːsəs ˈweɪd/
Pronunciation: roh VUR-suhs wayd
French: Roe v. Wade

Definition: The 1973 Supreme Court ruling that recognised a constitutional right to abortion.

Example: Roe v. Wade shaped abortion law for nearly fifty years.

Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization

IPA: /dɒbz vɜːsəs ˈdʒæksən ˈwɪmɪnz helθ ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dobz VUR-suhs JAK-suhn WIM-inz health or-guh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization

Definition: The 2022 Supreme Court ruling that overturned Roe v. Wade.

Example: Dobbs returned abortion regulation largely to the states.

Planned Parenthood v. Casey

IPA: /plænd ˈpeərənhʊd vɜːsəs ˈkeɪsi/
Pronunciation: pland PAIR-uhn-hood VUR-suhs KAY-see
French: Planned Parenthood v. Casey

Definition: The 1992 Supreme Court ruling that upheld Roe while allowing some restrictions.

Example: Casey introduced the undue burden standard.

undue burden

IPA: /ˌʌnˈdjuː ˈbɜːdən/
Pronunciation: un-DYOO BUR-duhn
French: contrainte excessive ; obstacle indu

Definition: A legal standard from Casey: a restriction that places a substantial obstacle in the path of abortion access.

Example: The undue burden standard shaped abortion law before Dobbs.

constitutional right to abortion

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl raɪt tə əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl rite tuh uh-BOR-shuhn
French: droit constitutionnel à l’avortement

Definition: A right to abortion protected by constitutional interpretation.

Example: Dobbs held that there was no federal constitutional right to abortion.

right to privacy

IPA: /ˌraɪt tə ˈprɪvəsi/
Pronunciation: rite tuh PRIV-uh-see
French: droit à la vie privée

Definition: The right to personal autonomy and private life, used in earlier abortion jurisprudence.

Example: Roe was connected to the right to privacy.

substantive due process

IPA: /ˈsʌbstəntɪv ˌdjuː ˈprəʊses/
Pronunciation: SUB-stuhn-tiv dyoo PROH-sess
French: due process substantiel

Definition: A doctrine protecting certain fundamental rights from government interference.

Example: Substantive due process has been central to privacy and liberty cases.

Fourteenth Amendment

IPA: /ˌfɔːˈtiːnθ əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: for-TEENTH uh-MEND-muhnt
French: Quatorzième amendement

Definition: The constitutional amendment protecting citizenship, due process and equal protection.

Example: Earlier abortion rights were linked to the Fourteenth Amendment.

judicial precedent

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl PRES-i-duhnt
French: précédent judiciaire

Definition: A previous court decision that guides future cases.

Example: Dobbs overturned a major judicial precedent.

stare decisis

IPA: /ˌsteəri dɪˈsaɪsɪs/
Pronunciation: STAIR-ee di-SY-sis
French: stare decisis ; respect des précédents

Definition: The legal principle that courts should generally follow precedent.

Example: Critics argued that Dobbs weakened stare decisis.

to overturn Roe

IPA: /ˌəʊvəˈtɜːn ˈrəʊ/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-turn roh
French: renverser Roe v. Wade

Definition: To reverse the Roe v. Wade ruling.

Example: The Supreme Court voted to overturn Roe.

to uphold a restriction

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld ə rɪˈstrɪkʃən/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD uh ri-STRIK-shuhn
French: confirmer une restriction

Definition: For a court to decide that a restriction is legally valid.

Example: The Court upheld the state restriction.

to strike down a ban

IPA: /straɪk daʊn ə ˈbæn/
Pronunciation: stryke down uh ban
French: annuler une interdiction

Definition: For a court to invalidate an abortion ban.

Example: A state court struck down the ban.

to return the issue to the states

IPA: /rɪˈtɜːn ði ˈɪʃuː tə ðə ˈsteɪts/
Pronunciation: ri-TURN thee ISH-oo tuh thuh stayts
French: renvoyer la question aux États

Definition: To let states decide abortion policy instead of federal constitutional law.

Example: Dobbs returned the issue to the states.

state-by-state patchwork

IPA: /ˌsteɪt baɪ steɪt ˈpætʃwɜːk/
Pronunciation: state-by-state PATCH-wurk
French: mosaïque État par État

Definition: A situation where abortion laws differ widely across states.

Example: Dobbs created a state-by-state patchwork of abortion access.

red state

IPA: /ˌred ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: red state
French: État républicain

Definition: A US state that usually votes Republican.

Example: Many red states passed strict abortion bans.

blue state

IPA: /ˌbluː ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: bloo state
French: État démocrate

Definition: A US state that usually votes Democratic.

Example: Blue states moved to protect abortion access.

C. State-by-state access and political geography

abortion haven

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən ˈheɪvən/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn HAY-vuhn
French: refuge pour l’accès à l’avortement

Definition: A state or place where abortion remains accessible to people from restrictive states.

Example: Some blue states became abortion havens.

abortion desert

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən ˈdezət/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn DEZ-uht
French: désert médical pour l’avortement

Definition: An area with little or no practical access to abortion services.

Example: Large parts of the South became abortion deserts.

interstate travel

IPA: /ˌɪntəsteɪt ˈtrævəl/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-state TRAV-uhl
French: déplacement entre États

Definition: Travel between states, often to access abortion services after bans.

Example: Interstate travel for abortion became politically contentious.

out-of-state care

IPA: /ˌaʊt əv steɪt ˈkeə/
Pronunciation: owt-ov-state care
French: soins dans un autre État

Definition: Healthcare obtained in another state.

Example: Many patients had to seek out-of-state care.

shield law

IPA: /ˈʃiːld lɔː/
Pronunciation: sheeld law
French: loi bouclier

Definition: A law protecting providers or patients from out-of-state abortion investigations.

Example: Some states passed shield laws to protect abortion providers.

extraterritorial enforcement

IPA: /ˌekstrəˌterɪˈtɔːriəl ɪnˈfɔːsmənt/
Pronunciation: ek-struh-ter-i-TOR-ee-uhl in-FORSS-muhnt
French: application extraterritoriale

Definition: Attempts to apply a state’s law beyond its borders.

Example: Extraterritorial enforcement raised complex legal questions.

pro-choice

IPA: /ˌprəʊ ˈtʃɔɪs/
Pronunciation: proh choice
French: pro-choice ; favorable au droit à l’avortement

Definition: Supporting legal access to abortion as a matter of choice and rights.

Example: Pro-choice campaigners defended reproductive freedom.

pro-life

IPA: /ˌprəʊ ˈlaɪf/
Pronunciation: proh life
French: pro-life ; anti-avortement

Definition: Opposing abortion on the grounds of protecting unborn life.

Example: Pro-life activists celebrated the Dobbs ruling.

anti-abortion

IPA: /ˌænti əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: AN-tee uh-BOR-shuhn
French: anti-avortement

Definition: Opposed to abortion.

Example: Anti-abortion groups pushed for stricter bans.

abortion opponent

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən əˈpəʊnənt/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn uh-POH-nuhnt
French: opposant à l’avortement

Definition: A person who opposes abortion.

Example: Abortion opponents argue that life begins before birth.

abortion advocate

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən ˈædvəkət/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn AD-vuh-kuht
French: défenseur du droit à l’avortement

Definition: A person who supports abortion rights or access.

Example: Abortion advocates warned of health risks after bans.

D. Political camps, values and elections

reproductive justice

IPA: /ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: ree-pruh-DUK-tiv JUS-tis
French: justice reproductive

Definition: A framework linking reproductive rights to social justice, race, poverty and healthcare access.

Example: Reproductive justice emphasises inequality in access to care.

women’s rights

IPA: /ˈwɪmɪnz raɪts/
Pronunciation: WIM-inz rites
French: droits des femmes

Definition: Rights concerning women’s equality, freedom and autonomy.

Example: Many see abortion access as a women’s rights issue.

gender equality

IPA: /ˌdʒendər iˈkwɒləti/
Pronunciation: JEN-duhr i-KWOL-uh-tee
French: égalité de genre

Definition: Equal rights and opportunities regardless of gender.

Example: Abortion restrictions can affect gender equality.

religious conservatism

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs kənˈsɜːvətɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs kuhn-SUR-vuh-tiz-uhm
French: conservatisme religieux

Definition: A political outlook based on conservative religious values.

Example: Religious conservatism has strongly shaped anti-abortion politics.

evangelical voters

IPA: /ˌiːvænˈdʒelɪkəl ˈvəʊtəz/
Pronunciation: ee-van-JEL-i-kuhl VOH-tuhz
French: électeurs évangéliques

Definition: Voters belonging to or influenced by evangelical Christianity.

Example: Evangelical voters have been key to the anti-abortion movement.

Catholic voters

IPA: /ˈkæθəlɪk ˈvəʊtəz/
Pronunciation: KATH-uh-lik VOH-tuhz
French: électeurs catholiques

Definition: Voters belonging to or influenced by Catholicism.

Example: Catholic voters are divided on abortion policy.

moral issue

IPA: /ˈmɒrəl ˈɪʃuː/
Pronunciation: MOR-uhl ISH-oo
French: enjeu moral

Definition: An issue framed in terms of right and wrong.

Example: Abortion is often framed as a moral issue.

culture-war issue

IPA: /ˈkʌltʃə wɔː ˈɪʃuː/
Pronunciation: KUL-chuh war ISH-oo
French: enjeu de guerre culturelle

Definition: A divisive issue linked to values, identity and social conflict.

Example: Abortion has become a defining culture-war issue.

single-issue voter

IPA: /ˌsɪŋɡəl ˈɪʃuː ˈvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: SING-guhl ISH-oo VOH-tuh
French: électeur motivé par une seule question

Definition: A voter who bases their choice mainly on one issue.

Example: Some single-issue voters focus almost entirely on abortion.

gender gap

IPA: /ˈdʒendə ɡæp/
Pronunciation: JEN-duh gap
French: écart hommes-femmes

Definition: A difference between men and women in political attitudes or voting.

Example: Dobbs may have widened the gender gap in some elections.

backlash

IPA: /ˈbæklæʃ/
Pronunciation: BAK-lash
French: réaction hostile ; retour de bâton

Definition: A strong negative reaction against a decision or social change.

Example: The ruling triggered a political backlash.

ballot measure

IPA: /ˈbælət ˌmeʒə/
Pronunciation: BAL-uht MEZH-uh
French: référendum d’initiative ; mesure soumise au vote

Definition: A policy question directly voted on by citizens.

Example: Abortion ballot measures mobilised voters in several states.

referendum

IPA: /ˌrefəˈrendəm/
Pronunciation: ref-uh-REN-duhm
French: référendum

Definition: A direct vote by citizens on a specific issue.

Example: The referendum protected abortion rights in the state constitution.

constitutional amendment at state level

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl əˈmendmənt ət steɪt ˈlevəl/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl uh-MEND-muhnt at state LEV-uhl
French: amendement constitutionnel au niveau d’un État

Definition: A formal change to a state constitution.

Example: Voters approved a state constitutional amendment on abortion.

maternal mortality

IPA: /məˌtɜːnəl mɔːˈtæləti/
Pronunciation: muh-TUR-nuhl mor-TAL-uh-tee
French: mortalité maternelle

Definition: Deaths related to pregnancy or childbirth.

Example: The US already has a high maternal mortality rate compared with similar countries.

healthcare access

IPA: /ˈhelθkeər ˈækses/
Pronunciation: HELTH-care AK-sess
French: accès aux soins

Definition: The practical ability to obtain medical care.

Example: Abortion bans can worsen unequal healthcare access.

low-income women

IPA: /ˌləʊ ˈɪnkʌm ˈwɪmɪn/
Pronunciation: loh IN-kum WIM-in
French: femmes à faibles revenus

Definition: Women with limited income or financial resources.

Example: Low-income women are disproportionately affected by travel costs.

E. Healthcare impact, legal risks and high-value verbs

rural women

IPA: /ˌrʊərəl ˈwɪmɪn/
Pronunciation: ROOR-uhl WIM-in
French: femmes rurales

Definition: Women living in rural areas.

Example: Rural women may live far from abortion providers.

minority women

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈwɪmɪn/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee WIM-in
French: femmes issues de minorités

Definition: Women belonging to racial, ethnic or other minority groups.

Example: Minority women often face greater barriers to healthcare.

medical uncertainty

IPA: /ˈmedɪkəl ʌnˈsɜːtənti/
Pronunciation: MED-i-kuhl un-SUR-tuhn-tee
French: incertitude médicale

Definition: Unclear legal or clinical conditions affecting medical decisions.

Example: Medical uncertainty can delay emergency care.

chilling effect

IPA: /ˈtʃɪlɪŋ ɪˌfekt/
Pronunciation: CHIL-ing i-FEKT
French: effet dissuasif ; effet paralysant

Definition: A situation where fear of punishment discourages lawful or necessary action.

Example: The threat of prosecution had a chilling effect on doctors.

criminal liability

IPA: /ˈkrɪmɪnəl ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: KRIM-i-nuhl ly-uh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité pénale

Definition: Legal responsibility for a crime.

Example: Doctors feared criminal liability under unclear abortion laws.

civil liability

IPA: /ˈsɪvəl ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl ly-uh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité civile

Definition: Legal responsibility in a civil lawsuit rather than a criminal case.

Example: Some laws create civil liability for helping someone get an abortion.

bounty law

IPA: /ˈbaʊnti lɔː/
Pronunciation: BOWN-tee law
French: loi à prime ; mécanisme de dénonciation privée

Definition: A law allowing private citizens to sue people who help provide banned abortions.

Example: Texas’s bounty law allowed private lawsuits against abortion providers.

to criminalise abortion

IPA: /ˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪz əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: KRIM-i-nuh-lyz uh-BOR-shuhn
French: criminaliser l’avortement

Definition: To make abortion a crime.

Example: Activists warned that some states could criminalise abortion.

to protect abortion access

IPA: /prəˈtekt əˈbɔːʃən ˈækses/
Pronunciation: pruh-TEKT uh-BOR-shuhn AK-sess
French: protéger l’accès à l’avortement

Definition: To defend or preserve the ability to obtain abortion care.

Example: Blue states moved to protect abortion access.

to restrict abortion access

IPA: /rɪˈstrɪkt əˈbɔːʃən ˈækses/
Pronunciation: ri-STRIKT uh-BOR-shuhn AK-sess
French: restreindre l’accès à l’avortement

Definition: To limit the ability to obtain abortion care.

Example: Waiting periods and clinic regulations can restrict abortion access.

to ban abortion

IPA: /bæn əˈbɔːʃən/
Pronunciation: ban uh-BOR-shuhn
French: interdire l’avortement

Definition: To prohibit abortion by law.

Example: Several states moved to ban abortion after Dobbs.

to codify Roe

IPA: /ˈkəʊdɪfaɪ ˈrəʊ/
Pronunciation: KOH-di-fy roh
French: inscrire Roe dans la loi

Definition: To pass a law protecting abortion rights similar to Roe v. Wade.

Example: Democrats called on Congress to codify Roe.

to cross state lines

IPA: /krɒs steɪt laɪnz/
Pronunciation: kross state lynz
French: franchir les frontières entre États

Definition: To travel from one state to another, often to access abortion care.

Example: Patients crossed state lines to obtain abortions.

to seek care out of state

IPA: /siːk keər aʊt əv steɪt/
Pronunciation: seek care out ov state
French: chercher des soins dans un autre État

Definition: To obtain healthcare in another state.

Example: Many patients had to seek care out of state.

to prosecute providers

IPA: /ˈprɒsɪkjuːt prəˈvaɪdəz/
Pronunciation: PROS-i-kyoot pruh-VY-duhz
French: poursuivre des prestataires de soins

Definition: To bring criminal charges against healthcare providers.

Example: Some laws threaten to prosecute providers.

to deter doctors

IPA: /dɪˈtɜː ˈdɒktəz/
Pronunciation: di-TUR DOK-tuhz
French: dissuader les médecins

Definition: To discourage doctors from acting because of legal risk.

Example: Unclear laws may deter doctors from providing emergency care.

to mobilise voters

IPA: /ˈməʊbɪlaɪz ˈvəʊtəz/
Pronunciation: MOH-bi-lyz VOH-tuhz
French: mobiliser les électeurs

Definition: To encourage voters to act or vote on an issue.

Example: Abortion rights mobilised voters in midterm elections.

to galvanise the electorate

IPA: /ˈɡælvənaɪz ði ɪˈlektərət/
Pronunciation: GAL-vuh-nyz thee i-LEK-tuh-ruht
French: galvaniser l’électorat

Definition: To strongly motivate voters to take political action.

Example: The Dobbs decision galvanised the electorate.

to reshape American politics

IPA: /riːˈʃeɪp əˈmerɪkən ˈpɒlɪtɪks/
Pronunciation: ree-SHAYP uh-MER-i-kuhn POL-i-tiks
French: remodeler la politique américaine

Definition: To significantly change American political debate or voting behaviour.

Example: The abortion issue has reshaped American politics.
