Chapter 8 — Articles: A, An, The and Ø
A2 → B1 grammar for precision, clarity and avoiding classic French-speaker mistakes.

Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master English articles: a, an, the and the zero article Ø. Articles are small, but they are absolutely central to clear academic English, especially when discussing politics, society, economics, media, institutions and abstract ideas.

The golden rule

a / an = one non-specific singular countable noun
the = specific / known / defined noun
Ø = general plural or uncountable noun
Main useArticles show whether you are speaking generally, introducing something, or referring to something specific.
CPGE habitUse articles carefully in academic phrases: the rise of populism, freedom of speech, an important argument, the cost of living.
Key trapDo not put the before abstract ideas in general: Democracy is fragile, not The democracy is fragile.

A. Lesson — What are articles?

Articles

French: articles

Rule / explanation: Articles are small words placed before nouns: a, an, the, or no article Ø.

Example / answer: The article discusses a crisis in British politics.

A / an

French: article indéfini

Rule / explanation: Use a or an with singular countable nouns when something is mentioned for the first time or is not specific.

Example / answer: The journalist mentions a new reform.

The

French: article défini

Rule / explanation: Use the when the noun is specific, already known, unique, or defined by context.

Example / answer: The reform has divided public opinion.

Zero article Ø

French: article zéro

Rule / explanation: Use no article with plural nouns or uncountable nouns when speaking generally.

Example / answer: Politicians need trust. / Information spreads quickly online.

Countable nouns

French: noms dénombrables

Rule / explanation: Singular countable nouns usually need an article or determiner.

Example / answer: Wrong: government introduced reform. Correct: The government introduced a reform.

Uncountable nouns

French: noms indénombrables

Rule / explanation: Uncountable nouns do not take a/an.

Example / answer: Wrong: an information. Correct: information / a piece of information.

Specific vs general

French: spécifique vs général

Rule / explanation: Articles often show whether you mean one specific thing or a general category.

Example / answer: The media are powerful. / Media coverage matters.

CPGE use

French: usage CPGE

Rule / explanation: Articles are essential for clear essays, summaries and press reviews.

Example / answer: The author criticises the government’s response to inflation.

B. A and an

A + consonant sound

French: a + son consonne

Rule / explanation: Use a before a consonant sound.

Example / answer: a policy, a government, a university

An + vowel sound

French: an + son voyelle

Rule / explanation: Use an before a vowel sound.

Example / answer: an article, an argument, an issue

Sound, not spelling

French: son et non orthographe

Rule / explanation: Choose a/an according to pronunciation, not spelling.

Example / answer: an hour, a European country, a useful example

First mention

French: première mention

Rule / explanation: Use a/an when introducing something for the first time.

Example / answer: The article describes a political scandal.

One among many

French: un parmi d’autres

Rule / explanation: Use a/an when something is one example of a category.

Example / answer: This is a serious problem.

Jobs and roles

French: métier / rôle

Rule / explanation: Use a/an for one person’s job or role.

Example / answer: She is a journalist. He is an economist.

Definitions

French: définition

Rule / explanation: Use a/an to define a singular countable noun.

Example / answer: A referendum is a direct vote on a question.

A/an with adjectives

French: article + adjectif

Rule / explanation: The article goes before the adjective, not just the noun.

Example / answer: an important issue, a controversial reform

C. The

Second mention

French: deuxième mention

Rule / explanation: Use the when the noun has already been mentioned.

Example / answer: The article mentions a reform. The reform is unpopular.

Specific reference

French: référence spécifique

Rule / explanation: Use the when both reader and writer know which thing is meant.

Example / answer: The government announced new measures.

Unique things

French: éléments uniques

Rule / explanation: Use the for unique nouns or institutions when context makes them unique.

Example / answer: the Prime Minister, the Constitution, the Internet

Defined by a phrase

French: défini par complément

Rule / explanation: Use the when the noun is defined by what follows.

Example / answer: the rise of populism, the cost of living, the future of democracy

Superlatives

French: superlatifs

Rule / explanation: Use the before superlatives.

Example / answer: the most important argument

Ordinal numbers

French: nombres ordinaux

Rule / explanation: Use the before first, second, third, etc.

Example / answer: the first reason, the second example

Groups of people

French: groupes sociaux

Rule / explanation: Use the + adjective to refer to a group.

Example / answer: the rich, the poor, the unemployed

Institutions and newspapers

French: institutions et journaux

Rule / explanation: Many institutions and newspapers take the.

Example / answer: the BBC, the Guardian, the United Nations

D. Zero article Ø

General plural nouns

French: pluriel général

Rule / explanation: Use no article for plural nouns in general.

Example / answer: Politicians often promise reform.

General uncountable nouns

French: indénombrable général

Rule / explanation: Use no article for uncountable nouns in general.

Example / answer: Democracy depends on trust.

Abstract ideas

French: idées abstraites

Rule / explanation: Use no article when discussing abstract ideas generally.

Example / answer: Freedom is difficult to define.

Languages

French: langues

Rule / explanation: Use no article before languages.

Example / answer: English is used in international institutions.

Academic subjects

French: matières

Rule / explanation: Use no article before subjects in general.

Example / answer: Economics helps explain inequality.

Meals

French: repas

Rule / explanation: Usually no article before meals in general.

Example / answer: Students discussed politics over lunch.

Countries

French: pays

Rule / explanation: Most country names take no article.

Example / answer: France, Britain and Canada face different challenges.

Continents and cities

French: continents et villes

Rule / explanation: Use no article before most continents and cities.

Example / answer: Europe, London, Paris

E. Special cases for CPGE topics

The United Kingdom / the United States

French: pays avec the

Rule / explanation: Some country names take the, especially plural or political union names.

Example / answer: the UK, the US, the Netherlands

Government

French: gouvernement

Rule / explanation: Use the government when referring to a specific government.

Example / answer: The government is under pressure.

Society

French: société

Rule / explanation: Use society with no article for the general concept; the society only for a specific organisation or society.

Example / answer: Society is changing rapidly.

Media

French: médias

Rule / explanation: Use the media when referring to mass media as an institution.

Example / answer: The media influence public opinion.

Press

French: presse

Rule / explanation: Use the press to refer to newspapers and journalists collectively.

Example / answer: The press criticised the minister.

Internet

French: internet

Rule / explanation: Both the Internet and internet exist; the Internet is still common in formal contexts.

Example / answer: The Internet has transformed political debate.

Environment

French: environnement

Rule / explanation: Use the environment when referring to the natural world as a whole.

Example / answer: Climate change threatens the environment.

Economy

French: économie

Rule / explanation: Use the economy when referring to a specific economic system.

Example / answer: The economy is slowing down.

F. Common CPGE phrases with articles

the cost of living

French: coût de la vie

Rule / explanation: A fixed phrase referring to everyday expenses.

Example / answer: The cost of living has become a central political issue.

the welfare state

French: État-providence

Rule / explanation: A state system providing social support.

Example / answer: The welfare state expanded after 1945.

the rule of law

French: État de droit

Rule / explanation: The principle that law applies equally to all.

Example / answer: Democracy depends on the rule of law.

freedom of speech

French: liberté d’expression

Rule / explanation: Usually no article in the general expression.

Example / answer: Freedom of speech has limits.

the right to vote

French: droit de vote

Rule / explanation: Use the with a specific right.

Example / answer: The right to vote is central to democracy.

public opinion

French: opinion publique

Rule / explanation: Usually no article in the general expression.

Example / answer: Public opinion remains divided.

social media

French: réseaux sociaux

Rule / explanation: Usually no article when used generally.

Example / answer: Social media amplify outrage.

the housing crisis

French: crise du logement

Rule / explanation: Use the for a specific crisis.

Example / answer: The housing crisis affects young adults.

G. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Missing a/an with singular countable nouns

French: oubli de a/an

Rule / explanation: A singular countable noun usually needs an article or determiner.

Example / answer: Wrong: This is important issue. Correct: This is an important issue.

Using the for general ideas

French: the abusif

Rule / explanation: Do not use the for general abstract ideas.

Example / answer: Wrong: The democracy is fragile. Correct: Democracy is fragile.

Using a/an with uncountable nouns

French: a/an avec indénombrable

Rule / explanation: Uncountable nouns cannot take a/an.

Example / answer: Wrong: an information. Correct: a piece of information.

Forgetting the with defined nouns

French: oubli de the

Rule / explanation: Use the when the noun is defined by a following phrase.

Example / answer: Wrong: rise of populism. Correct: the rise of populism.

The before most countries

French: the devant pays

Rule / explanation: Most country names take no article.

Example / answer: Wrong: the France. Correct: France.

Confusing society and the society

French: society

Rule / explanation: Use society for the general idea.

Example / answer: Wrong: The society is changing. Correct: Society is changing.

Forgetting the with superlatives

French: superlatif

Rule / explanation: Superlatives normally take the.

Example / answer: Wrong: most important issue. Correct: the most important issue.

Overusing the media singular verb

French: media

Rule / explanation: The media is often plural in formal English, though singular is increasingly common.

Example / answer: Formal: The media are influential.

H. Exercises

Exercise 1 — Choose the right article

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: This is ___ important argument. (a / an / the / Ø)

Example / answer: Answer: an

Exercise 2 — Choose the right article

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: ___ democracy depends on trust. (The / Ø)

Example / answer: Answer: Ø Democracy depends on trust.

Exercise 3 — Choose the right article

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: The article discusses ___ rise of populism. (a / an / the / Ø)

Example / answer: Answer: the

Exercise 4 — Choose the right article

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: She is ___ economist. (a / an / the / Ø)

Example / answer: Answer: an

Exercise 5 — Correct the mistake

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: The society is becoming more unequal.

Example / answer: Answer: Society is becoming more unequal.

Exercise 6 — Correct the mistake

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: This article gives an information about poverty.

Example / answer: Answer: This article gives information / a piece of information about poverty.

Exercise 7 — Correct the mistake

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: France and the Britain have different institutions.

Example / answer: Answer: France and Britain have different institutions.

Exercise 8 — Correct the mistake

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: It is most convincing argument.

Example / answer: Answer: It is the most convincing argument.

Exercise 9 — Insert articles

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: ___ government announced ___ reform to address ___ housing crisis.

Example / answer: Answer: The government announced a reform to address the housing crisis.

Exercise 10 — Translate

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: La liberté d’expression est essentielle dans une démocratie.

Example / answer: Answer: Freedom of speech is essential in a democracy.

Exercise 11 — Translate

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: L’article analyse le coût de la vie au Royaume-Uni.

Example / answer: Answer: The article analyses the cost of living in the United Kingdom / the UK.

Exercise 12 — Mini concours challenge

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: Write 6 sentences using: a, an, the, Ø general plural, Ø abstract noun, the + superlative.

Example / answer: Possible answer: A reform may be necessary. An argument can be convincing. The government is under pressure. Voters want change. Democracy requires trust. This is the most urgent issue.
