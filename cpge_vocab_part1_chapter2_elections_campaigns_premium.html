A. Elections and voting basics

election

IPA: /ɪˈlekʃən/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-shuhn
French: élection

Definition: A formal process in which people vote to choose a political leader or representative.

Example: The next general election is expected to focus on the economy and immigration.

general election

IPA: /ˌdʒenərəl ɪˈlekʃən/
Pronunciation: JEN-uh-ruhl i-LEK-shuhn
French: élections législatives ; élections générales

Definition: An election in which voters choose representatives for the national legislature, and often indirectly determine the next government.

Example: The party hopes to win a majority in the next general election.

presidential election

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ɪˈlekʃən/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl i-LEK-shuhn
French: élection présidentielle

Definition: An election in which voters choose a president.

Example: The presidential election has deeply divided the country.

midterm elections

IPA: /ˈmɪdtɜːm ɪˌlekʃənz/
Pronunciation: MID-term i-LEK-shuhnz
French: élections de mi-mandat

Definition: Elections held halfway through a president’s term, especially in the United States.

Example: Midterm elections often become a referendum on the president’s record.

primary election

IPA: /ˈpraɪməri ɪˌlekʃən/
Pronunciation: PRY-muh-ree i-LEK-shuhn
French: primaire

Definition: An election in which voters or party members choose a party’s candidate for a later election.

Example: The primary election revealed deep divisions within the party.

by-election

IPA: /ˈbaɪ ɪˌlekʃən/
Pronunciation: BY i-LEK-shuhn
French: élection partielle

Definition: An election held to fill a political seat that has become vacant between general elections.

Example: The by-election was seen as a test for the government.

ballot

IPA: /ˈbælət/
Pronunciation: BAL-uht
French: scrutin ; bulletin de vote

Definition: The process of voting, or the paper or electronic form used to vote.

Example: Voters will decide the issue by secret ballot.

ballot paper

IPA: /ˈbælət ˌpeɪpə/
Pronunciation: BAL-uht PAY-puh
French: bulletin de vote

Definition: The paper on which voters mark their choice in an election.

Example: The candidate’s name was printed at the top of the ballot paper.

polling station

IPA: /ˈpəʊlɪŋ ˌsteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: POH-ling STAY-shuhn
French: bureau de vote

Definition: A place where people go to vote in an election.

Example: Polling stations opened at 7 a.m. across the country.

polls

IPA: /pəʊlz/
Pronunciation: pohlz
French: sondages

Definition: Surveys that measure public opinion, especially before an election.

Example: The polls suggest that the race is too close to call.

opinion poll

IPA: /əˈpɪnjən pəʊl/
Pronunciation: uh-PIN-yuhn pohl
French: sondage d'opinion

Definition: A survey that asks people what they think about a political issue, party or candidate.

Example: An opinion poll showed growing support for the opposition.

exit poll

IPA: /ˈeksɪt pəʊl/
Pronunciation: EK-sit pohl
French: sondage de sortie des urnes

Definition: A survey of voters taken just after they have voted, used to predict election results.

Example: The exit poll predicted a landslide victory for the opposition.

voter

IPA: /ˈvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: VOH-tuh
French: électeur ; électrice

Definition: A person who has the right to vote or who votes in an election.

Example: Young voters played a decisive role in the election.

voter registration

IPA: /ˈvəʊtə ˌredʒɪˈstreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: VOH-tuh rej-i-STRAY-shuhn
French: inscription sur les listes électorales

Definition: The process of officially recording people so that they are allowed to vote.

Example: Campaigners encouraged voter registration among students.

eligible voter

IPA: /ˈelɪdʒəbəl ˈvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: EL-i-juh-buhl VOH-tuh
French: électeur inscrit ou autorisé à voter

Definition: A person who meets the legal requirements to vote.

Example: Millions of eligible voters stayed home on election day.

constituency

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənsi/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhn-see
French: circonscription électorale

Definition: A geographical area whose voters elect a representative.

Example: The candidate has represented the same constituency for twenty years.

electoral district

IPA: /ɪˌlektərəl ˈdɪstrɪkt/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruhl DIS-trikt
French: circonscription électorale

Definition: An area that elects one or more representatives.

Example: The new map changed the boundaries of several electoral districts.

candidate

IPA: /ˈkændɪdət/
Pronunciation: KAN-di-duht
French: candidat ; candidate

Definition: A person who competes in an election or applies for a position.

Example: The candidate promised to restore trust in politics.

B. Candidates, parties and campaign tools

incumbent

IPA: /ɪnˈkʌmbənt/
Pronunciation: in-KUM-buhnt
French: titulaire du poste ; sortant

Definition: The person currently holding a political office.

Example: The incumbent president faces a strong challenge from the opposition.

running mate

IPA: /ˈrʌnɪŋ meɪt/
Pronunciation: RUN-ing mayt
French: colistier ; colistière

Definition: A candidate who runs for a lower office alongside another candidate, especially a vice-presidential candidate.

Example: The presidential candidate announced her running mate in July.

front-runner

IPA: /ˌfrʌnt ˈrʌnə/
Pronunciation: frunt RUN-uh
French: favori ; favorite

Definition: The candidate who seems most likely to win an election or nomination.

Example: The former governor quickly became the front-runner.

underdog

IPA: /ˈʌndədɒɡ/
Pronunciation: UN-duh-dog
French: outsider ; candidat donné perdant

Definition: A candidate or party expected to lose, but who may still surprise observers.

Example: The underdog gained momentum after a strong debate performance.

manifesto

IPA: /ˌmænɪˈfestəʊ/
Pronunciation: man-i-FES-toh
French: programme électoral ; manifeste

Definition: A public document in which a political party presents its ideas, promises and programme.

Example: The party manifesto focused on housing, healthcare and climate policy.

platform

IPA: /ˈplætfɔːm/
Pronunciation: PLAT-form
French: programme ; plateforme politique

Definition: The set of policies and principles supported by a political party or candidate.

Example: The candidate ran on a platform of tax cuts and stricter border controls.

pledge

IPA: /pledʒ/
Pronunciation: plej
French: engagement ; promesse

Definition: A serious public promise, especially during an election campaign.

Example: The prime minister broke a key campaign pledge on taxes.

campaign

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN
French: campagne

Definition: A planned series of activities designed to win an election or influence public opinion.

Example: The campaign focused heavily on the cost-of-living crisis.

campaign trail

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn treɪl/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN trayl
French: tournée de campagne

Definition: The series of events, visits and speeches made by candidates while campaigning.

Example: The candidate spent three weeks on the campaign trail.

campaign rally

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn ˈræli/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN RAL-ee
French: meeting de campagne

Definition: A public meeting where supporters gather to hear a candidate speak.

Example: Thousands attended the campaign rally in Manchester.

canvassing

IPA: /ˈkænvəsɪŋ/
Pronunciation: KAN-vuh-sing
French: porte-à-porte électoral ; démarchage électoral

Definition: The practice of contacting voters directly to persuade them or gather information about their voting intentions.

Example: Volunteers spent the weekend canvassing in marginal areas.

door-knocking

IPA: /ˈdɔː ˌnɒkɪŋ/
Pronunciation: DAW NOK-ing
French: porte-à-porte

Definition: Campaigning by visiting voters at home and speaking to them directly.

Example: Door-knocking remains an important part of local campaigns.

phone banking

IPA: /ˈfəʊn ˌbæŋkɪŋ/
Pronunciation: FOHN BANK-ing
French: appels téléphoniques de campagne

Definition: A campaign activity in which volunteers call voters to persuade them or remind them to vote.

Example: Phone banking helped the campaign contact thousands of undecided voters.

leaflet

IPA: /ˈliːflət/
Pronunciation: LEEF-luht
French: tract ; prospectus

Definition: A printed sheet used to give political information or persuade voters.

Example: The leaflet summarised the candidate’s main proposals.

political ad

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl æd/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl ad
French: publicité politique

Definition: A political advertisement designed to influence voters.

Example: The political ad accused the opposition of being weak on crime.

negative campaigning

IPA: /ˈneɡətɪv kæmˈpeɪnɪŋ/
Pronunciation: NEG-uh-tiv kam-PAY-ning
French: campagne négative

Definition: A strategy that attacks an opponent rather than promoting one’s own ideas.

Example: Negative campaigning may discourage voters from taking part.

attack ad

IPA: /əˈtæk æd/
Pronunciation: uh-TAK ad
French: publicité d’attaque

Definition: A political advertisement that criticises or attacks an opponent.

Example: The attack ad damaged the candidate’s reputation.

spin

IPA: /spɪn/
Pronunciation: spin
French: communication orientée ; présentation biaisée

Definition: A favourable or strategic interpretation of events designed to influence public opinion.

Example: The minister’s team tried to put a positive spin on the poor results.

spin doctor

IPA: /ˈspɪn ˌdɒktə/
Pronunciation: SPIN dok-tuh
French: conseiller en communication politique

Definition: A political adviser who manages public image and media messaging.

Example: The spin doctor helped reshape the candidate’s image after the scandal.

slogan

IPA: /ˈsləʊɡən/
Pronunciation: SLOH-guhn
French: slogan

Definition: A short memorable phrase used in a campaign.

Example: The slogan was simple, emotional and easy to remember.

soundbite

IPA: /ˈsaʊndbaɪt/
Pronunciation: SOWND-byt
French: petite phrase ; formule choc

Definition: A short phrase from a speech or interview that is memorable and widely repeated.

Example: The debate produced several powerful soundbites.

debate

IPA: /dɪˈbeɪt/
Pronunciation: di-BAYT
French: débat

Definition: A formal public discussion between candidates or political opponents.

Example: The televised debate attracted millions of viewers.

C. Public opinion and voter behaviour

approval rating

IPA: /əˈpruːvəl ˈreɪtɪŋ/
Pronunciation: uh-PROO-vuhl RAY-ting
French: cote de popularité ; taux d’approbation

Definition: The percentage of people who say they approve of a leader or government.

Example: The president’s approval rating fell after the crisis.

public support

IPA: /ˈpʌblɪk səˈpɔːt/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik suh-PORT
French: soutien populaire

Definition: The approval or backing that a person, policy or party receives from citizens.

Example: The reform lost public support after weeks of protests.

swing voter

IPA: /ˈswɪŋ ˌvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: swing VOH-tuh
French: électeur indécis ; électeur flottant

Definition: A voter who has not firmly decided which party or candidate to support.

Example: Swing voters may decide the outcome in key states.

undecided voter

IPA: /ˌʌndɪˈsaɪdɪd ˈvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: un-di-SY-did VOH-tuh
French: électeur indécis

Definition: A voter who has not yet chosen a candidate or party.

Example: Both candidates tried to appeal to undecided voters.

floating voter

IPA: /ˌfləʊtɪŋ ˈvəʊtə/
Pronunciation: FLOH-ting VOH-tuh
French: électeur flottant

Definition: A voter who does not always vote for the same party.

Example: Floating voters are crucial in close elections.

marginal seat

IPA: /ˈmɑːdʒɪnəl siːt/
Pronunciation: MAR-ji-nuhl seet
French: siège disputé ; circonscription marginale

Definition: A parliamentary seat that is won by a small margin and could easily change hands.

Example: The party targeted marginal seats in the north of England.

safe seat

IPA: /ˌseɪf ˈsiːt/
Pronunciation: sayf seet
French: circonscription acquise ; siège sûr

Definition: A parliamentary seat that one party is very likely to keep.

Example: The minister represents a safe seat.

battleground state

IPA: /ˈbætəlɡraʊnd steɪt/
Pronunciation: BAT-uhl-ground stayt
French: État clé ; État disputé

Definition: A state where the result is uncertain and which may decide an election, especially in the United States.

Example: Pennsylvania was one of the most important battleground states.

D. Results, majorities and electoral geography

swing state

IPA: /ˈswɪŋ steɪt/
Pronunciation: swing stayt
French: État pivot ; État indécis

Definition: A US state where both major parties have a realistic chance of winning.

Example: Candidates spend huge sums of money in swing states.

landslide victory

IPA: /ˈlændslaɪd ˈvɪktəri/
Pronunciation: LAND-slyd VIK-tuh-ree
French: victoire écrasante

Definition: An election victory by a very large margin.

Example: The opposition won a landslide victory.

narrow victory

IPA: /ˈnærəʊ ˈvɪktəri/
Pronunciation: NAR-oh VIK-tuh-ree
French: victoire serrée

Definition: An election victory by a small margin.

Example: The president secured a narrow victory in the second round.

hung parliament

IPA: /ˌhʌŋ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: hung PAR-luh-muhnt
French: parlement sans majorité absolue

Definition: A parliament in which no party has an overall majority.

Example: The election resulted in a hung parliament.

coalition government

IPA: /ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: koh-uh-LISH-uhn GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement de coalition

Definition: A government formed by two or more political parties.

Example: A coalition government was formed after weeks of negotiations.

majority

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité

Definition: The number by which one candidate or party has more votes or seats than another; also more than half.

Example: The ruling party lost its majority in parliament.

minority government

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement minoritaire

Definition: A government that does not control more than half the seats in parliament.

Example: The prime minister tried to govern with a minority government.

proportional representation

IPA: /prəˌpɔːʃənəl ˌreprɪzenˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: pruh-POR-shuh-nuhl rep-ri-zen-TAY-shuhn
French: représentation proportionnelle

Definition: An electoral system in which parties receive seats roughly in proportion to their share of the vote.

Example: Proportional representation often benefits smaller parties.

first-past-the-post

IPA: /ˌfɜːst pɑːst ðə ˈpəʊst/
Pronunciation: first past thuh pohst
French: scrutin uninominal majoritaire à un tour

Definition: An electoral system in which the candidate with the most votes in a constituency wins, even without an absolute majority.

Example: The UK uses first-past-the-post for general elections.

run-off election

IPA: /ˈrʌn ɒf ɪˌlekʃən/
Pronunciation: RUN-off i-LEK-shuhn
French: second tour ; scrutin de départage

Definition: A second election held when no candidate wins enough votes in the first round.

Example: The two leading candidates faced each other in a run-off election.

E. Electoral systems and democratic integrity

electoral college

IPA: /ɪˌlektərəl ˈkɒlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruhl KOL-ij
French: collège électoral

Definition: The system used in US presidential elections in which states appoint electors who formally choose the president.

Example: A candidate can win the popular vote but lose the Electoral College.

popular vote

IPA: /ˌpɒpjələ ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: POP-yuh-luh voht
French: vote populaire ; suffrage exprimé

Definition: The total number of votes cast by citizens, as opposed to votes in an electoral college or parliament.

Example: She won the popular vote but lost the presidency.

voter suppression

IPA: /ˈvəʊtə səˌpreʃən/
Pronunciation: VOH-tuh suh-PRESH-uhn
French: suppression du vote ; entraves au vote

Definition: Actions or rules that make it harder for certain groups of people to vote.

Example: Civil rights groups denounced the law as voter suppression.

gerrymandering

IPA: /ˈdʒerimændərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: JER-ee-man-duh-ring
French: charcutage électoral ; découpage électoral partisan

Definition: The manipulation of electoral district boundaries to benefit a party or group.

Example: Gerrymandering can distort representation in parliament or congress.

vote rigging

IPA: /ˈvəʊt ˌrɪɡɪŋ/
Pronunciation: voht RIG-ing
French: fraude électorale ; trucage du vote

Definition: Illegal manipulation of an election result.

Example: The opposition accused the government of vote rigging.

electoral fraud

IPA: /ɪˌlektərəl ˈfrɔːd/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruhl fraud
French: fraude électorale

Definition: Illegal interference with the process or results of an election.

Example: International observers reported signs of electoral fraud.

peaceful transfer of power

IPA: /ˈpiːsfəl ˈtrænsfɜːr əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: PEES-fuhl TRANS-fur ov POW-uh
French: transition pacifique du pouvoir

Definition: The orderly handover of political authority from one leader or government to another after an election.

Example: A peaceful transfer of power is a core feature of democracy.
