Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master the Past Continuous. It covers was/were + V-ing, background situations, interrupted actions, parallel actions, narrative context, contrast with the Past Simple and common French-speaker mistakes.

The golden rule

was / were + verb-ing
The government was debating the bill when the scandal broke.
Main useUse the Past Continuous to describe an action or situation in progress at a specific moment in the past.
CPGE habitUse it to set the scene before analysing an event: While the economy was slowing down, public anger was growing.
Key trapUse Past Simple for the interrupting event: was debating = background; broke = sudden event.

A. Lesson — What is the Past Continuous?

Action in progress

French: action en cours

Rule / explanation: Use the Past Continuous for an action that was happening at a specific past moment.

Example / answer: At the time, the government was trying to calm public anger.

Background situation

French: arrière-plan

Rule / explanation: It often sets the background for a main event in the Past Simple.

Example / answer: The economy was slowing down when the crisis began.

Temporary past activity

French: activité temporaire passée

Rule / explanation: It describes temporary situations around a past period.

Example / answer: During the campaign, journalists were focusing on immigration.

Parallel past actions

French: actions parallèles

Rule / explanation: Use it when two actions were happening at the same time.

Example / answer: While voters were protesting, ministers were defending the reform.

B. Form and structure

Affirmative

French: forme affirmative

Rule / explanation: Use was/were + verb-ing.

Example / answer: The author was analysing the causes of distrust.

Negative

French: forme négative

Rule / explanation: Use was not / were not + verb-ing.

Example / answer: The government was not listening to local authorities.

Question

French: question

Rule / explanation: Put was/were before the subject.

Example / answer: Was the journalist investigating the scandal?

Wh-question

French: question en wh-

Rule / explanation: Use wh-word + was/were + subject + V-ing.

Example / answer: Why were voters losing trust?

C. Past Continuous vs Past Simple

Background vs event

French: arrière-plan vs événement

Rule / explanation: Past Continuous gives context; Past Simple gives the main event.

Example / answer: The country was facing inflation when the election took place.

Long action vs short action

French: action longue vs action brève

Rule / explanation: The longer action is often Past Continuous; the shorter one is Past Simple.

Example / answer: The minister was speaking when protesters interrupted him.

Repeated finished events

French: événements terminés répétés

Rule / explanation: Use Past Simple for repeated completed actions in the past.

Example / answer: The newspaper published three articles that week.

Past situation in progress

French: situation en cours

Rule / explanation: Use Past Continuous when the focus is on the ongoing nature of the situation.

Example / answer: Public trust was declining before the scandal erupted.

D. While, when and as

While + Past Continuous

French: pendant que

Rule / explanation: Use while to introduce a continuing action.

Example / answer: While the government was negotiating, unions were preparing strikes.

When + Past Simple

French: quand

Rule / explanation: Use when for the event that happens during the background action.

Example / answer: The debate was intensifying when the report was published.

As + Past Continuous

French: alors que / pendant que

Rule / explanation: As can show two developments happening together.

Example / answer: As inequality was rising, trust in institutions was falling.

During + noun

French: pendant + nom

Rule / explanation: During is followed by a noun, not a full clause.

Example / answer: During the recession, unemployment was rising.

E. CPGE article-summary and history use

Historical context

French: contexte historique

Rule / explanation: Use Past Continuous to describe what was happening around a historical event.

Example / answer: Britain was undergoing major social change when the policy was introduced.

Article background

French: arrière-plan de l’article

Rule / explanation: Useful when explaining why an article was written at a specific time.

Example / answer: The article was published while the government was facing growing criticism.

Developing trends

French: tendances en développement

Rule / explanation: Use it to show that a trend was already in progress.

Example / answer: Rents were increasing before the crisis became national news.

Narrative opening

French: ouverture narrative

Rule / explanation: It can make a presentation or essay introduction more dynamic.

Example / answer: While voters were losing faith in institutions, populist parties were gaining ground.

F. State verbs and limits

State verbs

French: verbes d’état

Rule / explanation: Some verbs are rarely used in continuous forms: know, believe, understand, own, seem.

Example / answer: Wrong: voters were knowing. Correct: voters knew.

Believe

French: croire

Rule / explanation: Use Past Simple for beliefs in the past.

Example / answer: Many voters believed the system was unfair.

Understand

French: comprendre

Rule / explanation: Usually not continuous.

Example / answer: The author understood the complexity of the issue.

Dynamic meaning possible

French: sens dynamique possible

Rule / explanation: Some verbs can be continuous when they describe activity, not state.

Example / answer: The committee was considering several options.

G. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Using Past Continuous for every past action

French: emploi excessif

Rule / explanation: Use Past Simple for completed events.

Example / answer: Wrong: The article was appeared yesterday. Correct: The article appeared yesterday.

Forgetting was/were

French: oubli de l’auxiliaire

Rule / explanation: Past Continuous needs was/were.

Example / answer: Wrong: The government facing criticism. Correct: The government was facing criticism.

Using did with Past Continuous

French: did incorrect

Rule / explanation: Questions use was/were, not did.

Example / answer: Wrong: Did the government was negotiating? Correct: Was the government negotiating?

State verbs in continuous

French: verbes d’état

Rule / explanation: Avoid continuous forms with most state verbs.

Example / answer: Wrong: citizens were believing. Correct: citizens believed.
