This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse diplomacy: diplomatic actors, embassies, negotiation, mediation, treaties, peace talks, sanctions, soft power, sovereignty, international organisations, alliances, diplomatic crises and the search for peaceful solutions in a tense international order.

A. Core diplomacy and diplomatic actors

diplomacy

IPA: /dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie

Definition: The practice of managing relations between countries through dialogue, negotiation and representation.

Example: Diplomacy is essential to prevent conflict and build cooperation.

diplomatic relations

IPA: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk rɪˈleɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: dip-luh-MAT-ik ri-LAY-shuhnz
French: relations diplomatiques

Definition: Official relations between governments.

Example: The two countries restored diplomatic relations after years of tension.

foreign policy

IPA: /ˌfɒrən ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn POL-uh-see
French: politique étrangère

Definition: A government’s strategy in dealing with other countries.

Example: Foreign policy reflects national interests and values.

international relations

IPA: /ˌɪntəˈnæʃənəl rɪˈleɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-NASH-uh-nuhl ri-LAY-shuhnz
French: relations internationales

Definition: The study or practice of relations between states and international actors.

Example: International relations are shaped by power, law, trade and diplomacy.

statecraft

IPA: /ˈsteɪtkrɑːft/
Pronunciation: STATE-kraft
French: art de gouverner ; art diplomatique

Definition: The skill of governing and conducting foreign affairs.

Example: Statecraft requires strategy, patience and compromise.

diplomat

IPA: /ˈdɪpləmæt/
Pronunciation: DIP-luh-mat
French: diplomate

Definition: An official representing a country abroad.

Example: A diplomat must defend national interests while maintaining dialogue.

ambassador

IPA: /æmˈbæsədə/
Pronunciation: am-BAS-uh-duh
French: ambassadeur ; ambassadrice

Definition: The highest-ranking diplomatic representative of a country abroad.

Example: The ambassador met government officials to discuss the crisis.

embassy

IPA: /ˈembəsi/
Pronunciation: EM-buh-see
French: ambassade

Definition: The official office of an ambassador in a foreign country.

Example: Citizens can seek assistance from their embassy abroad.

consulate

IPA: /ˈkɒnsjʊlət/
Pronunciation: KON-syuh-luht
French: consulat

Definition: An office that helps citizens and promotes relations in another country.

Example: The consulate provides visas and assistance to nationals.

consul

IPA: /ˈkɒnsəl/
Pronunciation: KON-suhl
French: consul

Definition: An official representing a country, often dealing with citizens and visas.

Example: The consul helped citizens affected by the emergency.

envoy

IPA: /ˈenvɔɪ/
Pronunciation: EN-voy
French: émissaire ; envoyé

Definition: A person sent as a representative on a diplomatic mission.

Example: A special envoy was appointed to negotiate peace.

special envoy

IPA: /ˌspeʃəl ˈenvɔɪ/
Pronunciation: SPESH-uhl EN-voy
French: envoyé spécial

Definition: A representative sent to deal with a specific diplomatic issue.

Example: The UN special envoy tried to mediate between the parties.

diplomatic mission

IPA: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ˈmɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dip-luh-MAT-ik MISH-uhn
French: mission diplomatique

Definition: A group or office representing a country abroad.

Example: The diplomatic mission remained open despite rising tensions.

foreign ministry

IPA: /ˌfɒrən ˈmɪnɪstri/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn MIN-i-stree
French: ministère des Affaires étrangères

Definition: A government department responsible for foreign affairs.

Example: The foreign ministry issued a statement condemning the attack.

foreign minister

IPA: /ˌfɒrən ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn MIN-i-stuh
French: ministre des Affaires étrangères

Definition: A government official responsible for foreign policy.

Example: The foreign minister called for renewed negotiations.

secretary of state

IPA: /ˌsekrətəri əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: SEK-ruh-tuh-ree ov state
French: secrétaire d’État américain

Definition: In the US, the minister responsible for foreign affairs.

Example: The Secretary of State travelled to the region for talks.

diplomatic corps

IPA: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk kɔː/
Pronunciation: dip-luh-MAT-ik kor
French: corps diplomatique

Definition: All diplomats posted in a particular country.

Example: The diplomatic corps attended the official ceremony.

protocol

IPA: /ˈprəʊtəkɒl/
Pronunciation: PROH-tuh-kol
French: protocole

Definition: Formal rules governing diplomatic behaviour and ceremonies.

Example: Diplomatic protocol matters during state visits.

diplomatic immunity

IPA: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ɪˈmjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: dip-luh-MAT-ik im-YOO-nuh-tee
French: immunité diplomatique

Definition: Legal protection granted to diplomats from certain host-country laws.

Example: Diplomatic immunity is meant to protect diplomats from political pressure.

persona non grata

IPA: /pəˌsəʊnə nɒn ˈɡrɑːtə/
Pronunciation: puh-SOH-nuh non GRAH-tuh
French: persona non grata

Definition: A diplomat no longer accepted by a host country.

Example: The ambassador was declared persona non grata.

bilateral diplomacy

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈlætərəl dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: by-LAT-uh-ruhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie bilatérale

Definition: Diplomacy between two countries.

Example: Bilateral diplomacy can solve practical disputes.

multilateral diplomacy

IPA: /ˌmʌltiˈlætərəl dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: MUL-tee-LAT-uh-ruhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie multilatérale

Definition: Diplomacy involving several countries or international organisations.

Example: Climate change requires multilateral diplomacy.

regional diplomacy

IPA: /ˌriːdʒənəl dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: REE-juh-nuhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie régionale

Definition: Diplomacy focused on a specific region.

Example: Regional diplomacy is crucial in the Middle East.

shuttle diplomacy

IPA: /ˈʃʌtəl dɪˌpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: SHUT-uhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: navette diplomatique

Definition: Negotiation in which a mediator travels between parties who do not meet directly.

Example: Shuttle diplomacy helped keep communication open.

quiet diplomacy

IPA: /ˌkwaɪət dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: KWY-uht di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie discrète

Definition: Discreet diplomacy conducted away from public attention.

Example: Quiet diplomacy can be more effective than public pressure.

public diplomacy

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie publique

Definition: Efforts to influence foreign publics and improve a country’s image.

Example: Public diplomacy uses culture, media and education.

digital diplomacy

IPA: /ˌdɪdʒɪtəl dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: DIJ-i-tuhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie numérique

Definition: Diplomacy conducted or supported through digital tools and social media.

Example: Digital diplomacy allows governments to communicate directly with foreign audiences.

summit diplomacy

IPA: /ˈsʌmɪt dɪˌpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: SUM-it di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie de sommet

Definition: Diplomacy conducted through meetings between heads of state or government.

Example: Summit diplomacy can produce symbolic breakthroughs.

track-two diplomacy

IPA: /ˌtræk tuː dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: track-too di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie parallèle

Definition: Informal diplomacy involving non-official actors such as experts or NGOs.

Example: Track-two diplomacy can prepare the ground for official talks.

back-channel diplomacy

IPA: /ˌbæk ˈtʃænəl dɪˈpləʊməsi/
Pronunciation: bak-CHAN-uhl di-PLOH-muh-see
French: diplomatie de canal secret

Definition: Secret or unofficial communication between governments.

Example: Back-channel diplomacy can help when public talks are impossible.

B. Negotiation, agreements and peace processes

negotiation

IPA: /nɪˌɡəʊʃiˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ni-GOH-shee-AY-shuhn
French: négociation

Definition: Discussion aimed at reaching an agreement.

Example: Negotiation requires compromise and trust.

talks

IPA: /tɔːks/
Pronunciation: tawks
French: pourparlers

Definition: Formal or informal discussions between parties.

Example: Peace talks resumed after months of deadlock.

peace talks

IPA: /ˈpiːs tɔːks/
Pronunciation: peace tawks
French: pourparlers de paix

Definition: Negotiations aimed at ending a conflict.

Example: Peace talks collapsed after renewed fighting.

dialogue

IPA: /ˈdaɪəlɒɡ/
Pronunciation: DY-uh-log
French: dialogue

Definition: Open discussion between parties.

Example: Dialogue is necessary even between rivals.

consultation

IPA: /ˌkɒnsəlˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-suhl-TAY-shuhn
French: consultation

Definition: Discussion before making a decision.

Example: The allies held consultations before responding.

mediation

IPA: /ˌmiːdiˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: mee-dee-AY-shuhn
French: médiation

Definition: Intervention by a neutral third party to help resolve a dispute.

Example: Mediation can help parties reach a compromise.

mediator

IPA: /ˈmiːdieɪtə/
Pronunciation: MEE-dee-ay-tuh
French: médiateur ; médiatrice

Definition: A neutral person or actor helping parties reach agreement.

Example: The mediator proposed a ceasefire plan.

arbitration

IPA: /ˌɑːbɪˈtreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ar-bi-TRAY-shuhn
French: arbitrage

Definition: Settlement of a dispute by a neutral authority whose decision may be binding.

Example: Arbitration is often used in legal or trade disputes.

compromise

IPA: /ˈkɒmprəmaɪz/
Pronunciation: KOM-pruh-myze
French: compromis

Definition: An agreement in which each side gives up part of what it wants.

Example: Diplomacy often depends on compromise.

concession

IPA: /kənˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SESH-uhn
French: concession

Definition: Something given up or granted during negotiation.

Example: Both sides made concessions to reach a deal.

deadlock

IPA: /ˈdedlɒk/
Pronunciation: DED-lok
French: impasse

Definition: A situation where no progress is possible because sides cannot agree.

Example: Negotiations reached a deadlock.

breakthrough

IPA: /ˈbreɪkθruː/
Pronunciation: BRAYK-throo
French: percée

Definition: A sudden important advance in negotiations.

Example: The prisoner exchange marked a diplomatic breakthrough.

stalemate

IPA: /ˈsteɪlmeɪt/
Pronunciation: STAYL-mayt
French: impasse ; blocage

Definition: A situation where neither side can make progress.

Example: The talks ended in stalemate.

agreement

IPA: /əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-GREE-muhnt
French: accord

Definition: A shared decision or arrangement between parties.

Example: The agreement included security guarantees.

accord

IPA: /əˈkɔːd/
Pronunciation: uh-KORD
French: accord

Definition: A formal agreement between countries or groups.

Example: The peace accord was signed after years of conflict.

treaty

IPA: /ˈtriːti/
Pronunciation: TREE-tee
French: traité

Definition: A formal written agreement between states.

Example: The treaty was ratified by parliament.

pact

IPA: /pækt/
Pronunciation: pakt
French: pacte

Definition: A formal agreement between countries or groups.

Example: The defence pact strengthened regional security.

deal

IPA: /diːl/
Pronunciation: deel
French: accord

Definition: An agreement, often after negotiation.

Example: The trade deal reduced tariffs.

memorandum of understanding

IPA: /ˌmeməˈrændəm əv ˌʌndəˈstændɪŋ/
Pronunciation: mem-uh-RAN-duhm ov un-duh-STAN-ding
French: protocole d’accord

Definition: A written statement of shared intentions, usually less binding than a treaty.

Example: The two countries signed a memorandum of understanding.

MOU

IPA: /ˌem əʊ ˈjuː/
Pronunciation: em-oh-yoo
French: protocole d’accord

Definition: Abbreviation for memorandum of understanding.

Example: The MOU outlined future cooperation.

ratification

IPA: /ˌrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rat-i-fi-KAY-shuhn
French: ratification

Definition: Formal approval of a treaty or agreement.

Example: Ratification by parliament was required.

signatory

IPA: /ˈsɪɡnətəri/
Pronunciation: SIG-nuh-tuh-ree
French: signataire

Definition: A state or actor that has signed an agreement.

Example: All signatories promised to respect the ceasefire.

clause

IPA: /klɔːz/
Pronunciation: klawz
French: clause

Definition: A specific provision in a legal agreement.

Example: The treaty contains a mutual-defence clause.

framework agreement

IPA: /ˈfreɪmwɜːk əˌɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: FRAYM-work uh-GREE-muhnt
French: accord-cadre

Definition: An agreement setting broad principles for future cooperation.

Example: The framework agreement left details for later negotiations.

ceasefire

IPA: /ˈsiːsfaɪə/
Pronunciation: SEESS-fire
French: cessez-le-feu

Definition: An agreement to stop fighting temporarily or permanently.

Example: The ceasefire allowed humanitarian aid to enter the area.

truce

IPA: /truːs/
Pronunciation: trooss
French: trêve

Definition: A temporary stop in fighting.

Example: The truce was fragile but welcomed by civilians.

armistice

IPA: /ˈɑːmɪstɪs/
Pronunciation: AR-mi-stis
French: armistice

Definition: A formal agreement to stop fighting, often before a peace treaty.

Example: The armistice ended active hostilities.

peace agreement

IPA: /ˈpiːs əˌɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: peace uh-GREE-muhnt
French: accord de paix

Definition: An agreement designed to end a conflict.

Example: The peace agreement created a power-sharing system.

peace process

IPA: /ˈpiːs ˌprəʊses/
Pronunciation: peace PROH-ses
French: processus de paix

Definition: A series of negotiations aimed at ending conflict.

Example: The peace process remains vulnerable to spoilers.

conflict resolution

IPA: /ˈkɒnflɪkt ˌrezəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: KON-flikt rez-uh-LOO-shuhn
French: résolution des conflits

Definition: Methods used to end or reduce conflict.

Example: Conflict resolution requires political will.

de-escalation

IPA: /ˌdiː eskəˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-es-kuh-LAY-shuhn
French: désescalade

Definition: Reduction of tension or violence.

Example: Diplomats called for immediate de-escalation.

escalation

IPA: /ˌeskəˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: es-kuh-LAY-shuhn
French: escalade

Definition: An increase in tension, violence or conflict.

Example: Military escalation can undermine diplomacy.

deterrence

IPA: /dɪˈterəns/
Pronunciation: di-TERR-uhns
French: dissuasion

Definition: Preventing action by threatening serious consequences.

Example: Deterrence can prevent war but also increase tension.

appeasement

IPA: /əˈpiːzmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-PEEZ-muhnt
French: apaisement ; politique de conciliation

Definition: Making concessions to an aggressor to avoid conflict; often used negatively.

Example: Critics accused the government of appeasement.

conciliation

IPA: /kənˌsɪliˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kuhn-sil-ee-AY-shuhn
French: conciliation

Definition: Efforts to reduce hostility and restore trust.

Example: Conciliation can rebuild communication after a crisis.

reconciliation

IPA: /ˌrekənsɪliˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rek-uhn-sil-ee-AY-shuhn
French: réconciliation

Definition: Restoring friendly relations after conflict.

Example: Reconciliation requires truth, justice and time.

normalisation

IPA: /ˌnɔːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: NOR-muh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: normalisation

Definition: The process of making relations normal again.

Example: Normalisation of relations followed years of hostility.

rapprochement

IPA: /ræˈprɒʃmɒ̃/
Pronunciation: ra-PROSH-mon
French: rapprochement

Definition: An improvement in relations between countries.

Example: The summit signalled a rapprochement between former rivals.

recognition

IPA: /ˌrekəɡˈnɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rek-uhg-NISH-uhn
French: reconnaissance

Definition: Official acceptance of a state or government as legitimate.

Example: Diplomatic recognition can be highly political.

non-recognition

IPA: /ˌnɒn rekəɡˈnɪʃən/
Pronunciation: non-rek-uhg-NISH-uhn
French: non-reconnaissance

Definition: Refusal to officially recognise a state, government or claim.

Example: Non-recognition can isolate a regime.

sovereignty

IPA: /ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté

Definition: A state’s authority to govern itself.

Example: Diplomacy must respect national sovereignty.

territorial integrity

IPA: /ˌterɪˈtɔːriəl ɪnˈteɡrəti/
Pronunciation: terr-i-TOR-ee-uhl in-TEG-ruh-tee
French: intégrité territoriale

Definition: The principle that a state’s borders should not be violated.

Example: The UN reaffirmed the country’s territorial integrity.

self-determination

IPA: /ˌself dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: self di-tur-mi-NAY-shuhn
French: autodétermination

Definition: The right of people to decide their political status.

Example: Self-determination can clash with territorial integrity.

national interest

IPA: /ˌnæʃənəl ˈɪntrəst/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl IN-truhst
French: intérêt national

Definition: A country’s strategic, economic or political priorities.

Example: Governments justify foreign policy through national interest.

C. Power, sovereignty, sanctions and diplomatic tools

strategic interest

IPA: /strəˌtiːdʒɪk ˈɪntrəst/
Pronunciation: struh-TEE-jik IN-truhst
French: intérêt stratégique

Definition: A long-term interest linked to security or power.

Example: Energy security is a strategic interest.

sphere of influence

IPA: /ˌsfɪər əv ˈɪnfluəns/
Pronunciation: sfeer ov IN-floo-uhns
French: sphère d’influence

Definition: A region where a powerful state has strong influence.

Example: Great powers often try to maintain spheres of influence.

balance of power

IPA: /ˌbæləns əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: BAL-uhns ov POW-uh
French: équilibre des puissances

Definition: A situation where power is distributed to prevent domination by one actor.

Example: The balance of power shaped European diplomacy.

power politics

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ˌpɒlətɪks/
Pronunciation: POW-uh POL-uh-tiks
French: politique de puissance

Definition: International relations based mainly on power and national interest.

Example: Power politics often undermines international law.

realpolitik

IPA: /reɪˈɑːlpɒlɪtiːk/
Pronunciation: ray-AL-pol-i-teek
French: realpolitik

Definition: Pragmatic politics based on power rather than ideals.

Example: Realpolitik prioritises interests over moral principles.

soft power

IPA: /ˌsɒft ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: soft POW-uh
French: soft power ; puissance douce

Definition: Influence through attraction, culture and values.

Example: Cultural diplomacy can strengthen soft power.

hard power

IPA: /ˌhɑːd ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: hard POW-uh
French: hard power ; puissance coercitive

Definition: Influence through military or economic pressure.

Example: Sanctions and military force are forms of hard power.

smart power

IPA: /ˌsmɑːt ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: smart POW-uh
French: smart power ; puissance intelligente

Definition: A strategy combining soft and hard power.

Example: Smart power blends persuasion and pressure.

influence

IPA: /ˈɪnfluəns/
Pronunciation: IN-floo-uhns
French: influence

Definition: The ability to affect decisions or behaviour.

Example: Diplomacy is a tool of influence.

leverage

IPA: /ˈliːvərɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: LEE-vuh-rij
French: levier ; moyen de pression

Definition: Power or advantage used to influence negotiations.

Example: Sanctions gave negotiators leverage.

pressure

IPA: /ˈpreʃə/
Pronunciation: PRESH-uh
French: pression

Definition: Force or persuasion used to influence someone.

Example: International pressure forced the regime to negotiate.

coercion

IPA: /kəʊˈɜːʃən/
Pronunciation: koh-UR-shuhn
French: coercition

Definition: Forcing someone to act through threats or pressure.

Example: Coercion can backfire if it hardens resistance.

sanctions

IPA: /ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions

Definition: Economic or political penalties imposed to change behaviour.

Example: Sanctions are often used as a diplomatic tool.

economic sanctions

IPA: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: ee-kuh-NOM-ik SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions économiques

Definition: Economic restrictions imposed on a state, firm or individual.

Example: Economic sanctions can hurt civilians as well as leaders.

targeted sanctions

IPA: /ˈtɑːɡɪtɪd ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: TAR-git-id SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions ciblées

Definition: Sanctions aimed at specific individuals, firms or sectors.

Example: Targeted sanctions may freeze assets and restrict travel.

arms embargo

IPA: /ˈɑːmz emˌbɑːɡəʊ/
Pronunciation: armz em-BAR-goh
French: embargo sur les armes

Definition: A ban on selling or supplying weapons.

Example: The UN imposed an arms embargo.

trade embargo

IPA: /ˈtreɪd emˌbɑːɡəʊ/

French: embargo commercial

Example: A trade embargo can isolate an economy.

travel ban

IPA: /ˈtrævəl bæn/
Pronunciation: TRAV-uhl ban
French: interdiction de voyager

Example: Officials were placed under a travel ban.

asset freeze

IPA: /ˈæset friːz/

French: gel des avoirs

Example: The sanctions included an asset freeze.

diplomatic pressure

French: pression diplomatique

condemnation

French: condamnation

statement

French: déclaration

French: déclaration commune

communiqué

resolution
