A. Core China, regime and human-rights vocabulary

China

IPA: /ˈtʃaɪnə/
Pronunciation: CHY-nuh
French: Chine

Definition: A major East Asian power and the world’s second-largest economy.

Example: China has become central to debates about global power and economic dependency.

People’s Republic of China

IPA: /ˌpiːpəlz rɪˌpʌblɪk əv ˈtʃaɪnə/
Pronunciation: PEE-puhlz ri-PUB-lik ov CHY-nuh
French: République populaire de Chine

Definition: The official name of mainland China, governed by the Chinese Communist Party.

Example: The People’s Republic of China was founded in 1949.

PRC

IPA: /ˌpiː ɑː ˈsiː/
Pronunciation: pee-ar-see
French: RPC

Definition: Abbreviation for People’s Republic of China.

Example: The PRC claims Taiwan as part of its territory.

Chinese Communist Party

IPA: /ˌtʃaɪniːz ˈkɒmjʊnɪst ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: chy-NEEZ KOM-yuh-nist PAR-tee
French: Parti communiste chinois

Definition: The ruling party of the People’s Republic of China.

Example: The Chinese Communist Party controls the state, army and key institutions.

CCP

IPA: /ˌsiː siː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: see-see-pee
French: PCC

Definition: Abbreviation for Chinese Communist Party.

Example: The CCP presents political stability as essential to national development.

one-party state

IPA: /ˌwʌn ˈpɑːti steɪt/
Pronunciation: wun PAR-tee state
French: État à parti unique

Definition: A political system dominated by a single ruling party.

Example: China is a one-party state.

authoritarianism

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: autoritarisme

Definition: A system of government with limited political freedoms and strong central control.

Example: Chinese authoritarianism combines party control, nationalism and economic performance.

authoritarian regime

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən reɪˈʒiːm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uhn ray-ZHEEM
French: régime autoritaire

Definition: A government that restricts political pluralism and civil liberties.

Example: Critics describe China as an authoritarian regime.

party-state

IPA: /ˈpɑːti steɪt/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee state
French: parti-État

Definition: A system in which the ruling party and state institutions are closely fused.

Example: In China, the party-state shapes political, economic and social life.

centralised power

IPA: /ˈsentrəlaɪzd ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: SEN-truh-lyzd POW-uh
French: pouvoir centralisé

Definition: Power concentrated in central institutions or leaders.

Example: Centralised power allows rapid decisions but limits accountability.

political control

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl kənˈtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl kuhn-TROHL
French: contrôle politique

Definition: Control over political expression, institutions and opposition.

Example: Political control has tightened in recent years.

state censorship

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈsensəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: state SEN-suh-ship
French: censure d’État

Definition: Government control or suppression of information.

Example: State censorship limits criticism of the government.

surveillance state

IPA: /səˈveɪləns steɪt/
Pronunciation: suh-VAY-luhns state
French: État de surveillance

Definition: A state using extensive monitoring of citizens.

Example: China is often described as a surveillance state.

social credit system

IPA: /ˌsəʊʃəl ˈkredɪt ˌsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: SOH-shuhl KRED-it SIS-tuhm
French: système de crédit social

Definition: A system using data and records to assess trustworthiness or compliance.

Example: The social credit system raises concerns about privacy and control.

Great Firewall

IPA: /ˌɡreɪt ˈfaɪəwɔːl/
Pronunciation: great FY-uh-wall
French: Grand pare-feu chinois

Definition: The system controlling and filtering internet access in China.

Example: The Great Firewall blocks many foreign websites and platforms.

human rights

IPA: /ˌhjuːmən ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: HYOO-muhn rites
French: droits humains

Definition: Basic rights and freedoms belonging to all people.

Example: Human rights concerns shape Western policy towards China.

civil liberties

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈlɪbətiz/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl LIB-uh-teez
French: libertés civiles

Definition: Basic freedoms such as speech, assembly and religion.

Example: Civil liberties are heavily restricted in China.

freedom of speech

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov speech
French: liberté d’expression

Definition: The right to express opinions freely.

Example: Freedom of speech is limited by censorship.

freedom of the press

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ðə ˈpres/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov thuh press
French: liberté de la presse

Definition: The right of media to report without censorship or intimidation.

Example: Freedom of the press is restricted by state control.

political dissident

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl ˈdɪsɪdənt/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl DIS-i-duhnt
French: dissident politique

Definition: A person who publicly disagrees with the government.

Example: Political dissidents may face surveillance or imprisonment.

crackdown

IPA: /ˈkrækdaʊn/
Pronunciation: KRAK-down
French: répression

Definition: Strong official action to stop dissent, protest or illegal activity.

Example: The crackdown on dissent drew international criticism.

repression

IPA: /rɪˈpreʃən/
Pronunciation: ri-PRESH-uhn
French: répression

Definition: Forceful control or suppression of people or opposition.

Example: Repression can silence civil society.

Xinjiang

IPA: /ˌʃɪnˈdʒæŋ/
Pronunciation: shin-JANG
French: Xinjiang

Definition: A region in northwestern China with a large Uyghur population.

Example: Xinjiang has become a major human-rights issue.

Uyghurs

IPA: /ˈwiːɡʊəz/
Pronunciation: WEE-goorz
French: Ouïghours

Definition: A mostly Muslim Turkic ethnic group living mainly in Xinjiang.

Example: Uyghurs have faced mass surveillance and repression.

re-education camp

IPA: /ˌriː edjʊˈkeɪʃən kæmp/
Pronunciation: ree-ej-yoo-KAY-shuhn camp
French: camp de rééducation

Definition: A detention facility officially described as ideological or vocational training.

Example: Reports of re-education camps in Xinjiang have alarmed rights groups.

forced labour

IPA: /ˌfɔːst ˈleɪbə/
Pronunciation: forst LAY-buh
French: travail forcé

Definition: Work imposed under threat or coercion.

Example: Forced labour allegations affect global supply chains.

Hong Kong

IPA: /ˌhɒŋ ˈkɒŋ/
Pronunciation: hong KONG
French: Hong Kong

Definition: A Chinese special administrative region with a distinct legal and political history.

Example: Hong Kong’s freedoms have narrowed under the national security law.

one country, two systems

IPA: /ˌwʌn ˈkʌntri ˌtuː ˈsɪstəmz/
Pronunciation: wun KUN-tree too SIS-tuhmz
French: un pays, deux systèmes

Definition: The principle meant to preserve Hong Kong’s autonomy within China.

Example: One country, two systems has been weakened in practice.

national security law

IPA: /ˌnæʃənəl sɪˈkjʊərəti lɔː/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl si-KYOOR-uh-tee law
French: loi sur la sécurité nationale

Definition: A law aimed at protecting state security, often criticised when used against dissent.

Example: Hong Kong’s national security law has restricted opposition politics.

Tibet

IPA: /tɪˈbet/
Pronunciation: ti-BET
French: Tibet

Definition: A region under Chinese control with a distinct culture and religious identity.

Example: Tibet remains a sensitive issue in Chinese politics.

economic rise

IPA: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk raɪz/
Pronunciation: ee-kuh-NOM-ik ryze
French: essor économique

Definition: Rapid growth in economic power and influence.

Example: China’s economic rise has reshaped global trade.

B. Economy, trade, technology and state capitalism

economic powerhouse

IPA: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈpaʊəhaʊs/
Pronunciation: ee-kuh-NOM-ik POW-uh-house
French: puissance économique

Definition: A country or region with great economic strength.

Example: China is an economic powerhouse.

manufacturing hub

IPA: /ˌmænjʊˈfæktʃərɪŋ hʌb/
Pronunciation: man-yuh-FAK-chuh-ring hub
French: centre manufacturier

Definition: A major centre for industrial production.

Example: China became the world’s manufacturing hub.

factory of the world

IPA: /ˌfæktəri əv ðə ˈwɜːld/
Pronunciation: FAK-tuh-ree ov thuh world
French: usine du monde

Definition: A phrase describing China’s central role in global manufacturing.

Example: China was long called the factory of the world.

export-led growth

IPA: /ˌekspɔːt led ˈɡrəʊθ/
Pronunciation: EK-sport-led growth
French: croissance tirée par les exportations

Definition: Economic growth driven by selling goods abroad.

Example: Export-led growth helped China develop rapidly.

global supply chain

IPA: /ˌɡləʊbəl səˈplaɪ tʃeɪn/
Pronunciation: GLOH-buhl suh-PLY chayn
French: chaîne d’approvisionnement mondiale

Definition: A worldwide network used to produce and deliver goods.

Example: China is deeply embedded in global supply chains.

supply-chain dependency

IPA: /səˈplaɪ tʃeɪn dɪˈpendənsi/
Pronunciation: suh-PLY chayn di-PEN-duhn-see
French: dépendance aux chaînes d’approvisionnement

Definition: Reliance on a supply chain controlled by or linked to another country.

Example: The pandemic exposed supply-chain dependency on China.

trade surplus

IPA: /ˈtreɪd ˌsɜːpləs/
Pronunciation: trade SUR-pluhs
French: excédent commercial

Definition: A situation where a country exports more than it imports.

Example: China runs large trade surpluses with several countries.

trade deficit

IPA: /ˈtreɪd ˌdefɪsɪt/
Pronunciation: trade DEF-i-sit
French: déficit commercial

Definition: A situation where a country imports more than it exports.

Example: The US trade deficit with China is politically sensitive.

foreign direct investment

IPA: /ˌfɒrən dəˌrekt ɪnˈvestmənt/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn di-REKT in-VEST-muhnt
French: investissement direct étranger

Definition: Investment by a company in productive activity abroad.

Example: Foreign direct investment helped China integrate into the global economy.

FDI

IPA: /ˌef diː ˈaɪ/
Pronunciation: eff-dee-eye
French: IDE

Definition: Abbreviation for foreign direct investment.

Example: China attracted huge amounts of FDI.

state capitalism

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈkæpɪtəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: state KAP-i-tuhl-iz-uhm
French: capitalisme d’État

Definition: An economic system where the state plays a powerful role in markets and firms.

Example: Chinese state capitalism combines markets with party control.

state-owned enterprise

IPA: /ˌsteɪt əʊnd ˈentəpraɪz/
Pronunciation: state-ohnd EN-tuh-pryze
French: entreprise publique

Definition: A company owned or controlled by the government.

Example: State-owned enterprises remain important in China.

SOE

IPA: /ˌes əʊ ˈiː/
Pronunciation: ess-oh-ee
French: entreprise publique

Definition: Abbreviation for state-owned enterprise.

Example: SOEs receive strong state support.

industrial policy

IPA: /ɪnˈdʌstriəl ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: in-DUS-tree-uhl POL-uh-see
French: politique industrielle

Definition: Government strategy to develop selected industries.

Example: China uses industrial policy to support strategic sectors.

subsidy

IPA: /ˈsʌbsɪdi/
Pronunciation: SUB-si-dee
French: subvention

Definition: Financial support from the state to firms or consumers.

Example: Chinese subsidies are a source of trade tension.

overcapacity

IPA: /ˌəʊvəkəˈpæsəti/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-kuh-PAS-uh-tee
French: surcapacité

Definition: Producing more than markets can absorb.

Example: Chinese overcapacity in some sectors worries Western governments.

dumping

IPA: /ˈdʌmpɪŋ/
Pronunciation: DUMP-ing
French: dumping

Definition: Selling goods abroad at artificially low prices.

Example: Accusations of dumping fuel trade disputes.

technology transfer

IPA: /tekˈnɒlədʒi ˈtrænsfɜː/
Pronunciation: tek-NOL-uh-jee TRANS-fur
French: transfert de technologie

Definition: The movement of knowledge, technology or know-how between actors.

Example: Forced technology transfer has been a major complaint from foreign firms.

intellectual property

IPA: /ˌɪntəˈlektʃuəl ˈprɒpəti/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-LEK-choo-uhl PROP-uh-tee
French: propriété intellectuelle

Definition: Legal rights over inventions, designs, brands or creative works.

Example: Intellectual property protection is central to trade disputes with China.

IP theft

IPA: /ˌaɪ ˈpiː θeft/
Pronunciation: eye-pee theft
French: vol de propriété intellectuelle

Definition: The stealing of intellectual property.

Example: US officials have accused Chinese actors of IP theft.

innovation race

IPA: /ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən reɪs/

French: course à l’innovation

French: secteur de haute technologie

Definition: Industries based on advanced technology.

semiconductors

IPA: /ˌsemikənˈdʌktəz/
Pronunciation: SEM-ee-kuhn-DUK-tuhz
French: semi-conducteurs

Definition: Computer chips used in electronics and advanced technology.

Example: Semiconductors are central to US-China competition.

chip war

IPA: /ˈtʃɪp wɔː/
Pronunciation: chip war
French: guerre des puces

artificial intelligence

Pronunciation: ar-ti-FISH-uhl in-TEL-i-juhns
French: intelligence artificielle

AI race

French: 5G

Definition: Fifth-generation mobile network technology.

Huawei

IPA: /ˈhwɑːweɪ/

French: Huawei

Example: Huawei became a symbol of technology rivalry.

digital authoritarianism

French: BRI ; nouvelles routes de la soie

French: investissement portuaire

C. Global influence, Belt and Road and strategic resources

strategic foothold

IPA: /strəˌtiːdʒɪk ˈfʊthəʊld/
Pronunciation: struh-TEE-jik FOOT-hohld
French: point d’appui stratégique

Definition: A position giving long-term strategic influence.

soft power

IPA: /ˌsɒft ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: soft POW-uh
French: soft power ; puissance douce

IPA: /ˌʃɑːp ˈpaʊə/

French: sharp power ; pouvoir d’influence coercitif

influence operation

Pronunciation: IN-floo-uhns op-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: opération d’influence

energy security

IPA: /ˈenədʒi sɪˌkjʊərəti/

French: sécurité énergétique

rare earths

IPA: /ˌreər ˈɜːθs/

French: terres rares
