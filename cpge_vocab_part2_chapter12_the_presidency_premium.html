Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse the US presidency: executive power, presidential roles, the White House, the Cabinet, foreign policy, war powers, executive orders, impeachment, checks and balances, and presidential leadership. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as executive order, approval rating, commander-in-chief, executive privilege, impeachment, State of the Union and presidential agenda.
For oral exams Use this vocabulary to explain how presidents act, how their power is limited, and how crises often expand presidential authority.

A. The presidential office and symbolic roles

presidency

IPA: /ˈprezɪdənsi/
Pronunciation: PREZ-i-duhn-see
French: présidence

Definition: The office, role or period of service of a president.

Example: The presidency gives one person enormous visibility and symbolic power.

President

IPA: /ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: PREZ-i-duhnt
French: président

Definition: The elected head of state and head of government in the United States.

Example: The President appoints judges, signs bills and directs foreign policy.

head of state

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: hed ov stayt
French: chef de l’État

Definition: The official representative of a country, especially in ceremonial and diplomatic matters.

Example: In the US, the President is both head of state and head of government.

head of government

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: hed ov GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: chef du gouvernement

Definition: The political leader responsible for running the government.

Example: The President acts as head of government by leading the executive branch.

commander-in-chief

IPA: /kəˌmɑːndər ɪn ˈtʃiːf/
Pronunciation: kuh-MAN-duhr in cheef
French: commandant en chef

Definition: The president’s constitutional role as head of the armed forces.

Example: As commander-in-chief, the President can direct military operations.

chief executive

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf ɪɡˈzekjətɪv/
Pronunciation: cheef ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv
French: chef de l’exécutif

Definition: The president’s role as leader of the executive branch.

Example: As chief executive, the President oversees federal agencies.

chief diplomat

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf ˈdɪpləmæt/
Pronunciation: cheef DIP-luh-mat
French: chef de la diplomatie

Definition: The president’s role in directing foreign policy and representing the country abroad.

Example: As chief diplomat, the President meets foreign leaders.

party leader

IPA: /ˈpɑːti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de parti

Definition: The president’s political role as leader of their party.

Example: The President also acts as party leader during election campaigns.

bully pulpit

IPA: /ˈbʊli ˈpʊlpɪt/
Pronunciation: BUL-ee PUL-pit
French: tribune présidentielle ; pouvoir d’influence médiatique

Definition: The president’s ability to use the visibility of the office to influence public opinion.

Example: The President used the bully pulpit to push for healthcare reform.

Oval Office

IPA: /ˌəʊvəl ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: OH-vuhl OFF-is
French: Bureau ovale

Definition: The president’s official office in the White House.

Example: The speech was delivered from the Oval Office.

White House

IPA: /ˌwaɪt ˈhaʊs/
Pronunciation: white howss
French: Maison-Blanche

Definition: The official residence and workplace of the US President; also used to refer to the president’s administration.

Example: The White House denied the allegations.

administration

IPA: /ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ad-min-i-STRAY-shuhn
French: administration ; gouvernement présidentiel

Definition: The government led by a particular president.

Example: The Biden administration introduced new climate measures.

Vice President

IPA: /ˌvaɪs ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: vyss PREZ-i-duhnt
French: vice-président ; vice-présidente

Definition: The official who replaces the president if needed and presides over the Senate.

Example: The Vice President cast a tie-breaking vote in the Senate.

First Lady

IPA: /ˌfɜːst ˈleɪdi/
Pronunciation: first LAY-dee
French: Première dame

Definition: The wife of the President, often involved in public causes but holding no elected office.

Example: The First Lady launched a campaign on children’s health.

presidential term

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl tɜːm/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl term
French: mandat présidentiel

Definition: The period during which a president holds office, four years in the United States.

Example: A presidential term lasts four years.

term limit

IPA: /ˈtɜːm ˌlɪmɪt/
Pronunciation: term LIM-it
French: limite de mandat

Definition: A legal limit on how many terms someone can serve.

Example: The Twenty-Second Amendment created a two-term limit for presidents.

lame duck

IPA: /ˌleɪm ˈdʌk/
Pronunciation: laym duck
French: responsable en fin de mandat ; canard boiteux

Definition: An elected official whose successor has been chosen or whose time in office is ending, reducing their power.

Example: A lame-duck president may struggle to pass major reforms.

president-elect

IPA: /ˌprezɪdənt ɪˈlekt/
Pronunciation: PREZ-i-duhnt i-LEKT
French: président élu

Definition: A person elected president but not yet sworn into office.

Example: The president-elect announced his Cabinet nominees.

inauguration

IPA: /ɪˌnɔːɡjəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: i-naw-gyuh-RAY-shuhn
French: investiture

Definition: The ceremony marking the beginning of a president’s term.

Example: The inauguration takes place in January.

inaugural address

IPA: /ɪˌnɔːɡjərəl əˈdres/
Pronunciation: i-NAW-gyuh-ruhl uh-DRESS
French: discours d’investiture

Definition: The speech given by a president at the start of their term.

Example: The inaugural address called for national unity.

oath of office

IPA: /ˌəʊθ əv ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: ohth ov OFF-is
French: serment d’investiture

Definition: The formal promise made by a president before taking office.

Example: The President takes the oath of office before assuming power.

to take office

IPA: /teɪk ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: tayk OFF-is
French: entrer en fonction

Definition: To officially begin a political position.

Example: The new President took office in January.

to be sworn in

IPA: /bi ˌswɔːn ˈɪn/
Pronunciation: bee sworn in
French: prêter serment

Definition: To officially take an oath and begin a position.

Example: The President was sworn in on the steps of the Capitol.

executive branch

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv brɑːntʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv branch
French: pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The branch of government led by the President and responsible for enforcing laws.

Example: The executive branch includes federal departments and agencies.

B. Executive powers and the administration

executive power

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv POW-uh
French: pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The authority to enforce laws and manage government operations.

Example: Executive power tends to expand during emergencies.

executive authority

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: autorité exécutive

Definition: The legal power held by the executive branch.

Example: The order tested the limits of executive authority.

executive order

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OR-duh
French: décret présidentiel

Definition: A directive issued by the President to manage federal government operations.

Example: The President signed an executive order on immigration.

presidential memorandum

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ˌmeməˈrændəm/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl mem-uh-RAN-duhm
French: mémorandum présidentiel

Definition: A presidential directive used to manage executive policy or administration.

Example: The administration issued a presidential memorandum on workplace rules.

proclamation

IPA: /ˌprɒkləˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: prok-luh-MAY-shuhn
French: proclamation

Definition: An official public statement issued by the President.

Example: The President issued a proclamation marking a national holiday.

state of emergency

IPA: /ˌsteɪt əv ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi/
Pronunciation: stayt ov i-MUR-juhn-see
French: état d’urgence

Definition: A situation in which the executive claims special powers because of a crisis.

Example: The President declared a state of emergency after the disaster.

emergency powers

IPA: /ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: i-MUR-juhn-see POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs d’urgence

Definition: Special powers used by the executive during a crisis.

Example: Emergency powers can raise concerns about civil liberties.

pardon

IPA: /ˈpɑːdən/
Pronunciation: PAR-duhn
French: grâce présidentielle

Definition: The president’s power to forgive a federal crime or remove punishment.

Example: The President granted a controversial pardon.

commutation

IPA: /ˌkɒmjʊˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kom-yoo-TAY-shuhn
French: commutation de peine

Definition: A reduction of a criminal sentence by executive authority.

Example: The President issued a commutation for the prisoner.

cabinet

IPA: /ˈkæbɪnət/
Pronunciation: KAB-i-nuht
French: cabinet ; gouvernement présidentiel

Definition: The group of senior officials heading departments and advising the President.

Example: The Cabinet discussed the crisis at the White House.

cabinet secretary

IPA: /ˌkæbɪnət ˈsekrətəri/
Pronunciation: KAB-i-nuht SEK-ruh-tuh-ree
French: secrétaire de cabinet ; ministre américain

Definition: The head of a federal department in the president’s Cabinet.

Example: The Cabinet secretary defended the administration’s policy.

Secretary of State

IPA: /ˌsekrətəri əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: SEK-ruh-tuh-ree ov stayt
French: secrétaire d’État américain ; ministre des Affaires étrangères

Definition: The Cabinet official responsible for foreign affairs.

Example: The Secretary of State met European allies.

Secretary of Defense

IPA: /ˌsekrətəri əv dɪˈfens/
Pronunciation: SEK-ruh-tuh-ree ov di-FENSS
French: secrétaire à la Défense

Definition: The Cabinet official responsible for the Department of Defense.

Example: The Secretary of Defense briefed Congress.

Attorney General

IPA: /əˌtɜːni ˈdʒenərəl/
Pronunciation: uh-TUR-nee JEN-uh-ruhl
French: ministre de la Justice ; procureur général

Definition: The head of the Department of Justice and chief legal officer of the federal government.

Example: The Attorney General announced a federal investigation.

Department of Justice

IPA: /dɪˌpɑːtmənt əv ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: di-PART-muhnt ov JUS-tis
French: ministère de la Justice

Definition: The federal department responsible for law enforcement and legal affairs.

Example: The Department of Justice challenged the state law.

federal agency

IPA: /ˈfedərəl ˈeɪdʒənsi/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl AY-juhn-see
French: agence fédérale

Definition: A government organisation responsible for a specific area of federal policy.

Example: Federal agencies implement presidential policy.

bureaucracy

IPA: /bjʊəˈrɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: byoo-ROK-ruh-see
French: bureaucratie ; administration

Definition: The administrative system of departments and officials that carries out government policy.

Example: Presidents often struggle to control the federal bureaucracy.

civil servant

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈsɜːvənt/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl SUR-vuhnt
French: fonctionnaire

Definition: A professional government employee who works in the public administration.

Example: Civil servants are expected to serve different administrations.

adviser

IPA: /ədˈvaɪzə/
Pronunciation: ad-VY-zuh
French: conseiller ; conseillère

Definition: A person who gives advice to the President or a political leader.

Example: Senior advisers shaped the President’s response.

White House staff

IPA: /ˌwaɪt haʊs ˈstɑːf/
Pronunciation: white howss staff
French: personnel de la Maison-Blanche

Definition: The team of advisers and employees working directly for the President.

Example: White House staff coordinated the announcement.

Chief of Staff

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf əv ˈstɑːf/
Pronunciation: cheef ov staff
French: chef de cabinet de la Maison-Blanche

Definition: The senior aide who manages the White House staff and controls access to the President.

Example: The Chief of Staff resigned after the scandal.

National Security Council

IPA: /ˌnæʃənəl sɪˈkjʊərəti ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl si-KYOOR-uh-tee KOWN-suhl
French: Conseil de sécurité nationale

Definition: A White House body advising the President on national security and foreign policy.

Example: The National Security Council met after the attack.

presidential adviser

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ədˈvaɪzə/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl ad-VY-zuh
French: conseiller présidentiel

Definition: A person who advises the President on policy, politics or strategy.

Example: A presidential adviser defended the decision.

press secretary

IPA: /ˈpres ˌsekrətəri/
Pronunciation: press SEK-ruh-tuh-ree
French: porte-parole de la Maison-Blanche

Definition: The official who speaks to the media on behalf of the White House.

Example: The press secretary answered questions about the policy.

briefing room

IPA: /ˈbriːfɪŋ ruːm/
Pronunciation: BREEF-ing room
French: salle de presse ; salle de briefing

Definition: The room where White House press briefings take place.

Example: Reporters gathered in the briefing room.

State of the Union

IPA: /ˌsteɪt əv ði ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: stayt ov thee YOO-nee-uhn
French: discours sur l’état de l’Union

Definition: The annual speech in which the President addresses Congress and presents priorities.

Example: The State of the Union focused on the economy.

C. Presidential agenda, leadership and communication

approval rating

IPA: /əˈpruːvəl ˈreɪtɪŋ/
Pronunciation: uh-PROO-vuhl RAY-ting
French: taux d’approbation ; cote de popularité

Definition: The percentage of people who approve of the President’s performance.

Example: The President’s approval rating fell sharply.

public mandate

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk ˈmændeɪt/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik MAN-dayt
French: mandat populaire

Definition: The authority a president claims from voters to carry out a programme.

Example: The President claimed a public mandate for reform.

honeymoon period

IPA: /ˈhʌnimuːn ˌpɪəriəd/
Pronunciation: HUN-ee-moon PEER-ee-uhd
French: état de grâce

Definition: The early period of a presidency when public and political support may be higher.

Example: The honeymoon period ended after the first major crisis.

presidential agenda

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl əˈdʒendə/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl uh-JEN-duh
French: programme présidentiel ; agenda présidentiel

Definition: The president’s list of policy priorities.

Example: Congress blocked much of the presidential agenda.

domestic agenda

IPA: /dəˈmestɪk əˈdʒendə/
Pronunciation: duh-MES-tik uh-JEN-duh
French: programme intérieur

Definition: A president’s priorities on internal national issues.

Example: The domestic agenda focused on healthcare and jobs.

foreign policy agenda

IPA: /ˈfɒrən ˈpɒləsi əˈdʒendə/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn POL-uh-see uh-JEN-duh
French: agenda de politique étrangère

Definition: A president’s priorities in international relations.

Example: The foreign policy agenda included support for allies.

campaign promise

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn ˈprɒmɪs/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN PROM-is
French: promesse de campagne

Definition: A promise made during an election campaign.

Example: The tax cut fulfilled a major campaign promise.

policy priority

IPA: /ˈpɒləsi praɪˈɒrəti/
Pronunciation: POL-uh-see pry-OR-uh-tee
French: priorité politique

Definition: An issue or action considered especially important by an administration.

Example: Climate change became a policy priority.

legacy

IPA: /ˈleɡəsi/
Pronunciation: LEG-uh-see
French: héritage politique

Definition: The long-term impact by which a president is remembered.

Example: The President hoped healthcare reform would define his legacy.

presidential leadership

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ˈliːdəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl LEE-duh-ship
French: leadership présidentiel

Definition: The ability of a president to guide policy, persuade others and respond to crises.

Example: Presidential leadership is tested during national emergencies.

crisis management

IPA: /ˈkraɪsɪs ˈmænɪdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: KRY-sis MAN-ij-muhnt
French: gestion de crise

Definition: The handling of emergencies or serious problems by leaders or institutions.

Example: The pandemic became a test of presidential crisis management.

Commander-in-chief power

IPA: /kəˌmɑːndər ɪn ˈtʃiːf ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: kuh-MAN-duhr in cheef POW-uh
French: pouvoir de commandant en chef

Definition: The president’s authority over the military as commander-in-chief.

Example: Commander-in-chief power is central in wartime.

war powers

IPA: /ˈwɔː ˌpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: war POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs de guerre

Definition: The legal and constitutional powers related to starting or conducting war.

Example: War powers are divided between Congress and the President.

War Powers Resolution

IPA: /ˈwɔː ˌpaʊəz ˌrezəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: war POW-uhz rez-uh-LOO-shuhn
French: War Powers Resolution ; loi sur les pouvoirs de guerre

Definition: A US law intended to limit the president’s ability to deploy troops without congressional approval.

Example: The War Powers Resolution remains controversial.

D. Foreign policy, war powers and national security

treaty

IPA: /ˈtriːti/
Pronunciation: TREE-tee
French: traité

Definition: A formal agreement between countries, which in the US requires Senate approval.

Example: The treaty required a two-thirds Senate vote.

executive agreement

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv uh-GREE-muhnt
French: accord exécutif

Definition: An international agreement made by the President without the full treaty process.

Example: Presidents often use executive agreements in foreign policy.

sanctions

IPA: /ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions

Definition: Political or economic penalties imposed on a country, group or individual.

Example: The President announced new sanctions against the regime.

diplomatic recognition

IPA: /ˌdɪpləˈmætɪk ˌrekəɡˈnɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dip-luh-MAT-ik rek-uhg-NISH-uhn
French: reconnaissance diplomatique

Definition: The formal acceptance of another state or government as legitimate.

Example: The administration granted diplomatic recognition to the new government.

national security

IPA: /ˌnæʃənəl sɪˈkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité nationale

Definition: The protection of a country from threats, especially military, terrorist or strategic threats.

Example: National security arguments are often used to justify secrecy.

executive privilege

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv PRIV-uh-lij
French: privilège exécutif

Definition: The claimed right of the President to keep some communications confidential.

Example: The President invoked executive privilege to withhold documents.

classified information

IPA: /ˌklæsɪfaɪd ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: KLAS-i-fyd in-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: information classifiée

Definition: Secret government information whose release is restricted for security reasons.

Example: The investigation concerned classified information.

checks and balances

IPA: /ˌtʃeks ən ˈbælənsɪz/
Pronunciation: cheks uhn BAL-uhn-siz
French: freins et contrepoids ; contre-pouvoirs

Definition: A system in which each branch of government can limit the power of the others.

Example: Checks and balances limit presidential power.

congressional oversight

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle du Congrès

Definition: The power of Congress to monitor and investigate the executive branch.

Example: Congressional oversight can expose executive misconduct.

confirmation hearing

IPA: /ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn HEER-ing
French: audition de confirmation

Definition: A Senate hearing used to examine a presidential nominee.

Example: The confirmation hearing became highly partisan.

Senate confirmation

IPA: /ˌsenət ˌkɒnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht kon-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: confirmation par le Sénat

Definition: The Senate’s formal approval of a presidential appointment.

Example: Supreme Court justices require Senate confirmation.

impeachment

IPA: /ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt/
Pronunciation: im-PEECH-muhnt
French: procédure de destitution

Definition: The process of charging a president or high official with serious misconduct.

Example: Impeachment is a constitutional check on presidential abuse.

E. Checks, impeachment and high-value presidential verbs

House impeachment

IPA: /ˌhaʊs ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt/
Pronunciation: howss im-PEECH-muhnt
French: mise en accusation par la Chambre

Definition: The House of Representatives’ formal accusation of a president or official.

Example: House impeachment does not automatically remove a president.

Senate trial

IPA: /ˌsenət ˈtraɪəl/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht TRY-uhl
French: procès au Sénat

Definition: The Senate process that follows impeachment and decides whether to remove the official.

Example: The Senate trial ended in acquittal.

removal from office

IPA: /rɪˈmuːvəl frəm ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: ri-MOO-vuhl frum OFF-is
French: destitution ; révocation

Definition: The act of forcing an official to leave their position.

Example: Removal from office requires conviction in the Senate.

executive overreach

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˌəʊvəˈriːtʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OH-vuh-reech
French: abus de pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The use of presidential or executive power beyond proper limits.

Example: Critics accused the administration of executive overreach.

imperial presidency

IPA: /ɪmˈpɪəriəl ˈprezɪdənsi/
Pronunciation: im-PEER-ee-uhl PREZ-i-duhn-see
French: présidence impériale

Definition: A term used to criticise a presidency seen as too powerful and insufficiently checked.

Example: The war revived fears of an imperial presidency.

unitary executive theory

IPA: /ˈjuːnɪtəri ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈθɪəri/
Pronunciation: YOO-ni-tuh-ree ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv THEE-uh-ree
French: théorie de l’exécutif unitaire

Definition: The theory that the President has broad control over the executive branch.

Example: Supporters of unitary executive theory favour strong presidential control.

abuse of office

IPA: /əˌbjuːs əv ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: uh-BYOOSS ov OFF-is
French: abus de fonction

Definition: The improper use of an official position for personal or political gain.

Example: The inquiry examined allegations of abuse of office.

obstruction of justice

IPA: /əbˌstrʌkʃən əv ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: uhb-STRUK-shuhn ov JUS-tis
French: entrave à la justice

Definition: Interference with an investigation or legal process.

Example: The report raised questions about obstruction of justice.

to sign a bill into law

IPA: /saɪn ə ˈbɪl ˈɪntuː ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: syne uh bil IN-too law
French: promulguer une loi

Definition: To approve a bill officially so that it becomes law.

Example: The President signed the bill into law.

to veto legislation

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: opposer son veto à une loi

Definition: To reject legislation passed by Congress.

Example: The President threatened to veto the legislation.

to issue an executive order

IPA: /ˈɪʃuː ən ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: ISH-oo uhn ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OR-duh
French: émettre un décret présidentiel

Definition: To formally release a presidential directive.

Example: The President issued an executive order on border security.

to appoint judges

IPA: /əˈpɔɪnt ˈdʒʌdʒɪz/
Pronunciation: uh-POINT JUJ-iz
French: nommer des juges

Definition: To choose judges for official positions.

Example: Presidents can shape the judiciary by appointing judges.

to nominate a justice

IPA: /ˈnɒmɪneɪt ə ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: NOM-i-nayt uh JUS-tis
French: nommer un juge à la Cour suprême

Definition: To formally propose someone for the Supreme Court.

Example: The President nominated a conservative justice.

to face impeachment

IPA: /feɪs ɪmˈpiːtʃmənt/
Pronunciation: fays im-PEECH-muhnt
French: faire face à une procédure de destitution

Definition: To be subject to possible impeachment proceedings.

Example: The President faced impeachment after the scandal.

to invoke executive privilege

IPA: /ɪnˈvəʊk ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: in-VOHK ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv PRIV-uh-lij
French: invoquer le privilège exécutif

Definition: To claim the right to keep presidential communications confidential.

Example: The White House invoked executive privilege.

to bypass Congress

IPA: /ˈbaɪpɑːs ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: BY-pass KON-gress
French: contourner le Congrès

Definition: To act without going through Congress.

Example: The executive order was seen as an attempt to bypass Congress.

to expand presidential power

IPA: /ɪkˈspænd ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: ik-SPAND prez-i-DEN-shuhl POW-uh
French: étendre le pouvoir présidentiel

Definition: To increase the authority or influence of the presidency.

Example: National crises often expand presidential power.

to constrain the president

IPA: /kənˈstreɪn ðə ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STRAYN thuh PREZ-i-duhnt
French: limiter le président

Definition: To limit what the president can do.

Example: Courts and Congress can constrain the president.
