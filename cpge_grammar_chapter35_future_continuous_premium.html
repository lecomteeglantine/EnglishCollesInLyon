Chapter 35 — Future Continuous
B2 grammar for actions in progress in the future, projections, schedules and polite questions.

Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master the Future Continuous. It covers will be + V-ing, future actions in progress, expected developments, planned situations, polite questions, contrast with Future Simple and common French-speaker mistakes.

The golden rule

will be + verb-ing
This time next year, voters will be discussing the consequences of the reform.
Main useUse the Future Continuous for an action or situation that will be in progress at a specific future moment.
CPGE habitUse it for projected developments: By then, governments will be trying to adapt to climate pressure.
Key trapFuture Continuous focuses on an ongoing future process, not a completed result.

A. Lesson — What is the Future Continuous?

Future action in progress

French: action en cours dans le futur

Rule / explanation: Use it for something that will be happening at a specific future time.

Example / answer: At this time tomorrow, Parliament will be debating the bill.

Expected future development

French: évolution attendue

Rule / explanation: Use it for trends or processes expected to continue in the future.

Example / answer: In the coming years, cities will be facing growing housing pressure.

Future background

French: arrière-plan futur

Rule / explanation: It can set the context for another future event.

Example / answer: When the next election takes place, voters will be judging the reform’s results.

Analytical value

French: valeur analytique

Rule / explanation: It helps describe future processes rather than simple predictions.

Example / answer: Future governments will be dealing with the consequences of today’s choices.

B. Form and structure

Affirmative

French: forme affirmative

Rule / explanation: Use will be + verb-ing for all subjects.

Example / answer: The committee will be reviewing the proposal next week.

Negative

French: forme négative

Rule / explanation: Use will not be / won’t be + verb-ing.

Example / answer: The government will not be funding the project next year.

Question

French: question

Rule / explanation: Put will before the subject.

Example / answer: Will Parliament be debating the bill tomorrow?

Wh-question

French: question en wh-

Rule / explanation: Use wh-word + will + subject + be + V-ing.

Example / answer: What will voters be discussing after the reform?

C. Future Continuous vs Future Simple

Future Simple

French: futur simple

Rule / explanation: Use will + verb for prediction, decision or future fact.

Example / answer: The reform will affect young voters.

Future Continuous

French: futur continu

Rule / explanation: Use will be + V-ing for an action in progress at a future time.

Example / answer: Next year, young voters will be feeling the effects of the reform.

Prediction vs process

French: prédiction vs processus

Rule / explanation: Future Simple presents a result; Future Continuous presents an ongoing process.

Example / answer: The crisis will worsen. / The crisis will be worsening throughout the winter.

Neutral future plan

French: plan futur neutre

Rule / explanation: Future Continuous can sound neutral, arranged or expected.

Example / answer: The minister will be meeting union leaders tomorrow.

D. Time markers

This time tomorrow / next year

French: à cette heure demain / l’an prochain

Rule / explanation: Useful to locate a future action in progress.

Example / answer: This time next year, the country will be preparing for another election.

At this time in the future

French: à ce moment-là dans le futur

Rule / explanation: Use at + time to define the future moment.

Example / answer: At 9 a.m. tomorrow, MPs will be discussing the budget.

In the coming years

French: dans les années à venir

Rule / explanation: Useful for long-term trends.

Example / answer: In the coming years, governments will be adapting to demographic change.

When + Present Simple

French: quand + présent

Rule / explanation: In future time clauses, use Present Simple after when.

Example / answer: When voters return to the polls, they will be assessing the government’s record.

E. Polite and indirect questions

Will you be...?

French: question polie

Rule / explanation: Future Continuous can make questions sound less direct or intrusive.

Example / answer: Will you be attending the conference?

Arrangements

French: arrangements prévus

Rule / explanation: It asks about expected plans rather than demanding a decision.

Example / answer: Will the minister be answering questions after the speech?

Neutral tone

French: ton neutre

Rule / explanation: Useful in formal interviews and academic exchanges.

Example / answer: Will the committee be publishing the results soon?

CPGE oral use

French: usage oral CPGE

Rule / explanation: It can help students ask polished questions in presentations.

Example / answer: Will we be discussing the article’s historical context?

F. CPGE essay and data patterns

Future social trends

French: tendances sociales futures

Rule / explanation: Use it for trends expected to continue.

Example / answer: Future generations will be paying the price of today’s housing crisis.

Climate pressure

French: pression climatique

Rule / explanation: Useful for long-term ecological analysis.

Example / answer: Governments will be dealing with more frequent climate emergencies.

Political consequences

French: conséquences politiques

Rule / explanation: Use it to discuss future political dynamics.

Example / answer: Parties will be competing for voters who feel economically insecure.

Data commentary

French: commentaire de données

Rule / explanation: Use it for projected trends on graphs or forecasts.

Example / answer: The proportion of elderly voters will be increasing over the next decade.

G. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Forgetting be

French: oubli de be

Rule / explanation: Future Continuous needs will be + V-ing.

Example / answer: Wrong: The government will discussing. Correct: The government will be discussing.

Using infinitive after be

French: infinitif incorrect

Rule / explanation: Use the -ing form after will be.

Example / answer: Wrong: will be discuss. Correct: will be discussing.

Will after when

French: pas de will après when

Rule / explanation: Use Present Simple in future time clauses.

Example / answer: Wrong: When voters will return. Correct: When voters return.

Confusing with Future Perfect

French: futur continu vs futur antérieur

Rule / explanation: Future Continuous = process; Future Perfect = completed before future time.

Example / answer: will be debating = in progress; will have debated = completed.
