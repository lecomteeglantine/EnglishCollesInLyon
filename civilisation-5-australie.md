## 1. ESSENTIAL FACTS
- **Official name:** the Commonwealth of Australia. **Capital:** **Canberra** (a purpose-built city — not Sydney or Melbourne!).
- **Population:** ~27 million. **Largest cities:** Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Adelaide.
- **Currency:** the Australian dollar (AUD).
- **System:** a **federal parliamentary democracy** and a **constitutional monarchy**.
- **Head of state:** **King Charles III**, represented by the **Governor-General** (Sam Mostyn — as of early 2026, to be checked).
- **Head of government:** the **Prime Minister** (**Anthony Albanese**, Labor — as of early 2026, to be checked).
- **Language:** English (de facto); plus many **Indigenous languages**.
- **International standing:** member of the **G20** and the **Commonwealth**; a close **US ally** (ANZUS, and **AUKUS** since 2021); deeply tied to **Asia**, with **China** as its main trading partner.

> ⚠️ **Key idea:** Australia is a **European-settler society in the Asia-Pacific**, home to the **world's oldest living cultures**, and still working out whether it looks to **Britain, the US or Asia**.

---

## 2. GEOGRAPHY AND THE STATES
- The world's **sixth-largest country** — and the only nation that is a **whole continent**. Mostly **arid** (the famous **Outback**); the population clusters on the **east and south-east coasts**.
- **6 states** (New South Wales, Victoria, Queensland, South Australia, Western Australia, Tasmania) + **2 mainland territories** (the **ACT**, around Canberra, and the **Northern Territory**).
- Unique wildlife and ecosystems (marsupials, the **Great Barrier Reef**), but acute exposure to **drought, bushfires and floods**.

> 💡 **Colle angle:** Australia's identity is shaped by a vast, harsh, beautiful land — the **Outback** and the **beach** — and by its growing **vulnerability to climate change**.

---

## 3. THE POLITICAL SYSTEM, THE MONARCHY AND THE CONSTITUTION
- A **federal constitutional monarchy** on the Westminster model: the **King** is head of state, represented by the **Governor-General**; real power lies with the elected government.
- **The Constitution (1901)** created the federation by uniting the British colonies.
- **The 1975 "Dismissal":** in a famous crisis, the Governor-General **dismissed** Prime Minister **Gough Whitlam** — a reminder that the Crown's reserve powers still exist.
- **The republic question:** a **1999 referendum** on replacing the monarchy with a republic was **rejected**; the debate revived after the death of Elizabeth II.

> 💡 **Colle angle:** an independent nation that still has a **British king** as head of state — and that **chose**, by referendum, to keep him (for now).

---

## 4. THE INSTITUTIONS

### 4.1 The Crown
- The **King**, represented by the **Governor-General** (federally) and **State Governors**; the role is ceremonial, but its **reserve powers** proved real in 1975.

### 4.2 Parliament and government
- **Parliament** has two houses: the **House of Representatives** (lower) and a powerful **Senate** (upper), where the states are **equally represented**.
- The **Prime Minister** leads the majority in the House and heads the **Cabinet**.

### 4.3 Justice
- The **High Court of Australia** is the apex court; it interprets the Constitution (e.g. the landmark **Mabo** decision on native title).

---

## 5. THE ELECTORAL SYSTEM AND THE PARTIES

### 5.1 A distinctive voting system
- **Compulsory voting** (a fine for not voting → turnout above 90%) — unusual among democracies.
- **Preferential (ranked) voting** in the House and **proportional representation** in the Senate make the system more representative than simple FPTP.

### 5.2 The parties
- **The Australian Labor Party (the ALP):** centre-left (note the spelling: *Labor*, no "u").
- **The Coalition:** the **Liberal Party** (centre-right) usually allied with the rural **National Party**.
- **The Greens**, and a rising number of **independents** (the "teal" independents).

---

## 6. INDIGENOUS AUSTRALIA AND RECONCILIATION ⭐
- **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples** are the world's **oldest continuous cultures** (~65,000 years), with deep ties to the land (*Country*) and rich traditions (the *Dreaming*, art, languages).
- Colonisation brought **dispossession**, frontier violence and the doctrine of ***terra nullius*** ("nobody's land").
- Key milestones: the **1967 referendum** (counting Indigenous people, federal power); the **Mabo decision (1992)**, which **overturned terra nullius** and recognised **native title**; the national **Apology to the Stolen Generations (2008)**.
- The **2023 "Voice" referendum** — to create an Indigenous advisory body to Parliament — was **rejected**, a setback for reconciliation.
- Indigenous Australians still face major gaps in health, life expectancy and incarceration (the *"Closing the Gap"* agenda).

> 👉 **Colle tip:** this is the single richest Australian theme — memory, justice, identity — and a natural **comparison** with Canada and New Zealand.

## 7. HISTORY, PERIOD BY PERIOD ⭐

### 7.1 Deep time and colonisation
- Indigenous peoples have lived on the continent for **at least 65,000 years**.
- **1770:** James Cook charts the east coast and claims it for Britain.
- **1788 — the First Fleet:** Britain establishes a **penal colony** at Sydney; the **transportation of convicts** begins. Colonisation spreads, dispossessing Indigenous peoples (the *frontier wars*).

### 7.2 Gold, federation and "White Australia"
- The **gold rushes (1850s)** bring a wave of immigration and prosperity.
- **1901 — Federation:** the colonies unite as the **Commonwealth of Australia**. The new nation immediately adopts the **White Australia Policy**, restricting non-European immigration.

### 7.3 War, identity and the US turn
- **1915 — Gallipoli:** the costly ANZAC campaign in WWI becomes a **founding national myth** (commemorated on **ANZAC Day**).
- After WWII, Australia shifts its main alliance from **Britain to the United States** (**ANZUS, 1951**) and launches **mass immigration** to "populate or perish".

### 7.4 Modern Australia
- The **White Australia Policy is dismantled** (by the early 1970s) → a turn to **multiculturalism** and to **Asia**.
- **1967 referendum** (Indigenous rights); the **1975 Dismissal**; the **Mabo decision (1992)**; the **1999 republic referendum (No)**.
- **2008:** Prime Minister Kevin Rudd's **Apology** to the Stolen Generations.
- **2019-2020 — the "Black Summer" bushfires**; **2023 — the Voice referendum (No)**.

> 💡 **Colle angle:** in a century, Australia moved from a "British" outpost with a whites-only policy to a multicultural Asia-Pacific nation — while still facing its **colonial foundations**.

## 8. SOCIETY

### 8.1 From "White Australia" to multiculturalism ⭐
- Australia went from a **whites-only** immigration policy to one of the world's **most multicultural** societies: roughly **30% of people were born overseas**, with large **Asian** communities.
- Multiculturalism is broadly embraced (and showcased by the public broadcaster **SBS**), though debates over integration and identity continue.

### 8.2 Asylum seekers and border policy ⭐
- A highly controversial **hardline border policy**: boats are turned back, and asylum seekers have been sent to **offshore detention** (on **Nauru** and **Manus Island**). Defended as deterrence, condemned by human-rights groups.

### 8.3 National character
- A self-image of **egalitarianism**: the **"fair go"**, **"mateship"**, informality, and the **larrikin** spirit (cheeky irreverence). Sport is close to a national religion.

---

## 9. THE MAJOR CONTEMPORARY DEBATES ⭐
1. **Indigenous reconciliation:** after the 2023 Voice defeat — what next? Treaty, truth-telling, "Closing the Gap".
2. **Becoming a republic:** should Australia finally replace the monarch with an Australian head of state?
3. **Asylum and immigration:** offshore detention vs human rights; immigration levels and housing.
4. **Climate change:** a top **fossil-fuel exporter** facing bushfires, floods and a dying **Great Barrier Reef**.
5. **China vs the US:** economic dependence on China vs the security alliance with the US (**AUKUS**).
6. **Housing affordability** and the cost of living.

---

## 10. THE ECONOMY
- A wealthy, resource-rich economy — long called **"the lucky country"**.
- A **mining giant**: iron ore, coal, natural gas (a leading **fossil-fuel exporter**), plus agriculture.
- **China is the dominant trading partner**, which creates both prosperity and **strategic vulnerability**.
- Key tensions: **resources vs climate**, dependence on China, and a sharp **housing crisis**.

---

## 11. CULTURE AND NATIONAL IDENTITY
- An identity built on **egalitarianism** (the "fair go"), **mateship**, the **beach** and the **bush**, and a love of **sport** (cricket, **Australian rules football / AFL**, rugby league, swimming).
- The **ANZAC legend** as a national myth.
- **Indigenous cultures** as the world's oldest art and storytelling traditions.
- **Cultural figures:** Patrick White (Nobel, literature), films (*Gallipoli*, *Picnic at Hanging Rock*), music (AC/DC, Kylie Minogue), and the global stereotype of *Crocodile Dundee*.

## 🗣️ LANGUAGE INDICATIONS (how to talk about Australia correctly)

The English-usage traps to avoid when discussing Australia:
- Say **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples** / **Indigenous Australians** / **First Nations** — avoid outdated terms.
- **Canberra** is the capital — **not** Sydney.
- The **Prime Minister** is the **federal** head of government; a **Premier** heads a **state**.
- The party is spelled **"Labor"** (no "u"); the **"Coalition"** = the Liberal + National parties (centre-right).
- **"Australian (rules) football" / AFL** is a distinct sport — not soccer or rugby.
- Australia keeps the **King** as head of state, via the **Governor-General** — it is **not** a republic.
- Useful identity words: the **"fair go"**, **"mateship"**, the **"larrikin"**, the **Outback**, the **bush**.

> 👉 Getting these right signals that you genuinely **know the country**.

## 📅 Australia in 10 dates (and why each still matters)
- **1788 — the First Fleet** → the convict origins of white Australia and the start of Indigenous dispossession.
- **1851 — the gold rushes** → immigration, prosperity and a more diverse colony.
- **1901 — Federation + White Australia** → the nation is born — and immediately closes its doors to non-Europeans.
- **1915 — Gallipoli (ANZAC)** → the founding national myth of sacrifice and "mateship".
- **1967 — the referendum** → Indigenous Australians are at last fully counted as citizens.
- **1975 — the Dismissal** → the Crown's reserve powers prove startlingly real.
- **1992 — the Mabo decision** → terra nullius is overturned; native title is recognised.
- **1999 — the republic referendum** → Australians vote (narrowly) to keep the monarchy.
- **2008 — the Apology** → the nation says sorry to the Stolen Generations.
- **2023 — the Voice referendum** → Australia rejects an Indigenous Voice to Parliament.

## ♻️ Australia's great paradoxes (where the best questions are born)
- A **European-settler nation** in the **Asia-Pacific**.
- Home to the world's **oldest living cultures**, yet one of the **youngest nations** — still facing its treatment of them.
- A nation **built on immigration** that ran a **"White Australia"** policy and now detains asylum seekers **offshore**.
- An **independent** country that keeps the **British king** — and voted to do so.
- A **climate-vulnerable continent** that is a top **fossil-fuel exporter**.
- Tied **economically to China**, **strategically to the US**.

## 🗂️ Theme-by-theme colle cards

### 1. Indigenous Australians & reconciliation
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples are the world's oldest living cultures, and reconciliation with them remains Australia's deepest national challenge."* — **Enriched:** *"The 2023 defeat of the Voice showed how unfinished that reconciliation still is."*
- **✅ Must know:** ~65,000 years; terra nullius; Mabo (1992, native title); the Apology (2008); the Voice referendum (2023, No). **❌ Mistakes:** outdated terms; assuming reconciliation is complete.
- **⚠️ Trap:** *Australia has reconciled with its past* → the Voice defeat and persistent gaps show it has **not**.
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on the Voice, the Stolen Generations, native title, "Closing the Gap", Australia Day. The press covers it because of **memory, justice, identity**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** colonisation, memory, justice, identity, reconciliation.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia's first peoples have lived here for over 60,000 years."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia is one of the youngest nations on the oldest inhabited land on earth."*
- **🚪 Opening:** a future treaty · reconciliation in Canada and NZ · the politics of memory.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** Indigenous Australians, terra nullius, native title, the Stolen Generations, the Apology, the Voice, reconciliation, dispossession, sovereignty, Closing the Gap.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** historical justice, national identity, the legitimacy of the settler state.
- **♻️ Paradox:** a young nation on the oldest continuously inhabited land.
- **🌍 Compare with:** Canada, New Zealand (which has a treaty — the Treaty of Waitangi — Australia does not).
- **📖 Cultural references:** *Rabbit-Proof Fence*; *The Secret River* (Kate Grenville); *Dark Emu* (Bruce Pascoe).
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Aboriginal people were treated badly."* → 🟠 *"Mabo recognised native title and the Apology addressed the Stolen Generations."* → ✅ *"From terra nullius to the failed Voice, Australia's reconciliation is a still-open wound: legal and symbolic gains have not closed the gap, nor settled the question of Indigenous sovereignty."*
- **🇫🇷 French-speaker trap:** say **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples / Indigenous Australians** — never outdated terms.
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Can a settler nation truly reconcile with its past? · Why did the Voice fail?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("Reconciliation in Australia"):** I. The oldest cultures and their dispossession — II. Legal and symbolic milestones — III. An unfinished reckoning (the Voice and beyond).

- **⚠️ Trap:** *Australia is just a transplanted Britain* → its **convict past** and **ANZAC myth** forged a distinct identity.
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on national identity, ANZAC Day, the convict legacy, Australia Day debates. The press covers it because of **identity, memory, nationhood**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** national identity, memory, the British legacy, nationhood.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia was founded as a prison."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia turned the shame of its convict origins into a badge of egalitarian pride."*
- **🚪 Opening:** the meaning of Australia Day · national myths elsewhere · the British legacy.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** the First Fleet, a convict, transportation, a penal colony, the frontier, ANZAC, mateship, the larrikin, nationhood, identity.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** national identity, the myths a nation chooses, the British inheritance.
- **♻️ Paradox:** a proud nation that began as a dumping ground for convicts.
- **🌍 Compare with:** the US (another British settler nation with founding myths).
- **📖 Cultural references:** *The Fatal Shore* (Robert Hughes); the film *Gallipoli*.
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australia started as a British colony."* → 🟠 *"Australia began as a penal colony and built the ANZAC legend."* → ✅ *"Australia transformed humiliating origins — a convict colony — into a national mythology of mateship and sacrifice, crystallised at Gallipoli."*

### 3. From White Australia to multiculturalism

### 4. Asylum seekers & border policy
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Australia runs one of the world's harshest border policies, turning back boats and detaining asylum seekers offshore."* — **Enriched:** *"'Stop the boats' is defended as life-saving deterrence and condemned as a human-rights failure."*

- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on small boats, offshore detention, refugees, deterrence, human rights. The press covers it because of **human rights, sovereignty, deterrence**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** immigration, human rights, sovereignty, borders, deterrence.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia sends asylum seekers to islands far from its shores."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia's border policy poses a brutal question: can cruelty be justified if it saves lives at sea?"*
- **🚪 Opening:** border policies elsewhere (the EU, the UK's Rwanda plan) · the ethics of deterrence · refugee rights.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** asylum seekers, refugees, offshore detention, boat turn-backs, deterrence, "stop the boats", human rights, border protection, sovereignty, processing.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** human rights vs sovereignty, the ethics of deterrence.
- **♻️ Paradox:** a welcoming multicultural nation with one of the world's toughest asylum regimes.
- **🌍 Compare with:** the EU, the UK (the Rwanda plan).
- **📖 Cultural references:** the film *Lucky Miles*; reporting on Nauru and Manus.
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australia is tough on immigrants."* → 🟠 *"Australia detains asylum seekers offshore to deter boats."* → ✅ *"Australia's border regime exposes a stark moral trade-off: a deterrent defended for stopping deadly sea crossings, yet condemned for the indefinite offshore detention it entails."*
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Is offshore detention ever justified? · Can a nation be both multicultural and harsh on asylum?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("Australia's border policy"):** I. A hardline deterrent — II. The human and legal costs — III. Sovereignty vs human rights.

### 6. Climate, bushfires & the Great Barrier Reef
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Australia is acutely exposed to climate change — bushfires, floods, a bleaching Reef — yet remains a top fossil-fuel exporter."* — **Enriched:** *"Few countries embody the climate contradiction as sharply as Australia."*
- **✅ Must know:** the "Black Summer" bushfires (2019-20); the Great Barrier Reef's bleaching; a leading coal and gas exporter. **❌ Mistakes:** seeing Australia as only a victim of climate change.
- **⚠️ Trap:** *Australia is a climate victim* → it is also a major **cause** as a fossil-fuel exporter.
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on bushfires, floods, the Reef, coal exports, climate policy. The press covers it because of **climate, the economy, the future**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** climate, the economy, energy, globalisation, the environment.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia is on the front line of climate change."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia is both a victim of climate change and one of its enablers."*
- **🚪 Opening:** the energy transition · the future of the Reef · climate and the resource economy.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** bushfires, drought, the Great Barrier Reef, coral bleaching, fossil fuels, coal exports, emissions, the energy transition, climate policy, sustainability.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** climate vs the economy, intergenerational justice, global responsibility.
- **♻️ Paradox:** a climate-vulnerable continent that profits hugely from exporting coal.
- **🌍 Compare with:** Canada (oil), Norway (oil + climate ambition).
- **📖 Cultural references:** images of the Black Summer fires; the Reef in nature documentaries.
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australia has bushfires."* → 🟠 *"Australia suffers from climate change but exports coal."* → ✅ *"Australia distils the global climate dilemma into one nation: a continent already scorched by climate change yet economically dependent on selling the fossil fuels that drive it."*
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Can a fossil-fuel exporter be a climate leader? · Will the Great Barrier Reef survive?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("Australia and climate"):** I. A continent on the front line — II. A fossil-fuel economy — III. The transition and its tensions.

### 7. The resource economy & China
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Australia is a resource superpower whose prosperity depends heavily on exporting minerals to China."* — **Enriched:** *"The 'lucky country' is rich — but bound to a partner it increasingly distrusts."*
- **✅ Must know:** iron ore, coal, gas; "the lucky country"; China as the dominant trading partner; recent trade tensions. **❌ Mistakes:** ignoring how China-dependent the economy is.
- **⚠️ Trap:** *Australia is simply prosperous* → its wealth is **narrowly based** on resources and on **China**.
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on mining, China trade, economic dependence, the lucky country. The press covers it because of **the economy, geopolitics, vulnerability**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** globalisation, the economy, geopolitics, China, trade.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia got rich digging up minerals and selling them to Asia."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia's prosperity rests on a customer it now treats as a rival."*
- **🚪 Opening:** diversifying away from China · the limits of a resource economy · the lucky country reconsidered.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** the lucky country, mining, iron ore, fossil-fuel exports, the resource boom, trade dependence, diversification, economic security, globalisation, China.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** economic dependence, strategic vulnerability, the durability of resource wealth.
- **♻️ Paradox:** thriving by selling to a country it views as a strategic threat.
- **🌍 Compare with:** Canada (resources + a giant neighbour), Gulf states (resource dependence).
- **📖 Cultural references:** the mining-boom imagery of Western Australia.
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australia trades with China."* → 🟠 *"Australia's resource economy depends on China."* → ✅ *"Australia's wealth illustrates the strategic dilemma of the age: economic dependence on China funds the very prosperity it now seeks to protect from Chinese leverage."*
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Is Australia too dependent on China? · Is 'the lucky country' running out of luck?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("The lucky country and China"):** I. A resource superpower — II. Dependence on China — III. Prosperity vs strategic security.

### 8. Australia, the US and Asia
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Australia is a Western nation in Asia, anchored to the US for security through AUKUS while economically tied to China."* — **Enriched:** *"Geography pulls Australia towards Asia; history and security pull it towards the US."*
- **✅ Must know:** ANZUS (1951); AUKUS (2021, with the US & UK); the Quad; geographic place in Asia. **❌ Mistakes:** treating Australia as simply "Western" with no Asian dimension.
- **⚠️ Trap:** *Australia just follows the US* → it must constantly **balance** the US alliance against its Asian economy.
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on AUKUS, China, the Indo-Pacific, defence, regional security. The press covers it because of **security, geopolitics, identity**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** geopolitics, alliances, identity, globalisation, China.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australia sits in Asia but allies with the West."* / *sophisticated* — *"Australia's foreign policy is a permanent balancing act between its market and its protector."*
- **🚪 Opening:** the Indo-Pacific order · the AUKUS submarines · Australia's place in Asia.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** the US alliance, ANZUS, AUKUS, the Quad, the Indo-Pacific, deterrence, sovereignty, the Asian century, balancing, soft power.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** security vs economy, identity, regional order.
- **♻️ Paradox:** a Western ally whose prosperity and geography are Asian.
- **🌍 Compare with:** other US allies in Asia (Japan, South Korea).
- **📖 Cultural references:** debates over the "Asian century" and Australia's place in it.
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australia is allied with the US."* → 🟠 *"Australia balances the US alliance and Asian trade."* → ✅ *"Australia's strategic identity is a tightrope: AUKUS binds it ever closer to the US even as its prosperity, and its geography, lie squarely in an Asia dominated by China."*
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Is Australia Western or Asian? · Can it rely on both China and the US?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("Australia in the world"):** I. A Western, US-aligned nation — II. An Asian economy and geography — III. Balancing security and prosperity.

### 9. "The lucky country": egalitarianism, mateship & sport
- **⏱️ 30-second sentence:** *"Australia sees itself as an egalitarian land of the 'fair go', mateship and sport — a self-image both cherished and questioned."* — **Enriched:** *"The phrase 'the lucky country' was coined as a criticism, not a compliment."*
- **✅ Must know:** the "fair go"; mateship; the larrikin; sport as near-religion; *The Lucky Country* (Donald Horne, 1964). **❌ Mistakes:** taking "the lucky country" as pure praise.
- **⚠️ Trap:** *"the lucky country" is a boast* → Horne meant it **ironically** (lucky, not clever).
- **📰 How it comes up:** documents on national character, egalitarianism, sport, inequality, identity. The press covers it because of **identity, values, social cohesion**.
- **🔄 Use it to talk about:** national identity, values, inequality, sport, egalitarianism.
- **🎣 Hook:** *simple* — *"Australians pride themselves on giving everyone a 'fair go'."* / *sophisticated* — *"'The lucky country' was a warning, not a compliment — and the irony still bites."*
- **🚪 Opening:** the gap between the egalitarian ideal and reality · sport and identity · housing inequality.
- **🔑 10 words to drop:** the fair go, mateship, egalitarianism, the larrikin, the lucky country, classlessness (myth), inequality, national character, informality, identity.
- **🎭 Hidden stakes:** the gap between egalitarian ideals and real inequality.
- **♻️ Paradox:** an egalitarian self-image alongside real and rising inequality (e.g. housing).
- **🌍 Compare with:** the US (the American Dream and its limits).
- **📖 Cultural references:** *The Lucky Country* (Donald Horne); *Crocodile Dundee* (the larrikin stereotype).
- **✍️ Model exploitation:** ❌ *"Australians value equality."* → 🟠 *"The 'fair go' is central to Australian identity."* → ✅ *"The egalitarian ideal of the 'fair go' is both a genuine value and a comforting myth — one increasingly strained by housing costs and entrenched disadvantage."*
- **❓ Colle questions:** *Is Australia still 'the lucky country'? · Does the 'fair go' match reality?*
- **📝 Ready plan ("The lucky country"):** I. An egalitarian self-image — II. The reality of inequality — III. A myth under pressure.

# 🏆 IF YOU ONLY LEARN 20 THINGS

Short on time? Learn these twenty ideas cold — they are the highest-scoring points on almost any Australian document.
1. The **Commonwealth of Australia**: a federal parliamentary democracy + a constitutional monarchy (King Charles III, via the Governor-General).
2. The capital is **Canberra** — not Sydney or Melbourne.
3. **6 states + 2 territories**; a continent-country, mostly arid (the Outback).
4. **Compulsory voting** + **preferential voting** — distinctive features.
5. **Labor** (centre-left) vs **the Coalition** (Liberal + National); plus the Greens.
6. Indigenous Australians = **Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples** — the world's oldest living cultures (~65,000 years).
7. **1788:** the First Fleet — a penal colony; convict transportation.
8. **"Terra nullius"** justified dispossession; overturned by **Mabo (1992)** — native title.
9. The **Stolen Generations**; the national **Apology (2008)**.
10. The **2023 "Voice" referendum** was rejected.
11. **Federation = 1901**; the **White Australia Policy** began then.
12. White Australia was dismantled by the 1970s → **multiculturalism** (~30% born overseas).
13. **Gallipoli (1915)** and **ANZAC Day** = the founding national myth.
14. The **1975 "Dismissal"** of PM Whitlam by the Governor-General.
15. The **1999 republic referendum** was rejected.
16. A **resource economy** (iron ore, coal, gas) — a top fossil-fuel exporter.
17. **China** is the main trading partner; the **US** is the key ally (ANZUS, **AUKUS 2021**).
18. Climate-vulnerable: the **Black Summer bushfires (2019-20)**, the **Great Barrier Reef**.
19. Controversial **offshore detention** of asylum seekers (Nauru, Manus).
20. **"Labor"** has no "u"; a **"Premier"** heads a state, the **PM** is federal.

> 👉 Learn these twenty cold and you can hold your own on almost any Australian document.
