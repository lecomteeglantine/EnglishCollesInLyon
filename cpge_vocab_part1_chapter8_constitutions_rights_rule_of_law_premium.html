A. Constitutions and constitutional principles

constitution

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution

Definition: A set of fundamental rules that define how a state is organised and how power is limited.

Example: A constitution can protect citizens from abuses of power.

written constitution

IPA: /ˌrɪtən ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: RIT-uhn kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution écrite

Definition: A constitution contained mainly in one formal written document.

Example: The United States has a written constitution.

unwritten constitution

IPA: /ˌʌnˈrɪtən ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: un-RIT-uhn kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution non codifiée

Definition: A constitution based on laws, conventions, precedents and traditions rather than one single codified text.

Example: The UK is often described as having an unwritten constitution.

constitutional framework

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈfreɪmwɜːk/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl FRAYM-wurk
French: cadre constitutionnel

Definition: The overall structure of constitutional rules, institutions and principles in a political system.

Example: The crisis exposed weaknesses in the constitutional framework.

constitutional principle

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈprɪnsəpəl/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl PRIN-suh-puhl
French: principe constitutionnel

Definition: A fundamental idea guiding constitutional interpretation and political practice.

Example: The separation of powers is a key constitutional principle.

constitutional convention

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl kənˈvenʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl kuhn-VEN-shuhn
French: convention constitutionnelle

Definition: An unwritten political rule or practice considered binding by tradition.

Example: By constitutional convention, the British monarch remains politically neutral.

constitutional amendment

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl uh-MEND-muhnt
French: amendement constitutionnel

Definition: A formal change made to a constitution.

Example: The First Amendment protects freedom of speech.

constitutional law

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl law
French: droit constitutionnel

Definition: The area of law dealing with the constitution, institutions, rights and limits on power.

Example: The ruling reshaped constitutional law.

constitutional court

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl court
French: cour constitutionnelle

Definition: A court responsible for deciding whether laws comply with the constitution.

Example: The constitutional court struck down the law.

constitutional crisis

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈkraɪsɪs/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl KRY-sis
French: crise constitutionnelle

Definition: A serious conflict or breakdown involving constitutional rules or institutions.

Example: The refusal to accept the election result triggered a constitutional crisis.

constitutional order

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl OR-duh
French: ordre constitutionnel

Definition: The established constitutional rules and institutions of a state.

Example: The court said the decree threatened the constitutional order.

founding document

IPA: /ˈfaʊndɪŋ ˈdɒkjəmənt/
Pronunciation: FOWN-ding DOK-yuh-muhnt
French: texte fondateur

Definition: A document that establishes the basic principles of a political system.

Example: The Constitution is one of America’s founding documents.

founding fathers

IPA: /ˌfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑːðəz/
Pronunciation: FOWN-ding FAR-thuhz
French: Pères fondateurs

Definition: The political leaders who helped create the United States and its constitutional system.

Example: The Founding Fathers designed a system of checks and balances.

Bill of Rights

IPA: /ˌbɪl əv ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: bil ov rites
French: déclaration des droits ; Bill of Rights

Definition: A formal list of fundamental rights protected by law or by a constitution.

Example: The American Bill of Rights protects individual freedoms.

preamble

IPA: /ˈpriːæmbəl/
Pronunciation: PREE-am-buhl
French: préambule

Definition: The introductory statement of a constitution or legal text, often explaining its aims.

Example: The preamble sets out the values behind the constitution.

article

IPA: /ˈɑːtɪkəl/
Pronunciation: AR-ti-kuhl
French: article

Definition: A numbered section of a constitution, treaty or legal text.

Example: Article I of the US Constitution deals with Congress.

clause

IPA: /klɔːz/
Pronunciation: klawz
French: clause ; disposition

Definition: A specific provision or section in a legal or constitutional text.

Example: The clause has been interpreted differently by judges.

provision

IPA: /prəˈvɪʒən/
Pronunciation: pruh-VIZH-uhn
French: disposition

Definition: A rule, condition or requirement included in a legal document.

Example: The provision protects citizens from arbitrary detention.

to enshrine

IPA: /ɪnˈʃraɪn/
Pronunciation: in-SHRYNE
French: inscrire ; consacrer

Definition: To formally include a right or principle in a law or constitution.

Example: Freedom of religion is enshrined in the constitution.

to amend the constitution

IPA: /əˈmend ðə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND thuh kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: modifier la constitution

Definition: To make a formal change to a constitution.

Example: It is difficult to amend the US Constitution.

to interpret the constitution

IPA: /ɪnˈtɜːprɪt ðə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: in-TUR-prit thuh kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: interpréter la constitution

Definition: To decide what constitutional rules mean and how they apply.

Example: The Supreme Court interprets the Constitution.

B. Rule of law and limits on power

rule of law

IPA: /ˌruːl əv ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: rool ov law
French: État de droit ; primauté du droit

Definition: The principle that everyone, including leaders and governments, must obey the law.

Example: The rule of law prevents leaders from acting above the law.

constitutionalism

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl-iz-uhm
French: constitutionnalisme

Definition: The belief that government power must be limited by constitutional rules.

Example: Constitutionalism is designed to prevent arbitrary rule.

limited government

IPA: /ˌlɪmɪtɪd ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: LIM-it-id GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement limité

Definition: A system in which government power is restricted by law, rights and institutions.

Example: Limited government is central to liberal democracy.

arbitrary power

IPA: /ˈɑːbɪtrəri ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: AR-bi-truh-ree POW-uh
French: pouvoir arbitraire

Definition: Power exercised without clear legal limits, fairness or accountability.

Example: The rule of law protects citizens from arbitrary power.

abuse of power

IPA: /əˌbjuːs əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: uh-BYOOSS ov POW-uh
French: abus de pouvoir

Definition: The improper or illegal use of authority.

Example: Independent courts can challenge abuses of power.

checks and balances

IPA: /ˌtʃeks ən ˈbælənsɪz/
Pronunciation: cheks uhn BAL-uhn-siz
French: freins et contrepoids ; contre-pouvoirs

Definition: A system in which branches of government limit and supervise one another.

Example: Checks and balances prevent the concentration of power.

separation of powers

IPA: /ˌsepəˈreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: sep-uh-RAY-shuhn ov POW-uhz
French: séparation des pouvoirs

Definition: The division of state power among the executive, legislature and judiciary.

Example: The separation of powers protects citizens from tyranny.

executive power

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv POW-uh
French: pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The authority to implement laws and run government.

Example: Executive power often expands during crises.

legislative power

IPA: /ˈledʒɪslətɪv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: LEDJ-is-luh-tiv POW-uh
French: pouvoir législatif

Definition: The authority to make laws.

Example: Legislative power belongs to parliament or congress.

judicial power

IPA: /dʒuːˈdɪʃəl ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl POW-uh
French: pouvoir judiciaire

Definition: The authority of courts to interpret and apply the law.

Example: Judicial power can limit executive overreach.

executive overreach

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv ˌəʊvəˈriːtʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv OH-vuh-reech
French: abus de pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The use of executive authority beyond proper legal or constitutional limits.

Example: Critics accused the president of executive overreach.

concentration of power

IPA: /ˌkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: kon-suhn-TRAY-shuhn ov POW-uh
French: concentration du pouvoir

Definition: The accumulation of political authority in one institution or leader.

Example: The concentration of power in the executive alarmed observers.

constitutional safeguard

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈseɪfɡɑːd/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl SAYF-gard
French: garantie constitutionnelle

Definition: A constitutional rule or mechanism designed to prevent abuses of power.

Example: Constitutional safeguards can prevent democratic erosion.

institutional safeguard

IPA: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈseɪfɡɑːd/
Pronunciation: in-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl SAYF-gard
French: garantie institutionnelle

Definition: An institutional mechanism that protects democracy, rights or accountability.

Example: Independent regulators can act as institutional safeguards.

oversight

IPA: /ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle ; surveillance institutionnelle

Definition: The monitoring of government actions by another body or institution.

Example: Congressional oversight can limit presidential power.

accountability

IPA: /əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: uh-kown-tuh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité ; obligation de rendre des comptes

Definition: The obligation of leaders and institutions to explain and take responsibility for their actions.

Example: Accountability is essential in a democracy.

transparency

IPA: /trænsˈpærənsi/
Pronunciation: trans-PAIR-uhn-see
French: transparence

Definition: Openness that allows citizens to understand how decisions are made.

Example: Transparency helps rebuild trust in institutions.

civil liberties

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈlɪbətiz/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl LIB-uh-teez
French: libertés civiles

Definition: Basic freedoms protected by law, such as freedom of speech or assembly.

Example: Civil liberties can be threatened during a state of emergency.

C. Rights and freedoms

civil rights

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl rites
French: droits civiques

Definition: Rights protecting people from discrimination and guaranteeing equal treatment under the law.

Example: The civil rights movement fought segregation.

human rights

IPA: /ˌhjuːmən ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: HYOO-muhn rites
French: droits humains ; droits de l’homme

Definition: Fundamental rights believed to belong to every human being.

Example: Human rights organisations criticised the crackdown.

political rights

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl rites
French: droits politiques

Definition: Rights allowing people to participate in political life, such as voting or standing for office.

Example: Political rights mean little without free elections.

constitutional rights

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl rites
French: droits constitutionnels

Definition: Rights protected by a constitution.

Example: The ruling expanded constitutional rights.

individual rights

IPA: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: in-di-VIJ-oo-uhl rites
French: droits individuels

Definition: Rights belonging to each person as an individual.

Example: Individual rights may conflict with collective security.

minority rights

IPA: /maɪˈnɒrəti ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: my-NOR-uh-tee rites
French: droits des minorités

Definition: Rights protecting groups that are smaller in number or less powerful.

Example: Liberal democracy must protect minority rights.

voting rights

IPA: /ˈvəʊtɪŋ raɪts/
Pronunciation: VOH-ting rites
French: droit de vote ; droits électoraux

Definition: The legal rights that allow citizens to vote.

Example: Voting rights remain a central issue in American politics.

freedom of speech

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov speech
French: liberté d’expression

Definition: The right to express opinions and ideas without unjustified government restriction.

Example: Freedom of speech protects unpopular opinions too.

freedom of the press

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ðə ˈpres/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov thuh press
French: liberté de la presse

Definition: The right of journalists to report news and criticise power without censorship.

Example: Freedom of the press is vital for accountability.

freedom of religion

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv rɪˈlɪdʒən/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov ri-LIJ-uhn
French: liberté religieuse

Definition: The right to practise, change or reject a religion.

Example: Freedom of religion is protected by the First Amendment.

freedom of assembly

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv əˈsembli/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov uh-SEM-blee
French: liberté de réunion ; droit de rassemblement

Definition: The right to gather peacefully in public.

Example: Freedom of assembly protects peaceful protest.

freedom of association

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov uh-soh-see-AY-shuhn
French: liberté d’association

Definition: The right to join groups, unions, parties or organisations.

Example: Freedom of association protects trade unions.

right to privacy

IPA: /ˌraɪt tə ˈprɪvəsi/
Pronunciation: rite tuh PRIV-uh-see
French: droit à la vie privée

Definition: The right to keep personal life and information protected from unjustified interference.

Example: Digital surveillance raises questions about the right to privacy.

right to protest

IPA: /ˌraɪt tə prəˈtest/
Pronunciation: rite tuh pruh-TEST
French: droit de manifester

Definition: The right to demonstrate publicly and peacefully.

Example: The right to protest is a basic democratic freedom.

due process

IPA: /ˌdjuː ˈprəʊses/
Pronunciation: dyoo PROH-sess
French: procédure régulière ; garanties procédurales

Definition: Fair legal procedures that must be followed before someone is punished or deprived of rights.

Example: Due process protects citizens from arbitrary punishment.

equal protection

IPA: /ˌiːkwəl prəˈtekʃən/
Pronunciation: EE-kwuhl pruh-TEK-shuhn
French: égale protection de la loi

Definition: The principle that the law must protect people equally.

Example: Equal protection is central to civil rights cases.

equality before the law

IPA: /iˈkwɒləti bɪˌfɔː ðə ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: i-KWOL-uh-tee bi-FOR thuh law
French: égalité devant la loi

Definition: The principle that all people are subject to the same laws and legal protections.

Example: Equality before the law is a pillar of the rule of law.

judiciary

IPA: /dʒuːˈdɪʃəri/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uh-ree
French: pouvoir judiciaire ; système judiciaire

Definition: The system of courts and judges that interprets and applies the law.

Example: An independent judiciary is a safeguard against authoritarianism.

judge

IPA: /dʒʌdʒ/
Pronunciation: juj
French: juge

Definition: A public official who decides cases in a court.

Example: The judge ruled that the measure was unconstitutional.

justice

IPA: /ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: JUS-tis
French: juge ; magistrat d’une haute cour

Definition: A judge of a high court, especially a supreme court.

Example: The justice wrote a powerful dissenting opinion.

D. Courts and judicial review

chief justice

IPA: /ˌtʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: cheef JUS-tis
French: président de la Cour suprême

Definition: The leading judge of a supreme court.

Example: The Chief Justice presided over the case.

supreme court

IPA: /suːˌpriːm ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: soo-PREEM court
French: cour suprême

Definition: The highest court in a legal system.

Example: The Supreme Court overturned the previous ruling.

federal court

IPA: /ˈfedərəl kɔːt/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl court
French: tribunal fédéral

Definition: A court dealing with federal law, especially in the United States.

Example: The case was heard in a federal court.

appellate court

IPA: /əˈpelət kɔːt/
Pronunciation: uh-PEL-uht court
French: cour d’appel

Definition: A court that reviews decisions made by lower courts.

Example: The appellate court upheld the ruling.

lower court

IPA: /ˌləʊə ˈkɔːt/
Pronunciation: LOH-uh court
French: juridiction inférieure

Definition: A court whose decisions may be reviewed by a higher court.

Example: The lower court had ruled against the government.

court ruling

IPA: /ˈkɔːt ˌruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: court ROO-ling
French: décision de justice

Definition: An official decision made by a court.

Example: The court ruling sparked nationwide protests.

landmark ruling

IPA: /ˈlændmɑːk ˌruːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: LAND-mark ROO-ling
French: arrêt historique ; décision majeure

Definition: A court decision that is highly important and changes law or society.

Example: The landmark ruling transformed abortion rights.

judicial review

IPA: /dʒuːˌdɪʃəl rɪˈvjuː/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uhl ri-VYOO
French: contrôle juridictionnel ; contrôle de constitutionnalité

Definition: The power of courts to examine whether laws or actions are constitutional or lawful.

Example: Judicial review allows courts to limit government power.

precedent

IPA: /ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: PRES-i-duhnt
French: précédent juridique

Definition: A previous legal decision that guides future cases.

Example: The ruling set an important precedent.

legal challenge

IPA: /ˈliːɡəl ˈtʃælɪndʒ/
Pronunciation: LEE-guhl CHAL-inj
French: recours juridique ; contestation en justice

Definition: A formal attempt to oppose a law or decision in court.

Example: Civil rights groups launched a legal challenge.

lawsuit

IPA: /ˈlɔːsuːt/
Pronunciation: LAW-soot
French: procès ; action en justice

Definition: A legal case brought before a court.

Example: The company faced a lawsuit over privacy violations.

litigation

IPA: /ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: lit-i-GAY-shuhn
French: contentieux ; procédure judiciaire

Definition: The process of taking legal action in court.

Example: The policy led to years of litigation.

plaintiff

IPA: /ˈpleɪntɪf/
Pronunciation: PLAYN-tif
French: plaignant ; demandeur

Definition: The person or group that brings a case against another party in court.

Example: The plaintiff argued that the law violated free speech.

defendant

IPA: /dɪˈfendənt/
Pronunciation: di-FEN-duhnt
French: défendeur ; accusé

Definition: The person or organisation accused or sued in a legal case.

Example: The defendant denied any wrongdoing.

legal interpretation

IPA: /ˈliːɡəl ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: LEE-guhl in-tur-pri-TAY-shuhn
French: interprétation juridique

Definition: The process of deciding what a law means.

Example: The case depends on legal interpretation.

constitutional interpretation

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl in-tur-pri-TAY-shuhn
French: interprétation constitutionnelle

Definition: The process of deciding what constitutional provisions mean.

Example: Constitutional interpretation often divides judges.

originalism

IPA: /əˈrɪdʒənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: uh-RIJ-uh-nuhl-iz-uhm
French: originalisme

Definition: A method of constitutional interpretation based on the original meaning of the text.

Example: Originalism is influential among conservative judges.

living constitution

IPA: /ˌlɪvɪŋ ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: LIV-ing kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution vivante

Definition: The view that a constitution should be interpreted in light of changing society.

Example: Supporters of a living constitution argue that rights evolve over time.

dissenting opinion

IPA: /dɪˈsentɪŋ əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: di-SEN-ting uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion dissidente

Definition: An opinion written by a judge who disagrees with the majority decision.

Example: The dissenting opinion warned of serious consequences.

majority opinion

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti əˈpɪnjən/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee uh-PIN-yuhn
French: opinion majoritaire

Definition: The official opinion of the majority of judges in a case.

Example: The majority opinion upheld the law.

to uphold the constitution

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld ðə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD thuh kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: défendre la constitution

Definition: To defend or maintain constitutional rules and principles.

Example: Courts are expected to uphold the constitution.

IPA: /rɪˈstrɪkt ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: ri-STRIKT rites
French: restreindre les droits

Definition: To limit legal protections or freedoms.

Example: The new law restricts voting rights.

to weaken institutions

IPA: /ˈwiːkən ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənz/
Pronunciation: WEE-kuhn in-sti-TOO-shuhnz
French: affaiblir les institutions

Definition: To reduce the independence or effectiveness of institutions.

Example: Authoritarian leaders often weaken institutions.

to undermine the rule of law

IPA: /ˌʌndəˈmaɪn ðə ˌruːl əv ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: un-duh-MYNE thuh rool ov law
French: saper l’État de droit

Definition: To damage respect for legal limits and equal application of the law.

Example: Political interference in courts can undermine the rule of law.

to strengthen safeguards

IPA: /ˈstreŋθən ˈseɪfɡɑːdz/
Pronunciation: STRENG-thuhn SAYF-gardz
French: renforcer les garanties

Definition: To make protective rules or institutions stronger.

Example: The reform aimed to strengthen democratic safeguards.

to set a precedent

IPA: /set ə ˈpresɪdənt/
Pronunciation: set uh PRES-i-duhnt
French: créer un précédent

Definition: To establish a legal example that may guide future decisions.

Example: The case set a precedent for future privacy disputes.

to trigger a constitutional crisis

IPA: /ˈtrɪɡə ə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈkraɪsɪs/
Pronunciation: TRIG-uh uh kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl KRY-sis
French: déclencher une crise constitutionnelle

Definition: To cause a serious conflict involving constitutional rules or institutions.

Example: The refusal to leave office could trigger a constitutional crisis.
