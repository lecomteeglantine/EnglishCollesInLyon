Essential vocabulary for khôlles, essays, press reviews and competitive exams.

Chapter objective

This chapter gives CPGE students the core vocabulary needed to discuss political systems clearly and accurately: regimes, institutions, power, legitimacy, representation and democratic standards. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a simple pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For comprehension Learn the terms first in English and French, then identify them in newspaper articles.
For oral exams Reuse the example sentences and adapt them to current affairs.
For essays Pay attention to collocations such as to hold power, to uphold the rule of law, or to undermine democracy.

A. Core political systems

democracy

IPA: /dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie

Definition: A political system in which citizens choose their leaders through elections and have political rights and freedoms.

Example: A healthy democracy requires free elections, independent courts and a free press.

representative democracy

IPA: /ˌreprɪˈzentətɪv dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tiv di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie représentative

Definition: A form of democracy in which citizens elect representatives to make political decisions on their behalf.

Example: In a representative democracy, voters do not vote on every law directly.

direct democracy

IPA: /dəˌrekt dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: duh-REKT di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie directe

Definition: A system in which citizens vote directly on laws or major decisions, often through referendums.

Example: Referendums are often presented as a tool of direct democracy.

republic

IPA: /rɪˈpʌblɪk/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-lik
French: république

Definition: A state in which power is held by elected representatives and not by a hereditary monarch.

Example: The United States is a federal republic.

monarchy

IPA: /ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie

Definition: A political system in which a king or queen is the head of state, usually by inheritance.

Example: The United Kingdom is a constitutional monarchy.

constitutional monarchy

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie constitutionnelle

Definition: A monarchy in which the monarch’s powers are limited by a constitution and elected institutions.

Example: In a constitutional monarchy, the monarch usually has a symbolic role.

parliamentary system

IPA: /ˌpɑːləˈmentəri ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: par-luh-MEN-tuh-ree SIS-tuhm
French: régime parlementaire

Definition: A system in which the government depends on the support of parliament to remain in power.

Example: In a parliamentary system, the prime minister usually leads the majority in parliament.

presidential system

IPA: /ˌprezɪˈdenʃəl ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: prez-i-DEN-shuhl SIS-tuhm
French: régime présidentiel

Definition: A system in which the president is both head of state and head of government, with powers separate from the legislature.

Example: The American political system is often described as a presidential system.

federal system

IPA: /ˈfedərəl ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl SIS-tuhm
French: système fédéral

Definition: A system in which power is divided between a central government and regional governments.

Example: Federal systems can give states or regions significant autonomy.

unitary state

IPA: /ˈjuːnɪtəri steɪt/
Pronunciation: YOO-ni-tuh-ree stayt
French: État unitaire

Definition: A state in which political authority is mainly held by the central government.

Example: France is often described as a unitary state, despite decentralisation.

regime

IPA: /reɪˈʒiːm/
Pronunciation: ray-ZHEEM
French: régime

Definition: A form of government, especially one considered in terms of how power is organised or exercised.

Example: The article describes how the regime tightened its control over the media.

authoritarianism

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: autoritarisme

Definition: A political system in which power is concentrated in the hands of a leader or ruling group and political freedoms are limited.

Example: Authoritarianism often weakens independent institutions and civil liberties.

dictatorship

IPA: /dɪkˈteɪtəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: dik-TAY-tuh-ship
French: dictature

Definition: A system in which one person or a small group holds absolute political power, usually without free elections.

Example: The dictatorship banned opposition parties and censored the press.

autocracy

IPA: /ɔːˈtɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: aw-TOK-ruh-see
French: autocratie

Definition: A system in which one ruler has unlimited power and is not effectively controlled by laws or institutions.

Example: Critics argue that the country has gradually moved towards autocracy.

theocracy

IPA: /θiˈɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: thee-OK-ruh-see
French: théocratie

Definition: A system in which religious authorities or religious law play the central role in government.

Example: In a theocracy, political legitimacy is often based on religious authority.

oligarchy

IPA: /ˈɒlɪɡɑːki/
Pronunciation: OL-i-gar-kee
French: oligarchie

Definition: A system in which a small wealthy or powerful group controls political power.

Example: Some observers fear that economic inequality can turn a democracy into an oligarchy.

B. Power, legitimacy and institutions

sovereignty

IPA: /ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté

Definition: The supreme authority of a state to govern itself and make its own decisions.

Example: Brexit supporters often argued that leaving the EU would restore national sovereignty.

legitimacy

IPA: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
Pronunciation: li-JIT-i-muh-see
French: légitimité

Definition: The belief that a government, institution or leader has the right to rule or make decisions.

Example: A government may lose legitimacy if it ignores the rule of law.

authority

IPA: /ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: autorité

Definition: The official power to give orders, make decisions or enforce rules.

Example: The crisis challenged the authority of the central government.

governance

IPA: /ˈɡʌvənəns/
Pronunciation: GUV-uh-nuhns
French: gouvernance

Definition: The way a country, institution or organisation is managed and controlled.

Example: Good governance requires transparency, accountability and competence.

separation of powers

IPA: /ˌsepəˈreɪʃən əv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: sep-uh-RAY-shuhn ov POW-uhz
French: séparation des pouvoirs

Definition: The division of political power among different branches of government, usually the executive, legislature and judiciary.

Example: The separation of powers is meant to prevent one branch from becoming too powerful.

checks and balances

IPA: /ˌtʃeks ən ˈbælənsɪz/
Pronunciation: cheks uhn BAL-uhn-siz
French: contre-pouvoirs ; système de freins et contrepoids

Definition: A system in which each branch of government can limit the power of the others.

Example: Checks and balances are central to the American Constitution.

rule of law

IPA: /ˌruːl əv ˈlɔː/
Pronunciation: rool ov law
French: État de droit ; primauté du droit

Definition: The principle that everyone, including leaders and governments, must obey the law.

Example: Independent courts are essential to uphold the rule of law.

constitution

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: constitution

Definition: A set of fundamental rules that define how a state is organised and how power is limited.

Example: A constitution can protect citizens from abuses of power.

executive branch

IPA: /ɪɡˈzekjətɪv brɑːntʃ/
Pronunciation: ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv branch
French: pouvoir exécutif

Definition: The part of government responsible for implementing laws and running the state.

Example: In the United States, the president leads the executive branch.

legislature

IPA: /ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: pouvoir législatif ; assemblée législative

Definition: The institution or body that makes laws, such as a parliament or congress.

Example: The legislature debated the new immigration bill for several weeks.

judiciary

IPA: /dʒuːˈdɪʃəri/
Pronunciation: joo-DISH-uh-ree
French: pouvoir judiciaire ; système judiciaire

Definition: The system of courts and judges responsible for interpreting and applying the law.

Example: An independent judiciary is a key safeguard against authoritarianism.

civil service

IPA: /ˈsɪvəl ˈsɜːvɪs/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl SUR-vis
French: fonction publique ; administration

Definition: The permanent professional administration that helps implement government policy.

Example: The civil service is expected to remain politically neutral.

C. Elections, representation and participation

electorate

IPA: /ɪˈlektərət/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tuh-ruht
French: électorat

Definition: All the people who are allowed to vote in an election.

Example: The electorate was deeply divided over the issue of immigration.

suffrage

IPA: /ˈsʌfrɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: SUF-rij
French: droit de vote ; suffrage

Definition: The right to vote in political elections.

Example: Universal suffrage was achieved gradually in many democracies.

franchise

IPA: /ˈfræntʃaɪz/
Pronunciation: FRAN-chize
French: droit de vote ; franchise électorale

Definition: The legal right to vote; in politics, it is often used as a synonym for suffrage.

Example: Expanding the franchise changed the balance of political power.

constituency

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənsi/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhn-see
French: circonscription électorale

Definition: A geographical area whose voters elect a representative.

Example: The MP returned to her constituency after the parliamentary debate.

turnout

IPA: /ˈtɜːnaʊt/
Pronunciation: TURN-out
French: taux de participation

Definition: The percentage or number of voters who actually vote in an election.

Example: Low turnout can raise questions about democratic engagement.

representation

IPA: /ˌreprɪzenˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rep-ri-zen-TAY-shuhn
French: représentation

Definition: The process or principle by which elected officials speak and act for citizens.

Example: Many critics argue that young people lack political representation.

mandate

IPA: /ˈmændeɪt/
Pronunciation: MAN-dayt
French: mandat

Definition: The authority given to a politician or government by voters to carry out a programme.

Example: The prime minister claimed she had a clear mandate for reform.

referendum

IPA: /ˌrefəˈrendəm/
Pronunciation: ref-uh-REN-duhm
French: référendum

Definition: A vote in which citizens are asked to accept or reject a specific proposal.

Example: The Brexit referendum reshaped British politics.

D. Democratic quality and political decline

civil liberties

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈlɪbətiz/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl LIB-uh-teez
French: libertés civiles

Definition: Basic freedoms protected by law, such as freedom of speech, religion and assembly.

Example: Emergency laws can sometimes threaten civil liberties.

human rights

IPA: /ˌhjuːmən ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: HYOO-muhn rites
French: droits humains ; droits de l'homme

Definition: Fundamental rights believed to belong to every person, regardless of nationality, status or identity.

Example: Human rights organisations condemned the arrests of opposition leaders.

accountability

IPA: /əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: uh-kown-tuh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité ; obligation de rendre des comptes

Definition: The obligation of leaders and institutions to explain their decisions and accept responsibility for them.

Example: Investigative journalism can increase political accountability.

transparency

IPA: /trænsˈpærənsi/
Pronunciation: trans-PAIR-uhn-see
French: transparence

Definition: Openness in decision-making, allowing citizens to see how and why decisions are made.

Example: Transparency is crucial to rebuild trust in public institutions.

corruption

IPA: /kəˈrʌpʃən/
Pronunciation: kuh-RUP-shuhn
French: corruption

Definition: Dishonest or illegal behaviour by people in power, often involving bribery or personal gain.

Example: Corruption scandals can destroy public trust in government.

censorship

IPA: /ˈsensəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: SEN-suh-ship
French: censure

Definition: The control or suppression of information, speech, books or media by authorities.

Example: Censorship often increases when governments fear dissent.

dissent

IPA: /dɪˈsent/
Pronunciation: di-SENT
French: dissidence ; désaccord politique

Definition: Public disagreement with official policies, dominant opinions or political authorities.

Example: Authoritarian regimes often try to silence dissent.

crackdown

IPA: /ˈkrækdaʊn/
Pronunciation: KRAK-down
French: répression ; durcissement

Definition: Strong action taken by authorities to stop or punish behaviour they oppose.

Example: The crackdown on protesters was widely criticised abroad.

democratic backsliding

IPA: /ˌdeməˈkrætɪk ˈbækslaɪdɪŋ/
Pronunciation: dem-uh-KRAT-ik BAK-sly-ding
French: recul démocratique ; régression démocratique

Definition: The gradual weakening of democratic institutions, rights and norms.

Example: Attacks on courts and journalists are often seen as signs of democratic backsliding.

illiberal democracy

IPA: /ɪˈlɪbərəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: i-LIB-uh-ruhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie illibérale

Definition: A political system with elections but weak civil liberties, limited checks and balances, and pressure on independent institutions.

Example: An illiberal democracy may hold elections while restricting media freedom.

freedom of speech

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov speech
French: liberté d'expression

Definition: The right to express opinions and ideas without unjustified government restriction.

Example: Freedom of speech is often at the centre of political debates in liberal democracies.

freedom of the press

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ðə ˈpres/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov thuh press
French: liberté de la presse

Definition: The right of journalists and media organisations to report news and criticise power without censorship.

Example: Freedom of the press is essential for exposing corruption.

E. High-value verbs and collocations

to govern

IPA: /ˈɡʌvən/
Pronunciation: GUV-uhn
French: gouverner

Definition: To officially control and manage a country, region or institution.

Example: It is easier to win an election than to govern effectively.

to rule

IPA: /ruːl/
Pronunciation: rool
French: gouverner ; régner

Definition: To control a country or people, especially with official or political authority.

Example: The king ruled for more than thirty years.

to hold power

IPA: /həʊld ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: hohld POW-uh
French: détenir le pouvoir

Definition: To possess political authority or control over institutions.

Example: The party has held power for more than a decade.

to come to power

IPA: /kʌm tə ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: kum tuh POW-uh
French: arriver au pouvoir

Definition: To begin governing after an election, revolution or political change.

Example: The new coalition came to power after months of political deadlock.

to step down

IPA: /step ˈdaʊn/
Pronunciation: step down
French: démissionner ; quitter ses fonctions

Definition: To leave an important position voluntarily, especially in politics.

Example: The minister was forced to step down after the scandal.

to undermine

IPA: /ˌʌndəˈmaɪn/
Pronunciation: un-duh-MYNE
French: saper ; affaiblir

Definition: To weaken something gradually, especially trust, authority, democracy or institutions.

Example: Disinformation can undermine trust in democratic institutions.

to uphold

IPA: /ʌpˈhəʊld/
Pronunciation: up-HOHLD
French: défendre ; faire respecter

Definition: To defend or maintain a law, principle or right.

Example: Courts are expected to uphold constitutional rights.

to enforce

IPA: /ɪnˈfɔːs/
Pronunciation: in-FORSS
French: faire appliquer

Definition: To make sure that laws, rules or decisions are obeyed.

Example: A law is meaningless if the state cannot enforce it.

to curb

IPA: /kɜːb/
Pronunciation: curb
French: limiter ; freiner ; endiguer

Definition: To limit or control something that is considered harmful or excessive.

Example: New rules were introduced to curb corruption.

to grant rights

IPA: /ɡrɑːnt ˈraɪts/
Pronunciation: grahnt rites
French: accorder des droits

Definition: To officially give people legal or political rights.

Example: The reform granted voting rights to millions of citizens.

Exam-ready phrases

Phrase Use
It raises questions about democratic legitimacy. Use when discussing elections, leaders or institutions.
The reform may strengthen checks and balances. Use when discussing institutional reforms.
The crisis exposed the weaknesses of the political system. Use when analysing instability or scandal.
This trend points to democratic backsliding. Use when discussing threats to democracy.
The government claimed it had a clear mandate. Use when discussing elections and public support.
