Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse religion in contemporary societies: belief, secularisation, religious diversity, freedom of religion, secularism, religious symbols, faith schools, culture wars, extremism, discrimination, social justice, religious nationalism and the tension between pluralism and public order.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as secularism, religious freedom, faith school, headscarf, religious right, Islamophobia, antisemitism, religious nationalism and culture war.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain how religion can create community and solidarity, but also raise tensions around identity, equality, secular law, free speech and political power.
For essaysMemorise the collocations: to protect religious freedom, to separate church and state, to promote tolerance, to defend pluralism, to balance rights.

A. Core religion, belief and secularisation vocabulary

religion

IPA: /rɪˈlɪdʒən/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhn
French: religion

Definition: A system of beliefs, practices and values concerning the sacred, God or gods.

Example: Religion continues to shape politics, identity and public debate.

faith

IPA: /feɪθ/
Pronunciation: faith
French: foi

Definition: Strong belief in a religion, God or spiritual principles.

Example: Faith can provide meaning, community and moral guidance.

belief

IPA: /bɪˈliːf/
Pronunciation: bi-LEEF
French: croyance

Definition: Something accepted as true, especially in religious or moral matters.

Example: Religious beliefs can influence political choices.

religious belief

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs bɪˈliːf/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs bi-LEEF
French: croyance religieuse

Definition: A belief connected to a religion or spiritual tradition.

Example: Democracies protect freedom of religious belief.

spirituality

IPA: /ˌspɪrɪtʃuˈæləti/
Pronunciation: spir-i-choo-AL-uh-tee
French: spiritualité

Definition: Personal search for meaning, connection or transcendence, not always linked to organised religion.

Example: Many people describe themselves as spiritual but not religious.

organised religion

IPA: /ˌɔːɡənaɪzd rɪˈlɪdʒən/
Pronunciation: OR-guh-nyzd ri-LIJ-uhn
French: religion organisée

Definition: Religion practised through institutions, clergy and formal rules.

Example: Organised religion has lost influence in many Western societies.

institutional religion

IPA: /ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl rɪˈlɪdʒən/
Pronunciation: in-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl ri-LIJ-uhn
French: religion institutionnelle

Definition: Religion expressed through established institutions and hierarchies.

Example: Institutional religion is often criticised for conservatism or abuse of power.

religious practice

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈpræktɪs/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs PRAK-tis
French: pratique religieuse

Definition: Actions connected to religion, such as prayer, worship or rituals.

Example: Religious practice has declined faster than religious identity in some countries.

worship

IPA: /ˈwɜːʃɪp/
Pronunciation: WUR-ship
French: culte ; adoration

Definition: Acts of devotion, prayer or praise directed towards God or a deity.

Example: Places of worship can also serve as community centres.

prayer

IPA: /preə/
Pronunciation: prayer
French: prière

Definition: Words or thoughts addressed to God or a spiritual power.

Example: Prayer can be private or collective.

ritual

IPA: /ˈrɪtʃuəl/
Pronunciation: RICH-oo-uhl
French: rituel

Definition: A formal repeated religious or symbolic action.

Example: Rituals help create a sense of belonging.

ceremony

IPA: /ˈserəməni/
Pronunciation: SERR-uh-muh-nee
French: cérémonie

Definition: A formal event, often religious or symbolic.

Example: Religious ceremonies mark birth, marriage and death.

sacred

IPA: /ˈseɪkrɪd/
Pronunciation: SAY-krid
French: sacré

Definition: Connected with God, religion or spiritual importance.

Example: Sacred places often become politically sensitive.

secular

IPA: /ˈsekjələ/
Pronunciation: SEK-yuh-luh
French: laïque ; séculier

Definition: Not religious or not controlled by religion.

Example: A secular state does not impose one religion.

secularism

IPA: /ˈsekjələrɪzəm/
Pronunciation: SEK-yuh-luh-riz-uhm
French: laïcité ; sécularisme

Definition: The principle that religion and state should be separate.

Example: Secularism can protect both believers and non-believers.

secularisation

IPA: /ˌsekjələraɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: sek-yuh-luh-ry-ZAY-shuhn
French: sécularisation

Definition: The decline of religious influence in society and institutions.

Example: Secularisation has transformed many Western societies.

secular society

IPA: /ˌsekjələ səˈsaɪəti/
Pronunciation: SEK-yuh-luh suh-SY-uh-tee
French: société sécularisée

Definition: A society where religion has limited formal influence on public life.

Example: Britain is often described as a secular society with religious traditions.

post-secular

IPA: /ˌpəʊst ˈsekjələ/
Pronunciation: post SEK-yuh-luh
French: post-séculier

Definition: Describing a society where religion remains publicly relevant despite secularisation.

Example: Some scholars argue that Europe has entered a post-secular age.

atheism

IPA: /ˈeɪθiɪzəm/
Pronunciation: AY-thee-iz-uhm
French: athéisme

Definition: The belief that God or gods do not exist.

Example: Atheism has become more visible in public debate.

atheist

IPA: /ˈeɪθiɪst/
Pronunciation: AY-thee-ist
French: athée

Definition: A person who does not believe in God or gods.

Example: Atheists may still defend freedom of religion.

agnostic

IPA: /æɡˈnɒstɪk/
Pronunciation: ag-NOS-tik
French: agnostique

Definition: A person who believes it is impossible or uncertain to know whether God exists.

Example: Many young people identify as agnostic rather than religious.

non-believer

IPA: /ˌnɒn bɪˈliːvə/
Pronunciation: non bi-LEE-vuh
French: non-croyant

Definition: A person without religious belief.

Example: Non-believers also need protection from discrimination.

religious affiliation

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs əˌfɪliˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs uh-fil-ee-AY-shuhn
French: appartenance religieuse

Definition: Connection or membership of a religious group.

Example: Religious affiliation has declined in census data.

religious identity

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité religieuse

Definition: The part of identity connected to religion.

Example: Religious identity can overlap with ethnicity and culture.

religious community

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs kəˈmjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee
French: communauté religieuse

Definition: A group of people sharing a religion or place of worship.

Example: Religious communities often provide social support.

denomination

IPA: /dɪˌnɒmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: di-nom-i-NAY-shuhn
French: confession ; dénomination

Definition: A branch or group within a religion, especially Christianity.

Example: Christian denominations differ in doctrine and worship.

sect

IPA: /sekt/
Pronunciation: sekt
French: secte ; groupe religieux

Definition: A smaller religious group, sometimes seen as separate or extreme.

Example: The word sect can be pejorative.

cult

IPA: /kʌlt/
Pronunciation: kult
French: secte ; culte controversé

Definition: A controversial term for a religious or spiritual group seen as controlling or extreme.

Example: The term cult is often used negatively.

clergy

IPA: /ˈklɜːdʒi/
Pronunciation: KLUR-jee
French: clergé

Definition: Religious leaders such as priests, ministers, rabbis or imams.

Example: The clergy can influence moral and political debate.

priest

IPA: /priːst/
Pronunciation: preest
French: prêtre

Definition: A religious leader in some Christian traditions.

Example: The priest led the ceremony.

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv ˈwɜːʃɪp/

D. Religion, politics, culture wars and extremism

fundamentalism

IPA: /ˌfʌndəˈmentəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: fun-duh-MEN-tuh-liz-uhm
French: fondamentalisme

Definition: Strict adherence to religious texts or principles, often rejecting liberal interpretation.

Example: Fundamentalism can exist in different religions.

religious fundamentalism

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˌfʌndəˈmentəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs fun-duh-MEN-tuh-liz-uhm
French: fondamentalisme religieux

Definition: A strict, literal and often politically assertive form of religion.

Example: Religious fundamentalism can challenge pluralism.

extremism

IPA: /ɪkˈstriːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ik-STREEM-iz-uhm
French: extrémisme

Definition: Extreme political or religious views that may reject democratic norms or justify violence.

Example: Extremism should not be confused with ordinary religious belief.

religious extremism

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ɪkˈstriːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs ik-STREEM-iz-uhm
French: extrémisme religieux

Definition: Extremism justified by religious ideology.

Example: Religious extremism can fuel terrorism and discrimination.

radicalisation

IPA: /ˌrædɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rad-i-kuh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: radicalisation

Definition: The process of adopting extreme views, sometimes leading to violence.

Example: Online propaganda can contribute to radicalisation.

terrorism

IPA: /ˈterərɪzəm/
Pronunciation: TERR-uh-riz-uhm
French: terrorisme

Definition: The use of violence against civilians for political or ideological aims.

Example: Terrorism is often wrongly used to stigmatise whole communities.

Islamophobia

IPA: /ɪzˌlæməˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: iz-lam-uh-FOH-bee-uh
French: islamophobie

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against Muslims.

Example: Islamophobia increased after several terrorist attacks.

antisemitism

IPA: /ˌæntiˈsemɪtɪzəm/
Pronunciation: AN-tee-SEM-i-tiz-uhm
French: antisémitisme

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against Jewish people.

Example: Antisemitism remains a serious threat in many societies.

anti-Catholicism

IPA: /ˌænti kəˈθɒlɪsɪzəm/
Pronunciation: AN-tee kuh-THOL-i-siz-uhm
French: anticatholicisme

Definition: Hostility or prejudice against Catholics.

Example: Anti-Catholicism shaped parts of British history.

hate speech

IPA: /ˈheɪt spiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: hate speech
French: discours de haine

Definition: Speech attacking people based on identity, religion or group membership.

Example: Hate speech can target religious minorities.

hate crime

IPA: /ˈheɪt kraɪm/
Pronunciation: hate kryme
French: crime de haine

Definition: A crime motivated by hostility towards identity such as religion or ethnicity.

Example: Religious hate crime can make communities feel unsafe.

blasphemy law

IPA: /ˈblæsfəmi lɔː/
Pronunciation: BLAS-fuh-mee law
French: loi sur le blasphème

Definition: A law punishing speech considered insulting to religion.

Example: Blasphemy laws raise free speech concerns.

free speech

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: free speech
French: liberté d’expression

Definition: The right to express opinions without censorship or punishment.

Example: Free speech may clash with protection from religious hatred.

offence

IPA: /əˈfens/
Pronunciation: uh-FENSS
French: offense ; atteinte

Definition: Hurt or anger caused by something insulting or disrespectful.

Example: In liberal democracies, causing offence is not always illegal.

religious offence

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs əˈfens/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs uh-FENSS
French: offense religieuse

Definition: Insult or disrespect perceived towards religious beliefs or symbols.

Example: Debates over religious offence often involve satire.

satire

IPA: /ˈsætaɪə/
Pronunciation: SAT-eye-uh
French: satire

Definition: Humour or criticism using irony, exaggeration or ridicule.

Example: Religious satire tests the limits of tolerance and free speech.

culture war

IPA: /ˈkʌltʃə wɔː/
Pronunciation: KUL-chuh war
French: guerre culturelle

Definition: A political conflict over values, identity and social norms.

Example: Religion often becomes a culture-war issue.

moral values

IPA: /ˌmɒrəl ˈvæljuːz/
Pronunciation: MOR-uhl VAL-yooz
French: valeurs morales

Definition: Ideas about right and wrong behaviour.

Example: Religious groups often argue from moral values.

family values

IPA: /ˌfæməli ˈvæljuːz/
Pronunciation: FAM-uh-lee VAL-yooz
French: valeurs familiales

Definition: Conservative ideas about family, marriage and morality.

Example: Family values are often invoked in religious politics.

abortion debate

IPA: /əˈbɔːʃən dɪˌbeɪt/
Pronunciation: uh-BOR-shuhn di-BAYT
French: débat sur l’avortement

Definition: Public debate over the legality and morality of abortion.

Example: Religion plays a major role in the abortion debate.

reproductive rights

IPA: /ˌriːprəˈdʌktɪv raɪts/
Pronunciation: ree-pruh-DUK-tiv rites
French: droits reproductifs

Definition: Rights concerning contraception, pregnancy and abortion.

Example: Reproductive rights often clash with religious conservatism.

same-sex marriage

IPA: /ˌseɪm seks ˈmærɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: same-seks MARR-ij
French: mariage homosexuel

Definition: Marriage between two people of the same sex.

Example: Many religious groups opposed same-sex marriage.

LGBTQ+ rights

IPA: /ˌel dʒiː biː tiː ˈkjuː plʌs raɪts/
Pronunciation: el-jee-bee-tee-KYOO-plus rites
French: droits LGBTQ+

Definition: Rights protecting lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and queer people.

Example: LGBTQ+ rights can conflict with conservative religious doctrine.

conscientious objection

IPA: /ˌkɒnʃiˈenʃəs əbˈdʒekʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-shee-EN-shuhs ub-JEK-shuhn
French: objection de conscience

Definition: Refusal to do something because of moral or religious beliefs.

Example: Conscientious objection is debated in healthcare and military service.

religious exemption

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ɪɡˈzempʃən/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs ig-ZEMP-shuhn
French: exemption religieuse

Definition: An exception to a rule granted for religious reasons.

Example: Religious exemptions can conflict with equality law.

faith-based organisation

IPA: /ˈfeɪθ beɪst ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: faith-bayst OR-guh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: organisation confessionnelle

Definition: An organisation inspired by or connected to religion.

Example: Faith-based organisations often provide charity and social services.

charity

IPA: /ˈtʃærəti/
Pronunciation: CHARR-uh-tee
French: association caritative

Definition: An organisation helping people in need.

Example: Religious charities play a major role in humanitarian work.

humanitarian work

IPA: /hjuːˌmænɪˈteəriən wɜːk/
Pronunciation: hyoo-man-i-TAIR-ee-uhn work
French: action humanitaire

Definition: Work intended to reduce suffering and protect human dignity.

Example: Many religious groups are involved in humanitarian work.

social justice

IPA: /ˌsəʊʃəl ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: SOH-shuhl JUS-tis
French: justice sociale

Definition: Fairness in society, especially regarding poverty, rights and opportunity.

Example: Religion can inspire movements for social justice.

liberation theology

IPA: /ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən θiˌɒlədʒi/
Pronunciation: lib-uh-RAY-shuhn thee-OL-uh-jee
French: théologie de la libération

Definition: A Christian movement linking faith with the struggle against poverty and oppression.

Example: Liberation theology connects religion with social justice.

civil rights movement

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts ˌmuːvmənt/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl rites MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement des droits civiques

Definition: A movement fighting for equal rights, especially for African Americans in the US.

Example: Churches played a major role in the civil rights movement.

religious nationalism

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme religieux

Definition: Nationalism that links national identity to a religion.

Example: Religious nationalism can exclude minorities.

Christian nationalism

IPA: /ˌkrɪstʃən ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: KRIS-chuhn NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme chrétien

Definition: A political ideology linking national identity to Christianity.

Example: Christian nationalism has become a concern in US politics.

Hindu nationalism

IPA: /ˌhɪnduː ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: HIN-doo NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme hindou

Definition: A political ideology linking Indian national identity to Hinduism.

Example: Hindu nationalism raises concerns about minority rights.

communalism

IPA: /kəˈmjuːnəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kuh-MYOO-nuh-liz-uhm
French: communautarisme conflictuel ; tensions communautaires

Definition: Political conflict organised around religious or ethnic communities.

Example: Communalism can fuel violence between religious groups.

sectarianism

IPA: /sekˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: sek-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: sectarisme

Definition: Hostility between religious or communal groups.

Example: Sectarianism has shaped conflicts in Northern Ireland and the Middle East.

E. Social role, nationalism, conflict and high-value verbs

religious conflict

IPA: /rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈkɒnflɪkt/
Pronunciation: ri-LIJ-uhs KON-flikt
French: conflit religieux

Definition: Conflict involving religious identity, belief or institutions.

Example: Religious conflict is often mixed with politics, territory and power.

to practise a religion

IPA: /ˈpræktɪs ə rɪˈlɪdʒən/
Pronunciation: PRAK-tis uh ri-LIJ-uhn
French: pratiquer une religion

Definition: To follow the rituals, beliefs or duties of a religion.

Example: Citizens must be free to practise a religion.

to worship

IPA: /ˈwɜːʃɪp/
Pronunciation: WUR-ship
French: adorer ; pratiquer le culte

Definition: To show devotion to God or a deity.

Example: People gather to worship every week.

to pray

IPA: /preɪ/
Pronunciation: pray
French: prier

Definition: To speak or think words addressed to God or a spiritual power.

Example: Some students ask for a quiet room to pray.

to convert to

IPA: /kənˈvɜːt tuː/
Pronunciation: kuhn-VURT too
French: se convertir à

Definition: To change to a different religion or belief.

Example: He converted to Islam as an adult.

to discriminate against

IPA: /dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt əˌɡenst/
Pronunciation: di-SKRIM-i-nayt uh-GENST
French: discriminer

Definition: To treat someone unfairly because of identity or belief.

Example: Employers must not discriminate against religious minorities.

to persecute

IPA: /ˈpɜːsɪkjuːt/
Pronunciation: PUR-si-kyoot
French: persécuter

Definition: To mistreat people because of identity, belief or religion.

Example: Authoritarian regimes may persecute religious minorities.

to protect religious freedom

IPA: /prəˈtekt rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈfriːdəm/
Pronunciation: pruh-TEKT ri-LIJ-uhs FREE-duhm
French: protéger la liberté religieuse

Definition: To defend the right to believe, worship or not believe.

Example: Liberal democracies must protect religious freedom.

to separate church and state

IPA: /ˈsepəreɪt ˌtʃɜːtʃ ənd ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: SEP-uh-rayt church and state
French: séparer l’Église et l’État

Definition: To keep religious institutions and government distinct.

Example: Secularism seeks to separate church and state.

to challenge secular values

IPA: /ˈtʃælɪndʒ ˈsekjələ ˈvæljuːz/
Pronunciation: CHAL-inj SEK-yuh-luh VAL-yooz
French: remettre en cause les valeurs laïques

Definition: To question values based on non-religious public principles.

Example: Religious conservatism can challenge secular values.

to fuel religious tensions

IPA: /ˈfjuːəl rɪˌlɪdʒəs ˈtenʃənz/
Pronunciation: FYOO-uhl ri-LIJ-uhs TEN-shuhnz
French: alimenter les tensions religieuses

Definition: To intensify conflict between religious groups.

Example: Inflammatory rhetoric can fuel religious tensions.

to promote tolerance

IPA: /prəˈməʊt ˈtɒlərəns/
Pronunciation: pruh-MOHT TOL-uh-ruhns
French: promouvoir la tolérance

Definition: To encourage acceptance of different beliefs and identities.

Example: Education can promote tolerance in diverse societies.

to defend pluralism

IPA: /dɪˈfend ˈplʊərəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: di-FEND PLOOR-uh-liz-uhm
French: défendre le pluralisme

Definition: To protect coexistence between different beliefs and groups.

Example: Democracies must defend pluralism against extremism.

to balance rights

IPA: /ˈbæləns raɪts/
Pronunciation: BAL-uhns rites
French: équilibrer les droits

Definition: To weigh different rights or freedoms against each other.

Example: Courts often balance rights in religious freedom cases.

to clash with secular law

IPA: /klæʃ wɪð ˈsekjələ lɔː/
Pronunciation: klash with SEK-yuh-luh law
French: entrer en conflit avec la loi laïque

Definition: To conflict with non-religious state law.

Example: Religious practices may sometimes clash with secular law.

to shape public debate

IPA: /ʃeɪp ˌpʌblɪk dɪˈbeɪt/
Pronunciation: shayp PUB-lik di-BAYT
French: façonner le débat public

Definition: To influence what people discuss and how they frame issues.

Example: Religion continues to shape public debate on morality and rights.
