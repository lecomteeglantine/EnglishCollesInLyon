Chapter objective

The golden rule

had + past participle
The government had ignored the warnings before the crisis erupted.
Main useUse the Past Perfect to show that one past event happened before another past event.
CPGE habitUse it to clarify chronology in article summaries and history: By the time the scandal broke, trust had already declined.
Key trapDo not use the Past Perfect for every past event. Use it only when you need to show “before another past moment”.

A. Lesson — What is the Past Perfect?

Earlier past

French: passé antérieur

Rule / explanation: The Past Perfect describes an action completed before another past action or moment.

Example / answer: The minister resigned after the report had been published.

Chronology

French: chronologie

Rule / explanation: It helps the reader understand which past event came first.

Example / answer: The policy failed because officials had underestimated the cost.

Past cause

French: cause passée

Rule / explanation: Use it for a past cause that explains a later past result.

Example / answer: Public anger grew because wages had stagnated for years.

CPGE relevance

French: pertinence CPGE

Rule / explanation: It is essential for article background, historical explanation and reported arguments.

Example / answer: The article explained that the crisis had begun long before the election.

B. Form and structure

Affirmative

French: forme affirmative

Rule / explanation: Use had + past participle for all subjects.

Example / answer: The government had promised reform.

Negative

French: forme négative

Rule / explanation: Use had not / hadn’t + past participle.

Example / answer: The author had not considered the historical context.

Question

French: question

Rule / explanation: Put had before the subject.

Example / answer: Had the government anticipated the backlash?

Short answer

French: réponse courte

Rule / explanation: Use had / hadn’t in short answers.

Example / answer: Yes, it had. / No, it hadn’t.

C. Past Perfect vs Past Simple

Past Simple sequence

French: suite chronologique

Rule / explanation: Use Past Simple for events told in chronological order.

Example / answer: The report appeared, the minister reacted, and the debate intensified.

Past Perfect flashback

French: retour en arrière

Rule / explanation: Use Past Perfect when you move backwards in time.

Example / answer: The debate intensified because the report had revealed new figures.

No need if order is clear

French: pas toujours nécessaire

Rule / explanation: If words like before/after already make chronology clear, Past Simple may be enough.

Example / answer: After the report appeared, the minister resigned.

Need for emphasis

French: insistance sur l’antériorité

Rule / explanation: Use Past Perfect if the earlier action matters analytically.

Example / answer: The minister resigned after he had lost the support of his party.

D. Time markers with Past Perfect

By the time

French: au moment où

Rule / explanation: Shows that something was already completed before a later moment.

Example / answer: By the time the election took place, trust had collapsed.

Already

French: déjà

Rule / explanation: Emphasises completion before a past moment.

Example / answer: The party had already lost support before the scandal.

Before

French: avant que / avant de

Rule / explanation: Introduces an earlier or later point depending on structure.

Example / answer: The government had ignored warnings before the crisis erupted.

After

French: après que

Rule / explanation: Often introduces the earlier action.

Example / answer: After the report had been released, the minister faced pressure.

E. Past Perfect in reported speech

Past Simple → Past Perfect

French: prétérit → past perfect

Rule / explanation: In reported speech, Past Simple often shifts back to Past Perfect.

Example / answer: “The crisis began in 2008” → She said the crisis had begun in 2008.

Present Perfect → Past Perfect

French: present perfect → past perfect

Rule / explanation: Present Perfect often becomes Past Perfect when reported in the past.

Example / answer: “The policy has failed” → He said the policy had failed.

Article summaries

French: résumés d’articles

Rule / explanation: Use it when summarising what an article said about earlier events.

Example / answer: The article argued that inequality had increased before the pandemic.

Backshift caution

French: attention à la concordance

Rule / explanation: If the reporting verb is present, backshift is not required.

Example / answer: The article says that inequality has increased.

F. CPGE historical and essay patterns

Long before

French: bien avant

Rule / explanation: Useful for historical context.

Example / answer: The tensions had emerged long before the referendum.

Previous failure

French: échec antérieur

Rule / explanation: Use it to explain why later action was difficult.

Example / answer: Earlier reforms had failed, which made voters sceptical.

Missed warnings

French: alertes ignorées

Rule / explanation: Useful in policy criticism.

Example / answer: Experts had warned the government, but their advice was ignored.

Cause of later distrust

French: cause d’une méfiance ultérieure

Rule / explanation: Use Past Perfect to connect earlier failures to later reactions.

Example / answer: Citizens distrusted the reform because previous promises had been broken.

G. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Using Past Perfect everywhere

French: past perfect abusif

Rule / explanation: Do not use Past Perfect for simple chronological narration.

Example / answer: Wrong: The report had appeared yesterday. Correct: The report appeared yesterday.

Forgetting had

French: oubli de had

Rule / explanation: Past Perfect needs had + past participle.

Example / answer: Wrong: it been underfunded. Correct: it had been underfunded.

Wrong participle

French: mauvais participe passé

Rule / explanation: Use the past participle, not the Past Simple form for irregular verbs.

Example / answer: Wrong: had went. Correct: had gone.

Past Perfect vs Present Perfect

French: confusion

Rule / explanation: Present Perfect links past to now; Past Perfect links earlier past to later past.

Example / answer: Trust has declined recently. / Trust had declined before the election.

H. Exercises

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: The government resigned after the report ___ (be published).

Example / answer: had been published

Exercise 2 — Choose the tense

French: exercice

Example / answer: appeared

Exercise 3 — Correct

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: The party lost support because it broken its promises.

Example / answer: The party lost support because it had broken its promises.

Exercise 4 — Reported speech

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: “The crisis began in 2008.” → She said...

Example / answer: She said the crisis had begun in 2008.

Exercise 5 — Translate

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: Le gouvernement avait ignoré les avertissements avant que la crise n’éclate.

Example / answer: The government had ignored the warnings before the crisis erupted.

Exercise 6 — Mini concours challenge
