A2 foundation grammar for history, news events, completed actions and article summaries.

Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master the Past Simple. It covers completed actions, finished time markers, regular and irregular verbs, negatives, questions, historical context, press article summaries and common mistakes made by French speakers.

The golden rule

Subject + Past Simple verb
The government introduced the reform in 2019.
Main useCompleted actions, finished periods, historical events and past news stories.
CPGE habitUse the Past Simple to give historical background or summarise completed events: The crisis began, the court ruled, voters rejected.
Key trapAfter did, use the base verb: Did voters reject...?, not Did voters rejected...?

A. Lesson — What is the Past Simple?

Past Simple

French: prétérit simple

Rule / explanation: The Past Simple is used for completed actions and finished situations in the past.

Example / answer: The government introduced the reform in 2019.

Finished time

French: moment terminé

Rule / explanation: Use it with a finished time expression: yesterday, last year, in 2008, during the pandemic.

Example / answer: The crisis began in 2008.

Completed action

French: action terminée

Rule / explanation: Use it when the action is over.

Example / answer: The article criticised the government’s response.

Past event

French: événement passé

Rule / explanation: Use it for events that happened at a specific moment in the past.

Example / answer: The court rejected the appeal.

Past sequence

French: suite d’événements

Rule / explanation: Use it to narrate events in order.

Example / answer: The minister resigned, the party collapsed, and voters demanded change.

Past facts

French: faits passés

Rule / explanation: Use it for facts that were true in the past but are no longer current.

Example / answer: The country relied heavily on coal in the twentieth century.

Biography and history

French: biographie et histoire

Rule / explanation: Use it for historical events and people’s lives.

Example / answer: Margaret Thatcher became Prime Minister in 1979.

Press article use

French: usage presse

Rule / explanation: Use it to summarise what happened in the news.

Example / answer: The strike disrupted transport across the country.

B. Form — Regular and irregular verbs

Regular verbs

French: verbes réguliers

Rule / explanation: Most regular verbs take -ed.

Example / answer: The government increased taxes.

Verbs ending in -e

French: verbes en -e

Rule / explanation: Add only -d.

Example / answer: The policy changed public opinion.

Consonant + y

French: y devient -ied

Rule / explanation: Change y to -ied.

Example / answer: The report identified several causes.

One vowel + one consonant

French: double consonne

Rule / explanation: Double the final consonant in short stressed verbs.

Example / answer: stop → stopped / ban → banned

Irregular verbs

French: verbes irréguliers

Rule / explanation: Irregular verbs must be memorised.

Example / answer: The crisis began in 2008.

Be in the past

French: être au passé

Rule / explanation: Use was / were.

Example / answer: The article was controversial.

Have in the past

French: avoir au passé

Rule / explanation: Use had.

Example / answer: The country had a strong industrial base.

Same form for all subjects

French: forme identique

Rule / explanation: Except be, the Past Simple form is the same for all subjects.

Example / answer: I voted. / They voted. / She voted.

C. Negative and question forms

Past negative

French: négation au passé

Rule / explanation: Use did not + base verb.

Example / answer: The reform did not reduce inequality.

Didn't

French: forme contractée

Rule / explanation: Contraction of did not; common in speech.

Example / answer: The policy didn't work.

No past form after did

French: base verbale après did

Rule / explanation: After did / did not, use the base verb.

Example / answer: Wrong: Did the policy reduced poverty? Correct: Did the policy reduce poverty?

Past question

French: question au passé

Rule / explanation: Use did + subject + base verb.

Example / answer: Did the government listen to experts?

Wh- question

French: question en wh-

Rule / explanation: Wh-word + did + subject + base verb.

Example / answer: Why did voters reject the proposal?

Be negative

French: négation avec be

Rule / explanation: Use was not / were not.

Example / answer: The results were not convincing.

Be question

French: question avec be

Rule / explanation: Put was / were before the subject.

Example / answer: Was the reform successful?

Short answers

French: réponses courtes

Rule / explanation: Use yes/no + subject + did / was / were.

Example / answer: Yes, it did. / No, it wasn't.

D. Time markers with the Past Simple

yesterday

French: hier

Rule / explanation: Used for the previous day.

Example / answer: The minister resigned yesterday.

last week / last year

French: la semaine dernière / l’année dernière

Rule / explanation: Used for a completed past period.

Example / answer: The report was published last year.

in + year

French: en + année

Rule / explanation: Used for a specific year.

Example / answer: The UK voted to leave the EU in 2016.

ago

French: il y a

Rule / explanation: Used to say how long before now something happened.

Example / answer: The crisis began fifteen years ago.

during

French: pendant ; durant

Rule / explanation: Used for something that happened within a period.

Example / answer: Unemployment rose during the recession.

when

French: quand

Rule / explanation: Used to introduce the moment something happened.

Example / answer: When prices rose, voters became angry.

then

French: puis ; ensuite

Rule / explanation: Used to continue a sequence of past events.

Example / answer: The scandal broke, then the minister resigned.

at the time

French: à l’époque

Rule / explanation: Used to refer to the period being discussed.

Example / answer: At the time, many analysts underestimated the crisis.

E. Common CPGE uses

Historical background

French: contexte historique

Rule / explanation: Use the Past Simple to give context.

Example / answer: The welfare state expanded after the Second World War.

News events

French: événements d’actualité

Rule / explanation: Use it for completed events reported in the press.

Example / answer: The court ruled against the government.

Political change

French: changement politique

Rule / explanation: Use it to describe elections, reforms and decisions.

Example / answer: Voters rejected the proposal in the referendum.

Economic developments

French: économie

Rule / explanation: Use it for past economic changes.

Example / answer: Inflation rose sharply after the energy shock.

Article summary

French: résumé d’article

Rule / explanation: Use it for events the article reports.

Example / answer: The article explained why the strike began.

Cause and consequence

French: cause et conséquence

Rule / explanation: Use it to describe what caused what in the past.

Example / answer: The scandal damaged public trust.

Data commentary

French: commentaire de données

Rule / explanation: Use it for a completed period in a graph.

Example / answer: Youth unemployment fell between 2015 and 2019.

Contrast with present

French: contraste passé/présent

Rule / explanation: Use it to compare past and present.

Example / answer: The party once defended free trade, but it now supports protectionism.

F. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Using present perfect for finished time

French: present perfect abusif

Rule / explanation: With finished time expressions, use Past Simple.

Example / answer: Wrong: The crisis has begun in 2008. Correct: The crisis began in 2008.

Using past form after did

French: forme passée après did

Rule / explanation: After did, use the base verb.

Example / answer: Wrong: Did voters rejected it? Correct: Did voters reject it?

Forgetting did in questions

French: oubli de did

Rule / explanation: Most Past Simple questions need did.

Example / answer: Wrong: Why voters rejected the reform? Correct: Why did voters reject the reform?

Forgetting did in negatives

French: oubli de did négatif

Rule / explanation: Most Past Simple negatives need did not.

Example / answer: Wrong: The policy not worked. Correct: The policy did not work.

Using was with ordinary verbs

French: was inutile

Rule / explanation: Do not use was before a normal Past Simple verb.

Example / answer: Wrong: The article was criticised the reform. Correct: The article criticised the reform.

Confusing was and were

French: was / were

Rule / explanation: Use was with I/he/she/it; were with you/we/they.

Example / answer: Wrong: The results was clear. Correct: The results were clear.

Pronunciation of -ed

French: prononciation de -ed

Rule / explanation: Regular -ed has three pronunciations: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/.

Example / answer: watched /t/, changed /d/, decided /ɪd/.

Overusing past continuous

French: past continuous abusif

Rule / explanation: Use Past Simple for completed past actions.

Example / answer: Wrong: The government was introduced the law. Correct: The government introduced the law.
