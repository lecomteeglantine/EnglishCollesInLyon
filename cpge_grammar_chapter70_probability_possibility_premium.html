The golden rule

Possible does not mean probable.
may / might / could = possible
likely / probably = probable
bound to / almost certain = very highly probable
Open possibilitymay, might, could, possible
Probabilitylikely, probably, expected to
Low probabilityunlikely, doubtful, little chance
Near certaintybound to, certain to, almost certain

A. Core distinction

Possibility

Rule / explanation: Something can happen or be true, without necessarily being likely.

Example / answer: The reform may create new administrative costs.

Probability

Rule / explanation: Probability evaluates how likely an event or explanation is.

Example / answer: The government is likely to revise the proposal.

Possible but unlikely

Rule / explanation: A scenario may be conceivable while remaining improbable.

Example / answer: An early election is possible, though still unlikely.

CPGE use

Rule / explanation: Distinguishing possibility from probability prevents exaggerated conclusions.

Example / answer: The policy could succeed, but success currently appears improbable.

B. May, might and could

May

Rule / explanation: A realistic but uncertain possibility.

Example / answer: The controversy may continue throughout the summer.

Might

Rule / explanation: A more tentative or remote possibility.

Example / answer: The coalition might lose its majority.

Could

Rule / explanation: One possible explanation or outcome among several.

Example / answer: The fall in turnout could reflect political fatigue.

Overlap

Rule / explanation: The three modals often overlap, though context changes their force.

Example / answer: The policy may / might / could produce unintended effects.

Negative forms

Rule / explanation: May not and might not express uncertain non-occurrence.

Example / answer: The reform may not deliver the promised savings.

C. Likely and unlikely

Be likely to

Rule / explanation: Use for a probable event or tendency.

Example / answer: The proposal is likely to face opposition.

It is likely that

Rule / explanation: A formal impersonal alternative.

Example / answer: It is likely that inflation will fall gradually.

Be unlikely to

Rule / explanation: Use for an improbable event.

Example / answer: The measure is unlikely to satisfy every stakeholder.

Highly likely / unlikely

Rule / explanation: Strengthen the assessment with highly.

Example / answer: The bill is highly unlikely to pass unchanged.

Comparatives

Rule / explanation: Compare two outcomes with more or less likely.

Example / answer: A compromise is more likely than an early election.

D. Probability adverbs

Probably

Rule / explanation: More likely than not.

Example / answer: The government will probably delay the vote.

Possibly

Rule / explanation: A weaker and more open possibility.

Example / answer: The discrepancy was possibly caused by a data error.

Certainly

Rule / explanation: Very high confidence.

Example / answer: The scandal will certainly influence the campaign.

Presumably

Rule / explanation: A reasonable conclusion based on available information.

Example / answer: Presumably, the committee will request further evidence.

Apparently

Rule / explanation: An appearance, inference or second-hand account.

Example / answer: Apparently, negotiations have resumed.

E. Adverb position

With be

Rule / explanation: Place the adverb after be.

Example / answer: The result is probably temporary.

Lexical verb

Rule / explanation: Place the adverb before the main verb.

Example / answer: The policy probably increases costs.

Auxiliary

Rule / explanation: Place it after the first auxiliary.

Example / answer: The government has probably underestimated public anger.

With will

Rule / explanation: Probably usually follows will.

Example / answer: The controversy will probably continue.

Sentence adverb

Rule / explanation: Possibly, apparently and presumably may come first.

Example / answer: Possibly, the survey excludes vulnerable groups.

F. Nouns of probability

Possibility of

Rule / explanation: Use before a noun or gerund.

Example / answer: The possibility of an early election cannot be excluded.

Possibility that

Rule / explanation: Use before a full clause.

Example / answer: There is a possibility that the figures are incomplete.

Likelihood of

Rule / explanation: A formal noun phrase for probability.

Example / answer: The likelihood of further delays has increased.

Likelihood that

Rule / explanation: Use before a clause.

Example / answer: The likelihood that Parliament will reject the bill remains high.

Chance

Rule / explanation: A flexible general expression of probability.

Example / answer: There is only a slight chance of immediate agreement.

G. Chance structures

There is a chance that

Rule / explanation: An open possibility.

Example / answer: There is a chance that the coalition will survive.

A good chance

Rule / explanation: A fairly probable outcome.

Example / answer: There is a good chance that the proposal will be amended.

Little chance

Rule / explanation: Weak probability.

Example / answer: There is little chance that the dispute will end quickly.

What are the chances of...?

Rule / explanation: Ask directly about probability.

Example / answer: What are the chances of the bill passing?

Stand a chance of

Rule / explanation: A realistic possibility of success.

Example / answer: The reform stands a chance of succeeding if funding improves.

H. Possible, plausible, conceivable

Possible

Rule / explanation: An open possibility.

Example / answer: It is possible that the sample is unrepresentative.

Plausible

Rule / explanation: Reasonable and compatible with the evidence.

Example / answer: A plausible explanation is that funding was insufficient.

Conceivable

Rule / explanation: Imaginable, even if unlikely.

Example / answer: It is conceivable that the reform will have no lasting effect.

Credible

Rule / explanation: Worthy of belief.

Example / answer: The report offers no credible alternative explanation.

Key distinction

Rule / explanation: Conceivable is weaker than plausible, and plausible is not necessarily probable.

Example / answer: Institutional collapse is conceivable but highly improbable.

I. Should for expectation

Expected outcome

Rule / explanation: Should predicts what is normally expected.

Example / answer: The additional funding should reduce waiting times.

Negative expectation

Rule / explanation: Should not predicts non-occurrence.

Example / answer: The delay should not affect the final result.

Past expectation

Rule / explanation: Should have refers to an expected past result.

Example / answer: The committee should have received the figures by now.

Not obligation

Rule / explanation: Context distinguishes probability from duty.

Example / answer: The train should arrive at six.

Academic prediction

Rule / explanation: Useful for model-based expectations.

Example / answer: If trends continue, turnout should remain stable.

J. Near certainty

Bound to

Rule / explanation: An outcome seen as virtually inevitable.

Example / answer: The decision is bound to provoke criticism.

Certain to

Rule / explanation: Very high confidence.

Example / answer: The reform is certain to affect local budgets.

Sure to

Rule / explanation: A confident prediction.

Example / answer: The controversy is sure to continue.

Almost certain

Rule / explanation: Near certainty with a small margin of doubt.

Example / answer: The bill is almost certain to pass.

Caution

Rule / explanation: These forms require stronger evidence than likely.

Example / answer: Do not use bound to when evidence is limited.

K. Past possibility

May have

Rule / explanation: Possible past action or cause.

Example / answer: Officials may have underestimated public opposition.

Might have

Rule / explanation: A more tentative past possibility.

Example / answer: The author might have overlooked a key variable.

Could have

Rule / explanation: A possible cause or unrealised alternative.

Example / answer: The delay could have resulted from legal uncertainty.

Must have

Rule / explanation: A strong past deduction.

Example / answer: The government must have anticipated some resistance.

Cannot have

Rule / explanation: A past event judged impossible.

Example / answer: The committee cannot have reviewed everything overnight.

L. Future scenarios

Best case

Rule / explanation: A favourable possibility.

Example / answer: In the best-case scenario, the reform could reduce both costs and delays.

Worst case

Rule / explanation: An adverse possibility.

Example / answer: In the worst-case scenario, the policy might deepen inequality.

Most likely

Rule / explanation: The central forecast.

Example / answer: The most likely outcome is a revised compromise.

Rank outcomes

Rule / explanation: Compare alternatives by plausibility.

Example / answer: An early election is possible, but a reshuffle is more likely.

Conditional future

Rule / explanation: Link likelihood to a condition.

Example / answer: If funding is maintained, the programme is likely to continue.

M. Conditional possibility

If

Rule / explanation: A possibility depends on a condition.

Example / answer: If inflation falls, confidence may recover.

Unless

Rule / explanation: A negatively framed necessary condition.

Example / answer: The reform is unlikely to succeed unless funding increases.

Provided that

Rule / explanation: A required condition for success.

Example / answer: The scheme could work provided that authorities cooperate.

As long as

Rule / explanation: A continuing condition.

Example / answer: The coalition may survive as long as its members remain united.

In the event that

Rule / explanation: A formal future possibility.

Example / answer: In the event that talks fail, a new vote may be called.

N. Risk language

Risk of

Rule / explanation: A possible harmful outcome.

Example / answer: There is a risk of further polarisation.

At risk of

Rule / explanation: Exposure to danger.

Example / answer: Rural services are at risk of disappearing.

Increase the likelihood

Rule / explanation: Make an outcome more probable.

Example / answer: Poor communication increases the likelihood of resistance.

Reduce the chances

Rule / explanation: Make an outcome less probable.

Example / answer: Consultation could reduce the chances of legal challenge.

Associated with

Rule / explanation: A relationship without proven causation.

Example / answer: Economic insecurity is associated with lower trust.

O. Source analysis

Preserve uncertainty

Rule / explanation: Do not strengthen may into will.

Example / answer: The journalist suggests that the decline may reflect political fatigue.

Question headlines

Rule / explanation: A headline may raise a possibility without asserting it.

Example / answer: Could the coalition collapse before autumn?

Strategic ambiguity

Rule / explanation: Possibility language can imply seriousness while limiting commitment.

Example / answer: The word allegedly distances the source from the accusation.

Compare confidence

Rule / explanation: Different sources may rank the same outcome differently.

Example / answer: One treats recession as likely; another calls it merely possible.

Evaluate evidence

Rule / explanation: Check whether confidence matches support.

Example / answer: The prediction appears more certain than the data justify.

P. CPGE essays

Rank explanations

Rule / explanation: Use more likely, less likely and most plausible.

Example / answer: Economic insecurity is more likely than demographics alone.

Compare scenarios

Rule / explanation: Use possible, probable and unlikely.

Example / answer: A gradual compromise is probable; immediate reform is less likely.

Avoid false certainty

Rule / explanation: Use likely instead of will when evidence is incomplete.

Example / answer: The reform is likely to improve efficiency.

Preserve alternatives

Rule / explanation: Use cannot rule out and remains possible.

Example / answer: A legal challenge cannot be ruled out.

Proportionate conclusion

Rule / explanation: Match the final claim to the evidence.

Example / answer: The policy is more likely to produce limited gains than structural change.

Q. Khôlle and debate

Offer a possibility

Rule / explanation: Use one possibility is that or it could be that.

Example / answer: One possibility is that the author addresses a sceptical audience.

Assess likelihood

Rule / explanation: Use seems likely or appears unlikely.

Example / answer: A compromise now seems more likely than an election.

Compare alternatives

Rule / explanation: Use more likely than or less plausible than.

Example / answer: Economic pressure is more likely than cultural change to explain the timing.

Invite discussion

Rule / explanation: Ask how likely is it that...?

Example / answer: How likely is it that the reform will survive a change of government?

Support claims

Rule / explanation: Give a reason for every probability judgement.

Example / answer: Because delays are repeated, further postponement appears likely.

R. Common mistakes

Possible vs likely

Rule / explanation: Possible means can happen; likely means probably will happen.

Example / answer: The bill is likely to pass.

Likely structure

Rule / explanation: Use subject + be likely to + infinitive.

Example / answer: The government is likely to respond.

Maybe / may be

Rule / explanation: Maybe is an adverb; may be is a modal phrase.

Example / answer: Maybe it will work. / It may be effective.

Chance of

Rule / explanation: Use chance of + noun or gerund.

Example / answer: There is little chance of the bill passing.

Double modal

Rule / explanation: Do not combine modal auxiliaries.

Example / answer: Wrong: may could fail. Correct: may fail.

No fixed percentages

Rule / explanation: Modal verbs do not equal exact percentages.

Example / answer: Might does not automatically mean 30 per cent.

S. Exercises

1. Possibility

Rule / explanation: Perhaps the reform will increase costs.

Example / answer: The reform may / might / could increase costs.

2. Probability

Rule / explanation: There is a strong chance that the proposal will be amended.

Example / answer: The proposal is likely to be amended.

3. Improbability

Rule / explanation: It probably will not satisfy every group.

Example / answer: It is unlikely to satisfy every group.

4. Compare

Rule / explanation: A compromise is more probable than an early election.

Example / answer: A compromise is more likely than an early election.

5. Correct

Rule / explanation: There is little chance to the bill pass.

Example / answer: There is little chance of the bill passing.

6. Condition

Rule / explanation: Funding must increase for the programme to continue.

Example / answer: The programme may continue provided that funding increases.

7. Translate

Rule / explanation: Il est fort probable que le gouvernement modifie sa proposition.

Example / answer: The government is very likely to amend its proposal.

8. Mini challenge

Rule / explanation: Rank three outcomes from most to least likely.

Example / answer: The most likely outcome is a revised compromise. A limited reshuffle is also possible. A complete collapse remains conceivable but highly unlikely.
