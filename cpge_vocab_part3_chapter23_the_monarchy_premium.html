A. Core monarchy vocabulary and succession

monarchy

IPA: /ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie

Definition: A political system in which a king or queen serves as head of state.

Example: The British monarchy is both a political institution and a national symbol.

constitutional monarchy

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie constitutionnelle

Definition: A system in which a monarch is head of state but political power is limited by law and exercised by elected institutions.

Example: The UK is a constitutional monarchy, not an absolute monarchy.

absolute monarchy

IPA: /ˌæbsəluːt ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: AB-suh-loot MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie absolue

Definition: A monarchy in which the monarch has very broad or unlimited political power.

Example: Britain moved away from absolute monarchy after centuries of constitutional conflict.

monarch

IPA: /ˈmɒnək/
Pronunciation: MON-uhk
French: monarque

Definition: A king or queen who serves as head of state.

Example: The monarch formally appoints the Prime Minister.

king

IPA: /kɪŋ/
Pronunciation: king
French: roi

Definition: A male monarch.

Example: The king performs ceremonial duties.

queen

IPA: /kwiːn/
Pronunciation: kween
French: reine

Definition: A female monarch.

Example: Queen Elizabeth II reigned for more than seventy years.

sovereign

IPA: /ˈsɒvrɪn/
Pronunciation: SOV-rin
French: souverain ; souveraine

Definition: A formal term for the monarch as the supreme symbolic authority of the state.

Example: The sovereign opens Parliament every year.

head of state

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: hed ov state
French: chef de l’État

Definition: The official representative of a country in constitutional and ceremonial matters.

Example: In Britain, the monarch is head of state.

head of government

IPA: /ˌhed əv ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: hed ov GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: chef du gouvernement

Definition: The political leader who runs the government.

Example: The Prime Minister, not the monarch, is head of government.

the Crown

IPA: /ðə ˈkraʊn/
Pronunciation: thuh crown
French: la Couronne

Definition: The legal and symbolic authority of the state, formally embodied by the monarch.

Example: Ministers exercise power in the name of the Crown.

Crown Estate

IPA: /ˌkraʊn ɪˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: crown i-STAYT
French: Crown Estate ; patrimoine de la Couronne

Definition: A large property portfolio belonging to the Crown but managed independently, with profits mostly going to the Treasury.

Example: The Crown Estate is often discussed in debates about royal finances.

royal family

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈfæməli/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl FAM-uh-lee
French: famille royale

Definition: The monarch’s family, especially senior members with public roles.

Example: The royal family attracts intense media attention.

House of Windsor

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˈwɪnzə/
Pronunciation: howss ov WIN-zuh
French: maison de Windsor

Definition: The current royal house of the United Kingdom.

Example: The House of Windsor has ruled Britain since 1917.

heir

IPA: /eə/
Pronunciation: air
French: héritier ; héritière

Definition: The person expected to inherit the throne.

Example: The heir to the throne often prepares for a lifetime of public duty.

heir apparent

IPA: /ˌeər əˈpærənt/
Pronunciation: air uh-PARR-uhnt
French: héritier présomptif direct

Definition: The person who is certain to inherit the throne unless they die first.

Example: The Prince of Wales is traditionally the heir apparent.

line of succession

IPA: /ˌlaɪn əv səkˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: line ov suhk-SESH-uhn
French: ordre de succession

Definition: The order in which people are entitled to inherit the throne.

Example: The line of succession is regulated by law.

succession

IPA: /səkˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: suhk-SESH-uhn
French: succession

Definition: The process by which the throne passes from one monarch to the next.

Example: The succession was carefully planned after the Queen’s death.

coronation

IPA: /ˌkɒrəˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kor-uh-NAY-shuhn
French: couronnement

Definition: The ceremony in which a monarch is formally crowned.

Example: The coronation is a religious and constitutional ceremony.

accession

IPA: /əkˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: uhk-SESH-uhn
French: avènement ; accession au trône

Definition: The moment when a new monarch takes the throne.

Example: The king’s accession occurred immediately after the queen’s death.

reign

IPA: /reɪn/
Pronunciation: rayn
French: règne

Definition: The period during which a monarch rules or serves as monarch.

Example: Elizabeth II’s reign was the longest in British history.

abdication

IPA: /ˌæbdɪˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ab-di-KAY-shuhn
French: abdication

Definition: The act of giving up the throne voluntarily.

Example: Edward VIII’s abdication created a constitutional crisis.

to abdicate

IPA: /ˈæbdɪkeɪt/
Pronunciation: AB-di-kayt
French: abdiquer

Definition: To give up the throne voluntarily.

Example: Edward VIII abdicated in 1936.

regent

IPA: /ˈriːdʒənt/
Pronunciation: REE-juhnt
French: régent ; régente

Definition: A person who rules temporarily when the monarch is unable to do so.

Example: A regent may be appointed if the monarch is incapacitated.

royal assent

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl əˈsent/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl uh-SENT
French: sanction royale

Definition: The monarch’s formal approval of a bill, making it law.

Example: Royal assent is now a constitutional formality.

B. Constitutional role, ceremonies and symbolic power

royal prerogative

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl prɪˈrɒɡətɪv/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl pri-ROG-uh-tiv
French: prérogative royale

Definition: Traditional executive powers formally held by the monarch but exercised by ministers.

Example: The royal prerogative is important in foreign policy and defence.

reserve powers

IPA: /rɪˈzɜːv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURV POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs de réserve

Definition: Exceptional powers that a monarch or representative may theoretically use in a constitutional crisis.

Example: Reserve powers are rarely used in modern Britain.

ceremonial role

IPA: /ˌserəˈməʊniəl rəʊl/
Pronunciation: ser-uh-MOH-nee-uhl rohl
French: rôle cérémoniel

Definition: A symbolic public role involving ceremonies rather than direct political power.

Example: The monarch’s role is largely ceremonial.

symbolic role

IPA: /sɪmˈbɒlɪk rəʊl/
Pronunciation: sim-BOL-ik rohl
French: rôle symbolique

Definition: A role based on representing unity, continuity or national identity.

Example: The monarchy plays a symbolic role in national life.

political neutrality

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl njuːˈtræləti/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl nyoo-TRAL-uh-tee
French: neutralité politique

Definition: The principle of not publicly taking sides in party politics.

Example: Political neutrality is essential to the modern monarchy.

above politics

IPA: /əˌbʌv ˈpɒlɪtɪks/
Pronunciation: uh-BUV POL-i-tiks
French: au-dessus de la politique

Definition: Not involved in partisan political conflict.

Example: The monarch is expected to remain above politics.

impartiality

IPA: /ˌɪmpɑːʃiˈæləti/
Pronunciation: im-par-shee-AL-uh-tee
French: impartialité

Definition: Fairness and neutrality between opposing sides.

Example: Royal impartiality helps preserve the monarchy’s legitimacy.

soft power

IPA: /ˈsɒft ˌpaʊə/
Pronunciation: soft POW-uh
French: soft power ; pouvoir d’influence

Definition: Influence based on attraction, culture and diplomacy rather than force.

Example: The royal family contributes to Britain’s soft power.

national unity

IPA: /ˌnæʃənəl ˈjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl YOO-nuh-tee
French: unité nationale

Definition: A sense that citizens belong to the same national community.

Example: Supporters argue that the monarchy represents national unity.

continuity

IPA: /ˌkɒntɪˈnjuːəti/
Pronunciation: kon-ti-NYOO-uh-tee
French: continuité

Definition: The sense of stability and connection with the past.

Example: The monarchy provides a sense of continuity during political change.

tradition

IPA: /trəˈdɪʃən/
Pronunciation: truh-DISH-uhn
French: tradition

Definition: Customs and practices inherited from the past.

Example: Royal ceremonies are rich in tradition.

pageantry

IPA: /ˈpædʒəntri/
Pronunciation: PAJ-uhn-tree
French: apparat ; cérémonial fastueux

Definition: Elaborate public ceremony, costumes and display.

Example: Royal pageantry attracts tourists and global media coverage.

pomp and circumstance

IPA: /ˌpɒmp ən ˈsɜːkəmstəns/
Pronunciation: pomp uhn SUR-kuhm-stans
French: apparat et solennité

Definition: Formal ceremony, splendour and spectacle.

Example: The coronation was full of pomp and circumstance.

royal ceremony

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈserəməni/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl SERR-uh-muh-nee
French: cérémonie royale

Definition: A formal public event involving the monarch or royal family.

Example: Royal ceremonies reinforce national tradition.

State Opening of Parliament

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈəʊpənɪŋ əv ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: state OH-puh-ning ov PAR-luh-muhnt
French: ouverture officielle du Parlement

Definition: The ceremony marking the beginning of a parliamentary session, including the monarch’s speech.

Example: The State Opening of Parliament combines politics and royal ritual.

King’s Speech

IPA: /ˌkɪŋz ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: kingz speech
French: discours du roi

Definition: The speech delivered by the monarch setting out the government’s legislative agenda.

Example: The King’s Speech is written by the government.

Queen’s Speech

IPA: /ˌkwiːnz ˈspiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: kweenz speech
French: discours de la reine

Definition: The same parliamentary speech when the monarch is a queen.

Example: The Queen’s Speech outlined the government’s priorities.

Privy Council

IPA: /ˌprɪvi ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: PRIV-ee KOWN-suhl
French: Conseil privé

Definition: A formal advisory body to the monarch, now mostly ceremonial but still used for certain official acts.

Example: Orders in Council are formally approved through the Privy Council.

Order in Council

IPA: /ˌɔːdər ɪn ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: OR-duh in KOWN-suhl
French: décret en conseil

Definition: A legal instrument formally made by the monarch with the Privy Council.

Example: Some executive decisions are made through Orders in Council.

to appoint the Prime Minister

IPA: /əˈpɔɪnt ðə ˌpraɪm ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: uh-POINT thuh prime MIN-i-stuh
French: nommer le Premier ministre

Definition: To formally name the person who can command Commons confidence as Prime Minister.

Example: The monarch appoints the Prime Minister after a general election.

to open Parliament

IPA: /ˈəʊpən ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: OH-puhn PAR-luh-muhnt
French: ouvrir le Parlement

Definition: To formally begin a parliamentary session through ceremony.

Example: The monarch opens Parliament in a highly symbolic ceremony.

to grant royal assent

IPA: /ɡrɑːnt ˌrɔɪəl əˈsent/
Pronunciation: grant ROY-uhl uh-SENT
French: donner la sanction royale

Definition: To formally approve a bill so that it becomes law.

Example: The monarch grants royal assent to bills passed by Parliament.

to act on advice

IPA: /ækt ɒn ədˈvaɪs/
Pronunciation: akt on ad-VYSS
French: agir sur avis ministériel

Definition: To follow the advice of ministers, as the monarch normally does.

Example: The monarch normally acts on the advice of the Prime Minister.

to remain politically neutral

IPA: /rɪˈmeɪn pəˌlɪtɪkəli ˈnjuːtrəl/
Pronunciation: ri-MAYN puh-LIT-i-kuh-lee NYOO-truhl
French: rester politiquement neutre

Definition: To avoid taking public political positions.

Example: The monarch must remain politically neutral.

to embody the nation

IPA: /ɪmˈbɒdi ðə ˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: im-BOD-ee thuh NAY-shuhn
French: incarner la nation

Definition: To represent the nation symbolically.

Example: Supporters claim the monarch embodies the nation.

C. Royal duties, Commonwealth and public service

to symbolise continuity

IPA: /ˈsɪmbəlaɪz ˌkɒntɪˈnjuːəti/
Pronunciation: SIM-buh-lyz kon-ti-NYOO-uh-tee
French: symboliser la continuité

Definition: To represent stability and historical connection.

Example: The monarchy symbolises continuity in a changing society.

royal household

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈhaʊshəʊld/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl HOWSS-hohld
French: maison royale

Definition: The organisation that supports the monarch and royal family.

Example: The royal household manages official engagements.

royal duties

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈdjuːtiz/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl DYOO-teez
French: devoirs royaux

Definition: Official responsibilities performed by members of the royal family.

Example: Senior royals carry out hundreds of royal duties each year.

official engagement

IPA: /əˌfɪʃəl ɪnˈɡeɪdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-FISH-uhl in-GAYJ-muhnt
French: engagement officiel

Definition: A public event attended by a royal as part of their duties.

Example: The princess attended an official engagement at a hospital.

patronage

IPA: /ˈpætrənɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: PAT-ruh-nij
French: patronage ; soutien officiel

Definition: Support given by a royal to a charity, organisation or cause.

Example: Royal patronage can raise a charity’s profile.

charity work

IPA: /ˈtʃærəti wɜːk/
Pronunciation: CHARR-uh-tee work
French: travail caritatif

Definition: Work supporting charitable causes.

Example: Charity work is a major part of modern royal activity.

public service

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk ˈsɜːvɪs/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik SUR-vis
French: service public

Definition: Work performed for the benefit of the public.

Example: The monarchy presents itself as devoted to public service.

royal tour

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈtʊə/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl tour
French: tournée royale

Definition: An official visit by a royal to another country or region.

Example: Royal tours can strengthen diplomatic ties but also provoke criticism.

Commonwealth

IPA: /ˈkɒmənwelθ/
Pronunciation: KOM-uhn-welth
French: Commonwealth

Definition: An association of countries, many formerly part of the British Empire.

Example: The monarch is Head of the Commonwealth.

Head of the Commonwealth

IPA: /ˌhed əv ðə ˈkɒmənwelθ/
Pronunciation: hed ov thuh KOM-uhn-welth
French: chef du Commonwealth

Definition: The symbolic leader of the Commonwealth.

Example: The title Head of the Commonwealth is not hereditary in the same way as the Crown.

Commonwealth realm

IPA: /ˈkɒmənwelθ relm/
Pronunciation: KOM-uhn-welth relm
French: royaume du Commonwealth

Definition: A Commonwealth country where the British monarch is also head of state.

Example: Canada and Australia are Commonwealth realms.

realm

IPA: /relm/
Pronunciation: relm
French: royaume

Definition: A kingdom or state under a monarch.

Example: The monarch is sovereign of several realms.

republicanism

IPA: /rɪˈpʌblɪkənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-li-kuh-niz-uhm
French: républicanisme

Definition: Support for replacing the monarchy with a republic.

Example: Republicanism remains a minority view in Britain, but it has gained visibility.

republican

IPA: /rɪˈpʌblɪkən/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-li-kuhn
French: républicain ; opposant à la monarchie

Definition: A person who supports replacing the monarchy with an elected head of state.

Example: Republicans argue that the monarchy is undemocratic.

anti-monarchist

IPA: /ˌænti ˈmɒnəkɪst/
Pronunciation: AN-tee MON-uh-kist
French: anti-monarchiste

Definition: Opposed to monarchy.

Example: Anti-monarchist campaigners protested during the coronation.

abolition of the monarchy

IPA: /ˌæbəˈlɪʃən əv ðə ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: ab-uh-LISH-uhn ov thuh MON-uh-kee
French: abolition de la monarchie

Definition: The act of ending the monarchy as an institution.

Example: Some campaigners call for the abolition of the monarchy.

elected head of state

IPA: /ɪˌlektɪd ˌhed əv ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: i-LEK-tid hed ov state
French: chef de l’État élu

Definition: A head of state chosen by election rather than inheritance.

Example: Republicans want an elected head of state.

hereditary privilege

IPA: /həˌredɪtəri ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: huh-RED-i-tuh-ree PRIV-uh-lij
French: privilège héréditaire

Definition: Advantages passed down by birth within a family.

Example: Critics say monarchy is based on hereditary privilege.

inherited status

IPA: /ɪnˌherɪtɪd ˈsteɪtəs/
Pronunciation: in-HER-i-tid STAY-tuhs
French: statut hérité

Definition: Social position received by birth rather than merit.

Example: The monarchy embodies inherited status.

transparency

IPA: /trænsˈpærənsi/

IPA: /ˌslɪmd daʊn ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: slimd-down MON-uh-kee
French: monarchie réduite ; monarchie allégée

Definition: A monarchy with fewer working royals and lower public cost.

Example: A slimmed-down monarchy may be more acceptable to taxpayers.

relevance

IPA: /ˈreləvəns/
Pronunciation: REL-uh-vuhns
French: pertinence

Definition: The quality of still being useful, meaningful or important.

Example: The monarchy must prove its relevance to younger generations.

anachronism

IPA: /əˈnækrənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: uh-NAK-ruh-niz-uhm
French: anachronisme

Definition: Something that seems outdated or belonging to a past era.

Example: Critics call the monarchy an anachronism.

colonial legacy

IPA: /kəˌləʊniəl ˈleɡəsi/
Pronunciation: kuh-LOH-nee-uhl LEG-uh-see
French: héritage colonial

Definition: The lasting effects of colonial rule and empire.

Example: The monarchy is increasingly judged through Britain’s colonial legacy.

British Empire

IPA: /ˌbrɪtɪʃ ˈempaɪə/
Pronunciation: BRIT-ish EM-py-uh
French: Empire britannique

Definition: The former global empire ruled or controlled by Britain.

Example: The monarchy was closely linked to the British Empire.

decolonisation

IPA: /ˌdiːkɒlənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-kol-uh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: décolonisation

Definition: The process by which colonies became independent from imperial rule.

Example: Decolonisation changed the monarchy’s global role.

Commonwealth criticism

IPA: /ˈkɒmənwelθ ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/
Pronunciation: KOM-uhn-welth KRIT-i-siz-uhm
French: critique de la monarchie dans le Commonwealth

Definition: Criticism of the monarchy’s role in Commonwealth countries.

Example: Commonwealth criticism has grown in some Caribbean countries.

reparations debate

IPA: /ˌrepəˈreɪʃənz dɪˌbeɪt/
Pronunciation: rep-uh-RAY-shuhnz di-BAYT
French: débat sur les réparations

Definition: Debate over compensation or apology for slavery and colonialism.

Example: The reparations debate has put pressure on the monarchy.

to defend the monarchy

IPA: /dɪˈfend ðə ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: di-FEND thuh MON-uh-kee
French: défendre la monarchie

Definition: To argue in favour of keeping the monarchy.

Example: Royalists defend the monarchy as a stabilising institution.

to abolish the monarchy

IPA: /əˈbɒlɪʃ ðə ˈmɒnəki/
Pronunciation: uh-BOL-ish thuh MON-uh-kee
French: abolir la monarchie

Definition: To end the monarchy and replace it with another system.

Example: Republicans want to abolish the monarchy.

to question royal privilege

IPA: /ˈkwestʃən ˈrɔɪəl ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: KWES-chuhn ROY-uhl PRIV-uh-lij
French: remettre en question les privilèges royaux

Definition: To challenge the advantages enjoyed by the royal family.

Example: Younger voters are more likely to question royal privilege.

to maintain political neutrality

IPA: /meɪnˈteɪn pəˌlɪtɪkəl njuːˈtræləti/
Pronunciation: mayn-TAYN puh-LIT-i-kuhl nyoo-TRAL-uh-tee
French: maintenir la neutralité politique

Definition: To avoid partisan political positions.

Example: The monarch must maintain political neutrality.

to attract media attention

IPA: /əˈtrækt ˈmiːdiə əˈtenʃən/
Pronunciation: uh-TRAKT MEE-dee-uh uh-TEN-shuhn
French: attirer l’attention des médias

Definition: To draw coverage from newspapers, television or online media.

Example: Royal disputes attract media attention worldwide.

to boost tourism

IPA: /buːst ˈtʊərɪzəm/
Pronunciation: boost TOOR-iz-uhm
French: stimuler le tourisme

Definition: To increase tourism activity or revenue.

Example: Supporters claim royal events boost tourism.

to damage public trust

IPA: /ˈdæmɪdʒ ˌpʌblɪk ˈtrʌst/
Pronunciation: DAM-ij PUB-lik trust
French: nuire à la confiance du public

Definition: To reduce public confidence in an institution.

Example: Scandals can damage public trust in the monarchy.

to adapt to modern society

IPA: /əˈdæpt tə ˈmɒdən səˈsaɪəti/
Pronunciation: uh-DAPT tuh MOD-uhn suh-SY-uh-tee
French: s’adapter à la société moderne

Definition: To change in order to remain relevant in contemporary life.

Example: The monarchy must adapt to modern society.
