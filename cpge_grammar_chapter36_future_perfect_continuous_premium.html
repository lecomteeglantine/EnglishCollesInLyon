Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master the Future Perfect Continuous. It covers will have been + V-ing, duration up to a future point, ongoing future processes, long-term projections, contrast with Future Perfect Simple and common French-speaker mistakes.

The golden rule

will have been + verb-ing
By 2030, governments will have been dealing with climate pressure for decades.
Main useUse the Future Perfect Continuous to show how long an action or process will have been continuing up to a future point.
CPGE habitUse it for long-term trends and projected pressure: By the next election, voters will have been struggling with inflation for years.
Key trapFuture Perfect Simple = completed result. Future Perfect Continuous = duration and ongoing process up to a future point.

A. Lesson — What is the Future Perfect Continuous?

Duration up to a future point

French: durée jusqu’à un moment futur

Rule / explanation: It describes an action or process that will continue until a future moment.

Example / answer: By next year, the committee will have been investigating the scandal for eighteen months.

Long-term process

French: processus de long terme

Rule / explanation: It highlights duration rather than a finished result.

Example / answer: By 2040, cities will have been adapting to climate change for decades.

Projected cause

French: cause projetée

Rule / explanation: Use it to explain why a future situation may exist.

Example / answer: Voters may be exhausted because they will have been facing economic pressure for years.

Analytical value

French: valeur analytique

Rule / explanation: It is useful when analysing slow political, social, economic or environmental trends.

Example / answer: By then, inequality will have been reshaping society for an entire generation.

B. Form and structure

Affirmative

French: forme affirmative

Rule / explanation: Use will have been + verb-ing for all subjects.

Example / answer: The government will have been negotiating for months.

Negative

French: forme négative

Rule / explanation: Use will not have been / won’t have been + verb-ing.

Example / answer: The policy will not have been helping the poorest households for long enough.

Question

French: question

Rule / explanation: Put will before the subject.

Example / answer: Will the committee have been working on the report for a year?

Wh-question

French: question en wh-

Rule / explanation: Use wh-word + will + subject + have been + V-ing.

Example / answer: How long will voters have been waiting for reform?

C. Future Perfect Continuous vs Future Perfect Simple

Process vs result

French: processus vs résultat

Rule / explanation: Continuous focuses on duration; simple focuses on completion.

Example / answer: By 2030, rents will have been rising for years. / Rents will have risen by 20%.

Ongoing trend

French: tendance en cours

Rule / explanation: Use continuous when the trend may still be continuing at the future point.

Example / answer: By then, public trust will have been declining for a decade.

Completed amount

French: quantité accomplie

Rule / explanation: Use simple form for completed numbers, quantities or milestones.

Example / answer: By 2030, the government will have built 100,000 homes.

Emphasis on exhaustion

French: insistance sur l’usure

Rule / explanation: Continuous often suggests strain, repetition or accumulated pressure.

Example / answer: By the next election, households will have been struggling with high prices for years.

D. Time markers and duration

For + duration

French: depuis / pendant

Rule / explanation: Use for with a period of time.

Example / answer: By June, the inquiry will have been running for two years.

Since + starting point

French: depuis

Rule / explanation: Use since with the starting point of the action.

Example / answer: By 2030, the country will have been adapting since the first major floods.

By the time + Present Simple

French: d’ici à ce que

Rule / explanation: In future time clauses, use Present Simple, not will.

Example / answer: By the time voters go to the polls, they will have been waiting for answers for months.

For decades / for generations

French: depuis des décennies / générations

Rule / explanation: Useful for long historical or social processes.

Example / answer: By then, inequality will have been shaping opportunities for generations.

E. CPGE policy, climate and society patterns

Climate adaptation

French: adaptation climatique

Rule / explanation: Use it for long environmental processes.

Example / answer: By 2050, governments will have been adapting to extreme weather for decades.

Housing pressure

French: pression du logement

Rule / explanation: Useful for social policy analysis.

Example / answer: By the next decade, young adults will have been facing unaffordable rents for years.

Democratic fatigue

French: fatigue démocratique

Rule / explanation: Use it to describe accumulated distrust or disappointment.

Example / answer: By the next election, citizens will have been losing faith in institutions for a long time.

Technological change

French: changement technologique

Rule / explanation: Useful for analysing AI, automation and labour markets.

Example / answer: By 2035, workers will have been adapting to automation for nearly twenty years.

F. Formal academic alternatives

Will have been undergoing

French: aura subi / traversé depuis

Rule / explanation: Formal phrase for long processes of change.

Example / answer: By then, British politics will have been undergoing deep transformation for years.

Will have been experiencing

French: aura connu depuis

Rule / explanation: Useful for societies, economies and communities.

Example / answer: The country will have been experiencing demographic change for decades.

Will have been struggling with

French: aura lutté contre depuis

Rule / explanation: Useful for persistent problems.

Example / answer: Families will have been struggling with rising costs for years.

Will have been trying to

French: aura essayé de depuis

Rule / explanation: Shows repeated effort up to a future moment.

Example / answer: Policymakers will have been trying to restore trust for an entire parliamentary term.

G. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Forgetting been

French: oubli de been

Rule / explanation: The structure needs will have been + V-ing.

Example / answer: Wrong: will have working. Correct: will have been working.

Using it for completed results

French: résultat terminé

Rule / explanation: Use Future Perfect Simple for completed results or quantities.

Example / answer: Better: The government will have built new homes, not will have been building 100,000 homes.

Will after by the time

French: pas de will dans la subordonnée

Rule / explanation: Use Present Simple in future time clauses.

Example / answer: Wrong: By the time voters will decide. Correct: By the time voters decide.

Overusing the tense

French: emploi excessif

Rule / explanation: This tense is powerful but rare. Use it when duration really matters.

Example / answer: Simple future is often enough: The reform will affect families.
