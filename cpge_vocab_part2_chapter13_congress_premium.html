Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse the US Congress: the House of Representatives, the Senate, committees, hearings, oversight, the legislative process, budget fights, partisan gridlock, lobbying and campaign finance. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as congressional hearing, subpoena, filibuster, appropriations, government shutdown, divided government and partisan gridlock.
For oral exams Use this vocabulary to explain why the US legislative process can be slow, conflictual and vulnerable to party polarisation.
For essays Memorise the collocations: to pass a bill, to block legislation, to subpoena witnesses, to reach across the aisle, to lobby Congress.

A. Members, chambers and congressional leadership

Congress

IPA: /ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: KON-gress
French: Congrès américain

Definition: The national legislature of the United States, made up of the Senate and the House of Representatives.

Example: Congress has the power to make federal laws and approve government spending.

bicameral legislature

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈkæmərəl ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: bye-KAM-uh-ruhl LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: parlement bicaméral

Definition: A legislature with two chambers or houses.

Example: Congress is a bicameral legislature.

House of Representatives

IPA: /ˌhaʊs əv ˌreprɪˈzentətɪvz/
Pronunciation: howss ov rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tivz
French: Chambre des représentants

Definition: The lower chamber of the US Congress, with members elected by congressional district.

Example: The House of Representatives has 435 voting members.

Senate

IPA: /ˈsenət/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht
French: Sénat

Definition: The upper chamber of the US Congress, with two senators from each state.

Example: The Senate confirms many presidential appointments.

representative

IPA: /ˌreprɪˈzentətɪv/
Pronunciation: rep-ri-ZEN-tuh-tiv
French: représentant ; élu de la Chambre

Definition: An elected member of the House of Representatives.

Example: The representative introduced a bill on healthcare.

senator

IPA: /ˈsenətə/
Pronunciation: SEN-uh-tuh
French: sénateur ; sénatrice

Definition: An elected member of the Senate.

Example: Each state elects two senators.

congressman

IPA: /ˈkɒŋɡresmən/
Pronunciation: KON-gress-muhn
French: membre masculin du Congrès

Definition: A male member of Congress, especially of the House of Representatives.

Example: The congressman supported the infrastructure bill.

congresswoman

IPA: /ˈkɒŋɡresˌwʊmən/
Pronunciation: KON-gress-woo-muhn
French: membre féminin du Congrès

Definition: A female member of Congress, especially of the House of Representatives.

Example: The congresswoman questioned the witness during the hearing.

member of Congress

IPA: /ˌmembər əv ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: MEM-buhr ov KON-gress
French: membre du Congrès

Definition: A senator or representative serving in the US Congress.

Example: Members of Congress often split their time between Washington and their home states.

congressional district

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈdɪstrɪkt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl DIS-trikt
French: circonscription congressionnelle

Definition: A geographical area that elects one member of the House of Representatives.

Example: Redistricting changed the boundaries of several congressional districts.

constituent

IPA: /kənˈstɪtjuənt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-STI-choo-uhnt
French: électeur d’une circonscription ; administré

Definition: A person represented by an elected official.

Example: The senator received thousands of emails from constituents.

congressional seat

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl siːt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl seet
French: siège au Congrès

Definition: A position held by a member of Congress.

Example: The party hopes to win several congressional seats.

term of office

IPA: /ˌtɜːm əv ˈɒfɪs/
Pronunciation: term ov OFF-is
French: mandat

Definition: The period during which an elected official serves.

Example: Representatives serve a two-year term of office.

re-election

IPA: /ˌriː ɪˈlekʃən/
Pronunciation: ree i-LEK-shuhn
French: réélection

Definition: The process of being elected again to the same office.

Example: Several senators are seeking re-election.

incumbent

IPA: /ɪnˈkʌmbənt/
Pronunciation: in-KUM-buhnt
French: sortant ; titulaire du poste

Definition: The person currently holding a political office.

Example: Incumbents often have an advantage in congressional races.

freshman lawmaker

IPA: /ˈfreʃmən ˈlɔːˌmeɪkə/
Pronunciation: FRESH-muhn LAW-may-kuh
French: nouvel élu au Congrès

Definition: A newly elected member of Congress serving their first term.

Example: The freshman lawmaker quickly gained media attention.

Speaker of the House

IPA: /ˌspiːkər əv ðə ˈhaʊs/
Pronunciation: SPEE-kuhr ov thuh howss
French: président de la Chambre des représentants

Definition: The presiding officer and political leader of the House of Representatives.

Example: The Speaker of the House controls much of the legislative agenda.

House majority leader

IPA: /ˌhaʊs məˈdʒɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: howss muh-JOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la majorité à la Chambre

Definition: The leader of the majority party in the House of Representatives.

Example: The House majority leader negotiated the final version of the bill.

House minority leader

IPA: /ˌhaʊs maɪˈnɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: howss my-NOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la minorité à la Chambre

Definition: The leader of the minority party in the House of Representatives.

Example: The House minority leader criticised the budget deal.

Senate majority leader

IPA: /ˌsenət məˈdʒɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht muh-JOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la majorité au Sénat

Definition: The leader of the majority party in the Senate.

Example: The Senate majority leader decides which bills reach the floor.

Senate minority leader

IPA: /ˌsenət maɪˈnɒrəti ˈliːdə/
Pronunciation: SEN-uht my-NOR-uh-tee LEE-duh
French: chef de la minorité au Sénat

Definition: The leader of the minority party in the Senate.

Example: The Senate minority leader opposed the nomination.

party caucus

IPA: /ˈpɑːti ˈkɔːkəs/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee KAW-kuhs
French: groupe parlementaire d’un parti

Definition: A group of lawmakers from the same party who meet to discuss strategy.

Example: The party caucus met before the vote.

congressional caucus

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈkɔːkəs/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl KAW-kuhs
French: caucus parlementaire ; groupe au Congrès

Definition: A group of members of Congress organised around a party, identity, region or issue.

Example: The Congressional Black Caucus pushed for police reform.

bipartisan group

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːtɪzən ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: bye-PAR-ti-zuhn group
French: groupe bipartisan ; groupe transpartisan

Definition: A group including members of both major parties.

Example: A bipartisan group introduced a compromise bill.

B. Committees, hearings and oversight

committee

IPA: /kəˈmɪti/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission ; comité

Definition: A smaller group of lawmakers that examines bills, policies or investigations.

Example: The committee held hearings on social media regulation.

standing committee

IPA: /ˈstændɪŋ kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: STAN-ding kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission permanente

Definition: A permanent congressional committee dealing with a specific policy area.

Example: The standing committee reviewed the defence budget.

select committee

IPA: /sɪˈlekt kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: si-LEKT kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission spéciale

Definition: A temporary or special committee created for a specific investigation or issue.

Example: The select committee investigated the attack on the Capitol.

joint committee

IPA: /ˌdʒɔɪnt kəˈmɪti/
Pronunciation: joynt kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission mixte

Definition: A committee including members from both the House and the Senate.

Example: A joint committee examined economic forecasts.

conference committee

IPA: /ˈkɒnfərəns kəˌmɪti/
Pronunciation: KON-fuh-ruhns kuh-MIT-ee
French: commission de conciliation

Definition: A temporary committee that reconciles different House and Senate versions of a bill.

Example: The conference committee produced a compromise version of the bill.

committee chair

IPA: /kəˈmɪti tʃeə/
Pronunciation: kuh-MIT-ee chair
French: président de commission

Definition: The lawmaker who leads a committee.

Example: The committee chair scheduled the hearing.

ranking member

IPA: /ˈræŋkɪŋ ˈmembə/
Pronunciation: RANK-ing MEM-buh
French: membre le plus haut placé de la minorité

Definition: The most senior member of the minority party on a committee.

Example: The ranking member issued a separate statement.

hearing

IPA: /ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: HEER-ing
French: audition

Definition: A formal session where lawmakers hear evidence or question witnesses.

Example: The hearing focused on data privacy.

congressional hearing

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈhɪərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl HEER-ing
French: audition au Congrès

Definition: A hearing organised by Congress to gather information or scrutinise officials.

Example: The congressional hearing was broadcast live.

witness

IPA: /ˈwɪtnəs/
Pronunciation: WIT-nuhs
French: témoin

Definition: A person who gives evidence before a committee or court.

Example: The witness described pressure from the White House.

testimony

IPA: /ˈtestɪməni/
Pronunciation: TES-ti-muh-nee
French: témoignage ; déposition

Definition: A formal statement given by a witness.

Example: Her testimony contradicted earlier accounts.

subpoena

IPA: /səˈpiːnə/
Pronunciation: suh-PEE-nuh
French: assignation ; citation à comparaître

Definition: A legal order requiring someone to provide documents or testify.

Example: The committee issued a subpoena for documents.

to subpoena

IPA: /səˈpiːnə/
Pronunciation: suh-PEE-nuh
French: assigner ; citer à comparaître

Definition: To order someone legally to provide evidence or appear before a committee or court.

Example: Congress voted to subpoena former officials.

oversight

IPA: /ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle institutionnel ; surveillance

Definition: The monitoring of government actions by Congress or another institution.

Example: Congressional oversight can expose abuses of power.

congressional oversight

IPA: /kənˈɡreʃənəl ˈəʊvəsaɪt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-GRESH-uh-nuhl OH-vuh-syte
French: contrôle du Congrès

Definition: The power of Congress to monitor the executive branch.

Example: Congressional oversight is a key check on the presidency.

scrutiny

IPA: /ˈskruːtəni/
Pronunciation: SKROO-tuh-nee
French: examen minutieux ; contrôle

Definition: Careful examination of decisions, policies or actions.

Example: The policy came under intense congressional scrutiny.

bill

IPA: /bɪl/
Pronunciation: bil
French: projet de loi

Definition: A proposed law debated and voted on by Congress.

Example: The bill was introduced in the House.

C. Law-making procedures and voting rules

legislation

IPA: /ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: législation

Definition: Laws or the process of making laws.

Example: Congress passed major civil rights legislation in the 1960s.

law-making process

IPA: /ˈlɔː meɪkɪŋ ˈprəʊses/
Pronunciation: LAW-may-king PROH-sess
French: processus législatif

Definition: The steps through which a bill becomes law.

Example: The law-making process can be slow and complex.

draft bill

IPA: /ˌdrɑːft ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: draft bil
French: avant-projet de loi

Definition: An early version of a proposed law.

Example: The draft bill was criticised by civil liberties groups.

sponsor

IPA: /ˈspɒnsə/
Pronunciation: SPON-suh
French: auteur ; parrain d’un projet de loi

Definition: A lawmaker who formally introduces or supports a bill.

Example: The bill’s sponsor defended the measure.

co-sponsor

IPA: /ˌkəʊ ˈspɒnsə/
Pronunciation: koh SPON-suh
French: co-parrain ; cosignataire

Definition: A lawmaker who adds their support to a bill introduced by another member.

Example: The proposal attracted dozens of co-sponsors.

amendment

IPA: /əˈmendmənt/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND-muhnt
French: amendement

Definition: A proposed change to a bill or law.

Example: The Senate adopted an amendment on border security.

markup

IPA: /ˈmɑːkʌp/
Pronunciation: MARK-up
French: examen et modification d’un projet en commission

Definition: A committee session where lawmakers revise and amend a bill.

Example: The bill changed significantly during markup.

floor debate

IPA: /ˌflɔː dɪˈbeɪt/
Pronunciation: floor di-BAYT
French: débat en séance plénière

Definition: A debate involving all members of a chamber before a vote.

Example: The floor debate lasted several hours.

floor vote

IPA: /ˌflɔː ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: floor voht
French: vote en séance plénière

Definition: A vote by the full House or Senate.

Example: The floor vote was delayed until Friday.

roll-call vote

IPA: /ˌrəʊl kɔːl ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: rohl-call voht
French: vote nominatif

Definition: A recorded vote showing how each lawmaker voted.

Example: The roll-call vote revealed divisions within the party.

voice vote

IPA: /ˈvɔɪs vəʊt/
Pronunciation: voyss voht
French: vote oral

Definition: A vote in which members say yes or no aloud and the result is judged by volume.

Example: The bill passed by voice vote.

majority vote

IPA: /məˈdʒɒrəti vəʊt/
Pronunciation: muh-JOR-uh-tee voht
French: vote à la majorité

Definition: A vote requiring more than half of those voting to approve something.

Example: Most bills pass by majority vote.

supermajority

IPA: /ˌsuːpəməˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: SOO-puh-muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: supermajorité ; majorité qualifiée

Definition: A majority larger than a simple majority, often two-thirds or three-fifths.

Example: Overriding a veto requires a supermajority.

simple majority

IPA: /ˌsɪmpəl məˈdʒɒrəti/
Pronunciation: SIM-puhl muh-JOR-uh-tee
French: majorité simple

Definition: More than half of the votes cast.

Example: A simple majority was enough to pass the bill.

cloture

IPA: /ˈkləʊtʃə/
Pronunciation: KLOH-chuh
French: clôture des débats au Sénat

Definition: A Senate procedure used to end debate and overcome a filibuster.

Example: The Senate failed to reach the votes needed for cloture.

filibuster

IPA: /ˈfɪlɪbʌstə/
Pronunciation: FIL-i-bus-tuh
French: obstruction parlementaire

Definition: A tactic used in the Senate to delay or block a vote through prolonged debate or procedural rules.

Example: The filibuster made it hard to pass voting rights legislation.

reconciliation

IPA: /ˌrekənsɪliˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rek-uhn-sil-ee-AY-shuhn
French: procédure de réconciliation budgétaire

Definition: A special budget procedure in Congress that allows some bills to pass the Senate with a simple majority.

Example: The party used reconciliation to pass parts of its economic agenda.

conference report

IPA: /ˈkɒnfərəns rɪˌpɔːt/
Pronunciation: KON-fuh-ruhns ri-PORT
French: rapport de conférence ; texte de compromis

Definition: The final compromise text produced by a conference committee.

Example: Both chambers approved the conference report.

to introduce a bill

IPA: /ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: in-truh-DYOOSS uh bil
French: présenter un projet de loi

Definition: To formally present a proposed law in Congress.

Example: A senator introduced a bill on gun safety.

to pass a bill

IPA: /pɑːs ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: pass uh bil
French: adopter un projet de loi

Definition: To approve a proposed law through a vote.

Example: The House passed the bill with bipartisan support.

to amend a bill

IPA: /əˈmend ə ˈbɪl/
Pronunciation: uh-MEND uh bil
French: amender un projet de loi

Definition: To change a proposed law.

Example: Lawmakers amended the bill in committee.

to block legislation

IPA: /blɒk ˌledʒɪsˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: blok lej-is-LAY-shuhn
French: bloquer une loi

Definition: To prevent a law from passing.

Example: A filibuster can block legislation in the Senate.

to send a bill to the president

IPA: /send ə ˈbɪl tə ðə ˈprezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: send uh bil tuh thuh PREZ-i-duhnt
French: transmettre un projet de loi au président

Definition: To pass a bill through Congress so that the president can sign or veto it.

Example: Congress sent the bill to the president.

appropriations

IPA: /əˌprəʊpriˈeɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: uh-proh-pree-AY-shuhnz
French: crédits budgétaires

Definition: Money formally allocated by Congress for government spending.

Example: The appropriations bill funded federal agencies.

budget resolution

IPA: /ˈbʌdʒɪt ˌrezəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: BUJ-it rez-uh-LOO-shuhn
French: résolution budgétaire

Definition: A congressional framework setting overall spending and revenue goals.

Example: The budget resolution set spending targets.

D. Budget, gridlock and party politics

spending bill

IPA: /ˈspendɪŋ bɪl/
Pronunciation: SPEN-ding bil
French: loi de finances ; texte de dépenses

Definition: A bill authorising government expenditure.

Example: The spending bill prevented a shutdown.

debt ceiling

IPA: /ˈdet ˌsiːlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: det SEE-ling
French: plafond de la dette

Definition: A legal limit on how much the federal government can borrow.

Example: A fight over the debt ceiling threatened financial turmoil.

government shutdown

IPA: /ˈɡʌvənmənt ˈʃʌtdaʊn/
Pronunciation: GUV-uhn-muhnt SHUT-down
French: fermeture partielle de l’administration fédérale

Definition: A situation in which parts of the federal government stop operating because funding has not been approved.

Example: The budget dispute caused a government shutdown.

pork-barrel spending

IPA: /ˈpɔːk bærəl ˈspendɪŋ/
Pronunciation: PORK BAR-uhl SPEN-ding
French: dépenses clientélistes ; financement local opportuniste

Definition: Government spending directed to local projects to please voters or lawmakers.

Example: Critics denounced the bill as pork-barrel spending.

earmark

IPA: /ˈɪəmɑːk/
Pronunciation: EER-mark
French: affectation budgétaire spécifique

Definition: Money set aside in a bill for a specific local project.

Example: The senator defended the earmark for a bridge project.

tax bill

IPA: /ˈtæks bɪl/
Pronunciation: taks bil
French: projet de loi fiscal

Definition: A proposed law dealing with taxation.

Example: The tax bill favoured high-income households.

budget deadlock

IPA: /ˈbʌdʒɪt ˈdedlɒk/
Pronunciation: BUJ-it DED-lok
French: blocage budgétaire

Definition: A situation in which lawmakers cannot agree on a budget.

Example: Budget deadlock raised fears of a shutdown.

gridlock

IPA: /ˈɡrɪdlɒk/
Pronunciation: GRID-lok
French: blocage institutionnel

Definition: A political situation in which institutions cannot make decisions because of disagreement.

Example: Partisan gridlock prevented major reforms.

partisan gridlock

IPA: /ˈpɑːtɪzən ˈɡrɪdlɒk/
Pronunciation: PAR-ti-zuhn GRID-lok
French: blocage partisan

Definition: Institutional blockage caused by intense party conflict.

Example: Partisan gridlock has become common in Washington.

divided government

IPA: /dɪˌvaɪdɪd ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: di-VY-did GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement divisé

Definition: A situation in which different parties control the presidency and at least one chamber of Congress.

Example: Divided government often leads to compromise or gridlock.

unified government

IPA: /ˌjuːnɪfaɪd ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: YOO-ni-fyd GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement unifié

Definition: A situation in which one party controls the presidency and both chambers of Congress.

Example: Unified government makes it easier to pass legislation.

bipartisan support

IPA: /ˌbaɪˈpɑːtɪzən səˈpɔːt/
Pronunciation: bye-PAR-ti-zuhn suh-PORT
French: soutien bipartisan ; soutien transpartisan

Definition: Support from both major parties.

Example: The bill received rare bipartisan support.

party-line vote

IPA: /ˌpɑːti laɪn ˈvəʊt/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee line voht
French: vote selon les lignes partisanes

Definition: A vote in which lawmakers vote according to party affiliation.

Example: The reform passed on a party-line vote.

whip count

IPA: /ˈwɪp kaʊnt/
Pronunciation: wip kownt
French: décompte des voix par les whips

Definition: An estimate of how lawmakers are likely to vote.

Example: The whip count suggested the bill would fail.

to reach across the aisle

IPA: /riːtʃ əˌkrɒs ði ˈaɪl/
Pronunciation: reech uh-kross thee aisle
French: coopérer avec l’autre camp

Definition: To cooperate with members of the opposing party.

Example: The senator tried to reach across the aisle.

to break ranks

IPA: /breɪk ˈræŋks/
Pronunciation: brayk ranks
French: rompre avec la ligne du parti

Definition: To refuse to follow one’s party position.

Example: Several Republicans broke ranks and supported the bill.

party discipline

IPA: /ˈpɑːti ˈdɪsəplɪn/
Pronunciation: PAR-tee DIS-uh-plin
French: discipline de parti

Definition: The pressure on lawmakers to vote with their party.

Example: Party discipline is weaker in the US Congress than in many parliaments.

lobbyist

IPA: /ˈlɒbiɪst/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee-ist
French: lobbyiste

Definition: A person paid to influence lawmakers or government decisions.

Example: Lobbyists contacted senators before the vote.

special interest group

IPA: /ˌspeʃəl ˈɪntrəst ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: SPESH-uhl IN-truhst group
French: groupe d’intérêt particulier

Definition: A group seeking influence for a specific cause, industry or profession.

Example: Special interest groups spent heavily on the campaign.

campaign donation

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn dəʊˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN doh-NAY-shuhn
French: don de campagne

Definition: Money given to support a political campaign.

Example: Campaign donations can give donors access to lawmakers.

PAC

IPA: /ˌpiː eɪ ˈsiː/
Pronunciation: pee ay see
French: PAC ; comité d’action politique

Definition: A political action committee that raises and spends money to support or oppose candidates.

Example: The PAC funded ads in several congressional races.

E. Lobbying, money and high-value congressional verbs

Super PAC

IPA: /ˈsuːpə pæk/
Pronunciation: SOO-puh pak
French: Super PAC

Definition: A political committee that can spend unlimited money independently of candidates.

Example: Super PAC spending has increased the cost of elections.

to lobby Congress

IPA: /ˈlɒbi ˈkɒŋɡres/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee KON-gress
French: faire pression sur le Congrès

Definition: To try to influence members of Congress or congressional policy.

Example: Tech companies lobbied Congress over data regulation.

to fund a campaign

IPA: /fʌnd ə kæmˈpeɪn/
Pronunciation: fund uh kam-PAYN
French: financer une campagne

Definition: To provide money for an electoral campaign.

Example: Wealthy donors helped fund the campaign.

to shape the agenda

IPA: /ʃeɪp ði əˈdʒendə/
Pronunciation: shayp thee uh-JEN-duh
French: façonner l’agenda politique

Definition: To influence which issues are discussed or prioritised.

Example: Committee chairs can shape the agenda.

to hold lawmakers accountable

IPA: /həʊld ˈlɔːˌmeɪkəz əˈkaʊntəbəl/
Pronunciation: hohld LAW-may-kuhz uh-KOWN-tuh-buhl
French: demander des comptes aux élus

Definition: To make elected officials answer for their decisions.

Example: Voters can hold lawmakers accountable at the next election.
