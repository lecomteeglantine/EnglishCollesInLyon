Part 10 — Concours Toolkit | Chapter 80 — Essential Verbs for Essays
High-value academic verbs for essays, khôlles, syntheses and press article analysis.

Chapter objective

This premium toolkit chapter gives CPGE students the essential verbs needed to write precise, analytical and elegant essays in English: verbs for presenting arguments, analysing evidence, criticising, defending, proposing, explaining causes and consequences, comparing, nuancing and concluding. The goal is to replace vague verbs such as talk about, say or show with sharper academic language.

How to use this chapter

For essaysUse these verbs to structure argumentation: argue, contend, demonstrate, challenge, undermine, exacerbate, mitigate, acknowledge, concede and qualify.
For khôllesUse them orally to sound analytical rather than descriptive: “The article highlights...”, “This raises questions about...”, “This example illustrates...”
For press reviewsAvoid saying “the text talks about.” Instead: the article examines, the author argues, the report reveals, the cartoon depicts, the figures suggest.

A. Presenting an argument

to argue

IPA: /ˈɑːɡjuː/
Pronunciation: AR-gyoo
French: soutenir ; faire valoir

Definition: To present reasons supporting a point of view.

Example: The author argues that democracy is under pressure.

to claim

IPA: /kleɪm/
Pronunciation: klaym
French: affirmer ; prétendre

Definition: To state that something is true, sometimes without proving it fully.

Example: The article claims that social media has changed political debate.

to assert

IPA: /əˈsɜːt/
Pronunciation: uh-SURT
French: affirmer avec force

Definition: To state something firmly and confidently.

Example: The columnist asserts that reform is urgently needed.

to contend

IPA: /kənˈtend/
Pronunciation: kuhn-TEND

Definition: To argue or maintain a position, especially in formal writing.

Example: Critics contend that the policy will increase inequality.

to maintain

IPA: /meɪnˈteɪn/
Pronunciation: mayn-TAYN
French: soutenir ; maintenir

Definition: To continue to argue that something is true.

Example: The government maintains that the reform is fair.

to suggest

IPA: /səˈdʒest/
Pronunciation: suh-JEST
French: suggérer

Definition: To make an argument more subtle and balanced.

Example: A good essay nuances the opposition between freedom and security.

to conclude

IPA: /kənˈkluːd/
Pronunciation: kuhn-KLOOD
French: conclure

Definition: To end by reaching a final judgement.

Example: We may conclude that reform is necessary but insufficient.
