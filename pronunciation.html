Make your English easy to follow.

You do not need to erase your accent. You need clear sounds, meaningful stress, natural rhythm and an intonation pattern that helps the listener follow your ideas.

Watch a short model, complete a focused task, imitate it, record yourself and then reuse the skill in class and in your colles.

02
🦷 SoundPriority

Master the two TH sounds

Focus: Distinguish /θ/ in think from /ð/ in this, without replacing them with /s/, /z/, /f/ or /v/.

Your goal: Produce both sounds clearly in words and connected speech.

Before watching

Place the tip of your tongue lightly between your teeth. Let the air pass; add voice only for /ð/.

While watching

Sort the examples into two columns: think / this. Repeat each group twice.

After watching

Say the challenge sentence slowly, then naturally: “Those three thinkers thought thoroughly.”

📡 SoundFoundation

Hear and produce minimal pairs

Focus: Train your ear before trying to change your pronunciation.

Your goal: Differentiate short and long vowels in high-frequency words.

Before watching

Say ship and sheep. Notice whether the vowel length and mouth position really change.

While watching

Keep a tally: write S for a short vowel and L for a long vowel before checking.

After watching

Create one sentence containing both words from a pair.

🗣️ RhythmEssential

Make key words stand out

Focus: Stress content words and reduce less important grammatical words.

Your goal: Make your argument easier to follow by highlighting meaning.

Before watching

Underline only the words that carry the message: “The government has introduced a new policy.”

While watching

Notice which words become longer, clearer and slightly louder.

After watching

06
🌱 RhythmIntermediate

Use the schwa naturally

Focus: Reduce unstressed vowels to /ə/ instead of pronouncing every written vowel fully.

Your goal: Sound less syllable-by-syllable and more natural.

Before watching

Compare the first vowel in about with the strong vowel in cat.

While watching

Circle every weak vowel in the video examples.

After watching

Practise the phrase “a matter of opinion” without giving equal weight to every syllable.

07
🪶 RhythmIntermediate

Reduce grammar words

Focus: Use weak forms for words such as to, of, for, can and have when they are not contrasted.

Your goal: Create a clearer contrast between key words and supporting words.

Before watching

Say “I can explain it” and “Yes, I CAN.” The second can must stay strong.

While watching

Write the weak words in lower case and the stressed words in CAPITALS.

After watching

Read a sentence from your latest presentation twice: once mechanically, then with weak forms.

08
🔗 RhythmIntermediate

Link words into thought groups

Focus: Connect a final consonant to the next vowel and avoid unnecessary pauses.

Your goal: Speak in meaningful chunks rather than isolated words.

Before watching

Join the sounds in “an‿important‿issue” and “take‿a look”.

While watching

Mark each link with ‿ and each real thought-group boundary with /.

After watching

Shadow one ten-second extract three times without reading.

🌊 RhythmAdvanced

Understand connected speech

Focus: Bring linking, elision, assimilation, weak forms and rhythm together.

Your goal: Recognise why authentic English sounds different from a written transcript.

Before watching

Listen to one short English clip without subtitles and write only the stressed words.

While watching

Identify one example each of linking, reduction and a sound that disappears.

After watching

Replay at normal speed and shadow the complete phrase, not each individual word.

11
🏁 SoundFoundation

Keep grammatical endings audible

Focus: Pronounce past-tense endings as /t/, /d/ or /ɪd/ without adding an extra syllable everywhere.

Your goal: Make tense and meaning audible during spontaneous speech.

Before watching

Sort worked, played and wanted into three different ending groups.

While watching

Notice the final sound of the verb, not its spelling.

After watching

Describe yesterday’s news using five regular past-tense verbs.

12
🎬 PracticeAll levels

Train with shadowing

Focus: Listen, imitate immediately and copy rhythm, reductions, linking and pitch as one complete pattern.

Your goal: Move pronunciation skills from controlled exercises into fluent speech.

Before watching

Choose headphones and stand or sit upright. Do not read the transcript on the first attempt.

While watching

Complete three rounds: listen only, whisper-shadow, then full-voice shadow.

After watching

Record the final round and compare rhythm rather than judging your accent.
