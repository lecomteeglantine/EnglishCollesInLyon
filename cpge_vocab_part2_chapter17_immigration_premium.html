Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse immigration in the United States: borders, legal status, asylum, refugees, deportation, detention, DACA, Dreamers, immigration reform, labour needs, national identity, xenophobia and the politics of border control. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

A. Core immigration vocabulary and legal status

immigration

IPA: /ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: immigration

Definition: The movement of people into a country to live, work or settle there.

Example: Immigration has long been central to American identity and political debate.

immigrant

IPA: /ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: IM-i-gruhnt
French: immigré ; immigrée

Definition: A person who moves to another country to live there.

Example: Many immigrants come to the United States in search of work, safety or opportunity.

migrant

IPA: /ˈmaɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: MY-gruhnt
French: migrant ; migrante

Definition: A person who moves from one place to another, often for work, safety or better living conditions.

Example: Migrants often face dangerous journeys and uncertain legal status.

migration

IPA: /maɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: my-GRAY-shuhn
French: migration

Definition: The movement of people from one place to another.

Example: Migration is driven by war, poverty, climate change and economic opportunity.

foreign-born

IPA: /ˌfɒrən ˈbɔːn/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn born
French: né à l’étranger

Definition: Born in another country.

Example: The foreign-born population has increased in several states.

first-generation immigrant

IPA: /ˌfɜːst dʒenəˈreɪʃən ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: first jen-uh-RAY-shuhn IM-i-gruhnt
French: immigré de première génération

Definition: A person who was born abroad and immigrated to another country.

Example: First-generation immigrants often face language barriers.

second-generation immigrant

IPA: /ˌsekənd dʒenəˈreɪʃən ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: SEK-uhnd jen-uh-RAY-shuhn IM-i-gruhnt
French: enfant d’immigrés ; immigré de deuxième génération

Definition: A person born in a country to immigrant parents.

Example: Second-generation immigrants often navigate two cultural identities.

diaspora

IPA: /daɪˈæspərə/
Pronunciation: dy-AS-puh-ruh
French: diaspora

Definition: A community of people living outside their ancestral homeland.

Example: The Mexican diaspora has strong cultural and economic ties with Mexico.

border

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh
French: frontière

Definition: The line separating two countries or political territories.

Example: The US-Mexico border is a major focus of immigration policy.

border security

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə sɪˌkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité aux frontières

Definition: Measures used to control and monitor a country’s borders.

Example: Border security is a key demand of many conservative politicians.

border control

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə kənˌtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh kuhn-TROHL
French: contrôle des frontières

Definition: The regulation of who enters and leaves a country.

Example: The government promised tougher border control.

border patrol

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə pəˌtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh puh-TROHL
French: patrouille frontalière

Definition: Officers responsible for monitoring and policing a country’s borders.

Example: Border Patrol agents detained migrants near the crossing.

border wall

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə wɔːl/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh wall
French: mur frontalier

Definition: A physical barrier built along a border to prevent unauthorised crossing.

Example: The border wall became a powerful political symbol.

border crossing

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə ˈkrɒsɪŋ/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh KROSS-ing
French: poste-frontière ; point de passage

Definition: An official place where people may enter or leave a country.

Example: The border crossing was closed after the incident.

undocumented immigrant

IPA: /ʌnˈdɒkjʊmentɪd ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: un-DOK-yuh-men-tid IM-i-gruhnt
French: sans-papiers ; immigré en situation irrégulière

Definition: An immigrant who does not have legal authorisation to live in a country.

Example: Undocumented immigrants often work in low-paid and insecure jobs.

illegal immigrant

IPA: /ɪˈliːɡəl ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: i-LEE-guhl IM-i-gruhnt
French: immigré illégal ; terme controversé

Definition: A controversial term for a person living in a country without legal permission.

Example: The term illegal immigrant is often criticised as dehumanising.

unauthorised immigrant

IPA: /ʌnˈɔːθəraɪzd ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: un-AW-thuh-ryzd IM-i-gruhnt
French: immigré non autorisé ; en situation irrégulière

Definition: A person living in a country without official authorisation.

Example: Unauthorised immigrants may fear reporting crimes.

legal immigrant

IPA: /ˈliːɡəl ˈɪmɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: LEE-guhl IM-i-gruhnt
French: immigré légal ; immigré régulier

Definition: A person who has official permission to live in a country.

Example: Legal immigrants can often apply for permanent residency.

lawful permanent resident

IPA: /ˈlɔːfəl ˈpɜːmənənt ˈrezɪdənt/
Pronunciation: LAW-fuhl PUR-muh-nuhnt REZ-i-duhnt
French: résident permanent légal

Definition: A non-citizen legally allowed to live permanently in the United States.

Example: A lawful permanent resident may later apply for citizenship.

green card

IPA: /ˈɡriːn kɑːd/
Pronunciation: green card
French: carte verte

Definition: A document giving a non-citizen permanent resident status in the United States.

Example: A green card allows immigrants to live and work permanently in the US.

visa

IPA: /ˈviːzə/
Pronunciation: VEE-zuh
French: visa

Definition: An official document allowing a person to enter, stay or work in a country for a specific purpose or period.

Example: Students need a visa to study in the United States.

work visa

IPA: /ˈwɜːk ˌviːzə/
Pronunciation: work VEE-zuh
French: visa de travail

Definition: A visa allowing a person to work legally in a country.

Example: Many technology companies rely on skilled workers with work visas.

asylum

IPA: /əˈsaɪləm/
Pronunciation: uh-SY-luhm
French: asile

Definition: Protection granted to someone fleeing persecution in their home country.

Example: The family applied for asylum after fleeing political violence.

asylum seeker

IPA: /əˈsaɪləm ˌsiːkə/
Pronunciation: uh-SY-luhm SEE-kuh
French: demandeur d’asile

Definition: A person who has requested protection as a refugee but whose claim has not yet been decided.

Example: Asylum seekers often wait months or years for a decision.

refugee

IPA: /ˌrefjʊˈdʒiː/
Pronunciation: REF-yoo-JEE
French: réfugié ; réfugiée

Definition: A person forced to leave their country because of war, persecution or violence.

Example: Refugees are protected under international law.

persecution

IPA: /ˌpɜːsɪˈkjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: pur-si-KYOO-shuhn
French: persécution

Definition: Mistreatment or violence against people because of identity, beliefs or politics.

Example: Victims of persecution may qualify for asylum.

humanitarian protection

IPA: /hjuːˌmænɪˈteəriən prəˈtekʃən/
Pronunciation: hyoo-man-i-TAIR-ee-uhn pruh-TEK-shuhn
French: protection humanitaire

Definition: Protection granted for humanitarian reasons to people at risk.

Example: Humanitarian protection is often debated during refugee crises.

resettlement

IPA: /ˌriːˈsetəlmənt/
Pronunciation: ree-SET-uhl-muhnt
French: réinstallation

Definition: The process of moving refugees to a safe country where they can rebuild their lives.

Example: Refugee resettlement programmes require international cooperation.

B. Asylum, refugees and legal pathways

family reunification

IPA: /ˌfæməli riːˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FAM-uh-lee ree-yoo-ni-fi-KAY-shuhn
French: regroupement familial

Definition: The process by which family members are allowed to join relatives in another country.

Example: Family reunification is a major pathway for legal immigration.

chain migration

IPA: /ˈtʃeɪn maɪˌɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: chayn my-GRAY-shuhn
French: migration en chaîne ; terme politisé

Definition: A politicised term for immigration through family sponsorship networks.

Example: Critics use the term chain migration to attack family-based immigration.

family-based immigration

IPA: /ˈfæməli beɪst ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FAM-uh-lee bayst im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: immigration familiale

Definition: Immigration based on sponsorship by family members already living in the country.

Example: Family-based immigration has long been part of US immigration policy.

employment-based immigration

IPA: /ɪmˈplɔɪmənt beɪst ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: im-PLOY-muhnt bayst im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: immigration professionnelle

Definition: Immigration based on work, skills or employer sponsorship.

Example: Employment-based immigration is important for skilled sectors.

skilled worker

IPA: /ˌskɪld ˈwɜːkə/
Pronunciation: skild WUR-kuh
French: travailleur qualifié

Definition: A worker with specialised training, education or expertise.

Example: The technology sector relies on skilled workers from abroad.

seasonal worker

IPA: /ˌsiːzənəl ˈwɜːkə/
Pronunciation: SEE-zuh-nuhl WUR-kuh
French: travailleur saisonnier

Definition: A worker employed for a limited season, often in agriculture or tourism.

Example: Seasonal workers harvest crops across the country.

migrant labour

IPA: /ˈmaɪɡrənt ˈleɪbə/
Pronunciation: MY-gruhnt LAY-buh
French: main-d’œuvre immigrée

Definition: Work done by migrants, often in sectors with labour shortages.

Example: Migrant labour is crucial to the US food supply chain.

labour shortage

IPA: /ˈleɪbə ˈʃɔːtɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: LAY-buh SHOR-tij
French: pénurie de main-d’œuvre

Definition: A situation in which there are not enough workers for available jobs.

Example: Immigration can help address labour shortages.

remittances

IPA: /rɪˈmɪtənsɪz/
Pronunciation: ri-MIT-uhn-siz
French: transferts d’argent ; remises migratoires

Definition: Money sent by migrants to family or communities in their home country.

Example: Remittances support families and local economies abroad.

naturalisation

IPA: /ˌnætʃərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: nach-uh-ruh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: naturalisation

Definition: The process of becoming a citizen of another country.

Example: Naturalisation requires meeting legal and language requirements.

citizenship

IPA: /ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp/
Pronunciation: SIT-i-zuhn-ship
French: citoyenneté

Definition: The legal status of being a member of a country with rights and duties.

Example: Citizenship gives immigrants the right to vote.

birthright citizenship

IPA: /ˌbɜːθraɪt ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp/
Pronunciation: BURTH-ryt SIT-i-zuhn-ship
French: citoyenneté de naissance ; droit du sol

Definition: Citizenship granted to anyone born in a country, especially under the Fourteenth Amendment in the US.

Example: Birthright citizenship is often debated in US immigration politics.

DACA

IPA: /ˈdækə/
Pronunciation: DAK-uh
French: DACA

Definition: Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals, a programme protecting some undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children.

Example: DACA protects many Dreamers from deportation.

Dreamers

IPA: /ˈdriːməz/
Pronunciation: DREE-muhz
French: Dreamers ; jeunes sans-papiers arrivés enfants

Definition: Undocumented immigrants brought to the US as children, often associated with DACA.

Example: Dreamers grew up in the United States but lack full legal security.

deportation

IPA: /ˌdiːpɔːˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-por-TAY-shuhn
French: expulsion ; reconduite à la frontière

Definition: The forced removal of a non-citizen from a country.

Example: Deportation can separate families.

removal proceedings

IPA: /rɪˈmuːvəl prəˈsiːdɪŋz/
Pronunciation: ri-MOO-vuhl pruh-SEE-dingz
French: procédure d’expulsion

Definition: Legal proceedings to decide whether a non-citizen should be deported.

Example: The immigrant faced removal proceedings.

detention centre

IPA: /dɪˈtenʃən ˌsentə/
Pronunciation: di-TEN-shuhn SEN-tuh
French: centre de rétention ; centre de détention

Definition: A facility where migrants or asylum seekers are held by authorities.

Example: Conditions in detention centres have been criticised.

immigration detention

IPA: /ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən dɪˈtenʃən/
Pronunciation: im-i-GRAY-shuhn di-TEN-shuhn
French: rétention administrative liée à l’immigration

Definition: The detention of migrants by immigration authorities.

Example: Immigration detention has raised human rights concerns.

family separation

IPA: /ˌfæməli ˌsepəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FAM-uh-lee sep-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: séparation des familles

Definition: The separation of migrant children from their parents by authorities.

Example: Family separation sparked national outrage.

zero-tolerance policy

IPA: /ˌzɪərəʊ ˈtɒlərəns ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: ZEER-oh TOL-uh-ruhns POL-uh-see
French: politique de tolérance zéro

Definition: A strict enforcement policy with little or no discretion.

Example: The zero-tolerance policy led to family separations.

immigration court

IPA: /ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən kɔːt/
Pronunciation: im-i-GRAY-shuhn court
French: tribunal de l’immigration

Definition: A court that handles immigration cases such as deportation and asylum claims.

Example: Immigration courts face huge backlogs.

backlog

IPA: /ˈbæklɒɡ/
Pronunciation: BAK-log
French: arriéré ; accumulation de dossiers

Definition: A large number of cases waiting to be processed.

Example: The asylum backlog has grown rapidly.

due process

IPA: /ˌdjuː ˈprəʊses/
Pronunciation: dyoo PROH-sess
French: procédure régulière ; garanties procédurales

Definition: Fair legal procedures before the state restricts rights or removes someone.

Example: Migrants are entitled to due process in immigration courts.

C. Deportation, detention and enforcement

sanctuary city

IPA: /ˈsæŋktʃuəri ˈsɪti/
Pronunciation: SANK-choo-air-ee SIT-ee
French: ville sanctuaire

Definition: A city that limits cooperation with federal immigration enforcement.

Example: Sanctuary cities are often criticised by immigration hardliners.

sanctuary policy

IPA: /ˈsæŋktʃuəri ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: SANK-choo-air-ee POL-uh-see
French: politique de ville sanctuaire

Definition: A policy limiting local cooperation with federal immigration enforcement.

Example: The sanctuary policy protected undocumented residents.

ICE

IPA: /ˌaɪ siː ˈiː/
Pronunciation: eye see ee
French: ICE ; agence américaine de contrôle de l’immigration

Definition: US Immigration and Customs Enforcement, the agency responsible for immigration enforcement inside the country.

Example: ICE raids have become politically controversial.

CBP

IPA: /ˌsiː biː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: see bee pee
French: CBP ; douanes et protection des frontières

Definition: US Customs and Border Protection, the agency responsible for border security and customs.

Example: CBP officers patrol the border and inspect ports of entry.

immigration enforcement

IPA: /ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən ɪnˈfɔːsmənt/
Pronunciation: im-i-GRAY-shuhn in-FORSS-muhnt
French: application des lois sur l’immigration

Definition: The implementation of immigration laws by authorities.

Example: Immigration enforcement increased after the policy change.

raid

IPA: /reɪd/
Pronunciation: rayd
French: descente ; raid

Definition: A sudden operation by authorities to arrest or inspect people.

Example: ICE raids created fear in immigrant communities.

to deport

IPA: /dɪˈpɔːt/
Pronunciation: di-PORT
French: expulser

Definition: To force a non-citizen to leave a country.

Example: The administration promised to deport more undocumented immigrants.

to detain

IPA: /dɪˈteɪn/
Pronunciation: di-TAYN
French: détenir ; placer en rétention

Definition: To hold someone in custody.

Example: Border authorities detained migrants near the crossing.

to grant asylum

IPA: /ɡrɑːnt əˈsaɪləm/
Pronunciation: grahnt uh-SY-luhm
French: accorder l’asile

Definition: To give legal protection to a person fleeing persecution.

Example: The judge granted asylum to the applicant.

to seek asylum

IPA: /siːk əˈsaɪləm/
Pronunciation: seek uh-SY-luhm
French: demander l’asile

Definition: To request protection in another country because of persecution or danger.

Example: The family crossed the border to seek asylum.

to process claims

IPA: /ˈprəʊses kleɪmz/
Pronunciation: PROH-sess klaymz
French: traiter des demandes

Definition: To examine and decide legal applications or requests.

Example: The government struggled to process asylum claims.

to tighten immigration rules

IPA: /ˈtaɪtən ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən ruːlz/
Pronunciation: TY-tuhn im-i-GRAY-shuhn roolz
French: durcir les règles d’immigration

Definition: To make immigration rules stricter.

Example: The administration moved to tighten immigration rules.

to secure the border

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊə ðə ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR thuh BOR-duh
French: sécuriser la frontière

Definition: To strengthen border control and prevent unauthorised crossings.

Example: Conservatives argued that the government had failed to secure the border.

to crack down on immigration

IPA: /ˈkræk daʊn ɒn ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: KRAK down on im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: durcir la répression de l’immigration

Definition: To take strict action against immigration, especially unauthorised immigration.

Example: The government vowed to crack down on illegal immigration.

to regularise status

IPA: /ˈreɡjʊləraɪz ˈsteɪtəs/
Pronunciation: REG-yuh-luh-ryz STAY-tuhs
French: régulariser la situation

Definition: To give legal status to people who were previously undocumented.

Example: Advocates called for a path to regularise status.

pathway to citizenship

IPA: /ˈpɑːθweɪ tə ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp/
Pronunciation: PATH-way tuh SIT-i-zuhn-ship
French: voie vers la citoyenneté

Definition: A legal process allowing immigrants to eventually become citizens.

Example: Democrats supported a pathway to citizenship for Dreamers.

amnesty

IPA: /ˈæmnəsti/
Pronunciation: AM-nuh-stee
French: amnistie

Definition: A policy forgiving immigration violations and granting legal status, often controversial.

Example: Opponents denounced the proposal as amnesty.

comprehensive immigration reform

IPA: /ˌkɒmprɪˈhensɪv ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən rɪˈfɔːm/
Pronunciation: kom-pri-HEN-siv im-i-GRAY-shuhn ri-FORM
French: réforme globale de l’immigration

Definition: Broad reform addressing legal immigration, border security, undocumented migrants and enforcement.

Example: Comprehensive immigration reform has repeatedly failed in Congress.

merit-based immigration

IPA: /ˈmerɪt beɪst ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: MER-it bayst im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: immigration au mérite ; immigration choisie

Definition: An immigration system prioritising skills, qualifications or economic contribution.

Example: Supporters of merit-based immigration emphasise skills and productivity.

points-based system

IPA: /ˈpɔɪnts beɪst ˈsɪstəm/
Pronunciation: points bayst SIS-tuhm
French: système à points

Definition: An immigration system that awards points for skills, education, language or work experience.

Example: A points-based system favours highly qualified applicants.

border crisis

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə ˈkraɪsɪs/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh KRY-sis
French: crise à la frontière

Definition: A politically loaded term describing a sharp increase in border crossings or pressure on border systems.

Example: Republicans described the rise in crossings as a border crisis.

pull factor

IPA: /ˈpʊl ˌfæktə/
Pronunciation: pull FAK-tuh
French: facteur d’attraction

Definition: A condition attracting migrants to a destination country.

Example: Jobs and family ties are powerful pull factors.

push factor

IPA: /ˈpʊʃ ˌfæktə/
Pronunciation: push FAK-tuh
French: facteur de départ ; facteur répulsif

Definition: A condition forcing or encouraging people to leave their home country.

Example: Violence and poverty are major push factors.

economic migrant

IPA: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈmaɪɡrənt/
Pronunciation: ee-kuh-NOM-ik MY-gruhnt
French: migrant économique

Definition: A person who migrates primarily for economic opportunities.

Example: Economic migrants may not qualify for asylum.

human trafficking

IPA: /ˌhjuːmən ˈtræfɪkɪŋ/
Pronunciation: HYOO-muhn TRAF-i-king
French: traite des êtres humains

Definition: The illegal exploitation of people through force, fraud or coercion.

Example: Migrants can be vulnerable to human trafficking.

D. Reform, border politics and migration pressures

smuggling

IPA: /ˈsmʌɡlɪŋ/
Pronunciation: SMUG-ling
French: passeurs ; trafic illicite

Definition: Illegally helping people or goods cross borders, usually for money.

Example: Smuggling networks profit from restrictive border policies.

people smuggler

IPA: /ˈpiːpəl ˌsmʌɡlə/
Pronunciation: PEE-puhl SMUG-luh
French: passeur

Definition: A person who illegally helps migrants cross borders for payment.

Example: People smugglers often exploit desperate migrants.

xenophobia

IPA: /ˌzenəˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: zen-uh-FOH-bee-uh
French: xénophobie

Definition: Fear or hostility towards foreigners or immigrants.

Example: Anti-immigration rhetoric can fuel xenophobia.

nativism

IPA: /ˈneɪtɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: NAY-tiv-iz-uhm
French: nativisme ; préférence nationale

Definition: A political attitude favouring native-born citizens over immigrants.

Example: Nativism has shaped several periods of US immigration history.

anti-immigration rhetoric

IPA: /ˌænti ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən ˈretərɪk/
Pronunciation: AN-tee im-i-GRAY-shuhn RET-uh-rik
French: rhétorique anti-immigration

Definition: Political language presenting immigration as a threat.

Example: Anti-immigration rhetoric intensified during the campaign.

scapegoating

IPA: /ˈskeɪpɡəʊtɪŋ/
Pronunciation: SKAYP-goht-ing
French: désignation d’un bouc émissaire

Definition: Blaming a group unfairly for social or economic problems.

Example: Scapegoating immigrants can mobilise angry voters.

cultural assimilation

IPA: /ˌkʌltʃərəl əˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: KUL-chuh-ruhl uh-sim-i-LAY-shuhn
French: assimilation culturelle

Definition: The process by which immigrants adopt the culture of the host country.

Example: Debates about cultural assimilation often reveal fears about national identity.

integration

IPA: /ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: in-ti-GRAY-shuhn
French: intégration

Definition: The process by which immigrants become part of social, economic and political life.

Example: Language learning can support integration.

multiculturalism

IPA: /ˌmʌltiˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: MUL-tee-KUL-chuh-ruh-liz-uhm
French: multiculturalisme

Definition: A model in which different cultural groups coexist and are recognised within society.

Example: Multiculturalism is often contrasted with assimilation.

melting pot

IPA: /ˈmeltɪŋ pɒt/
Pronunciation: MEL-ting pot
French: creuset culturel ; melting pot

Definition: A metaphor describing a society where different cultures blend into one.

Example: The United States has often been described as a melting pot.

salad bowl

IPA: /ˈsæləd bəʊl/
Pronunciation: SAL-uhd bohl
French: saladier culturel

Definition: A metaphor for multicultural society where groups keep distinct identities while coexisting.

Example: The salad bowl metaphor emphasises diversity rather than assimilation.

American identity

IPA: /əˌmerɪkən aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: uh-MER-i-kuhn eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité américaine

Definition: The sense of belonging, values and narratives associated with being American.

Example: Immigration constantly reshapes American identity.

nation of immigrants

IPA: /ˌneɪʃən əv ˈɪmɪɡrənts/
Pronunciation: NAY-shuhn ov IM-i-gruhnts
French: nation d’immigrants

Definition: A phrase describing the United States as a country built by immigrants.

Example: The phrase nation of immigrants is central to American self-image.

to contribute to the economy

IPA: /kənˈtrɪbjuːt tə ði iˈkɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: kuhn-TRIB-yoot tuh thee i-KON-uh-mee
French: contribuer à l’économie

Definition: To add value to economic activity through work, taxes or entrepreneurship.

Example: Immigrants contribute to the economy as workers and business owners.

to fill labour gaps

IPA: /fɪl ˈleɪbə ɡæps/
Pronunciation: fil LAY-buh gaps
French: combler les pénuries de main-d’œuvre

Definition: To provide workers where there are labour shortages.

Example: Migrant workers fill labour gaps in agriculture and healthcare.

to fuel political polarisation

IPA: /ˈfjuːəl pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: FYOO-uhl puh-LIT-i-kuhl poh-luh-ry-ZAY-shuhn
French: alimenter la polarisation politique

Definition: To make political divisions worse.

Example: Immigration debates often fuel political polarisation.

to frame immigrants as a threat

IPA: /freɪm ˈɪmɪɡrənts æz ə θret/
Pronunciation: fraym IM-i-gruhnts az uh thret
French: présenter les immigrés comme une menace

Definition: To present immigrants as dangerous or harmful.

Example: Populist campaigns often frame immigrants as a threat.

to balance security and compassion

IPA: /ˈbæləns sɪˈkjʊərəti ənd kəmˈpæʃən/
Pronunciation: BAL-uhns si-KYOOR-uh-tee and kuhm-PASH-uhn
French: concilier sécurité et compassion

Definition: To try to combine border control with humanitarian values.

Example: Immigration policy must balance security and compassion.
