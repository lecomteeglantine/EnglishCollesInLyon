This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse NATO: collective defence, Article 5, deterrence, military readiness, burden-sharing, enlargement, the eastern flank, Russia, Ukraine, nuclear deterrence, hybrid threats, cyber defence, European strategic autonomy and the future of the transatlantic alliance.

A. Core NATO and alliance vocabulary

NATO

IPA: /ˈneɪtəʊ/
Pronunciation: NAY-toh
French: OTAN

Definition: The North Atlantic Treaty Organization, a military and political alliance between North American and European states.

Example: NATO is based on collective defence among its members.

North Atlantic Treaty Organization

IPA: /ˌnɔːθ ətˌlæntɪk ˈtriːti ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: north-at-LAN-tik TREE-tee or-guh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: Organisation du traité de l’Atlantique nord

Definition: The full name of NATO.

Example: The North Atlantic Treaty Organization was created after the Second World War.

alliance

IPA: /əˈlaɪəns/
Pronunciation: uh-LY-uhns
French: alliance

Definition: A formal relationship between states for cooperation, usually on security or defence.

Example: An alliance can deter aggression by pooling military strength.

military alliance

IPA: /ˌmɪlɪtəri əˈlaɪəns/
Pronunciation: MIL-i-tuh-ree uh-LY-uhns
French: alliance militaire

Definition: An alliance based on military cooperation and mutual defence.

Example: NATO is the world’s most powerful military alliance.

political alliance

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl əˈlaɪəns/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl uh-LY-uhns
French: alliance politique

Definition: An alliance based on shared political interests and commitments.

Example: NATO is also a political alliance built on consultation.

collective defence

IPA: /kəˌlektɪv dɪˈfens/
Pronunciation: kuh-LEK-tiv di-FENS
French: défense collective

Definition: The principle that an attack on one member is treated as an attack on all.

Example: Collective defence is NATO’s core principle.

Article 5

IPA: /ˌɑːtɪkəl ˈfaɪv/
Pronunciation: AR-ti-kuhl five
French: Article 5

Definition: The NATO treaty clause stating that an attack on one ally is considered an attack on all.

Example: Article 5 has been invoked only once, after the 9/11 attacks.

North Atlantic Treaty

IPA: /ˌnɔːθ ətˌlæntɪk ˈtriːti/
Pronunciation: north-at-LAN-tik TREE-tee
French: traité de l’Atlantique nord

Definition: The founding treaty of NATO, signed in 1949.

Example: The North Atlantic Treaty created NATO.

Washington Treaty

IPA: /ˈwɒʃɪŋtən ˈtriːti/
Pronunciation: WOSH-ing-tuhn TREE-tee
French: traité de Washington

Definition: Another name for the North Atlantic Treaty.

Example: The Washington Treaty sets out NATO’s founding principles.

member state

IPA: /ˈmembə steɪt/
Pronunciation: MEM-buh state
French: État membre

Definition: A country that belongs to an organisation or alliance.

Example: Each NATO member state has a voice in decision-making.

ally

IPA: /ˈælaɪ/
Pronunciation: AL-eye
French: allié

Definition: A country cooperating with another, especially in defence or security.

Example: NATO allies coordinate military planning.

transatlantic alliance

IPA: /ˌtrænzətˈlæntɪk əˈlaɪəns/
Pronunciation: tranz-at-LAN-tik uh-LY-uhns
French: alliance transatlantique

Definition: The security partnership linking North America and Europe.

Example: The transatlantic alliance remains central to European security.

transatlantic relations

IPA: /ˌtrænzətˈlæntɪk rɪˈleɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: tranz-at-LAN-tik ri-LAY-shuhnz
French: relations transatlantiques

Definition: Relations between North America and Europe.

Example: NATO is a pillar of transatlantic relations.

Euro-Atlantic security

IPA: /ˌjʊərəʊ ətˌlæntɪk sɪˈkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: YOOR-oh at-LAN-tik si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité euro-atlantique

Definition: Security across Europe and North America.

Example: NATO presents itself as a guardian of Euro-Atlantic security.

deterrence

IPA: /dɪˈterəns/
Pronunciation: di-TERR-uhns
French: dissuasion

Definition: Preventing aggression by threatening credible consequences.

Example: Deterrence is meant to prevent war, not start it.

defence posture

IPA: /dɪˈfens ˈpɒstʃə/
Pronunciation: di-FENS POS-chuh
French: dispositif de défense

Definition: The way military forces are organised and prepared for defence.

Example: NATO strengthened its defence posture after Russia’s invasion of Ukraine.

forward defence

IPA: /ˌfɔːwəd dɪˈfens/
Pronunciation: FOR-wuhd di-FENS
French: défense avancée

Definition: Defence based on deploying forces near potential areas of conflict.

Example: Forward defence reassures exposed allies.

enhanced Forward Presence

IPA: /ɪnˌhɑːnst ˈfɔːwəd ˈprezəns/
Pronunciation: in-HANST FOR-wuhd PREZ-uhns
French: présence avancée renforcée

Definition: NATO deployments in eastern member states to deter aggression.

Example: Enhanced Forward Presence was created after Russia’s annexation of Crimea.

tripwire force

IPA: /ˈtrɪpwaɪə fɔːs/
Pronunciation: TRIP-wire force
French: force de déclenchement

Definition: A small force whose attack would trigger a wider allied response.

Example: A tripwire force signals alliance commitment.

rapid reaction force

IPA: /ˌræpɪd riˈækʃən fɔːs/
Pronunciation: RAP-id ree-AK-shuhn force
French: force de réaction rapide

Definition: Military forces ready to deploy quickly in a crisis.

Example: A rapid reaction force improves NATO readiness.

NATO Response Force

IPA: /ˌneɪtəʊ rɪˈspɒns fɔːs/
Pronunciation: NAY-toh ri-SPONS force
French: Force de réaction de l’OTAN

Definition: A multinational NATO force designed for rapid deployment.

Example: The NATO Response Force can be activated in crises.

readiness

IPA: /ˈredinəs/
Pronunciation: RED-ee-nuhs
French: état de préparation

Definition: The state of being prepared for action.

Example: Military readiness is vital for deterrence.

military readiness

IPA: /ˌmɪlɪtəri ˈredinəs/
Pronunciation: MIL-i-tuh-ree RED-ee-nuhs
French: préparation militaire

Definition: The ability of armed forces to respond effectively and quickly.

Example: NATO has increased military readiness on its eastern flank.

interoperability

IPA: /ˌɪntərɒpərəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: in-ter-op-uh-ruh-BIL-uh-tee
French: interopérabilité

Definition: The ability of different armed forces to work together effectively.

Example: NATO exercises improve interoperability among allies.

standardisation

IPA: /ˌstændədaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: STAN-duh-dy-ZAY-shuhn
French: standardisation

Definition: Making rules, equipment or procedures compatible.

Example: Standardisation helps NATO forces operate together.

joint exercise

IPA: /ˌdʒɔɪnt ˈeksəsaɪz/
Pronunciation: joint EK-suh-syze
French: exercice conjoint

Definition: A military training operation involving several forces or countries.

Example: Joint exercises test NATO’s ability to respond.

military exercise

IPA: /ˌmɪlɪtəri ˈeksəsaɪz/
Pronunciation: MIL-i-tuh-ree EK-suh-syze
French: exercice militaire

Definition: A training operation preparing forces for real missions.

Example: Large military exercises can also send strategic signals.

command structure

IPA: /kəˈmɑːnd ˌstrʌktʃə/
Pronunciation: kuh-MAND STRUK-chuh
French: structure de commandement

Definition: The hierarchy through which military authority is organised.

Example: NATO’s command structure coordinates allied forces.

Supreme Allied Commander Europe

IPA: /suːˌpriːm ˈælaɪd kəˈmɑːndər ˈjʊərəp/
Pronunciation: soo-PREEM AL-eyed kuh-MAN-duh YOOR-uhp
French: commandant suprême des forces alliées en Europe

Definition: The senior NATO commander responsible for military operations in Europe.

Example: The Supreme Allied Commander Europe is traditionally an American officer.

SACEUR

IPA: /ˈsækɜː/
Pronunciation: SAK-ur
French: SACEUR

Definition: Abbreviation for Supreme Allied Commander Europe.

Example: SACEUR is one of NATO’s most important military roles.

Secretary General

IPA: /ˌsekrətəri ˈdʒenərəl/
Pronunciation: SEK-ruh-tuh-ree JEN-uh-ruhl
French: secrétaire général

Definition: NATO’s chief political official and public representative.

Example: The Secretary General represents NATO in public diplomacy.

North Atlantic Council

IPA: /ˌnɔːθ ətˌlæntɪk ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: north-at-LAN-tik KOWN-suhl
French: Conseil de l’Atlantique nord

Definition: NATO’s principal political decision-making body.

Example: The North Atlantic Council makes decisions by consensus.

consensus

IPA: /kənˈsensəs/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SEN-suhs
French: consensus

Definition: General agreement among all members.

Example: NATO decisions require consensus among allies.

consultation

IPA: /ˌkɒnsəlˈteɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kon-suhl-TAY-shuhn
French: consultation

Definition: Discussion among allies before taking decisions.

Example: Article 4 allows NATO allies to request consultation.

Article 4

IPA: /ˌɑːtɪkəl ˈfɔː/
Pronunciation: AR-ti-kuhl four
French: Article 4

Definition: The NATO clause allowing members to consult when their security is threatened.

Example: Article 4 can be invoked during a security crisis.

B. Defence, deterrence, readiness and burden-sharing

burden-sharing

IPA: /ˈbɜːdən ˌʃeərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: BUR-duhn SHARE-ing
French: partage du fardeau

Definition: The distribution of costs and responsibilities among allies.

Example: Burden-sharing is a recurring source of tension within NATO.

defence spending

IPA: /dɪˈfens ˈspendɪŋ/
Pronunciation: di-FENS SPEN-ding
French: dépenses de défense

Definition: Government spending on military defence.

Example: NATO allies are expected to increase defence spending.

two-percent target

IPA: /ˌtuː pəˈsent ˈtɑːɡɪt/
Pronunciation: two-puh-SENT TAR-git
French: objectif des 2 %

Definition: NATO’s guideline that allies should spend 2% of GDP on defence.

Example: The two-percent target is debated in many NATO countries.

GDP

IPA: /ˌdʒiː diː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: jee-dee-pee
French: PIB

Definition: Gross Domestic Product, the value of goods and services produced in a country.

Example: Defence spending is often measured as a share of GDP.

defence budget

IPA: /dɪˈfens ˈbʌdʒɪt/
Pronunciation: di-FENS BUJ-it
French: budget de défense

Definition: Money allocated by a government to defence.

Example: The defence budget rose after the invasion.

military capability

IPA: /ˌmɪlɪtəri ˌkeɪpəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: MIL-i-tuh-ree kay-puh-BIL-uh-tee
French: capacité militaire

Definition: A country’s ability to perform military tasks.

Example: NATO depends on credible military capabilities.

capability gap

IPA: /ˌkeɪpəˈbɪləti ɡæp/
Pronunciation: kay-puh-BIL-uh-tee gap
French: écart capacitaire

Definition: A shortfall in military equipment, skills or capacity.

Example: Capability gaps weaken alliance readiness.

strategic concept

IPA: /strəˌtiːdʒɪk ˈkɒnsept/
Pronunciation: struh-TEE-jik KON-sept
French: concept stratégique

Definition: A NATO document defining threats, goals and priorities.

Example: NATO’s Strategic Concept identifies major security challenges.

security environment

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊərəti ɪnˌvaɪrənmənt/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR-uh-tee in-VY-ruhn-muhnt
French: environnement de sécurité

Definition: The overall context of threats and risks.

Example: The European security environment changed dramatically after 2022.

threat perception

IPA: /ˈθret pəˌsepʃən/
Pronunciation: thret puh-SEP-shuhn
French: perception de la menace

Definition: How actors understand or interpret threats.

Example: Different allies may have different threat perceptions.

eastern flank

IPA: /ˌiːstən ˈflæŋk/
Pronunciation: EES-tuhn flank
French: flanc oriental

Definition: NATO member states located near Russia and Belarus.

Example: NATO reinforced its eastern flank.

southern flank

IPA: /ˌsʌðən ˈflæŋk/
Pronunciation: SUTH-uhn flank
French: flanc sud

Definition: NATO’s southern area of concern, including the Mediterranean and Middle East.

Example: The southern flank faces instability, terrorism and migration pressures.

Baltic states

IPA: /ˈbɔːltɪk steɪts/
Pronunciation: BALL-tik states
French: États baltes

Definition: Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania.

Example: The Baltic states see NATO as essential to their security.

Black Sea region

IPA: /ˌblæk ˈsiː ˈriːdʒən/
Pronunciation: black sea REE-juhn
French: région de la mer Noire

Definition: The strategic region around the Black Sea.

Example: The Black Sea region has become a major security concern.

Russia

IPA: /ˈrʌʃə/
Pronunciation: RUSH-uh
French: Russie

Definition: A major Eurasian state often seen by NATO as a central security challenge.

Example: Russia’s actions have reshaped NATO strategy.

Russian aggression

IPA: /ˌrʌʃən əˈɡreʃən/
Pronunciation: RUSH-uhn uh-GRESH-uhn
French: agression russe

Definition: Aggressive military or political action by Russia.

Example: Russian aggression revived NATO’s collective-defence role.

Ukraine

IPA: /juːˈkreɪn/
Pronunciation: yoo-KRAYN
French: Ukraine

Definition: A European state invaded by Russia in 2022 and supported by NATO allies.

Example: Ukraine receives military and political support from NATO members.

Ukraine war

IPA: /juːˈkreɪn wɔː/
Pronunciation: yoo-KRAYN war
French: guerre en Ukraine

Definition: The war following Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022.

Example: The Ukraine war transformed European security debates.

military aid

IPA: /ˌmɪlɪtəri ˈeɪd/
Pronunciation: MIL-i-tuh-ree aid
French: aide militaire

Definition: Military support such as weapons, training or intelligence.

Example: NATO countries have provided military aid to Ukraine.

security assistance

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊərəti əˈsɪstəns/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR-uh-tee uh-SIS-tuhns
French: assistance sécuritaire

Definition: Support to strengthen another country’s defence and security.

Example: Security assistance can include training and equipment.

training mission

IPA: /ˈtreɪnɪŋ ˌmɪʃən/
Pronunciation: TRAY-ning MISH-uhn
French: mission de formation

Definition: A mission designed to train another country’s forces.

Example: Training missions improve partner forces’ capabilities.

partnership

IPA: /ˈpɑːtnəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: PART-nuh-ship
French: partenariat

Definition: A structured relationship for cooperation without full membership.

Example: NATO maintains partnerships with non-member states.

Partnership for Peace

IPA: /ˈpɑːtnəʃɪp fə piːs/
Pronunciation: PART-nuh-ship for peace
French: Partenariat pour la paix

Definition: A NATO programme for cooperation with non-member countries.

Example: Partnership for Peace was launched after the Cold War.

partner country

IPA: /ˈpɑːtnə ˈkʌntri/
Pronunciation: PART-nuh KUN-tree
French: pays partenaire

Definition: A country cooperating with NATO without being a member.

Example: Partner countries may take part in NATO missions.

membership

IPA: /ˈmembəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: MEM-buh-ship
French: adhésion ; appartenance

Definition: The status of belonging to an organisation.

Example: NATO membership gives security guarantees.

accession

IPA: /əkˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: ak-SESH-uhn
French: adhésion

Definition: The process of formally joining an organisation or treaty.

Example: Accession to NATO requires approval by all allies.

enlargement

IPA: /ɪnˈlɑːdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: in-LARJ-muhnt
French: élargissement

Definition: The expansion of an organisation by admitting new members.

Example: NATO enlargement is controversial in relations with Russia.

open-door policy

IPA: /ˌəʊpən ˈdɔː ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: OH-puhn door POL-uh-see
French: politique de la porte ouverte

Definition: NATO’s policy that European democracies may apply to join if they meet conditions.

Example: The open-door policy is defended as a sovereign choice.

aspirant country

IPA: /əˈspaɪərənt ˈkʌntri/
Pronunciation: uh-SPY-ruhant KUN-tree
French: pays candidat

Definition: A country seeking to join NATO.

Example: Aspirant countries must meet political and military standards.

Membership Action Plan

IPA: /ˈmembəʃɪp ˈækʃən plæn/
Pronunciation: MEM-buh-ship AK-shuhn plan
French: Plan d’action pour l’adhésion

Definition: A NATO programme helping countries prepare for possible membership.

Example: The Membership Action Plan guides reforms.

security guarantee

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊərəti ˌɡærənˈtiː/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR-uh-tee gar-uhn-TEE
French: garantie de sécurité

Definition: A promise to protect another state’s security.

Example: NATO membership provides a strong security guarantee.

neutrality

IPA: /njuːˈtræləti/
Pronunciation: nyoo-TRAL-uh-tee
French: neutralité

Definition: A policy of not joining military alliances or taking sides in conflicts.

Example: Finland and Sweden moved away from long-standing neutrality.

non-alignment

IPA: /ˌnɒn əˈlaɪnmənt/
Pronunciation: non-uh-LYNE-muhnt
French: non-alignement

Definition: A policy of not formally aligning with major military blocs.

Example: Non-alignment became harder to maintain after the Ukraine war.

Finland

IPA: /ˈfɪnlənd/
Pronunciation: FIN-luhnd
French: Finlande

Definition: A Nordic country that joined NATO in 2023.

Example: Finland’s NATO membership doubled NATO’s border with Russia.

Sweden

IPA: /ˈswiːdən/
Pronunciation: SWEE-duhn
French: Suède

Definition: A Nordic country that joined NATO in 2024.

Example: Sweden’s accession strengthened NATO in the Baltic region.

Cold War

IPA: /ˌkəʊld ˈwɔː/
Pronunciation: kohld war
French: guerre froide

Definition: The geopolitical rivalry between the Western bloc and the Soviet bloc after 1945.

Example: NATO was created during the Cold War.

Soviet Union

IPA: /ˌsəʊviət ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: SOH-vee-uht YOO-nee-uhn
French: Union soviétique

Definition: The communist state that existed from 1922 to 1991 and led the Eastern bloc.

Example: NATO originally deterred the Soviet Union.

C. Geography, Russia, Ukraine and NATO enlargement

Warsaw Pact

IPA: /ˌwɔːsɔː ˈpækt/
Pronunciation: WAR-saw pakt
French: pacte de Varsovie

Definition: The Soviet-led military alliance created in 1955.

Example: The Warsaw Pact was NATO’s Cold War rival.

containment

IPA: /kənˈteɪnmənt/
Pronunciation: kuhn-TAYN-muhnt
French: endiguement

Definition: A Cold War strategy to limit the spread of Soviet influence.

Example: NATO was part of the broader strategy of containment.

iron curtain

IPA: /ˌaɪən ˈkɜːtən/
Pronunciation: EYE-uhn KUR-tuhn
French: rideau de fer

Definition: The symbolic division between Western and Eastern Europe during the Cold War.

Example: NATO stood on one side of the iron curtain.

post-Cold War era

IPA: /ˌpəʊst kəʊld ˈwɔː ˈɪərə/
Pronunciation: post-kohld-war EER-uh
French: ère post-guerre froide

Definition: The period after the end of the Cold War.

Example: NATO had to redefine its role in the post-Cold War era.

out-of-area operation

IPA: /ˌaʊt əv ˈeəriə ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: out-ov-AIR-ee-uh op-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: opération hors zone

Definition: A military operation beyond NATO’s traditional territory.

Example: Out-of-area operations became important after the Cold War.

peacekeeping

IPA: /ˈpiːsˌkiːpɪŋ/
Pronunciation: PEACE-kee-ping
French: maintien de la paix

Definition: Military or civilian activity to help maintain peace after conflict.

Example: NATO was involved in peacekeeping in the Balkans.

crisis management

IPA: /ˈkraɪsɪs ˌmænɪdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: KRY-sis MAN-ij-muhnt
French: gestion de crise

Definition: Actions taken to handle and reduce a crisis.

Example: Crisis management is one of NATO’s core tasks.

collective security

IPA: /kəˌlektɪv sɪˈkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: kuh-LEK-tiv si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité collective

Definition: A system where states cooperate to prevent aggression and maintain peace.

Example: Collective security is broader than collective defence.

cooperative security

IPA: /kəʊˌɒpərətɪv sɪˈkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: koh-OP-uh-ruh-tiv si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité coopérative

Definition: Security built through partnerships, dialogue and cooperation.

Example: NATO promotes cooperative security with partners.

arms control

IPA: /ˈɑːmz kənˌtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: armz kuhn-TROHL
French: maîtrise des armements

Definition: Agreements limiting weapons or military capabilities.

Example: Arms control reduced Cold War risks.

nuclear deterrence

IPA: /ˌnjuːkliə dɪˈterəns/
Pronunciation: NYOO-klee-uh di-TERR-uhns
French: dissuasion nucléaire

Definition: Deterrence based on the threat of nuclear retaliation.

Example: NATO’s nuclear deterrence relies partly on US nuclear forces.

nuclear sharing

IPA: /ˌnjuːkliə ˈʃeərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: NYOO-klee-uh SHARE-ing
French: partage nucléaire

Definition: NATO arrangements in which some allies host or train for nuclear missions.

Example: Nuclear sharing is controversial but central to NATO deterrence.

extended deterrence

IPA: /ɪkˌstendɪd dɪˈterəns/
Pronunciation: ik-STEN-did di-TERR-uhns
French: dissuasion élargie

Definition: A nuclear or military guarantee protecting allies as well as one’s own territory.

Example: Extended deterrence reassures allies.

missile defence

IPA: /ˈmɪsaɪl dɪˌfens/
Pronunciation: MISS-ile di-FENS
French: défense antimissile

Definition: Systems designed to detect, intercept or destroy missiles.

Example: Missile defence is part of NATO’s security architecture.

air policing

IPA: /ˈeə pəˌliːsɪŋ/
Pronunciation: air puh-LEE-sing
French: police du ciel

Definition: NATO missions monitoring and protecting allied airspace.

Example: Air policing protects allies without their own full air-defence capacity.

Baltic air policing

IPA: /ˌbɔːltɪk ˈeə pəˌliːsɪŋ/
Pronunciation: BALL-tik air puh-LEE-sing
French: police du ciel dans les États baltes

Example: Baltic air policing reassures exposed allies.

hybrid threat

French: menace hybride

Definition: A threat combining military, cyber, economic and information tools.

Example: NATO warns about hybrid threats below the threshold of war.

cyber defence

IPA: /ˈsaɪbə dɪˌfens/
Pronunciation: SY-buh di-FENS
French: cyberdéfense

Definition: Protection against cyberattacks.

Example: Cyber defence has become a NATO priority.

cyberattack

IPA: /ˈsaɪbərətæk/
Pronunciation: SY-buh-uh-tak
French: cyberattaque

Definition: An attack on computer systems or networks.

Example: A major cyberattack can trigger allied consultations.

disinformation

IPA: /ˌdɪsɪnfəˈmeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dis-in-fuh-MAY-shuhn
French: désinformation

information warfare

Pronunciation: in-fuh-MAY-shuhn WAR-fair
French: guerre de l’information
