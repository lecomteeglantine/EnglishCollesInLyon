Chapter 84 — Avoiding Common Translation Errors
B2 → C1 strategies for avoiding false friends, structural calques, collocation errors and unnatural French-to-English translation.

Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter develops advanced control of common translation errors: false friends, uncountable nouns, collocations, verb patterns, lexical distinctions, structural calques and natural reformulation for CPGE writing and speaking.

The golden rule

Do not translate words. Translate meaning, function and register.
MEANING → NATURAL ENGLISH PATTERN → GRAMMAR CHECK → REGISTER CHECK
False friendactually ≠ actuellement; eventually ≠ éventuellement
Collocationmake a decision, conduct research, take action
Structureask someone to do; explain something to someone
Final testWould an English speaker naturally write or say this sentence?

A. Translation is not word substitution

Meaning first

Rule / explanation: Translate meaning, function and register rather than replacing each French word.

Example / answer: Il a fini par accepter → He eventually agreed.

Structure may change

Rule / explanation: Natural English may require a different word order or grammatical category.

Example / answer: Son refus de répondre a surpris la presse → The press was surprised that he refused to answer.

Context controls choice

Rule / explanation: The correct translation depends on tense, tone and intended meaning.

Example / answer: Actuellement → currently; en fait → actually.

CPGE relevance

Rule / explanation: Accurate translation improves essays, summaries, syntheses and khôlles.

Example / answer: A strong translation sounds as though it was originally written in English.

B. The anti-calque method

Identify the message

Rule / explanation: Ask what the sentence is doing: describing, evaluating, conceding, warning or hypothesising.

Example / answer: The communicative function determines the structure.

Find the English pattern

Rule / explanation: Choose an idiomatic English construction for that function.

Example / answer: Il faut que... may become must, should, need to or it is necessary to.

Check grammar

Rule / explanation: Verify tense, article, preposition, countability and word order.

Example / answer: Information is uncountable; depend takes on.

Check register

Rule / explanation: Choose language appropriate to an essay or oral examination.

Example / answer: Use investigate rather than look into in formal prose.

Read independently

Rule / explanation: Ignore the French wording and test whether the English sentence sounds natural.

Example / answer: A grammatically possible calque may still be unidiomatic.

C. Actually, currently and at present

Actually

Rule / explanation: Actually means in fact or contrary to an assumption.

Example / answer: Actually, the second article is more cautious.

Currently

Rule / explanation: Currently means at the present time.

Example / answer: The committee is currently examining the proposal.

At present

Rule / explanation: At present is a neutral alternative to currently.

Example / answer: At present, no agreement has been reached.

Actuellement

Rule / explanation: Translate actuellement with currently, at present or now.

Example / answer: Le gouvernement prépare actuellement un projet de loi → The government is currently preparing a bill.

En fait

Rule / explanation: Translate en fait with actually or in fact.

Example / answer: En fait, les chiffres ont diminué → In fact, the figures fell.

D. Eventually, possibly and perhaps

Eventually

Rule / explanation: Eventually means in the end, after time or difficulty.

Example / answer: After months of negotiation, the parties eventually agreed.

Possibly

Rule / explanation: Possibly expresses uncertainty.

Example / answer: The reform could possibly increase costs.

Perhaps

Rule / explanation: Perhaps is a neutral possibility marker.

Example / answer: Perhaps the author underestimates regional differences.

Éventuellement

Rule / explanation: Use possibly, perhaps, if necessary or potentially.

Example / answer: Le vote pourra éventuellement être reporté → The vote may be postponed if necessary.

Finalement

Rule / explanation: Use eventually, ultimately, finally or in the end according to context.

Example / answer: Ils ont finalement accepté → They eventually agreed.

E. Assist, attend and help

Attend

Rule / explanation: Attend means be present at an event, meeting or course.

Example / answer: She attended the conference.

Assist

Rule / explanation: Assist means help.

Example / answer: The lawyer assisted the committee.

Assist with

Rule / explanation: Use assist with before a task.

Example / answer: The team assisted with the investigation.

Assister à

Rule / explanation: Translate with attend, watch or witness.

Example / answer: Il a assisté au débat → He attended the debate.

Aider

Rule / explanation: Use help or assist according to register.

Example / answer: The measure may help small businesses.

F. Demand, request and ask

Demand

Rule / explanation: Demand expresses a strong requirement.

Example / answer: Protesters demanded the minister’s resignation.

Request

Rule / explanation: Request is formal and neutral.

Example / answer: The committee requested further evidence.

Ask

Rule / explanation: Ask is the normal general verb.

Example / answer: The journalist asked the minister to clarify the figures.

Demander

Rule / explanation: Choose according to force and structure.

Example / answer: Elle lui a demandé de partir → She asked him to leave.

Ask a question

Rule / explanation: Do not say demand a question.

Example / answer: The examiner asked a difficult question.

G. Pass, take and sit an examination

Take an exam

Rule / explanation: Take means participate in the examination.

Example / answer: She will take the entrance examination in June.

Sit an exam

Rule / explanation: Sit is common in British English.

Example / answer: He sat the examination last year.

Pass an exam

Rule / explanation: Pass means succeed.

Example / answer: She passed the examination with excellent marks.

Fail an exam

Rule / explanation: Fail means not achieve the required result.

Example / answer: He failed the first test.

Passer un examen

Rule / explanation: Use take or sit for participation and pass for success.

Example / answer: Elle a passé et réussi l’examen → She sat and passed the examination.

H. Make, do and take

Make

Rule / explanation: Use make for creating, producing or causing.

Example / answer: Make a decision, make progress, make a mistake.

Do

Rule / explanation: Use do for tasks, work and activities.

Example / answer: Do research, do an exercise, do one’s best.

Take

Rule / explanation: Use take in fixed combinations involving action or responsibility.

Example / answer: Take action, take measures, take responsibility.

Faire

Rule / explanation: French faire has many English equivalents.

Example / answer: Faire une analyse → conduct an analysis / analyse.

Collocation first

Rule / explanation: Learn the whole expression rather than translating faire alone.

Example / answer: Faire attention → pay attention.

I. Say, tell, speak and talk

Say

Rule / explanation: Say focuses on the words and does not take a person directly.

Example / answer: The minister said that the reform was necessary.

Tell

Rule / explanation: Tell normally takes a person as object.

Example / answer: The minister told journalists that the reform was necessary.

Speak

Rule / explanation: Speak is common for languages and formal communication.

Example / answer: She spoke to the committee.

Talk

Rule / explanation: Talk is often conversational and interactive.

Example / answer: They talked about the proposal.

Common error

Rule / explanation: Do not say say me or explain me.

Example / answer: Tell me / explain it to me.

J. Explain, describe and present

Explain something

Rule / explanation: Explain takes the subject matter directly.

Example / answer: The author explains the mechanism.

Explain to someone

Rule / explanation: The person follows to.

Example / answer: The teacher explained the rule to the students.

Describe something

Rule / explanation: Describe also takes the thing directly.

Example / answer: The article describes the consequences.

Present something

Rule / explanation: Present means introduce, display or set out.

Example / answer: The report presents three possible solutions.

Se présenter

Rule / explanation: Translate according to context.

Example / answer: Il s’est présenté → He introduced himself.

K. Uncountable academic nouns

Information

Rule / explanation: Information is uncountable.

Example / answer: The report provides useful information.

Advice

Rule / explanation: Advice is uncountable; advise is the verb.

Example / answer: She gave me valuable advice.

Research

Rule / explanation: Research is generally uncountable as an activity or field.

Example / answer: Recent research suggests that trust is declining.

Evidence

Rule / explanation: Evidence is generally uncountable.

Example / answer: The article provides insufficient evidence.

Counting

Rule / explanation: Use a piece of information, a piece of advice or a study.

Example / answer: Three studies support the conclusion.

L. Work, job and employment

Work

Rule / explanation: Work is normally uncountable as activity or labour.

Example / answer: She has too much work.

Job

Rule / explanation: A job is a countable position.

Example / answer: He applied for three jobs.

Employment

Rule / explanation: Employment is a formal uncountable term.

Example / answer: The policy may affect employment.

Travail

Rule / explanation: Choose activity, task, position or employment according to meaning.

Example / answer: Un travail difficile → a difficult task / a demanding job.

Works

Rule / explanation: Works may refer to artistic productions or construction activity.

Example / answer: The works of Shakespeare / roadworks.

M. Society, company and firm

Society

Rule / explanation: Society means the social system or an organised association.

Example / answer: Modern society depends on digital infrastructure.

Company

Rule / explanation: A company is a commercial organisation.

Example / answer: The company announced job cuts.

Firm

Rule / explanation: Firm is a common formal synonym for business company.

Example / answer: Small firms may face higher costs.

Société

Rule / explanation: Do not translate every société with society.

Example / answer: Une société américaine → an American company.

Association

Rule / explanation: An association is an organised group formed for a purpose.

Example / answer: A professional association opposed the proposal.

N. Politics and policy

Politics

Rule / explanation: Politics refers to political activity, debate or study.

Example / answer: She is interested in politics.

Policy

Rule / explanation: A policy is a plan or course of action.

Example / answer: The government introduced a new housing policy.

Politician

Rule / explanation: A politician is a person active in politics.

Example / answer: Several politicians criticised the bill.

Political

Rule / explanation: Political is the adjective.

Example / answer: The decision had major political consequences.

Politique

Rule / explanation: Choose politics, policy or political according to meaning.

Example / answer: La politique énergétique → energy policy.

O. Economic and economical

Economic

Rule / explanation: Economic relates to the economy, finance, production or trade.

Example / answer: The crisis had serious economic consequences.

Economical

Rule / explanation: Economical means inexpensive or resource-efficient.

Example / answer: The system is more economical to run.

Economy

Rule / explanation: Economy refers to the economic system.

Example / answer: The British economy grew slowly.

Économique

Rule / explanation: Most political and social contexts require economic.

Example / answer: La croissance économique → economic growth.

Économies

Rule / explanation: Économies often translates as savings.

Example / answer: The reform generated substantial savings.

P. Sensible and sensitive

Sensible

Rule / explanation: Sensible means reasonable or practical.

Example / answer: A gradual reform would be more sensible.

Sensitive

Rule / explanation: Sensitive means easily affected, delicate or confidential.

Example / answer: Healthcare data are highly sensitive.

Sensible

Rule / explanation: French sensible often becomes sensitive.

Example / answer: Un sujet sensible → a sensitive issue.

Raisonnable

Rule / explanation: Use reasonable or sensible.

Example / answer: Une solution raisonnable → a sensible solution.

Sensibiliser

Rule / explanation: Often use raise awareness.

Example / answer: The campaign aims to raise awareness of data privacy.

Q. Comprehensive and understanding

Comprehensive

Rule / explanation: Comprehensive means complete and wide-ranging.

Example / answer: The report provides a comprehensive overview.

Understanding

Rule / explanation: Understanding is the ability to comprehend or sympathise.

Example / answer: The teacher was understanding.

Comprehensible

Rule / explanation: Comprehensible means understandable.

Example / answer: The explanation is accurate but not easily comprehensible.

Compréhensif

Rule / explanation: Translate with understanding or sympathetic.

Example / answer: Un professeur compréhensif → an understanding teacher.

Complet

Rule / explanation: Comprehensive may translate complet in formal contexts.

Example / answer: Une étude complète → a comprehensive study.

R. Important, significant and substantial

Important

Rule / explanation: Important is correct but may be vague.

Example / answer: Public trust is important.

Significant

Rule / explanation: Significant means meaningful or noteworthy.

Example / answer: The decline was politically significant.

Substantial

Rule / explanation: Substantial refers to a large amount or degree.

Example / answer: The reform generated substantial savings.

Consequent

Rule / explanation: Consequent means resulting, not important.

Example / answer: The delays and consequent cost increases.

Conséquent

Rule / explanation: Use substantial, considerable or significant for large.

Example / answer: Une hausse conséquente → a substantial increase.

S. Control, check and monitor

Control

Rule / explanation: Control means direct, regulate or have power over.

Example / answer: The agency controls access to the database.

Check

Rule / explanation: Check means verify for accuracy or compliance.

Example / answer: The committee checked the figures.

Monitor

Rule / explanation: Monitor means observe continuously or regularly.

Example / answer: The regulator monitors compliance.

Contrôler

Rule / explanation: Choose control, check, inspect, audit or monitor.

Example / answer: Contrôler les passeports → check passports.

Supervise

Rule / explanation: Supervise means oversee people or a process.

Example / answer: The ministry supervised implementation.

T. Realise, carry out and achieve

Realise

Rule / explanation: Realise means become aware of something.

Example / answer: The government realised that the timetable was unrealistic.

Carry out

Rule / explanation: Carry out means perform or conduct.

Example / answer: Researchers carried out a survey.

Achieve

Rule / explanation: Achieve means successfully reach a goal.

Example / answer: The programme achieved its objective.

Réaliser

Rule / explanation: Translate according to meaning.

Example / answer: Réaliser une étude → conduct a study.

Produce

Rule / explanation: For practical or artistic creation, use create, make or produce.

Example / answer: The team produced a documentary.

U. Structural calques

I agree

Rule / explanation: Agree is a verb; do not say I am agree.

Example / answer: I agree with the author.

According to me

Rule / explanation: Use in my view, in my opinion or I would argue.

Example / answer: In my view, the conclusion is too broad.

It permits to

Rule / explanation: Use allows someone to or makes it possible to.

Example / answer: The reform allows local authorities to adapt funding.

People is

Rule / explanation: People normally takes a plural verb.

Example / answer: People are increasingly concerned.

One of the most important issue

Rule / explanation: After one of the, use a plural noun.

Example / answer: One of the most important issues.

I am here since

Rule / explanation: Use the present perfect for a state continuing from the past.

Example / answer: I have lived here since 2020.

V. Exercises and model corrections

1. False friend

Rule / explanation: Correct: Actually, the government is preparing a new bill.

Example / answer: Currently, the government is preparing a new bill.

2. Eventuality

Rule / explanation: Translate: Le vote pourra éventuellement être reporté.

Example / answer: The vote may be postponed if necessary.

3. Attend

Rule / explanation: Correct: She assisted to the conference.

Example / answer: She attended the conference.

4. Ask

Rule / explanation: Translate: Le journaliste a demandé au ministre d’expliquer les chiffres.

Example / answer: The journalist asked the minister to explain the figures.

5. Examination

Rule / explanation: Translate: Elle a passé l’examen mais ne l’a pas réussi.

Example / answer: She sat the examination but did not pass it.

6. Uncountable nouns

Rule / explanation: Correct: The article gives many informations and advices.

Example / answer: The article provides a great deal of information and useful advice.

7. Politics / policy

Rule / explanation: Translate: La politique du gouvernement a provoqué une crise politique.

Example / answer: The government’s policy caused a political crisis.

8. Economic / economical

Rule / explanation: Correct: The economical crisis affected many firms.

Example / answer: The economic crisis affected many firms.

9. Realise

Rule / explanation: Translate: Le comité a réalisé une étude complète.

Example / answer: The committee conducted a comprehensive study.

10. Structural calque

Rule / explanation: Correct: According to me, this measure permits to reduce costs.

Example / answer: In my view, this measure makes it possible to reduce costs.

11. Full sentence

Rule / explanation: Translate: Cette réforme n’est pas totalement inefficace, mais elle n’a pas permis de réduire les inégalités régionales.

Example / answer: The reform is not entirely ineffective, but it has not reduced regional inequalities.

12. Mini concours challenge

Rule / explanation: Translate naturally: Il faut éviter de présenter la technologie comme une solution miracle, car ses effets dépendent largement des conditions dans lesquelles elle est utilisée.

Example / answer: Technology should not be presented as a miracle solution, since its effects depend largely on the conditions under which it is used.
