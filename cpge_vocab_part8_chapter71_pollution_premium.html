Essential vocabulary for khôlles, essays, press reviews and competitive exams.

Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse pollution: waste, plastic, microplastics, air pollution, water pollution, soil contamination, chemical pollution, e-waste, persistent pollutants, noise and light pollution, public health, environmental injustice, regulation, monitoring, remediation and the political challenge of making polluters accountable.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as particulate matter, PM2.5, single-use plastic, microplastic, wastewater treatment, fertiliser runoff, PFAS, environmental injustice, polluter pays principle and remediation.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain pollution as an environmental, health, economic and social-justice issue: exposure is often unequal, and responsibility is often disputed.
For essaysMemorise the collocations: to release pollutants, to monitor air quality, to phase out single-use plastics, to clean up polluted sites, to hold polluters accountable.

A. Core pollution and waste vocabulary

pollution

IPA: /pəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-LOO-shuhn
French: pollution

Definition: The introduction of harmful substances, energy or waste into the environment.

Example: Pollution harms ecosystems, public health and the economy.

pollutant

IPA: /pəˈluːtənt/
Pronunciation: puh-LOO-tuhnt
French: polluant

Definition: A substance or form of energy that contaminates air, water or soil.

Example: Some pollutants persist in the environment for decades.

contamination

IPA: /kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kuhn-tam-i-NAY-shuhn
French: contamination

Definition: The presence of harmful or unwanted substances in a place, material or organism.

Example: Water contamination can make communities sick.

toxic substance

IPA: /ˌtɒksɪk ˈsʌbstəns/
Pronunciation: TOK-sik SUB-stuhns
French: substance toxique

Definition: A substance that can poison or harm living organisms.

Example: Toxic substances can accumulate in food chains.

hazardous waste

IPA: /ˌhæzədəs ˈweɪst/
Pronunciation: HAZ-uh-duhs wayst
French: déchet dangereux

Definition: Waste that is dangerous to health or the environment.

Example: Hazardous waste requires strict handling.

industrial waste

IPA: /ɪnˌdʌstriəl ˈweɪst/
Pronunciation: in-DUS-tree-uhl wayst
French: déchet industriel

Definition: Waste produced by factories and industrial activity.

Example: Industrial waste can pollute rivers and soil.

household waste

IPA: /ˈhaʊshəʊld weɪst/
Pronunciation: HOWS-hohld wayst
French: déchet ménager

Definition: Waste produced by homes and consumers.

Example: Household waste increases with consumer culture.

municipal waste

IPA: /mjuːˌnɪsɪpəl ˈweɪst/
Pronunciation: myoo-NIS-i-puhl wayst
French: déchet municipal

Definition: Waste collected by local authorities from homes, shops and offices.

Example: Municipal waste management is a major urban challenge.

solid waste

IPA: /ˌsɒlɪd ˈweɪst/
Pronunciation: SOL-id wayst
French: déchet solide

Definition: Non-liquid waste materials.

Example: Solid waste must be collected, treated or recycled.

chemical waste

IPA: /ˈkemɪkəl weɪst/
Pronunciation: KEM-i-kuhl wayst
French: déchet chimique

Definition: Waste containing harmful chemicals.

Example: Chemical waste can poison soil and water.

radioactive waste

IPA: /ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv weɪst/
Pronunciation: ray-dee-oh-AK-tiv wayst
French: déchet radioactif

Definition: Waste containing radioactive material.

Example: Radioactive waste must be stored safely for long periods.

e-waste

IPA: /ˈiː weɪst/
Pronunciation: ee-wayst
French: déchets électroniques

Definition: Discarded electronic devices and components.

Example: E-waste contains valuable metals but also toxic substances.

waste management

IPA: /ˈweɪst ˌmænɪdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: wayst MAN-ij-muhnt
French: gestion des déchets

Definition: The collection, treatment, recycling and disposal of waste.

Example: Waste management requires infrastructure and regulation.

waste disposal

IPA: /ˈweɪst dɪˌspəʊzəl/
Pronunciation: wayst di-SPOH-zuhl
French: élimination des déchets

Definition: The process of getting rid of waste.

Example: Unsafe waste disposal can contaminate land.

landfill

IPA: /ˈlændfɪl/
Pronunciation: LAND-fil
French: décharge

Definition: A site where waste is buried.

Example: Landfills can produce methane and leachate.

incineration

IPA: /ɪnˌsɪnəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: in-sin-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: incinération

Definition: Burning waste at high temperatures.

Example: Incineration reduces waste volume but can create emissions.

recycling

IPA: /ˌriːˈsaɪklɪŋ/
Pronunciation: ree-SY-kling
French: recyclage

Definition: Processing used materials so they can be used again.

Example: Recycling reduces waste but cannot solve overconsumption alone.

upcycling

IPA: /ˈʌpˌsaɪklɪŋ/
Pronunciation: UP-sy-kling
French: surcyclage

Definition: Reusing waste materials to create higher-value products.

Example: Upcycling turns waste into useful objects.

circular economy

IPA: /ˌsɜːkjʊlər iˈkɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: SUR-kyuh-luh i-KON-uh-mee
French: économie circulaire

Definition: An economic model based on reducing waste through reuse, repair and recycling.

Example: A circular economy aims to keep materials in use.

throwaway culture

IPA: /ˈθrəʊəweɪ ˌkʌltʃə/
Pronunciation: THROH-uh-way KUL-chuh
French: culture du jetable

Definition: A culture of buying, using briefly and discarding products.

Example: Throwaway culture fuels pollution.

IPA: /ˌsɪŋɡəl juːs ˈplæstɪk/
Pronunciation: SIN-guhl yooss PLAS-tik
French: plastique à usage unique

Pronunciation: PLAS-tik puh-LOO-shuhn

Pronunciation: MY-kroh-PLAS-tik
French: microplastique

Definition: Tiny plastic particles that persist in the environment.

Example: Microplastics have been found in water, soil and human bodies.

nanoplastic

IPA: /ˌnænəʊˈplæstɪk/
Pronunciation: NAN-oh-PLAS-tik
French: nanoplastique

Definition: Extremely small plastic particles at the nanometre scale.

Example: Nanoplastics may enter tissues and cells.

marine litter

IPA: /məˈriːn ˈlɪtə/
Pronunciation: muh-REEN LIT-uh
French: déchets marins

Definition: Waste found in seas and oceans.

Example: Marine litter threatens fish, birds and turtles.

ocean garbage patch

IPA: /ˈəʊʃən ˈɡɑːbɪdʒ pætʃ/
Pronunciation: OH-shuhn GAR-bij patch
French: zone d’accumulation de déchets océaniques

Definition: A large area where floating waste accumulates in the ocean.

Example: The ocean garbage patch is a symbol of plastic pollution.

air pollution

IPA: /ˈeə pəˌluːʃən/
Pronunciation: air puh-LOO-shuhn
French: pollution de l’air

Definition: The presence of harmful substances in the air.

Example: Air pollution causes millions of premature deaths.

Pronunciation: par-TIK-yuh-luht MAT-uh

Definition: Tiny particles in the air that can damage health.

Pronunciation: pee-em two-point-five
French: PM2,5

PM10

IPA: /ˌpiː em ˈten/
Pronunciation: pee-em-ten
French: PM10

Pronunciation: NY-truh-juhn dy-OK-syde

Pronunciation: ground-LEV-uhl OH-zohn
French: ozone troposphérique

decontamination

IPA: /ˌdiːkənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-kuhn-tam-i-NAY-shuhn
French: décontamination

Definition: Removing or neutralising contamination.

Example: Decontamination is necessary after chemical accidents.

clean-up operation

IPA: /ˈkliːn ʌp ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: clean-up op-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: opération de nettoyage

Definition: An organised effort to remove pollution or waste.

Example: Oil spills require major clean-up operations.

to pollute

IPA: /pəˈluːt/
Pronunciation: puh-LOOT
French: polluer

Definition: To contaminate the environment with harmful substances.

Example: Factories may pollute rivers if regulation is weak.

to contaminate water

IPA: /kənˈtæmɪneɪt ˈwɔːtə/
Pronunciation: kuhn-TAM-i-nayt WOR-tuh
French: contaminer l’eau

Definition: To make water unsafe or polluted.

Example: Chemicals can contaminate water supplies.

to release pollutants

IPA: /rɪˈliːs pəˈluːtənts/
Pronunciation: ri-LEESS puh-LOO-tuhnts
French: rejeter des polluants

Definition: To emit or discharge harmful substances.

Example: Vehicles release pollutants into the air.

to discharge waste

IPA: /dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ weɪst/
Pronunciation: dis-CHARJ wayst
French: rejeter des déchets

Definition: To release waste into the environment.

Example: Factories must not discharge waste into rivers.

to dump waste

IPA: /dʌmp weɪst/
Pronunciation: dump wayst
French: déverser / jeter des déchets

Definition: To leave waste illegally or carelessly.

Example: Illegal operators dump waste to avoid costs.

to leak chemicals

IPA: /liːk ˈkemɪkəlz/
Pronunciation: leek KEM-i-kuhlz
French: laisser fuir des produits chimiques

Definition: To release chemicals accidentally.

Example: Old storage tanks can leak chemicals.

to monitor air quality

IPA: /ˈmɒnɪtə eə ˌkwɒləti/
Pronunciation: MON-i-tuh air KWOL-uh-tee
French: surveiller la qualité de l’air

Definition: To measure pollution levels in the air.

Example: Cities monitor air quality near roads.

to enforce environmental standards

IPA: /ɪnˈfɔːs ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl ˈstændədz/
Pronunciation: in-FORSS in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl STAN-duhdz
French: faire appliquer les normes environnementales

Definition: To make sure environmental rules are respected.

Example: Governments must enforce environmental standards.

to reduce plastic waste

IPA: /rɪˈdjuːs ˈplæstɪk weɪst/
Pronunciation: ri-DYOOSS PLAS-tik wayst
French: réduire les déchets plastiques

Definition: To cut the amount of plastic discarded.

Example: Reusable packaging can reduce plastic waste.

to phase out single-use plastics

IPA: /feɪz aʊt ˌsɪŋɡəl juːs ˈplæstɪks/
Pronunciation: fayz out SIN-guhl-yooss PLAS-tiks
French: supprimer progressivement les plastiques à usage unique

Definition: To gradually stop using disposable plastics.

Example: Many countries want to phase out single-use plastics.

to clean up polluted sites

IPA: /kliːn ʌp pəˈluːtɪd saɪts/
Pronunciation: clean up puh-LOO-tid sytes
French: dépolluer des sites contaminés

Definition: To remove pollution from contaminated places.

Example: Public funds are often needed to clean up polluted sites.

to hold polluters accountable

IPA: /həʊld pəˈluːtəz əˈkaʊntəbəl/
Pronunciation: hohld puh-LOO-tuhz uh-KOWN-tuh-buhl
French: tenir les pollueurs responsables

Definition: To make polluters responsible for the harm they cause.

Example: Communities demand that authorities hold polluters accountable.

to protect public health

IPA: /prəˈtekt ˌpʌblɪk ˈhelθ/
Pronunciation: pruh-TEKT PUB-lik health
French: protéger la santé publique

Definition: To safeguard the health of the population.

Example: Reducing pollution protects public health.

to balance industry and environmental protection

IPA: /ˈbæləns ˈɪndəstri ənd ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl prəˈtekʃən/
Pronunciation: BAL-uhns IN-duh-stree and in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl pruh-TEK-shuhn
French: équilibrer industrie et protection de l’environnement

Definition: To reconcile economic activity with environmental safeguards.

Example: Governments must balance industry and environmental protection.
