Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse the far right, extremist rhetoric, exclusionary nationalism, hate speech, conspiracy theories, radicalisation and the mainstreaming of extreme ideas. Each entry includes the English term, IPA phonetics, a practical pronunciation guide, an English definition, a French translation, and an exam-ready example sentence.

How to use this chapter

For press articles Focus on terms such as far-right party, radicalisation, hate speech, replacement theory, mainstreaming and coded language.
For oral exams Be precise. Do not use “far right”, “right-wing”, “conservative” and “extremist” as if they meant the same thing. Explain the degree of radicalism and the threat to democratic norms.
For essays Memorise the collocations: to exploit fear, to fuel division, to incite hatred, to mainstream extremist ideas, to counter extremism.

A. Far-right ideologies and labels

the far right

IPA: /ðə ˌfɑː ˈraɪt/
Pronunciation: thuh far right
French: l’extrême droite

Definition: The part of the political spectrum associated with extreme nationalism, authoritarian views, anti-immigration politics and hostility to liberal democracy.

Example: The rise of the far right has become a major concern in several European democracies.

far-right party

IPA: /ˌfɑː raɪt ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: far right PAR-tee
French: parti d’extrême droite

Definition: A political party positioned on the extreme right of the political spectrum.

Example: The far-right party gained seats after campaigning on immigration and security.

extreme right

IPA: /ɪkˌstriːm ˈraɪt/
Pronunciation: ik-STREEM right
French: droite extrême ; extrême droite

Definition: A political current that rejects liberal democratic norms and promotes radical nationalist or authoritarian ideas.

Example: The report warned about the growing influence of the extreme right online.

radical right

IPA: /ˌrædɪkəl ˈraɪt/
Pronunciation: RAD-i-kuhl right
French: droite radicale

Definition: A form of right-wing politics that is strongly nationalist and anti-immigration but may still participate in elections.

Example: Radical right parties often combine electoral politics with anti-establishment rhetoric.

right-wing extremism

IPA: /ˌraɪt wɪŋ ɪkˈstriːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: right wing ik-STREEM-iz-uhm
French: extrémisme de droite

Definition: Extreme right-wing beliefs or actions that may threaten democratic values, minorities or public safety.

Example: Security services have identified right-wing extremism as a growing threat.

extremism

IPA: /ɪkˈstriːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ik-STREEM-iz-uhm
French: extrémisme

Definition: Political or religious views and actions that are far outside the mainstream and may reject democratic compromise.

Example: Extremism thrives when fear and resentment are exploited.

extremist

IPA: /ɪkˈstriːmɪst/
Pronunciation: ik-STREEM-ist
French: extrémiste

Definition: A person or group holding extreme political or ideological views.

Example: Extremist groups often recruit vulnerable people online.

ultranationalism

IPA: /ˌʌltrəˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: UL-truh-NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: ultranationalisme

Definition: An extreme form of nationalism that places the nation above other values and often excludes minorities or foreigners.

Example: Ultranationalism can fuel hostility towards migrants and international institutions.

ethno-nationalism

IPA: /ˌeθnəʊ ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ETH-noh NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: ethno-nationalisme

Definition: A form of nationalism based on ethnic identity and the idea that the nation should belong to one ethnic group.

Example: Ethno-nationalism defines belonging in ethnic rather than civic terms.

white nationalism

IPA: /ˌwaɪt ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: white NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme blanc

Definition: A racist ideology claiming that white people should maintain political and cultural dominance.

Example: White nationalism has been linked to violent extremist networks.

white supremacy

IPA: /ˌwaɪt səˈpreməsi/
Pronunciation: white suh-PREM-uh-see
French: suprémacisme blanc

Definition: The racist belief that white people are superior to other racial groups and should dominate society.

Example: White supremacy is incompatible with democratic equality.

supremacist

IPA: /suːˈpreməsɪst/
Pronunciation: soo-PREM-uh-sist
French: suprémaciste

Definition: A person who believes that one group is superior and should dominate others.

Example: Supremacist propaganda often targets minorities and migrants.

neo-Nazi

IPA: /ˌniːəʊ ˈnɑːtsi/
Pronunciation: nee-oh NAHT-see
French: néonazi

Definition: A person or group that supports modern versions of Nazi ideology.

Example: Neo-Nazi groups have used online forums to spread propaganda.

fascism

IPA: /ˈfæʃɪzəm/
Pronunciation: FASH-iz-uhm
French: fascisme

Definition: An authoritarian nationalist ideology that rejects democracy, glorifies strong leadership and often uses violence against opponents.

Example: Fascism relies on hierarchy, nationalism and hostility to political pluralism.

fascist

IPA: /ˈfæʃɪst/
Pronunciation: FASH-ist
French: fasciste

Definition: Relating to fascism, or a person who supports fascist ideas.

Example: The politician was accused of using fascist imagery.

authoritarian nationalism

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uhn NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme autoritaire

Definition: A form of nationalism that supports strong central authority and weakens democratic checks.

Example: Authoritarian nationalism often portrays dissent as betrayal.

national conservatism

IPA: /ˈnæʃənəl kənˈsɜːvətɪzəm/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl kuhn-SUR-vuh-tiz-uhm
French: conservatisme national

Definition: A conservative ideology emphasising national sovereignty, tradition, borders and cultural identity.

Example: National conservatism has become influential in several Western right-wing parties.

nativism

IPA: /ˈneɪtɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: NAY-tiv-iz-uhm
French: nativisme ; préférence nationale

Definition: A political attitude favouring native-born citizens over immigrants.

Example: Nativism often appears in far-right rhetoric.

B. Exclusion, identity and culture-war rhetoric

xenophobia

IPA: /ˌzenəˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: zen-uh-FOH-bee-uh
French: xénophobie

Definition: Fear or hatred of foreigners or people from other countries.

Example: Xenophobia can be intensified by economic insecurity and political propaganda.

racism

IPA: /ˈreɪsɪzəm/
Pronunciation: RAY-siz-uhm
French: racisme

Definition: The belief that people are inferior or superior because of race, or discrimination based on race.

Example: Racism remains a central concern in debates about far-right movements.

antisemitism

IPA: /ˌæntiˈsemɪtɪzəm/
Pronunciation: AN-tee-SEM-i-tiz-uhm
French: antisémitisme

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against Jewish people.

Example: Antisemitism has often been present in extremist conspiracy theories.

Islamophobia

IPA: /ɪzˌlæməˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: iz-LAM-uh-FOH-bee-uh
French: islamophobie

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against Muslims.

Example: Islamophobia can be used politically to portray Muslims as a threat.

homophobia

IPA: /ˌhəʊməˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: hoh-muh-FOH-bee-uh
French: homophobie

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against gay people.

Example: Extremist groups often combine nationalism with homophobia.

transphobia

IPA: /trænzˈfəʊbiə/
Pronunciation: tranz-FOH-bee-uh
French: transphobie

Definition: Hostility, prejudice or discrimination against transgender people.

Example: Transphobia has become a recurring theme in some culture-war narratives.

anti-immigration rhetoric

IPA: /ˌænti ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən ˈretərɪk/
Pronunciation: AN-tee im-i-GRAY-shuhn RET-uh-rik
French: rhétorique anti-immigration

Definition: Political language that presents immigration as a threat or problem.

Example: Anti-immigration rhetoric intensified during the campaign.

border control

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə kənˈtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh kuhn-TROHL
French: contrôle des frontières

Definition: Policies and measures used to regulate who enters a country.

Example: The party made border control a central campaign issue.

mass immigration

IPA: /ˌmæs ˌɪmɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: mass im-i-GRAY-shuhn
French: immigration massive

Definition: Large-scale immigration, often used in political debate with a negative connotation.

Example: The phrase mass immigration is often used in alarmist political speeches.

cultural threat

IPA: /ˈkʌltʃərəl θret/
Pronunciation: KUL-chuh-ruhl threat
French: menace culturelle

Definition: The idea that a group, policy or social change endangers a nation’s culture or identity.

Example: Far-right movements often frame immigration as a cultural threat.

identity politics

IPA: /aɪˈdentəti ˈpɒlɪtɪks/
Pronunciation: eye-DEN-tuh-tee POL-i-tiks
French: politique identitaire

Definition: Politics based on the interests, identities or experiences of particular groups.

Example: Far-right identity politics often focuses on national or ethnic identity.

culture war

IPA: /ˈkʌltʃə wɔː/
Pronunciation: KUL-chuh war
French: guerre culturelle

Definition: A political conflict over values, identity, religion, gender, education or national history.

Example: Culture-war issues are often used to mobilise angry voters.

traditional values

IPA: /trəˌdɪʃənəl ˈvæljuːz/
Pronunciation: truh-DISH-uh-nuhl VAL-yooz
French: valeurs traditionnelles

Definition: Values presented as rooted in family, religion, nation or older social norms.

Example: The candidate claimed to defend traditional values against liberal elites.

law and order

IPA: /ˌlɔː ən ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: law uhn OR-duh
French: ordre public ; loi et ordre

Definition: A political emphasis on policing, punishment and public security.

Example: Far-right parties often campaign on law and order.

strong borders

IPA: /ˌstrɒŋ ˈbɔːdəz/
Pronunciation: strong BOR-duhz
French: frontières strictes ; contrôle ferme des frontières

Definition: Strict border controls and limits on immigration.

Example: The movement demanded strong borders and tougher asylum rules.

national identity

IPA: /ˈnæʃənəl aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuhl eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité nationale

Definition: The sense of belonging to a nation and sharing its culture, history or values.

Example: Debates about national identity are often central to far-right politics.

sovereigntist

IPA: /ˈsɒvrəntɪst/
Pronunciation: SOV-ruhn-tist
French: souverainiste

Definition: A person or movement strongly defending national sovereignty against supranational institutions.

Example: Sovereigntist parties often criticise the European Union.

Euroscepticism

IPA: /ˌjʊərəʊˈskeptɪsɪzəm/
Pronunciation: YOOR-oh-SKEP-ti-siz-uhm
French: euroscepticisme

Definition: Criticism of the European Union or opposition to European integration.

Example: Euroscepticism has become a common feature of many far-right parties.

anti-globalism

IPA: /ˌænti ˈɡləʊbəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: AN-tee GLOH-buh-liz-uhm
French: anti-mondialisme

Definition: Opposition to globalism, often linked to hostility towards international institutions, elites or migration.

Example: Anti-globalism is often mixed with conspiracy theories.

replacement theory

IPA: /rɪˈpleɪsmənt ˈθɪəri/
Pronunciation: ri-PLAYS-muhnt THEE-uh-ree
French: théorie du grand remplacement

Definition: A racist conspiracy theory claiming that native populations are being deliberately replaced by immigrants.

Example: Replacement theory has inspired extremist propaganda and violence.

C. Conspiracy theories, hatred and violence

conspiracy theory

IPA: /kənˈspɪrəsi ˈθɪəri/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SPIR-uh-see THEE-uh-ree
French: théorie du complot

Definition: A belief that hidden powerful groups secretly control events, often without evidence.

Example: Conspiracy theories can help extremist narratives spread.

scapegoating

IPA: /ˈskeɪpɡəʊtɪŋ/
Pronunciation: SKAYP-goht-ing
French: désignation d’un bouc émissaire

Definition: The act of blaming a person or group unfairly for social or political problems.

Example: Scapegoating minorities is a common extremist strategy.

dehumanisation

IPA: /diːˌhjuːmənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-HYOO-muh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: déshumanisation

Definition: The act of portraying people as less than human, often to justify discrimination or violence.

Example: Dehumanisation can make violence against minorities seem acceptable.

hate speech

IPA: /ˈheɪt spiːtʃ/
Pronunciation: hayt speech
French: discours de haine

Definition: Speech that attacks or incites hatred against people based on identity, religion, race or other characteristics.

Example: Online hate speech can normalise extremist ideas.

incitement

IPA: /ɪnˈsaɪtmənt/
Pronunciation: in-SYTE-muhnt
French: incitation

Definition: The act of encouraging people to commit violence, hatred or illegal actions.

Example: The speech was investigated for incitement to violence.

political violence

IPA: /pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈvaɪələns/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl VY-uh-luhns
French: violence politique

Definition: Violence used to achieve political goals or intimidate opponents.

Example: Political violence can be fuelled by extremist propaganda.

domestic terrorism

IPA: /dəˌmestɪk ˈterərɪzəm/
Pronunciation: duh-MES-tik TER-uh-riz-uhm
French: terrorisme intérieur

Definition: Terrorism carried out within a country by its own citizens or residents.

Example: Domestic terrorism has become a major security concern.

radicalisation

IPA: /ˌrædɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rad-i-kuh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: radicalisation

Definition: The process by which someone adopts extreme political or religious views.

Example: Online communities can accelerate radicalisation.

online radicalisation

IPA: /ˌɒnlaɪn ˌrædɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ON-line rad-i-kuh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: radicalisation en ligne

Definition: Radicalisation that takes place through internet forums, social media or online propaganda.

Example: Online radicalisation is difficult for authorities to monitor.

extremist network

IPA: /ɪkˈstriːmɪst ˈnetwɜːk/
Pronunciation: ik-STREEM-ist NET-wurk
French: réseau extrémiste

Definition: A connected group of extremist individuals or organisations.

Example: Police uncovered an extremist network planning attacks.

militia

IPA: /məˈlɪʃə/
Pronunciation: muh-LISH-uh
French: milice

Definition: An armed group of civilians, often organised around political or ideological goals.

Example: Some far-right militias present themselves as defenders of freedom.

paramilitary group

IPA: /ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtəri ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: par-uh-MIL-i-tuh-ree group
French: groupe paramilitaire

Definition: An armed group organised like a military force but not part of the official army.

Example: The paramilitary group intimidated local communities.

vigilantism

IPA: /ˌvɪdʒɪˈlæntɪzəm/
Pronunciation: vij-i-LAN-tiz-uhm
French: justice expéditive ; vigilantisme

Definition: Taking the law into one’s own hands, often through intimidation or violence.

Example: Vigilantism can undermine the rule of law.

street movement

IPA: /ˈstriːt ˌmuːvmənt/
Pronunciation: street MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement de rue

Definition: A political movement that mobilises supporters through demonstrations, marches or confrontations in public spaces.

Example: The street movement organised rallies against immigration.

rally

IPA: /ˈræli/
Pronunciation: RAL-ee
French: rassemblement ; meeting

Definition: A large public meeting or demonstration in support of a cause or political figure.

Example: The rally attracted both supporters and counter-protesters.

counter-protest

IPA: /ˈkaʊntə ˌprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: KOWN-tuh PROH-test
French: contre-manifestation

Definition: A protest organised against another protest or movement.

Example: A counter-protest was organised by anti-racist groups.

dog whistle

IPA: /ˈdɒɡ ˌwɪsəl/
Pronunciation: dog WIS-uhl
French: message codé ; appel du pied

Definition: A coded political message understood by a particular audience but not always obvious to others.

Example: Critics accused the candidate of using racist dog whistles.

coded language

IPA: /ˌkəʊdɪd ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: KOH-did LANG-gwij
French: langage codé

Definition: Language that indirectly communicates controversial or extremist ideas.

Example: Coded language allows extremist ideas to reach mainstream audiences.

D. Mainstreaming and coded communication

mainstreaming

IPA: /ˈmeɪnstriːmɪŋ/
Pronunciation: MAYN-streem-ing
French: banalisation ; intégration dans le courant dominant

Definition: The process by which ideas once considered extreme become more widely accepted.

Example: The mainstreaming of far-right rhetoric has changed public debate.

normalisation

IPA: /ˌnɔːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: nor-muh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: normalisation ; banalisation

Definition: The process by which unacceptable ideas or behaviour come to seem normal.

Example: The normalisation of hate speech worries civil rights groups.

sanitised language

IPA: /ˈsænɪtaɪzd ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: SAN-i-tyzd LANG-gwij
French: langage édulcoré

Definition: Language that makes extreme ideas sound more acceptable or moderate.

Example: The group used sanitised language to appeal to mainstream voters.

respectability politics

IPA: /rɪˌspektəˈbɪləti ˈpɒlɪtɪks/
Pronunciation: ri-spek-tuh-BIL-uh-tee POL-i-tiks
French: stratégie de respectabilité

Definition: A strategy of appearing moderate or respectable to gain acceptance.

Example: The party adopted respectability politics to distance itself from its extremist roots.

to whitewash

IPA: /ˈwaɪtwɒʃ/
Pronunciation: WHITE-wosh
French: blanchir ; minimiser ; réhabiliter abusivement

Definition: To hide or minimise unpleasant facts about a person, group or history.

Example: The documentary was accused of trying to whitewash the movement’s violent past.

to rebrand

IPA: /ˌriːˈbrænd/
Pronunciation: ree-BRAND
French: rebaptiser ; changer d’image

Definition: To change the image of a party, movement or idea to make it more attractive.

Example: The party tried to rebrand itself as patriotic rather than extremist.

to radicalise

IPA: /ˈrædɪkəlaɪz/
Pronunciation: RAD-i-kuh-lyz
French: radicaliser

Definition: To make someone adopt extreme political or religious views.

Example: Online propaganda can radicalise isolated individuals.

to incite hatred

IPA: /ɪnˈsaɪt ˈheɪtrɪd/
Pronunciation: in-SYTE HAY-trid
French: inciter à la haine

Definition: To encourage hatred against a person or group.

Example: The post was removed because it incited hatred.

to spread propaganda

IPA: /spred ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/
Pronunciation: spred prop-uh-GAN-duh
French: diffuser de la propagande

Definition: To circulate biased or misleading political information.

Example: Extremist networks use memes to spread propaganda.

to fuel division

IPA: /ˈfjuːəl dɪˈvɪʒən/
Pronunciation: FYOO-uhl di-VIZH-uhn
French: attiser les divisions

Definition: To make social or political divisions worse.

Example: Inflammatory speeches can fuel division.

E. High-value verbs for analysis

to exploit fear

IPA: /ɪkˈsplɔɪt ˈfɪə/
Pronunciation: ik-SPLOYT fear
French: exploiter la peur

Definition: To use people’s fear for political advantage.

Example: The campaign exploited fear of crime and immigration.

to demonise opponents

IPA: /ˈdiːmənaɪz əˈpəʊnənts/
Pronunciation: DEE-muh-nyz uh-POH-nuhnts
French: diaboliser ses adversaires

Definition: To portray opponents as evil, dangerous or illegitimate.

Example: Extremist rhetoric often demonises opponents.

to target minorities

IPA: /ˈtɑːɡɪt maɪˈnɒrətiz/
Pronunciation: TAR-git my-NOR-uh-teez
French: cibler les minorités

Definition: To single out minority groups for blame, discrimination or attacks.

Example: Far-right propaganda frequently targets minorities.

to clamp down on

IPA: /ˈklæmp daʊn ɒn/
Pronunciation: klamp down on
French: réprimer ; sévir contre

Definition: To take strong action to stop or punish something.

Example: Authorities promised to clamp down on extremist violence.

to ban a group

IPA: /bæn ə ˈɡruːp/
Pronunciation: ban uh group
French: interdire un groupe

Definition: To officially prohibit an organisation from operating.

Example: The government decided to ban the group after violent incidents.

to monitor extremists

IPA: /ˈmɒnɪtə ɪkˈstriːmɪsts/
Pronunciation: MON-i-tuh ik-STREEM-ists
French: surveiller les extrémistes

Definition: To watch extremist individuals or groups for security reasons.

Example: Security agencies monitor extremists online and offline.

to counter extremism

IPA: /ˈkaʊntə ɪkˈstriːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: KOWN-tuh ik-STREEM-iz-uhm
French: lutter contre l’extrémisme

Definition: To take action to prevent or reduce extremist influence.

Example: Education can help counter extremism by strengthening critical thinking.
