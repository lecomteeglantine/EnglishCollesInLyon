Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse Northern Ireland: partition, unionism, nationalism, the Troubles, sectarian divisions, the Good Friday Agreement, Stormont, power-sharing, legacy issues, Brexit, the Northern Ireland Protocol, the Irish Sea border and the possibility of Irish unity.

A. Core geography, identity and constitutional vocabulary

Northern Ireland

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd
French: Irlande du Nord

Definition: A nation or region within the United Kingdom with a complex constitutional and political status.

Example: Northern Ireland remains one of the most sensitive issues in British politics.

Ulster

IPA: /ˈʌlstə/
Pronunciation: UL-stuh
French: Ulster

Definition: A historic province of Ireland; often used politically to refer to Northern Ireland, though not all Ulster is in the UK.

Example: The term Ulster is often associated with unionist identity.

the six counties

IPA: /ðə ˌsɪks ˈkaʊntiz/
Pronunciation: thuh six KOWN-teez
French: les six comtés

Definition: A nationalist term for Northern Ireland, referring to its six counties.

Example: Some Irish nationalists call Northern Ireland the six counties.

the province

IPA: /ðə ˈprɒvɪns/
Pronunciation: thuh PROV-inss
French: la province

Definition: A common term for Northern Ireland, especially in British or unionist contexts.

Example: The province has experienced decades of constitutional tension.

partition

IPA: /pɑːˈtɪʃən/
Pronunciation: par-TISH-uhn
French: partition

Definition: The division of Ireland into Northern Ireland and what became the Republic of Ireland.

Example: Partition created a border between Northern Ireland and the rest of Ireland.

Government of Ireland Act

IPA: /ˌɡʌvənmənt əv ˈaɪələnd ækt/
Pronunciation: GUV-uhn-muhnt ov EYE-uh-luhnd akt
French: Government of Ireland Act

Definition: The 1920 Act that partitioned Ireland and created Northern Ireland.

Example: The Government of Ireland Act laid the legal basis for partition.

Irish Free State

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ ˈfriː steɪt/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish free state
French: État libre d’Irlande

Definition: The state created in 1922 after the Anglo-Irish Treaty, later becoming the Republic of Ireland.

Example: The Irish Free State did not include Northern Ireland.

Republic of Ireland

IPA: /rɪˌpʌblɪk əv ˈaɪələnd/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-lik ov EYE-uh-luhnd
French: République d’Irlande

Definition: The sovereign state covering most of the island of Ireland.

Example: Northern Ireland shares a land border with the Republic of Ireland.

border

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh
French: frontière

Definition: The line separating Northern Ireland from the Republic of Ireland.

Example: The Irish border became central to Brexit negotiations.

Irish border

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish BOR-duh
French: frontière irlandaise

Definition: The border between Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.

Example: The Irish border is both a practical and symbolic issue.

hard border

IPA: /ˌhɑːd ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: hard BOR-duh
French: frontière dure

Definition: A border with physical checks, customs controls or infrastructure.

Example: The Good Friday Agreement helped remove the hard border.

soft border

IPA: /ˌsɒft ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: soft BOR-duh
French: frontière souple

Definition: A border with minimal or invisible checks.

Example: A soft border supported peace and everyday cross-border life.

cross-border cooperation

IPA: /ˌkrɒs ˈbɔːdə kəʊˌɒpəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kross BOR-duh koh-op-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: coopération transfrontalière

Definition: Cooperation between institutions or communities on both sides of a border.

Example: Cross-border cooperation is a key part of the peace process.

all-Ireland

IPA: /ˌɔːl ˈaɪələnd/
Pronunciation: awl EYE-uh-luhnd
French: panirlandais ; à l’échelle de l’île d’Irlande

Definition: Relating to the whole island of Ireland, north and south.

Example: Some institutions operate on an all-Ireland basis.

unionist

IPA: /ˈjuːniənɪst/
Pronunciation: YOO-nee-uhn-ist
French: unioniste

Definition: A person who supports Northern Ireland remaining part of the United Kingdom.

Example: Unionists usually identify strongly with Britain.

unionism

IPA: /ˈjuːniənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: YOO-nee-uhn-iz-uhm
French: unionisme

Definition: Support for Northern Ireland’s continued place in the United Kingdom.

Example: Unionism remains a major political force in Northern Ireland.

loyalist

IPA: /ˈlɔɪəlɪst/
Pronunciation: LOY-uh-list
French: loyaliste

Definition: A working-class or militant form of unionism, often linked historically to paramilitary groups.

Example: Loyalist groups opposed Irish republicanism.

loyalism

IPA: /ˈlɔɪəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: LOY-uh-liz-uhm
French: loyalisme

Definition: A strongly pro-British political identity in Northern Ireland, sometimes associated with Protestant working-class communities.

Example: Loyalism has often been linked to street protests and paramilitary history.

nationalist

IPA: /ˈnæʃənəlɪst/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuh-list
French: nationaliste irlandais

Definition: In Northern Ireland, a person who supports Irish national identity and often Irish unity.

Example: Nationalists generally support closer ties with the Republic of Ireland.

nationalism

IPA: /ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme irlandais

Definition: Support for Irish national identity and, in this context, often Irish unity.

Example: Irish nationalism challenges Northern Ireland’s place in the UK.

republican

IPA: /rɪˈpʌblɪkən/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-li-kuhn
French: républicain irlandais

Definition: A person who supports a united Irish republic and rejects British rule in Northern Ireland.

Example: Republicans traditionally opposed British sovereignty in Northern Ireland.

republicanism

IPA: /rɪˈpʌblɪkənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: ri-PUB-li-kuh-niz-uhm
French: républicanisme irlandais

Definition: The political movement supporting a united Irish republic.

Example: Republicanism was central to the conflict known as the Troubles.

Irish unity

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ ˈjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish YOO-nuh-tee
French: unité irlandaise

Definition: The idea that Northern Ireland should join the Republic of Ireland.

Example: Brexit renewed debates about Irish unity.

united Ireland

IPA: /juːˌnaɪtɪd ˈaɪələnd/
Pronunciation: yoo-NY-tid EYE-uh-luhnd
French: Irlande unifiée

Definition: A single Irish state including both Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.

Example: Support for a united Ireland has increased in some polls.

British identity

IPA: /ˌbrɪtɪʃ aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: BRIT-ish eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité britannique

Definition: A sense of belonging to Britain or the UK.

Example: British identity is central to many unionists.

Irish identity

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité irlandaise

Definition: A sense of belonging to Ireland.

Example: Irish identity is central to many nationalists.

Northern Irish identity

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪrɪʃ aɪˈdentəti/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-rish eye-DEN-tuh-tee
French: identité nord-irlandaise

Definition: A sense of belonging specifically to Northern Ireland.

Example: Northern Irish identity can bridge or complicate traditional divisions.

Protestant community

IPA: /ˈprɒtɪstənt kəˌmjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: PROT-i-stuhnt kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee
French: communauté protestante

Definition: The community historically associated with unionism and British identity.

Example: The Protestant community has often supported unionist parties.

Catholic community

IPA: /ˈkæθəlɪk kəˌmjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: KATH-uh-lik kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee
French: communauté catholique

Definition: The community historically associated with nationalism and Irish identity.

Example: The Catholic community has often supported nationalist parties.

sectarianism

IPA: /sekˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: sek-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: sectarisme

Definition: Hostility or discrimination between religious or communal groups.

Example: Sectarianism has left a deep mark on Northern Irish society.

sectarian divide

IPA: /sekˌteəriən dɪˈvaɪd/
Pronunciation: sek-TAIR-ee-uhn di-VYDE
French: fracture communautaire ; division sectaire

Definition: The division between Protestant/unionist and Catholic/nationalist communities.

Example: The sectarian divide affects housing, education and politics.

community division

IPA: /kəˌmjuːnəti dɪˈvɪʒən/
Pronunciation: kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee di-VIZH-uhn
French: division communautaire

Definition: Separation or tension between social or political communities.

Example: Community division remains visible in some neighbourhoods.

segregated housing

IPA: /ˌseɡrɪɡeɪtɪd ˈhaʊzɪŋ/
Pronunciation: SEG-ri-gay-tid HOW-zing
French: logement ségrégué

Definition: Housing patterns where communities live largely separately.

Example: Segregated housing reflects the legacy of the conflict.

integrated education

IPA: /ˌɪntɪɡreɪtɪd ˌedjʊˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: IN-ti-gray-tid ej-yoo-KAY-shuhn
French: éducation intégrée

Definition: Education bringing Protestant and Catholic pupils together.

Example: Integrated education is often promoted as a path to reconciliation.

peace wall

IPA: /ˈpiːs wɔːl/
Pronunciation: peace wall
French: mur de la paix

Definition: A barrier separating communities in parts of Northern Ireland.

Example: Peace walls still divide some Belfast communities.

interface area

IPA: /ˈɪntəfeɪs ˌeəriə/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-fays AIR-ee-uh
French: zone d’interface communautaire

Definition: An area where rival communities live next to each other and tensions may occur.

Example: Interface areas can be flashpoints during marching season.

marching season

IPA: /ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ ˌsiːzən/
Pronunciation: MAR-ching SEE-zuhn
French: saison des marches orangistes

Definition: The period when loyalist and unionist parades take place, often politically sensitive.

Example: Marching season can increase community tensions.

Orange Order

IPA: /ˈɒrɪndʒ ˌɔːdə/
Pronunciation: OR-inj OR-duh
French: Ordre d’Orange

Definition: A Protestant fraternal organisation associated with unionism and loyalist parades.

Example: The Orange Order is central to many unionist traditions.

parade

IPA: /pəˈreɪd/
Pronunciation: puh-RAYD
French: défilé ; marche

Definition: A public march, often politically or culturally symbolic in Northern Ireland.

Example: Parades can become controversial when routes pass nationalist areas.

commemoration

IPA: /kəˌmeməˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: kuh-mem-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: commémoration

Definition: An event remembering a person, group or historical event.

Example: Commemorations often reveal contested memories of the conflict.

contested memory

IPA: /kənˌtestɪd ˈmeməri/
Pronunciation: kuhn-TES-tid MEM-uh-ree
French: mémoire contestée

Definition: Disagreement over how the past should be remembered.

Example: Northern Ireland is marked by contested memory.

B. The Troubles: conflict, policing and paramilitaries

the Troubles

IPA: /ðə ˈtrʌbəlz/
Pronunciation: thuh TRUB-uhlz
French: les Troubles

Definition: The period of violent conflict in Northern Ireland, mainly from the late 1960s to 1998.

Example: The Troubles caused more than three thousand deaths.

civil rights movement

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts ˌmuːvmənt/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl rites MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement pour les droits civiques

Definition: A movement demanding equal rights, especially for Catholics in Northern Ireland in the 1960s.

Example: The civil rights movement challenged discrimination in housing and voting.

discrimination

IPA: /dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dis-krim-i-NAY-shuhn
French: discrimination

Definition: Unfair treatment based on identity, religion or community background.

Example: Discrimination against Catholics helped fuel unrest.

gerrymandering

IPA: /ˈdʒerimændərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: JERR-ee-man-duh-ring
French: charcutage électoral

Definition: Manipulating electoral boundaries to favour one group or party.

Example: Gerrymandering was one of the grievances raised by civil rights activists.

policing

IPA: /pəˈliːsɪŋ/
Pronunciation: puh-LEE-sing
French: maintien de l’ordre ; action policière

Definition: The work and methods of the police.

Example: Policing was deeply controversial during the Troubles.

Royal Ulster Constabulary

IPA: /ˌrɔɪəl ˈʌlstə kənˈstæbjʊləri/
Pronunciation: ROY-uhl UL-stuh kuhn-STAB-yuh-luh-ree
French: Royal Ulster Constabulary ; RUC

Definition: The former police force in Northern Ireland, replaced by the PSNI.

Example: The Royal Ulster Constabulary was distrusted by many nationalists.

RUC

IPA: /ˌɑː juː ˈsiː/
Pronunciation: ar-yoo-see
French: RUC

Definition: Abbreviation for Royal Ulster Constabulary.

Example: The RUC was reformed after the peace agreement.

Police Service of Northern Ireland

IPA: /pəˌliːs ˌsɜːvɪs əv ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd/
Pronunciation: puh-LEESS SUR-vis ov NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd
French: Police Service of Northern Ireland ; PSNI

Definition: The current police force in Northern Ireland.

Example: The PSNI was created as part of policing reform.

PSNI

IPA: /ˌpiː es en ˈaɪ/
Pronunciation: pee-ess-en-eye
French: PSNI

Definition: Abbreviation for Police Service of Northern Ireland.

Example: The PSNI aims to be more representative and trusted.

British Army

IPA: /ˌbrɪtɪʃ ˈɑːmi/
Pronunciation: BRIT-ish AR-mee
French: armée britannique

Definition: The UK’s army, deployed in Northern Ireland during the Troubles.

Example: The British Army’s role remains controversial in nationalist communities.

Operation Banner

IPA: /ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən ˈbænə/
Pronunciation: op-uh-RAY-shuhn BAN-uh
French: Operation Banner

Definition: The British Army’s long deployment in Northern Ireland from 1969 to 2007.

Example: Operation Banner was the longest continuous deployment in British military history.

Bloody Sunday

IPA: /ˌblʌdi ˈsʌndeɪ/
Pronunciation: BLUD-ee SUN-day
French: Bloody Sunday ; dimanche sanglant

Definition: The 1972 event in Derry/Londonderry when British soldiers shot civil rights marchers.

Example: Bloody Sunday became a symbol of injustice for many nationalists.

Derry/Londonderry

IPA: /ˈderi ˈlʌndənderi/
Pronunciation: DERR-ee LUN-duhn-derr-ee
French: Derry/Londonderry

Definition: A city in Northern Ireland whose name is politically contested.

Example: The name Derry or Londonderry can signal political identity.

paramilitary group

IPA: /ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtəri ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: par-uh-MIL-i-tuh-ree group
French: groupe paramilitaire

Definition: An armed organisation operating like a military force but outside the official army.

Example: Paramilitary groups were responsible for much of the violence.

IRA

IPA: /ˌaɪ ɑːr ˈeɪ/
Pronunciation: eye-ar-ay
French: IRA ; Armée républicaine irlandaise

Definition: The Irish Republican Army, a republican paramilitary organisation seeking to end British rule in Northern Ireland.

Example: The IRA carried out bombings and shootings during the Troubles.

Provisional IRA

IPA: /prəˌvɪʒənəl ˌaɪ ɑːr ˈeɪ/
Pronunciation: pruh-VIZH-uh-nuhl eye-ar-ay
French: IRA provisoire

Definition: The main republican paramilitary group during the Troubles.

Example: The Provisional IRA declared a ceasefire in the 1990s.

loyalist paramilitaries

IPA: /ˈlɔɪəlɪst ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtriz/
Pronunciation: LOY-uh-list par-uh-MIL-i-treez
French: paramilitaires loyalistes

Definition: Armed groups supporting the Union and opposing Irish republicanism.

Example: Loyalist paramilitaries targeted Catholic civilians and republicans.

UVF

IPA: /ˌjuː viː ˈef/
Pronunciation: yoo-vee-eff
French: UVF ; Ulster Volunteer Force

Definition: Ulster Volunteer Force, a loyalist paramilitary organisation.

Example: The UVF was one of the main loyalist paramilitary groups.

UDA

IPA: /ˌjuː diː ˈeɪ/
Pronunciation: yoo-dee-ay
French: UDA ; Ulster Defence Association

Definition: Ulster Defence Association, a loyalist paramilitary organisation.

Example: The UDA was linked to sectarian violence.

ceasefire

IPA: /ˈsiːsfaɪə/
Pronunciation: SEESS-fire
French: cessez-le-feu

Definition: An agreement or announcement to stop fighting.

Example: Ceasefires paved the way for peace negotiations.

peace process

IPA: /ˈpiːs ˌprəʊses/
Pronunciation: peace PROH-sess
French: processus de paix

Definition: The political process that led to the end of most violence and the creation of new institutions.

Example: The peace process required compromise from all sides.

Good Friday Agreement

IPA: /ˌɡʊd ˈfraɪdeɪ əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: good FRY-day uh-GREE-muhnt
French: Accord du Vendredi saint

Definition: The 1998 peace agreement that created power-sharing and recognised consent principles.

Example: The Good Friday Agreement is the cornerstone of peace in Northern Ireland.

Belfast Agreement

IPA: /ˌbelfɑːst əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: BEL-fast uh-GREE-muhnt
French: Accord de Belfast

Definition: Another name for the Good Friday Agreement.

Example: The Belfast Agreement created a framework for shared government.

principle of consent

IPA: /ˌprɪnsəpəl əv kənˈsent/
Pronunciation: PRIN-suh-puhl ov kuhn-SENT
French: principe de consentement

Definition: The idea that Northern Ireland’s status cannot change without majority consent.

Example: The principle of consent reassured unionists after the peace agreement.

C. Peace process, Stormont and political parties

power-sharing

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ˌʃeərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: POW-uh shair-ing
French: partage du pouvoir

Definition: A system in which rival communities share government power.

Example: Power-sharing is designed to prevent domination by one community.

cross-community support

IPA: /ˌkrɒs kəˈmjuːnəti səˈpɔːt/
Pronunciation: kross kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee suh-PORT
French: soutien intercommunautaire

Definition: Support from both unionist and nationalist representatives.

Example: Important decisions often require cross-community support.

Northern Ireland Assembly

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd əˈsembli/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd uh-SEM-blee
French: Assemblée d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The devolved legislature created by the peace process.

Example: The Northern Ireland Assembly sits at Stormont.

Stormont

IPA: /ˈstɔːmənt/
Pronunciation: STOR-muhnt
French: Stormont ; institutions nord-irlandaises

Definition: The Northern Ireland Assembly and Executive, named after the estate near Belfast.

Example: Stormont has repeatedly collapsed during political crises.

Northern Ireland Executive

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd ɪɡˈzekjʊtɪv/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv
French: Exécutif d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The devolved power-sharing government of Northern Ireland.

Example: The Northern Ireland Executive includes unionist and nationalist parties.

First Minister

IPA: /ˌfɜːst ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: first MIN-i-stuh
French: Premier ministre d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: One of the joint heads of Northern Ireland’s Executive.

Example: The First Minister shares power with the deputy First Minister.

deputy First Minister

IPA: /ˌdepjʊti ˌfɜːst ˈmɪnɪstə/
Pronunciation: DEP-yuh-tee first MIN-i-stuh
French: vice-Premier ministre d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The joint head of government alongside the First Minister.

Example: The deputy First Minister has equal power to the First Minister.

mandatory coalition

IPA: /ˌmændətəri ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən/
Pronunciation: MAN-duh-tuh-ree koh-uh-LISH-uhn
French: coalition obligatoire

Definition: A coalition government required by institutional rules, including major parties from different communities.

Example: Northern Ireland uses mandatory coalition to share power.

DUP

IPA: /ˌdiː juː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: dee-yoo-pee
French: DUP ; Parti unioniste démocrate

Definition: The Democratic Unionist Party, a major unionist party.

Example: The DUP opposed aspects of the Northern Ireland Protocol.

Sinn Féin

IPA: /ˌʃɪn ˈfeɪn/
Pronunciation: shin FAYN
French: Sinn Féin

Definition: A major Irish republican and nationalist party.

Example: Sinn Féin supports Irish unity.

Alliance Party

IPA: /əˈlaɪəns ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: uh-LY-uhns PAR-tee
French: Alliance Party ; Parti de l’Alliance

Definition: A cross-community party that does not identify as unionist or nationalist.

Example: The Alliance Party has grown as more voters reject traditional labels.

SDLP

IPA: /ˌes diː el ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: ess-dee-el-pee
French: SDLP ; Parti social-démocrate et travailliste

Definition: The Social Democratic and Labour Party, a nationalist party.

Example: The SDLP played an important role in the peace process.

Ulster Unionist Party

IPA: /ˌʌlstə ˈjuːniənɪst ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: UL-stuh YOO-nee-uhn-ist PAR-tee
French: Parti unioniste d’Ulster

Definition: A traditional unionist party in Northern Ireland.

Example: The Ulster Unionist Party supported the Good Friday Agreement.

UUP

IPA: /ˌjuː juː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: yoo-yoo-pee
French: UUP

Definition: Abbreviation for Ulster Unionist Party.

Example: The UUP was once the dominant unionist party.

legacy issue

IPA: /ˈleɡəsi ˌɪʃuː/
Pronunciation: LEG-uh-see ISH-oo
French: question héritée du conflit

Definition: An unresolved issue from the Troubles, especially justice and accountability for past violence.

Example: Legacy issues remain deeply sensitive.

victim

IPA: /ˈvɪktɪm/
Pronunciation: VIK-tim
French: victime

Definition: A person harmed, injured or killed by violence or injustice.

Example: Victims’ families continue to seek truth and justice.

reconciliation

IPA: /ˌrekənsɪliˈeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rek-uhn-sil-ee-AY-shuhn
French: réconciliation

Definition: The process of rebuilding trust after conflict.

Example: Reconciliation remains difficult when memories of violence are still raw.

truth recovery

IPA: /ˈtruːθ rɪˌkʌvəri/
Pronunciation: trooth ri-KUV-uh-ree
French: recherche de la vérité

Definition: Efforts to establish the truth about past violence or abuses.

Example: Truth recovery is important for victims’ families.

amnesty

IPA: /ˈæmnəsti/
Pronunciation: AM-nuh-stee
French: amnistie

Definition: A policy forgiving past crimes or preventing prosecution.

Example: Proposed amnesties for Troubles-era crimes have been controversial.

D. Legacy issues, Brexit and the Protocol

justice campaign

IPA: /ˈdʒʌstɪs kæmˌpeɪn/
Pronunciation: JUS-tis kam-PAYN
French: campagne pour la justice

Definition: A campaign demanding accountability for past wrongdoing.

Example: Justice campaigns keep legacy issues in the public eye.

Brexit

IPA: /ˈbreksɪt/
Pronunciation: BREK-sit
French: Brexit

Definition: The UK’s withdrawal from the European Union.

Example: Brexit destabilised Northern Ireland’s political settlement.

Northern Ireland Protocol

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd ˈprəʊtəkɒl/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd PROH-tuh-kol
French: protocole sur l’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The post-Brexit arrangement keeping Northern Ireland aligned with some EU rules to avoid a hard border.

Example: The Northern Ireland Protocol created checks in the Irish Sea.

Irish Sea border

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ siː ˈbɔːdə/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish see BOR-duh
French: frontière en mer d’Irlande

Definition: A term for checks between Great Britain and Northern Ireland after Brexit.
