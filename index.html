    From self-study to real progress

    English classes and colles are indispensable.

    Independent practice prepares you. Classes and real colles are where you speak spontaneously, test your understanding, receive feedback and turn preparation into lasting progress.

    1

    Prepare independently

    Review a skill, study vocabulary, explore a topic or prepare useful examples.

    2

    Use it in class

    Ask questions, test your understanding and actively reuse what you have learnt.

    3

    Test it in colles

    Turn preparation into structured, precise and convincing spoken English.

    4

    Improve through feedback

    Use your teachers’ comments to decide what you should work on next.

    5

    Go further at the CDI

    Read beyond online summaries, compare sources and strengthen your examples.

    A guided route into the site

    Build your foundations.

    Work through a few essential skills in a clear order, then reuse one small target aloud in class and in your next colle.

    This route may help if you often translate word for word, struggle to build complete sentences or lose confidence when speaking. It is not a separate or simplified course: it is a guided way into the same CPGE content.
    1

    Build a clear sentence

    Review word order, complete sentences, negatives and simple questions before adding complexity.

    Use it aloudProduce one complete sentence during your next English class.
    Start with sentence structure →
    2

    Control the essential tenses

    Focus first on the present, the past, the present perfect and the main ways of referring to the future.

    Use it aloudExplain one fact or event with a tense you have just reviewed.
    Review the essential tenses →
    3

    Build core vocabulary

    Learn useful verbs, simple connectors and a few expressions that help you explain, compare and qualify ideas.

    Use it aloudChoose two expressions and deliberately use them in class.

    Speak more clearly

    Start with TH sounds, final consonants, key-word stress, pauses and a less monotonous intonation.
