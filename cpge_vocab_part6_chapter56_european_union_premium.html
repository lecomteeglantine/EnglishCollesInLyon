Essential vocabulary for khôlles, essays, press reviews and competitive exams.

Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse the European Union: integration, institutions, EU law, the single market, the eurozone, migration, rule-of-law disputes, Euroscepticism, Brexit, strategic autonomy, European defence, trade, climate policy, digital regulation and the EU’s role as a geopolitical and normative power.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as European integration, single market, qualified majority voting, primacy of EU law, eurozone, rule of law, democratic deficit, strategic autonomy, Green Deal and de-risking.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain the EU as a hybrid political system: more than an international organisation, less than a federal state, and constantly balancing national sovereignty with common action.
For essaysMemorise the collocations: to deepen integration, to pool sovereignty, to uphold the rule of law, to reduce dependency, to act as a geopolitical power.

A. Core EU, integration and institutions

European Union

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn YOO-nee-uhn
French: Union européenne

Definition: A political and economic union of European countries that cooperate through shared institutions and laws.

Example: The European Union shapes trade, migration, regulation and geopolitics in Europe.

EU

IPA: /ˌiː ˈjuː/
Pronunciation: ee-yoo
French: UE

Definition: Abbreviation for European Union.

Example: The EU is both a market and a political project.

European integration

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn in-ti-GRAY-shuhn
French: intégration européenne

Definition: The process of European states becoming more politically, legally and economically connected.

Example: European integration began after the Second World War.

European project

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən ˈprɒdʒekt/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn PROJ-ekt
French: projet européen

Definition: The political idea of building cooperation and unity among European countries.

Example: The European project was designed to make war between members unthinkable.

supranational organisation

IPA: /ˌsuːprəˈnæʃənəl ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: soo-pruh-NASH-uh-nuhl or-guh-ny-ZAY-shuhn
French: organisation supranationale

Definition: An organisation with authority above national governments in some areas.

Example: The EU is more supranational than most international organisations.

supranationalism

IPA: /ˌsuːprəˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: soo-pruh-NASH-uh-nuhl-iz-uhm
French: supranationalisme

Definition: The principle that some decisions are made above the national level.

Example: Supranationalism is central to the EU’s legal order.

intergovernmentalism

IPA: /ˌɪntəɡʌvənˈmentəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-gov-uhn-MEN-tuhl-iz-uhm
French: intergouvernementalisme

Definition: Cooperation based on decisions made by national governments.

Example: Foreign policy in the EU remains largely intergovernmental.

member state

IPA: /ˈmembə steɪt/
Pronunciation: MEM-buh state
French: État membre

Definition: A country belonging to the European Union.

Example: Each member state is represented in the Council.

candidate country

IPA: /ˈkændɪdət ˈkʌntri/
Pronunciation: KAN-di-duht KUN-tree
French: pays candidat

Definition: A country officially seeking EU membership.

Example: Candidate countries must meet EU accession criteria.

accession

IPA: /əkˈseʃən/
Pronunciation: ak-SESH-uhn
French: adhésion

Definition: The process of formally joining the EU or another organisation.

Example: Accession to the EU requires long negotiations.

enlargement

IPA: /ɪnˈlɑːdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: in-LARJ-muhnt
French: élargissement

Definition: Expansion of the EU by admitting new member states.

Example: EU enlargement is both strategic and politically sensitive.

treaty

IPA: /ˈtriːti/
Pronunciation: TREE-tee
French: traité

Definition: A formal legal agreement between states.

Example: EU treaties define the powers of the institutions.

Treaty of Rome

IPA: /ˌtriːti əv ˈrəʊm/
Pronunciation: TREE-tee ov ROHM
French: traité de Rome

Definition: The 1957 treaty creating the European Economic Community.

Example: The Treaty of Rome laid the foundations of the common market.

Maastricht Treaty

IPA: /ˈmɑːstrɪxt ˌtriːti/
Pronunciation: MAH-strikt TREE-tee
French: traité de Maastricht

Definition: The 1992 treaty that created the European Union and paved the way for the euro.

Example: The Maastricht Treaty deepened European integration.

Lisbon Treaty

IPA: /ˈlɪzbən ˌtriːti/
Pronunciation: LIZ-buhn TREE-tee
French: traité de Lisbonne

Definition: The treaty that reformed EU institutions and rules, entering into force in 2009.

Example: The Lisbon Treaty strengthened the European Parliament.

founding fathers

IPA: /ˌfaʊndɪŋ ˈfɑːðəz/
Pronunciation: FOWN-ding FAR-thuhz
French: pères fondateurs

Definition: The early political figures associated with European integration.

Example: The founding fathers saw integration as a peace project.

Schuman Declaration

IPA: /ˈʃuːmən ˌdekləˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: SHOO-muhn dek-luh-RAY-shuhn
French: déclaration Schuman

Definition: The 1950 declaration proposing cooperation in coal and steel.

Example: The Schuman Declaration is often seen as the birth of European integration.

European Commission

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən kəˈmɪʃən/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn kuh-MISH-uhn
French: Commission européenne

Definition: The EU institution that proposes laws, enforces rules and represents the common European interest.

Example: The European Commission can launch infringement procedures.

Commissioner

IPA: /kəˈmɪʃənə/
Pronunciation: kuh-MISH-uh-nuh
French: commissaire européen

Definition: A member of the European Commission responsible for a policy area.

Example: Each Commissioner manages a specific portfolio.

European Parliament

Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement européen

Definition: The directly elected EU institution representing citizens.

Example: The European Parliament votes on legislation and the EU budget.

MEP

IPA: /ˌem iː ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: em-ee-pee
French: député européen

Definition: Member of the European Parliament.

Example: MEPs are elected by citizens in EU member states.

Council of the European Union

Pronunciation: KOWN-suhl ov thuh YOOR-uh-pee-uhn YOO-nee-uhn
French: Conseil de l’Union européenne

Definition: EU institution where national ministers adopt laws and coordinate policies.

Example: The Council of the European Union represents member-state governments.

European Council

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən ˈkaʊnsəl/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn KOWN-suhl
French: Conseil européen

Definition: EU institution bringing together heads of state or government to set broad priorities.

Example: The European Council sets the EU’s political direction.

Court of Justice of the European Union

IPA: /ˌkɔːt əv ˈdʒʌstɪs əv ðə ˌjʊərəpiːən ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: court ov JUS-tis ov thuh YOOR-uh-pee-uhn YOO-nee-uhn
French: Cour de justice de l’Union européenne

Definition: The EU court ensuring that EU law is interpreted and applied consistently.

Example: The Court of Justice protects the primacy of EU law.

CJEU

IPA: /ˌsiː dʒeɪ iː ˈjuː/
Pronunciation: see-jay-ee-yoo
French: CJUE

Definition: Abbreviation for Court of Justice of the European Union.

Example: The CJEU can rule against member states that breach EU law.

Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn SEN-truhl bank
French: Banque centrale européenne

ECB

IPA: /ˌiː siː ˈbiː/
Pronunciation: ee-see-bee
French: BCE

Definition: Abbreviation for European Central Bank.

Example: The ECB fights inflation while supporting financial stability.

European Court of Auditors

IPA: /ˌjʊərəpiːən kɔːt əv ˈɔːdɪtəz/
Pronunciation: YOOR-uh-pee-uhn court ov AW-di-tuhz
French: Cour des comptes européenne

Definition: EU institution checking whether EU funds are properly spent.

Example: The European Court of Auditors monitors EU spending.

qualified majority voting

IPA: /ˌkwɒlɪfaɪd məˈdʒɒrəti ˈvəʊtɪŋ/
Pronunciation: KWOL-i-fyd muh-JOR-uh-tee VOH-ting
French: vote à la majorité qualifiée

Definition: A voting system where decisions pass if they meet population and member-state thresholds.

Example: Qualified majority voting prevents one state from blocking many decisions.

unanimity

IPA: /ˌjuːnəˈnɪməti/
Pronunciation: yoo-nuh-NIM-uh-tee
French: unanimité

Definition: Agreement by all member states.

Example: Foreign policy and taxation often require unanimity.

veto

IPA: /ˈviːtəʊ/
Pronunciation: VEE-toh
French: veto

Definition: The power to block a decision.

Example: A national veto can slow EU decision-making.

subsidiarity

IPA: /səbˌsɪdiˈærəti/
Pronunciation: suhb-sid-ee-ARR-uh-tee
French: subsidiarité

Definition: The principle that decisions should be taken as close to citizens as possible.

Example: Subsidiarity limits unnecessary EU intervention.

proportionality

IPA: /prəˌpɔːʃəˈnæləti/
Pronunciation: pruh-por-shuh-NAL-uh-tee
French: proportionnalité

Definition: The principle that EU action should not exceed what is necessary.

Example: Proportionality is a key legal principle in EU law.

EU law

IPA: /ˌiː ˈjuː lɔː/
Pronunciation: ee-yoo law
French: droit de l’Union européenne

Definition: The body of law created by EU treaties, institutions and courts.

Example: EU law takes precedence in many areas.

primacy of EU law

IPA: /ˈpraɪməsi əv ˌiː ˈjuː lɔː/
Pronunciation: PRY-muh-see ov ee-yoo law
French: primauté du droit de l’UE

Definition: The principle that EU law takes priority over conflicting national law.

Example: The primacy of EU law is central to the legal order.

direct effect

IPA: /dəˌrekt ɪˈfekt/
Pronunciation: di-REKT i-FEKT
French: effet direct

Definition: The principle allowing individuals to invoke some EU rules before national courts.

Example: Direct effect makes EU law powerful for citizens.

regulation

IPA: /ˌreɡjʊˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: reg-yuh-LAY-shuhn
French: règlement

Definition: An EU legal act directly applicable in all member states.

Example: An EU regulation applies without national transposition.

directive

IPA: /dəˈrektɪv/
Pronunciation: di-REK-tiv
French: directive

Definition: An EU legal act setting goals that member states must transpose into national law.

Example: A directive gives states flexibility in implementation.

infringement procedure

IPA: /ɪnˈfrɪndʒmənt prəˌsiːdʒə/
Pronunciation: in-FRINJ-muhnt pruh-SEE-juh
French: procédure d’infraction

Definition: A legal process against a member state that breaches EU law.

Example: The Commission launched an infringement procedure.

single market

IPA: /ˌsɪŋɡəl ˈmɑːkɪt/
Pronunciation: SING-guhl MAR-kit
French: marché unique

Definition: The EU area allowing free movement of goods, services, capital and people.

Example: The single market is one of the EU’s greatest achievements.

IPA: /ɪnˌtɜːnəl ˈmɑːkɪt/

French: marché intérieur

Example: The internal market reduces barriers between member states.

four freedoms

IPA: /ˌfɔː ˈfriːdəmz/
Pronunciation: four FREE-duhmz
French: quatre libertés

Definition: Free movement of goods, services, capital and people in the EU.

Example: The four freedoms define the single market.

B. Law, single market, eurozone and budget

free movement of people

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈmuːvmənt əv ˈpiːpəl/
Pronunciation: free MOOV-muhnt ov PEE-puhl
French: libre circulation des personnes

Definition: The right of EU citizens to live and work in other member states.

Example: Free movement of people is politically controversial.

free movement of goods

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈmuːvmənt əv ɡʊdz/
Pronunciation: free MOOV-muhnt ov goods
French: libre circulation des marchandises

Definition: The removal of barriers to trade in goods between EU members.

Example: Free movement of goods supports cross-border trade.

free movement of services

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈmuːvmənt əv ˈsɜːvɪsɪz/
Pronunciation: free MOOV-muhnt ov SUR-vis-iz
French: libre circulation des services

Definition: The right to provide services across EU borders.

Example: Free movement of services affects workers and firms.

free movement of capital

IPA: /ˌfriː ˈmuːvmənt əv ˈkæpɪtəl/
Pronunciation: free MOOV-muhnt ov KAP-i-tuhl
French: libre circulation des capitaux

Definition: The right to move money and investment across EU borders.

IPA: /ˈkʌstəmz ˌjuːniən/
Pronunciation: KUS-tuhmz YOO-nee-uhn
French: union douanière

Definition: An area with no internal tariffs and a common external tariff.

Example: The EU customs union simplifies trade inside the bloc.

common external tariff

IPA: /ˌkɒmən ɪkˈstɜːnəl ˈtærɪf/
Pronunciation: KOM-uhn ik-STUR-nuhl TAR-if
French: tarif extérieur commun

Definition: A shared tariff applied to goods entering the EU from outside.

Example: The common external tariff is part of the customs union.

trade barrier

IPA: /ˈtreɪd ˌbæriə/
Pronunciation: trade BARR-ee-uh
French: barrière commerciale

Definition: A rule or cost that restricts trade.

Example: The single market removes many trade barriers.

non-tariff barrier

IPA: /ˌnɒn ˈtærɪf ˌbæriə/
Pronunciation: non-TAR-if BARR-ee-uh
French: barrière non tarifaire

Definition: A trade obstacle that is not a tariff, such as standards or paperwork.

Example: Regulatory divergence can create non-tariff barriers.

competition policy

IPA: /ˌkɒmpəˈtɪʃən ˈpɒləsi/
Pronunciation: kom-puh-TISH-uhn POL-uh-see
French: politique de concurrence

Definition: Rules designed to prevent monopolies, cartels and unfair market practices.

Example: EU competition policy can fine major corporations.

state aid

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈeɪd/
Pronunciation: state aid
French: aide d’État

Definition: Government support to companies that may distort competition.

Example: EU rules limit state aid to protect fair competition.

antitrust

IPA: /ˌæntiˈtrʌst/

French: antitrust ; droit de la concurrence
