Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse environmental activism: NGOs, grassroots movements, protests, civil disobedience, climate strikes, youth mobilisation, climate justice, media strategy, lobbying, green politics, legal action, Indigenous activism, anti-extraction movements, public backlash and the tension between disruption, legitimacy and democratic rights.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as grassroots activism, civil disobedience, disruptive protest, climate justice, eco-anxiety, greenwashing, strategic litigation, right to protest, Indigenous activism and green backlash.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain activism as a democratic force, a media strategy, a moral response to ecological crisis and a source of controversy when tactics become disruptive.

A. Core environmental activism vocabulary

environmental activism

IPA: /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl ˈæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme environnemental

Definition: Campaigning to protect the environment or demand stronger ecological policies.

Example: Environmental activism has become more visible as climate impacts intensify.

environmentalist

IPA: /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəlɪst/
Pronunciation: in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl-ist
French: écologiste ; défenseur de l’environnement

Definition: A person who works to protect the environment.

Example: Environmentalists often campaign against pollution and deforestation.

environmental movement

IPA: /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl ˈmuːvmənt/
Pronunciation: in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement écologiste

Definition: The broad social and political movement defending environmental protection.

Example: The environmental movement includes NGOs, scientists, citizens and activists.

climate activism

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme climatique

Definition: Campaigning for stronger action on climate change.

Example: Climate activism has pushed climate change into mainstream politics.

climate activist

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət ˌæktɪvɪst/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht AK-ti-vist
French: militant pour le climat

Definition: A person campaigning for climate action.

Example: Climate activists criticise governments for delaying action.

grassroots activism

IPA: /ˈɡrɑːsruːts ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: GRASS-roots AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: militantisme de terrain

Definition: Activism organised by ordinary people at local level.

Example: Grassroots activism can pressure local authorities.

grassroots movement

IPA: /ˈɡrɑːsruːts ˈmuːvmənt/
Pronunciation: GRASS-roots MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement de terrain

Definition: A movement driven by local citizens rather than elites.

Example: Grassroots movements can grow into national campaigns.

citizen mobilisation

IPA: /ˈsɪtɪzən ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: SIT-i-zuhn moh-bi-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: mobilisation citoyenne

Definition: The organisation of citizens to support a cause or protest.

Example: Citizen mobilisation can influence environmental policy.

advocacy

IPA: /ˈædvəkəsi/
Pronunciation: AD-vuh-kuh-see
French: plaidoyer ; défense d’une cause

Definition: Public support or argument for a cause or policy.

Example: Environmental advocacy often combines science and storytelling.

campaign

IPA: /kæmˈpeɪn/
Pronunciation: kam-PAYN
French: campagne

Definition: An organised effort to achieve a political, social or environmental goal.

Example: The campaign called for a ban on single-use plastic.

awareness campaign

IPA: /əˈweənəs kæmˌpeɪn/
Pronunciation: uh-WAIR-nuhs kam-PAYN
French: campagne de sensibilisation

Definition: A campaign designed to inform people about an issue.

Example: Awareness campaigns can change public behaviour.

pressure group

IPA: /ˈpreʃə ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: PRESH-uh group
French: groupe de pression

Definition: An organisation that tries to influence policy without seeking office.

Example: Pressure groups lobby for stronger environmental laws.

NGO

IPA: /ˌen dʒiː ˈəʊ/
Pronunciation: en-jee-oh
French: ONG

Definition: Non-governmental organisation.

Example: Environmental NGOs monitor governments and corporations.

civil society

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl səˈsaɪəti/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl suh-SY-uh-tee
French: société civile

Definition: Organisations and citizens outside government and business.

Example: Civil society plays a role in environmental governance.

coalition

IPA: /ˌkəʊəˈlɪʃən/
Pronunciation: koh-uh-LISH-uhn
French: coalition

Definition: A temporary alliance of groups working towards a common goal.

Example: A coalition of NGOs opposed the pipeline.

community organising

IPA: /kəˌmjuːnəti ˈɔːɡənaɪzɪŋ/
Pronunciation: kuh-MYOO-nuh-tee OR-guh-ny-zing
French: organisation communautaire

Definition: Building local collective power to demand change.

Example: Community organising helps residents fight pollution.

B. Protest tactics, youth mobilisation and climate justice

protest

IPA: /ˈprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: PROH-test
French: manifestation ; protestation

Definition: A public action showing opposition or demand for change.

Example: The protest called for urgent climate action.

demonstration

IPA: /ˌdemənˈstreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dem-uhn-STRAY-shuhn
French: manifestation

Definition: A public gathering to express a political or social demand.

Example: Thousands joined the environmental demonstration.

march

IPA: /mɑːtʃ/
Pronunciation: march
French: marche ; manifestation

Definition: A protest where people walk together publicly.

Example: The climate march attracted students and families.

rally

IPA: /ˈræli/
Pronunciation: RAL-ee
French: rassemblement

Definition: A large public meeting in support of a cause.

Example: Activists held a rally outside parliament.

sit-in

IPA: /ˈsɪt ɪn/
Pronunciation: SIT-in
French: sit-in

Definition: A protest where people sit in a place and refuse to leave.

Example: Students organised a sit-in to demand fossil fuel divestment.

blockade

IPA: /blɒˈkeɪd/
Pronunciation: blok-AYD
French: blocage ; blocus

Definition: An action preventing access to a place, road or facility.

Example: The blockade stopped trucks from entering the site.

occupation

IPA: /ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: ok-yuh-PAY-shuhn
French: occupation

Definition: Taking control of a place as a form of protest.

Example: The occupation of the building lasted several days.

direct action

IPA: /dəˌrekt ˈækʃən/
Pronunciation: di-REKT AK-shuhn
French: action directe

Definition: Immediate protest action, often outside normal political channels.

Example: Direct action is controversial but can attract attention.

non-violent protest

IPA: /ˌnɒn vaɪələnt ˈprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: non-VY-uh-luhnt PROH-test
French: manifestation non violente

Definition: Protest that avoids physical violence.

Example: Non-violent protest can still disrupt public life.

civil disobedience

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl dis-uh-BEE-dee-uhns
French: désobéissance civile

Definition: Deliberate non-violent breaking of laws considered unjust.

Example: Civil disobedience raises debates about legality and legitimacy.

symbolic action

IPA: /sɪmˌbɒlɪk ˈækʃən/
Pronunciation: sim-BOL-ik AK-shuhn
French: action symbolique

Definition: An action designed to create a powerful image or message.

Example: The action was intended as a symbolic warning.

disruptive protest

IPA: /dɪsˌrʌptɪv ˈprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: dis-RUP-tiv PROH-test
French: manifestation perturbatrice

Definition: A protest that interrupts normal activity to draw attention.

Example: Disruptive protest divides public opinion.

roadblock

IPA: /ˈrəʊdblɒk/
Pronunciation: ROHD-blok
French: barrage routier

Definition: A barrier or protest blocking a road.

Example: A roadblock can create public anger as well as media attention.

climate strike

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət straɪk/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht strike
French: grève pour le climat

Definition: A strike or school walkout demanding climate action.

Example: Youth climate strikes spread around the world.

school strike

IPA: /ˈskuːl straɪk/
Pronunciation: school strike
French: grève scolaire

Definition: A student strike from school to protest or demand change.

Example: The school strike movement inspired many young activists.

youth activism

IPA: /ˈjuːθ ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: yooth AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: militantisme des jeunes

Definition: Activism led by young people.

Example: Youth activism has reshaped climate politics.

intergenerational justice

IPA: /ˌɪntədʒenəˈreɪʃənəl ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: IN-tuh-jen-uh-RAY-shuhn-uhl JUS-tis
French: justice intergénérationnelle

Definition: Fairness between generations, especially regarding future impacts.

Example: Climate activism often appeals to intergenerational justice.

future generations

IPA: /ˌfjuːtʃə ˌdʒenəˈreɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: FYOO-chuh jen-uh-RAY-shuhnz
French: générations futures

Definition: People who will live in the future.

Example: Environmental policy affects future generations.

climate justice

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət ˌdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht JUS-tis
French: justice climatique

Definition: The idea that climate action should address fairness and responsibility.

Example: Climate justice links emissions, inequality and historical responsibility.

environmental justice

IPA: /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl ˈdʒʌstɪs/
Pronunciation: in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl JUS-tis
French: justice environnementale

Definition: Fair treatment regarding environmental benefits and harms.

Example: Environmental justice highlights unequal exposure to pollution.

eco-anxiety

IPA: /ˌiːkəʊ æŋˈzaɪəti/
Pronunciation: EE-koh ang-ZY-uh-tee
French: éco-anxiété

Definition: Anxiety caused by environmental crisis or climate change.

Example: Eco-anxiety is common among young people.

climate grief

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət ɡriːf/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht grief
French: deuil climatique

Definition: Sadness linked to environmental destruction or climate loss.

Example: Climate grief can motivate activism or despair.

doomism

IPA: /ˈduːmɪzəm/
Pronunciation: DOOM-iz-uhm
French: fatalisme écologique

Definition: The belief that ecological collapse is inevitable and action is pointless.

Example: Doomism can discourage political action.

climate communication

IPA: /ˈklaɪmət kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: KLY-muht kuh-myoo-ni-KAY-shuhn
French: communication climatique

Definition: How climate information is explained to the public.

Example: Effective climate communication avoids both denial and despair.

C. Communication, consumer activism and political strategy

media coverage

IPA: /ˈmiːdiə ˌkʌvərɪdʒ/
Pronunciation: MEE-dee-uh KUV-uh-rij
French: couverture médiatique

Definition: How much and how an issue is reported in the media.

Example: Activists use dramatic actions to gain media coverage.

social media campaign

IPA: /ˌsəʊʃəl ˈmiːdiə kæmˌpeɪn/
Pronunciation: SOH-shuhl MEE-dee-uh kam-PAYN
French: campagne sur les réseaux sociaux

Definition: A campaign spread through social media platforms.

Example: A social media campaign can mobilise people quickly.

hashtag activism

IPA: /ˈhæʃtæɡ ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: HASH-tag AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme par hashtag

Definition: Online activism organised around hashtags.

Example: Hashtag activism can raise awareness but may remain superficial.

slacktivism

IPA: /ˈslæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: SLAK-ti-viz-uhm
French: militantisme paresseux ; slacktivisme

Definition: Low-effort online activism with limited real-world impact.

Example: Critics dismiss some online campaigns as slacktivism.

petition

IPA: /pəˈtɪʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-TISH-uhn
French: pétition

Definition: A formal written request signed by many people.

Example: The petition demanded stronger air quality rules.

boycott

IPA: /ˈbɔɪkɒt/
Pronunciation: BOY-kot
French: boycott

Definition: Refusing to buy or use something as protest.

Example: Consumers organised a boycott of the polluting company.

consumer activism

IPA: /kənˈsjuːmər ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SYOO-muh AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme consommateur

Definition: Using buying choices to support political or ethical goals.

Example: Consumer activism can pressure brands.

ethical consumption

IPA: /ˌeθɪkəl kənˈsʌmpʃən/
Pronunciation: ETH-i-kuhl kuhn-SUMP-shuhn
French: consommation éthique

Definition: Buying goods according to moral or environmental values.

Example: Ethical consumption has limits when sustainable options are expensive.

green consumerism

IPA: /ˌɡriːn kənˈsjuːmərɪzəm/
Pronunciation: green kuhn-SYOO-muh-riz-uhm
French: consommation verte

Definition: Buying environmentally friendly products as a way to act.

Example: Green consumerism can shift blame from companies to individuals.

lobbying

IPA: /ˈlɒbiɪŋ/
Pronunciation: LOB-ee-ing
French: lobbying

Definition: Trying to influence political decisions.

Example: Environmental lobbying competes with fossil fuel lobbying.

policy advocacy

IPA: /ˈpɒləsi ˈædvəkəsi/
Pronunciation: POL-uh-see AD-vuh-kuh-see
French: plaidoyer politique

Definition: Arguing for specific policy changes.

Example: Policy advocacy requires evidence and political strategy.

environmental regulation

IPA: /ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmentəl ˌreɡjʊˈleɪʃən/
Pronunciation: in-vy-ruhn-MEN-tuhl reg-yuh-LAY-shuhn
French: réglementation environnementale

Definition: Rules protecting the environment from harm.

Example: Environmental regulation can limit pollution.

political will

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl ˈwɪl/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl wil
French: volonté politique

Definition: The determination of leaders to take action.

Example: Activists argue that the problem is political will, not technology.

green party

IPA: /ˈɡriːn ˌpɑːti/
Pronunciation: green PAR-tee
French: parti écologiste

Definition: A political party focused on environmental issues.

Example: Green parties have influenced European politics.

green politics

IPA: /ˌɡriːn ˈpɒlətɪks/
Pronunciation: green POL-uh-tiks
French: politique écologiste

Definition: Political ideas and movements focused on ecology and sustainability.

Example: Green politics connects climate, biodiversity and social justice.

degrowth

IPA: /ˌdiːˈɡrəʊθ/
Pronunciation: dee-GROHTH
French: décroissance

Definition: The idea that wealthy societies should reduce production and consumption.

Example: Degrowth challenges growth-based economic models.

green growth

IPA: /ˌɡriːn ˈɡrəʊθ/
Pronunciation: green grohth
French: croissance verte

Definition: Economic growth compatible with environmental protection.

Example: Green growth is defended by many governments and businesses.

system change

IPA: /ˈsɪstəm tʃeɪndʒ/
Pronunciation: SIS-tuhm chaynj
French: changement systémique

Definition: Deep transformation of economic, political or social structures.

Example: Many activists argue that system change is needed.

individual action

IPA: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl ˈækʃən/
Pronunciation: in-di-VIJ-oo-uhl AK-shuhn
French: action individuelle

Definition: Action taken by individuals, such as reducing consumption.

Example: Individual action matters, but cannot replace policy change.

collective action

IPA: /kəˌlektɪv ˈækʃən/
Pronunciation: kuh-LEK-tiv AK-shuhn
French: action collective

Definition: Action taken together by groups or societies.

Example: Collective action can change laws and markets.

structural change

IPA: /ˌstrʌktʃərəl ˈtʃeɪndʒ/
Pronunciation: STRUK-chuh-ruhl chaynj
French: changement structurel

Definition: Deep change in systems, institutions or economic structures.

Example: Structural change is needed to reduce emissions at scale.

right to protest

IPA: /ˌraɪt tə ˈprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: right tuh PROH-test
French: droit de manifester

Definition: The right to publicly oppose or demand change.

Example: The right to protest is central to democracy.

freedom of assembly

IPA: /ˌfriːdəm əv əˈsembli/
Pronunciation: FREE-duhm ov uh-SEM-blee
French: liberté de réunion

Definition: The right to gather peacefully in public.

Example: Freedom of assembly protects demonstrations.

D. Rights, backlash, litigation and frontline struggles

public order

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik OR-duh
French: ordre public

Definition: Social peace and lawful behaviour in public spaces.

Example: Governments often invoke public order to restrict protests.

criminalisation of protest

IPA: /ˌkrɪmɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈprəʊtest/
Pronunciation: krim-i-nuh-ly-ZAY-shuhn ov PROH-test
French: criminalisation de la contestation

Definition: Treating protest actions as criminal offences.

Example: Activists warn against the criminalisation of protest.

public backlash

IPA: /ˌpʌblɪk ˈbæklæʃ/
Pronunciation: PUB-lik BAK-lash
French: réaction hostile du public

Definition: A strong negative reaction from the public.

Example: Disruptive tactics can trigger public backlash.

legitimacy

IPA: /lɪˈdʒɪtɪməsi/
Pronunciation: li-JIT-i-muh-see
French: légitimité

Definition: The quality of being accepted as reasonable, lawful or justified.

Example: The legitimacy of civil disobedience is debated.

radicalisation

IPA: /ˌrædɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: rad-i-kuh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: radicalisation

Definition: Becoming more extreme in beliefs or tactics.

Example: Government inaction can fuel activist radicalisation.

eco-sabotage

IPA: /ˌiːkəʊ ˈsæbətɑːʒ/
Pronunciation: EE-koh SAB-uh-tahzh
French: éco-sabotage

Definition: Sabotage carried out for environmental aims.

Example: Eco-sabotage is illegal and highly controversial.

radical flank

IPA: /ˌrædɪkəl ˈflæŋk/
Pronunciation: RAD-i-kuhl flank
French: aile radicale

Definition: The more radical part of a movement.

Example: A radical flank can affect how moderates are perceived.

greenwashing

IPA: /ˈɡriːnˌwɒʃɪŋ/
Pronunciation: GREEN-wosh-ing
French: greenwashing ; écoblanchiment

Definition: Misleading environmental claims made to improve reputation.

Example: Activists expose corporate greenwashing.

corporate accountability

IPA: /ˈkɔːpərət əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: KOR-puh-ruht uh-kown-tuh-BIL-uh-tee
French: responsabilité des entreprises

Definition: Responsibility of companies for their actions and harm.

Example: Environmental activism demands corporate accountability.

shareholder activism

IPA: /ˈʃeəhəʊldə ˌæktɪvɪzəm/
Pronunciation: SHARE-hohl-duh AK-ti-viz-uhm
French: activisme actionnarial

Definition: Using shareholder power to pressure companies.

Example: Shareholder activism can push companies to cut emissions.

divestment

IPA: /daɪˈvestmənt/
Pronunciation: dy-VEST-muhnt
French: désinvestissement

Definition: Selling investments for ethical, political or financial reasons.

Example: Divestment campaigns target fossil fuel companies.

fossil fuel divestment

IPA: /ˈfɒsəl ˌfjuːəl daɪˈvestmənt/
Pronunciation: FOS-uhl FYOO-uhl dy-VEST-muhnt
French: désinvestissement des énergies fossiles

Definition: Selling investments in fossil fuel companies.

Example: Universities have faced fossil fuel divestment campaigns.

climate litigation

Pronunciation: KLY-muht lit-i-GAY-shuhn
French: contentieux climatique

Definition: Legal action related to climate change.

Example: Climate litigation is increasingly used by activists.

strategic litigation

IPA: /strəˌtiːdʒɪk ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: struh-TEE-jik lit-i-GAY-shuhn
French: contentieux stratégique

IPA: /ˈlɔːsuːt/

French: procès ; action en justice

legal challenge

French: recours juridique

French: étude d’impact environnemental

public consultation

French: consultation publique

participatory democracy

extractivism

Pronunciation: ik-STRAK-ti-viz-uhm
French: extractivisme

Definition: An economic model based on large-scale extraction of natural resources.

Example: Environmental activists criticise extractivism.

Pronunciation: AN-tee ik-STRAK-shuhn MOOV-muhnt
French: mouvement anti-extraction

Definition: A movement opposing mining, drilling or resource extraction.
