Chapter objective

This premium grammar chapter helps CPGE students master the Present Continuous. It covers current actions, temporary situations, ongoing changes, future arrangements, question forms, negatives and the key contrast with the Present Simple.

The golden rule

Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing
The government is preparing a new reform.
Main useActions happening now, temporary situations, ongoing changes, current debates and planned future arrangements.
CPGE habitUse the Present Continuous to describe what is changing now: Inequality is rising, trust is declining, platforms are transforming public debate.
Key trapThe Present Continuous always needs be: The article is discussing..., not The article discussing...

A. Lesson — What is the Present Continuous?

Present Continuous

French: présent continu

Rule / explanation: The Present Continuous is used for actions happening now, temporary situations, ongoing changes and planned future arrangements.

Example / answer: The government is negotiating a new agreement.

Action happening now

French: action en cours

Rule / explanation: Use it for something happening at or around the moment of speaking.

Example / answer: The journalist is interviewing climate activists.

Temporary situation

French: situation temporaire

Rule / explanation: Use it for something true for a limited time, not permanently.

Example / answer: Many students are preparing for their entrance exams.

Ongoing change

French: évolution en cours

Rule / explanation: Use it to describe trends currently developing.

Example / answer: Housing costs are rising in many major cities.

Current debate

French: débat actuel

Rule / explanation: Use it for issues being discussed at the moment.

Example / answer: Politicians are debating the future of public healthcare.

Future arrangement

French: futur arrangé

Rule / explanation: Use it for planned future events, especially with a time expression.

Example / answer: The Prime Minister is meeting EU leaders tomorrow.

Now vs generally

French: maintenant vs général

Rule / explanation: Present Continuous = temporary or current. Present Simple = general or repeated.

Example / answer: The article is discussing today’s crisis. / The newspaper discusses politics every week.

Press article use

French: usage presse

Rule / explanation: Journalists often use it for developing stories and current controversies.

Example / answer: The scandal is putting pressure on the minister.

B. Form — Affirmative sentences

I am + V-ing

French: je suis en train de

Rule / explanation: Use am with I.

Example / answer: I am analysing the article.

You / We / They are + V-ing

French: tu/nous/vous/ils sont en train de

Rule / explanation: Use are with plural or second-person subjects.

Example / answer: Young voters are demanding change.

He / She / It is + V-ing

French: il/elle est en train de

Rule / explanation: Use is with third-person singular subjects.

Example / answer: The government is facing criticism.

Verb + -ing

French: forme en -ing

Rule / explanation: Add -ing to the base verb.

Example / answer: debate → debating

Silent -e

French: e muet supprimé

Rule / explanation: Drop final silent -e before -ing.

Example / answer: make → making / take → taking

One vowel + one consonant

French: double consonne

Rule / explanation: Double the final consonant in short stressed verbs.

Example / answer: run → running / cut → cutting

Verbs ending in -ie

French: -ie devient -ying

Rule / explanation: Change -ie to -ying.

Example / answer: lie → lying

Be + adjective

French: être + adjectif

Rule / explanation: Do not use -ing with adjectives; use be + adjective.

Example / answer: The issue is complex, not “is complexing”.

C. Negative and question forms

Negative form

French: négation

Rule / explanation: Use am not / is not / are not + V-ing.

Example / answer: The article is not focusing on economic causes.

Contractions

French: formes contractées

Rule / explanation: Use isn't / aren't in speech or informal writing.

Example / answer: The government isn't listening to young voters.

Question form

French: question

Rule / explanation: Put am / is / are before the subject.

Example / answer: Is the government listening?

Wh- questions

French: questions en wh-

Rule / explanation: Wh-word + am/is/are + subject + V-ing.

Example / answer: Why are voters losing trust?

Short answers

French: réponses courtes

Rule / explanation: Use yes/no + subject + am/is/are.

Example / answer: Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

No do with continuous

French: pas de do

Rule / explanation: Do not use do/does/did with the Present Continuous.

Example / answer: Wrong: Does the government is changing? Correct: Is the government changing?

Question with future meaning

French: question futur arrangé

Rule / explanation: Use am/is/are + subject + V-ing for planned arrangements.

Example / answer: Is the minister speaking tomorrow?

Negative future arrangement

French: négation futur arrangé

Rule / explanation: Use am/is/are not + V-ing.

Example / answer: The committee is not meeting next week.

D. Present Continuous vs Present Simple

General fact vs current action

French: fait général vs action actuelle

Rule / explanation: Present Simple for general facts; Present Continuous for current actions.

Example / answer: The press influences voters. / The press is covering the scandal.

Habit vs temporary situation

French: habitude vs temporaire

Rule / explanation: Present Simple for habits; Present Continuous for temporary situations.

Example / answer: Students read articles weekly. / Students are reading this article today.

Stable situation vs change

French: stable vs changement

Rule / explanation: Present Simple for stable facts; Present Continuous for ongoing change.

Example / answer: The UK has an uncodified constitution. / The UK is debating constitutional reform.

State verbs

French: verbes d’état

Rule / explanation: Many state verbs usually take the Present Simple, not Continuous.

Example / answer: Wrong: I am believing. Correct: I believe.

Think

French: penser

Rule / explanation: Use think simple for opinion; continuous for mental activity.

Example / answer: I think the argument is weak. / I am thinking about the question.

Have

French: avoir

Rule / explanation: Use have simple for possession; continuous for activities.

Example / answer: The article has three parts. / The class is having a debate.

Look

French: look

Rule / explanation: Use look simple for appearance; continuous for active looking.

Example / answer: The policy looks unfair. / The journalist is looking at the data.

Current trends

French: tendances actuelles

Rule / explanation: Use continuous for changing trends.

Example / answer: Trust in institutions is declining.

E. Common CPGE uses

Describing current affairs

French: actualité

Rule / explanation: Use it for events happening in the news now.

Example / answer: The country is facing a constitutional crisis.

Analysing developing trends

French: tendances en cours

Rule / explanation: Use it for changes still in progress.

Example / answer: Political polarisation is increasing.

Commenting on a graph

French: graphique

Rule / explanation: Use it for trends visible over a current period.

Example / answer: The unemployment rate is falling gradually.

Describing temporary political pressure

French: pression temporaire

Rule / explanation: Use it for temporary situations around an event.

Example / answer: The scandal is damaging the minister’s reputation.

Future arrangements in politics

French: arrangements futurs

Rule / explanation: Use it for planned meetings, speeches or votes.

Example / answer: Parliament is voting on the bill next week.

Contrasting now and generally

French: contraste présent/général

Rule / explanation: Useful in nuanced analysis.

Example / answer: The party usually supports free trade, but it is currently defending protectionism.

Press review sentence

French: revue de presse

Rule / explanation: Use it for a developing story.

Example / answer: The article explains why unions are protesting.

Essay introduction

French: introduction

Rule / explanation: Use it to describe present-day transformations.

Example / answer: Democracies are struggling to regulate digital platforms.

F. Typical mistakes by French speakers

Using Present Simple for now

French: présent simple abusif

Rule / explanation: Use Present Continuous for an action happening now.

Example / answer: Wrong: The government negotiates now. Correct: The government is negotiating now.

Using Continuous for general facts

French: présent continu abusif

Rule / explanation: Use Present Simple for permanent or general facts.

Example / answer: Wrong: The UK is having an uncodified constitution. Correct: The UK has an uncodified constitution.

Forgetting be

French: oubli de be

Rule / explanation: The Present Continuous needs am/is/are.

Example / answer: Wrong: The article discussing. Correct: The article is discussing.

Using do in questions

French: do incorrect

Rule / explanation: Continuous questions use am/is/are, not do.

Example / answer: Wrong: Does the article is explaining? Correct: Is the article explaining?

Wrong -ing spelling

French: orthographe -ing

Rule / explanation: Watch verbs ending in -e or short stressed vowels.

Example / answer: Wrong: makeing. Correct: making.

State verbs in continuous

French: verbes d’état

Rule / explanation: Avoid continuous with know, believe, understand, need, seem.

Example / answer: Wrong: The author is believing. Correct: The author believes.

Confusing future forms

French: futur arrangé

Rule / explanation: Present Continuous future needs a planned arrangement.

Example / answer: Correct: The minister is meeting voters tomorrow.

Overusing “being”

French: being abusif

Rule / explanation: Do not translate French “être en train de” mechanically.

Example / answer: Wrong: The article is being talk about. Correct: The article is talking about / discussing.

G. Exercises

Exercise 1 — Add the correct form

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: The government ___ (face) strong criticism this week.

Example / answer: Answer: The government is facing strong criticism this week.

Exercise 2 — Add the correct form

French: exercice

Rule / explanation: Young people ___ (demand) stronger climate policies.

Example / answer: Answer: Young people are demanding stronger climate policies.

Example / answer: Answer: The journalist is not defending the reform.

Example / answer: Answer: Is the government preparing a new law?
