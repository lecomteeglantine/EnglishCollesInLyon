A. Core devolution vocabulary and constitutional concepts

devolution

IPA: /ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn
French: dévolution

Definition: The transfer of powers from central government to regional or national governments within a state.

Example: Devolution has transformed the constitutional structure of the United Kingdom.

devolved power

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD POW-uh
French: pouvoir dévolu

Definition: A power transferred from Westminster to a devolved administration.

Example: Health and education are devolved powers in Scotland.

devolved administration

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD ad-min-i-STRAY-shuhn
French: administration dévolue

Definition: A government in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland with powers transferred from Westminster.

Example: Devolved administrations can make policy in areas such as health and education.

devolved legislature

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ˈledʒɪslətʃə/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD LEDJ-is-luh-chuh
French: assemblée législative dévolue

Definition: A law-making body with powers transferred from Westminster.

Example: The Scottish Parliament is a devolved legislature.

reserved matter

IPA: /rɪˌzɜːvd ˈmætə/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURVD MAT-uh
French: compétence réservée

Definition: A policy area kept under Westminster control rather than devolved.

Example: Defence and immigration are reserved matters.

devolved matter

IPA: /dɪˌvɒlvd ˈmætə/
Pronunciation: di-VOLVD MAT-uh
French: compétence dévolue

Definition: A policy area controlled by a devolved administration.

Example: Education is a devolved matter in Scotland and Wales.

reserved powers

IPA: /rɪˌzɜːvd ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURVD POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs réservés

Definition: Powers kept by the central UK Parliament or government.

Example: Reserved powers remain under Westminster control.

transferred powers

IPA: /trænsˌfɜːd ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: trans-FURD POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs transférés

Definition: Powers moved from central government to a devolved institution.

Example: Transferred powers allow more local decision-making.

asymmetrical devolution

IPA: /ˌeɪsɪˈmetrɪkəl ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: ay-si-MET-ri-kuhl dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn
French: dévolution asymétrique

Definition: A system in which different parts of a state receive different levels of power.

Example: The UK has asymmetrical devolution because Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland do not have identical powers.

centralisation

IPA: /ˌsentrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: sen-truh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: centralisation

Definition: The concentration of power in central government.

Example: Britain has historically been a highly centralised state.

decentralisation

IPA: /ˌdiːsentrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: dee-sen-truh-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: décentralisation

Definition: The transfer or spread of power away from central government.

Example: Devolution is a form of decentralisation.

unitary state

IPA: /ˌjuːnɪtəri ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: YOO-ni-tuh-ree state
French: État unitaire

Definition: A state in which ultimate legal authority belongs to the central government.

Example: The UK remains a unitary state despite devolution.

federal state

IPA: /ˌfedərəl ˈsteɪt/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruhl state
French: État fédéral

Definition: A state where power is constitutionally divided between central and regional governments.

Example: The UK is not a federal state like the United States or Germany.

federalism

IPA: /ˈfedərəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: FED-uh-ruh-liz-uhm
French: fédéralisme

Definition: A system in which power is divided between national and regional governments.

Example: Some argue that federalism could solve the UK’s constitutional tensions.

quasi-federal

IPA: /ˌkweɪzaɪ ˈfedərəl/
Pronunciation: KWAY-zy FED-uh-ruhl
French: quasi fédéral

Definition: Partly similar to federalism without being fully federal.

Example: The UK has sometimes been described as quasi-federal.

multinational state

IPA: /ˌmʌltiˈnæʃənəl steɪt/
Pronunciation: MUL-tee-NASH-uh-nuhl state
French: État multinational

Definition: A state made up of several nations.

Example: The UK is a multinational state composed of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

four nations

IPA: /ˌfɔː ˈneɪʃənz/
Pronunciation: four NAY-shuhnz
French: quatre nations

Definition: England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland within the UK.

Example: Devolution reflects the different identities of the four nations.

Union

IPA: /ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: YOO-nee-uhn
French: Union britannique

Definition: The political union of the UK’s nations.

Example: Devolution has both preserved and challenged the Union.

Act of Union

IPA: /ˌækt əv ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: akt ov YOO-nee-uhn
French: Acte d’Union

Definition: A law creating or formalising a political union, especially between England and Scotland in 1707.

Example: The Act of Union created the Kingdom of Great Britain.

Westminster

IPA: /ˈwestmɪnstə/
Pronunciation: WEST-min-stuh
French: Westminster ; pouvoir central britannique

Definition: The UK Parliament and central government, based in London.

Example: Westminster retains sovereignty over devolved institutions.

parliamentary sovereignty

IPA: /ˌpɑːləˈmentəri ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: par-luh-MEN-tuh-ree SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté parlementaire

Definition: The principle that the UK Parliament can make or unmake any law.

Example: Parliamentary sovereignty means Westminster can legally alter devolved powers.

constitutional settlement

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl ˈsetəlmənt/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl SET-uhl-muhnt
French: arrangement constitutionnel

Definition: The set of rules and arrangements defining how political power is organised.

Example: Devolution created a new constitutional settlement.

devolution settlement

IPA: /ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən ˈsetəlmənt/
Pronunciation: dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn SET-uhl-muhnt
French: accord de dévolution ; cadre de dévolution

Definition: The legal and political arrangements governing devolved powers.

Example: The devolution settlement is different in each part of the UK.

constitutional reform

IPA: /ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃənəl rɪˈfɔːm/
Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl ri-FORM
French: réforme constitutionnelle

Definition: Changes to the rules and institutions of government.

Example: Devolution was part of wider constitutional reform under New Labour.

New Labour

IPA: /ˌnjuː ˈleɪbə/
Pronunciation: nyoo LAY-buh
French: New Labour

Definition: The modernised Labour Party associated with Tony Blair in the late 1990s and 2000s.

Example: New Labour introduced devolution after its 1997 election victory.

referendum

IPA: /ˌrefəˈrendəm/
Pronunciation: ref-uh-REN-duhm
French: référendum

Definition: A direct vote by citizens on a specific political question.

Example: Devolution was approved by referendums in Scotland and Wales.

devolution referendum

IPA: /ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən ˌrefəˈrendəm/
Pronunciation: dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn ref-uh-REN-duhm
French: référendum sur la dévolution

Definition: A referendum asking voters whether powers should be transferred to a devolved institution.

Example: The 1997 devolution referendum led to the creation of the Scottish Parliament.

Scottish Parliament

IPA: /ˌskɒtɪʃ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: SKOT-ish PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement écossais

Definition: The devolved legislature of Scotland.

Example: The Scottish Parliament can pass laws in devolved areas.

B. Scotland: Holyrood, the SNP and independence

Holyrood

IPA: /ˈhɒliruːd/
Pronunciation: HOL-i-rood
French: Holyrood ; Parlement écossais

Definition: The Scottish Parliament, named after its location in Edinburgh.

Example: Holyrood has become the centre of Scottish political life.

Scottish Government

IPA: /ˌskɒtɪʃ ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: SKOT-ish GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: gouvernement écossais

Definition: The devolved government of Scotland.

Example: The Scottish Government manages health, education and justice in Scotland.

First Minister of Scotland

IPA: /ˌfɜːst ˈmɪnɪstə əv ˈskɒtlənd/
Pronunciation: first MIN-i-stuh ov SKOT-luhnd
French: Premier ministre d’Écosse

Definition: The head of the Scottish Government.

Example: The First Minister of Scotland leads the devolved government.

Scottish National Party

IPA: /ˌskɒtɪʃ ˈnæʃənəl ˈpɑːti/
Pronunciation: SKOT-ish NASH-uh-nuhl PAR-tee
French: Parti national écossais ; SNP

Definition: A party supporting Scottish self-government and independence.

Example: The Scottish National Party has used devolution to push for independence.

SNP

IPA: /ˌes en ˈpiː/
Pronunciation: ess en pee
French: SNP

Definition: Abbreviation for the Scottish National Party.

Example: The SNP argues that Scotland should have the right to choose independence.

Scottish independence

IPA: /ˌskɒtɪʃ ˌɪndɪˈpendəns/
Pronunciation: SKOT-ish in-di-PEN-duhns
French: indépendance écossaise

Definition: The idea that Scotland should become an independent country.

Example: Scottish independence remains a major challenge to the Union.

independence referendum

IPA: /ˌɪndɪˈpendəns ˌrefəˈrendəm/
Pronunciation: in-di-PEN-duhns ref-uh-REN-duhm
French: référendum d’indépendance

Definition: A referendum on whether a territory should become independent.

Example: The 2014 independence referendum kept Scotland in the UK.

indyref2

IPA: /ˈɪndi ref tuː/
Pronunciation: IN-dee ref too
French: deuxième référendum d’indépendance écossais

Definition: Informal term for a proposed second Scottish independence referendum.

Example: The SNP has repeatedly called for indyref2.

self-determination

IPA: /ˌself dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən/
Pronunciation: self di-tur-mi-NAY-shuhn
French: autodétermination

Definition: The right of a people or nation to decide its political future.

Example: Scottish nationalists frame independence as self-determination.

Welsh Parliament

IPA: /ˌwelʃ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: Welsh PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement gallois ; Senedd

Definition: The devolved legislature of Wales, also known as the Senedd.

Example: The Welsh Parliament has gained more powers over time.

Northern Ireland Assembly

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd əˈsembli/

Stormont

IPA: /ˈstɔːmənt/
Pronunciation: STOR-muhnt
French: Stormont ; institutions nord-irlandaises

Definition: The Northern Ireland Assembly and executive, named after its location near Belfast.

Northern Ireland Executive

IPA: /ˌnɔːðən ˈaɪələnd ɪɡˈzekjʊtɪv/
Pronunciation: NOR-thuhn EYE-uh-luhnd ig-ZEK-yuh-tiv
French: Exécutif d’Irlande du Nord

Definition: The devolved government of Northern Ireland.

power-sharing

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ˌʃeərɪŋ/
Pronunciation: POW-uh shair-ing
French: partage du pouvoir

Definition: A system in which rival groups share government power.

Example: Power-sharing is central to Northern Ireland’s devolved institutions.

consociationalism

IPA: /kənˌsəʊʃiˈeɪʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: kuhn-soh-shee-AY-shuh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: consociationalisme ; démocratie consociative

Definition: A system where different communities share power to manage deep divisions.

Example: Northern Ireland uses consociationalism to balance unionist and nationalist communities.

Good Friday Agreement

IPA: /ˌɡʊd ˈfraɪdeɪ əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: good FRY-day uh-GREE-muhnt
French: Accord du Vendredi saint

Definition: The 1998 peace agreement that created power-sharing institutions in Northern Ireland.

Example: The Good Friday Agreement is the foundation of Northern Ireland devolution.

Belfast Agreement

IPA: /ˌbelfɑːst əˈɡriːmənt/
Pronunciation: BEL-fast uh-GREE-muhnt
French: Accord de Belfast

Definition: Another name for the Good Friday Agreement.

Example: The Belfast Agreement helped end decades of conflict.

unionist

IPA: /ˈjuːniənɪst/
Pronunciation: YOO-nee-uhn-ist
French: unioniste

Definition: A person who supports Northern Ireland remaining part of the UK.

Example: Unionists defend Northern Ireland’s place in the Union.

nationalist

IPA: /ˈnæʃənəlɪst/
Pronunciation: NASH-uh-nuh-list
French: nationaliste irlandais

Definition: In Northern Ireland, a person who supports Irish national identity and often Irish unity.

Example: Nationalists generally support closer ties with the Republic of Ireland.

Irish unity

IPA: /ˌaɪrɪʃ ˈjuːnəti/
Pronunciation: EYE-rish YOO-nuh-tee
French: unité irlandaise

Definition: The idea that Northern Ireland should join the Republic of Ireland.

Example: Brexit renewed debates about Irish unity.

border poll

IPA: /ˈbɔːdə pəʊl/
Pronunciation: BOR-duh pohl
French: référendum sur l’unification irlandaise

Definition: A referendum on whether Northern Ireland should remain in the UK or join a united Ireland.

Example: A border poll could be held if support for Irish unity appears likely.

direct rule

IPA: /dəˌrekt ˈruːl/
Pronunciation: di-REKT rool
French: administration directe depuis Londres

Definition: Governance of Northern Ireland directly from Westminster when devolved institutions collapse.

Example: Direct rule has returned during periods of political deadlock.

English question

IPA: /ˌɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈkwestʃən/
Pronunciation: ING-glish KWES-chuhn
French: question anglaise

Definition: The constitutional problem of how England should be governed within a devolved UK.

Example: The English question remains unresolved.

D. England, regional devolution and local government

English votes for English laws

IPA: /ˌɪŋɡlɪʃ vəʊts fər ˌɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈlɔːz/
Pronunciation: ING-glish vohts for ING-glish lawz
French: votes anglais pour les lois anglaises

Definition: A former procedure limiting some votes on England-only legislation to English MPs.

Example: English votes for English laws tried to address the English question.

English Parliament

IPA: /ˌɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈpɑːləmənt/
Pronunciation: ING-glish PAR-luh-muhnt
French: Parlement anglais

Definition: A proposed separate parliament for England.

Example: Some argue that an English Parliament would balance devolution.

regional devolution

IPA: /ˌriːdʒənəl ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: REE-juh-nuhl dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn
French: dévolution régionale

Definition: The transfer of powers to regions within England.

Example: Regional devolution in England has been uneven.

metro mayor

IPA: /ˌmetrəʊ ˈmeə/
Pronunciation: MET-roh MAY-uh
French: maire métropolitain

Definition: An elected mayor leading a combined authority in an English city-region.

Example: Metro mayors control transport, planning and local economic strategy.

combined authority

IPA: /kəmˌbaɪnd ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: kuhm-BYND aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: autorité combinée

Definition: A group of local councils working together under a shared governance structure.

Example: Combined authorities are central to English regional devolution.

city-region

IPA: /ˈsɪti ˌriːdʒən/
Pronunciation: SIT-ee REE-juhn
French: région urbaine

Definition: A city and its surrounding economic area.

Example: City-region devolution aims to boost local growth.

levelling up

IPA: /ˌlevəlɪŋ ˈʌp/
Pronunciation: LEV-uhl-ing up
French: rééquilibrage territorial ; levelling up

Definition: A policy slogan about reducing regional inequality.

Example: Levelling up was linked to English regional devolution.

regional inequality

IPA: /ˌriːdʒənəl ˌɪnɪˈkwɒləti/
Pronunciation: REE-juh-nuhl in-i-KWOL-uh-tee
French: inégalité régionale

Definition: Unequal wealth, services or opportunities between regions.

Example: Regional inequality strengthens demands for more local power.

local government

IPA: /ˌləʊkəl ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: LOH-kuhl GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: collectivités locales ; gouvernement local

Definition: Government at city, county or district level.

Example: Local government in England has fewer powers than devolved administrations.

local authority

IPA: /ˌləʊkəl ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: LOH-kuhl aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: collectivité locale ; autorité locale

Definition: A local council responsible for services in an area.

Example: Local authorities deliver social care, housing and waste services.

subsidiarity

IPA: /səbˌsɪdiˈærəti/
Pronunciation: suhb-sid-ee-ARR-uh-tee
French: subsidiarité

Definition: The principle that decisions should be taken as closely as possible to citizens.

Example: Subsidiarity is often used to justify devolution.

self-government

IPA: /ˌself ˈɡʌvənmənt/
Pronunciation: self GUV-uhn-muhnt
French: autonomie gouvernementale

Definition: The ability of a region or nation to govern its own affairs.

Example: Devolution gives Scotland a degree of self-government.

home rule

IPA: /ˌhəʊm ˈruːl/
Pronunciation: home rool
French: autonomie politique ; home rule

Definition: The right of a region or nation to govern itself within a larger state.

Example: Home rule has a long history in Irish and Scottish debates.

autonomy

IPA: /ɔːˈtɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: aw-TON-uh-mee
French: autonomie

Definition: The ability to make decisions independently.

Example: Devolution grants limited autonomy to the UK’s nations.

fiscal devolution

IPA: /ˌfɪskəl ˌdiːvəˈluːʃən/
Pronunciation: FIS-kuhl dee-vuh-LOO-shuhn
French: dévolution fiscale

Definition: The transfer of tax and spending powers to devolved governments.

Example: Fiscal devolution gives Scotland some tax powers.

tax-raising powers

IPA: /ˈtæks reɪzɪŋ ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: taks RAY-zing POW-uhz
French: pouvoirs fiscaux

Definition: The authority to create or vary taxes.

Example: Tax-raising powers make devolved governments more accountable.

Pronunciation: BAR-nit FOR-myuh-luh
French: formule Barnett

Definition: The formula used to allocate public spending to Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Example: The Barnett formula is often criticised as outdated.

policy divergence

postcode lottery

French: loterie du code postal ; inégalités territoriales

constitutional tension

Pronunciation: kon-sti-TOO-shuh-nuhl TEN-shuhn
French: tension constitutionnelle

Definition: A conflict or strain within constitutional arrangements.

Example: Brexit created constitutional tension between Westminster and Holyrood.

intergovernmental relations

Pronunciation: IN-tuh-guv-uhn-MEN-tuhl ri-LAY-shuhnz
French: relations intergouvernementales

legislative consent motion

Pronunciation: LEDJ-is-luh-tiv kuhn-SENT MOH-shuhn
French: motion de consentement législatif

Definition: A motion by which a devolved legislature agrees that Westminster may legislate on a devolved matter.

Example: Legislative consent motions became controversial during Brexit.

IPA: /ˈsuːəl kənˌvenʃən/
Pronunciation: SOO-uhl kuhn-VEN-shuhn
French: Convention Sewel

Definition: The convention that Westminster will not normally legislate on devolved matters without consent.

Example: The Sewel Convention has been tested repeatedly since Brexit.

to devolve power

IPA: /dɪˈvɒlv ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: di-VOLV POW-uh
French: dévoluer le pouvoir

Definition: To transfer power from central government to a lower or regional level.

Example: The UK devolved power to Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

to transfer powers

IPA: /trænsˈfɜː ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: trans-FUR POW-uhz
French: transférer des pouvoirs

Definition: To move powers from one institution to another.

Example: The reform transferred powers to the Welsh Parliament.

to reserve powers

IPA: /rɪˈzɜːv ˈpaʊəz/
Pronunciation: ri-ZURV POW-uhz
French: réserver des pouvoirs

Definition: To keep certain powers under central control.

Example: The UK government reserves powers over defence and immigration.

to grant autonomy

IPA: /ɡrɑːnt ɔːˈtɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: grant aw-TON-uh-mee
French: accorder l’autonomie

Definition: To give a region or institution self-governing powers.

Example: Devolution grants autonomy without full independence.

to strengthen the Union

IPA: /ˈstreŋθən ðə ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: STRENG-thuhn thuh YOO-nee-uhn
French: renforcer l’Union

Definition: To make the UK more stable and united.

Example: Some politicians argue that devolution can strengthen the Union.

to weaken the Union

IPA: /ˈwiːkən ðə ˈjuːniən/
Pronunciation: WEE-kuhn thuh YOO-nee-uhn
French: affaiblir l’Union

Definition: To make the UK less stable or more divided.

Example: Critics say devolution has weakened the Union.

to demand independence

IPA: /dɪˈmɑːnd ˌɪndɪˈpendəns/
Pronunciation: di-MAND in-di-PEN-duhns
French: réclamer l’indépendance

Definition: To call for a nation or region to become independent.

Example: Scottish nationalists continue to demand independence.

to challenge Westminster authority

IPA: /ˈtʃælɪndʒ ˈwestmɪnstər ɔːˈθɒrəti/
Pronunciation: CHAL-inj WEST-min-stuh aw-THOR-uh-tee
French: contester l’autorité de Westminster

Definition: To question or oppose the power of the UK central government.

Example: Devolved governments sometimes challenge Westminster authority.

to create policy divergence

IPA: /kriˈeɪt ˈpɒləsi daɪˈvɜːdʒəns/
Pronunciation: kree-AYT POL-uh-see dy-VUR-juhns
French: créer une divergence des politiques publiques

Definition: To produce different policies in different parts of the country.

Example: Devolution can create policy divergence across the UK.

to manage territorial tensions

IPA: /ˈmænɪdʒ ˌterɪˈtɔːriəl ˈtenʃənz/
Pronunciation: MAN-ij ter-i-TOR-ee-uhl TEN-shuhnz
French: gérer les tensions territoriales

Definition: To handle conflicts between regions or nations within a state.

Example: The UK must manage territorial tensions carefully.

to reshape the constitution

IPA: /riːˈʃeɪp ðə ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: ree-SHAYP thuh kon-sti-TOO-shuhn
French: remodeler la constitution

Definition: To significantly change constitutional arrangements.

Example: Devolution has reshaped the British constitution.
