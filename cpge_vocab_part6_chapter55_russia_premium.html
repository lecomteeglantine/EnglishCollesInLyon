Chapter objective

This premium chapter gives CPGE students the essential vocabulary needed to analyse Russia: authoritarianism, Putinism, propaganda, the post-Soviet space, Ukraine, Crimea, hybrid warfare, energy dependency, sanctions, nuclear deterrence, cyber operations, disinformation and Russia’s challenge to the European security order.

How to use this chapter

For press articlesFocus on terms such as Kremlin, authoritarian regime, sphere of influence, NATO enlargement, full-scale invasion, hybrid warfare, energy dependency, sanctions evasion, nuclear sabre-rattling and disinformation.
For oral examsUse this vocabulary to explain Russia as an authoritarian great power seeking security, influence and status, while using energy, military force, cyber tools and propaganda to shape its environment.
For essaysMemorise the collocations: to violate sovereignty, to weaponise energy, to impose sanctions, to deter Russia, to rebuild European security.

A. Core Russia, regime and domestic politics

Russia

IPA: /ˈrʌʃə/
Pronunciation: RUSH-uh
French: Russie

Definition: A major Eurasian state with significant military, energy and geopolitical power.

Example: Russia remains a central actor in European and global security.

Russian Federation

IPA: /ˌrʌʃən ˌfedəˈreɪʃən/
Pronunciation: RUSH-uhn fed-uh-RAY-shuhn
French: Fédération de Russie

Definition: The official name of Russia.

Example: The Russian Federation inherited much of the Soviet Union’s international status.

Kremlin

IPA: /ˈkremlɪn/
Pronunciation: KREM-lin
French: Kremlin

Definition: The seat of Russian political power; often used to mean the Russian government.

Example: The Kremlin controls much of Russia’s political system.

Moscow

IPA: /ˈmɒskəʊ/
Pronunciation: MOS-koh
French: Moscou

Definition: Russia’s capital; often used as shorthand for the Russian government.

Example: Moscow rejected the accusations.

Vladimir Putin

IPA: /ˌvlædɪmɪə ˈpuːtɪn/
Pronunciation: VLAD-i-meer POO-tin
French: Vladimir Poutine

Definition: Russia’s long-serving president and dominant political figure.

Example: Vladimir Putin has reshaped Russia’s domestic politics and foreign policy.

Putinism

IPA: /ˈpuːtɪnɪzəm/
Pronunciation: POO-tin-iz-uhm
French: poutinisme

Definition: A term describing Russia’s political system under Vladimir Putin.

Example: Putinism combines authoritarianism, nationalism and state control.

authoritarianism

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uh-niz-uhm
French: autoritarisme

Definition: A system of government with limited political freedoms and strong central control.

Example: Russian authoritarianism has intensified over time.

authoritarian regime

IPA: /ɔːˌθɒrɪˈteəriən reɪˈʒiːm/
Pronunciation: aw-thor-i-TAIR-ee-uhn ray-ZHEEM
French: régime autoritaire

Definition: A government that restricts opposition, pluralism and civil liberties.

Example: Many analysts describe Russia as an authoritarian regime.

managed democracy

IPA: /ˌmænɪdʒd dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: MAN-ijd di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie dirigée

Definition: A political system with elections but heavy state control over outcomes.

Example: Russia is often described as a managed democracy.

illiberal democracy

IPA: /ɪˌlɪbərəl dɪˈmɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: i-LIB-uh-ruhl di-MOK-ruh-see
French: démocratie illibérale

Definition: A system with elections but weak checks, rights and freedoms.

Example: The term illiberal democracy does not fully capture Russia’s authoritarian drift.

centralised power

IPA: /ˈsentrəlaɪzd ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: SEN-truh-lyzd POW-uh
French: pouvoir centralisé

Definition: Power concentrated in a central authority or leader.

Example: Centralised power allows the Kremlin to control institutions.

power vertical

IPA: /ˈpaʊə ˌvɜːtɪkəl/
Pronunciation: POW-uh VUR-ti-kuhl
French: verticale du pouvoir

Definition: A term for Russia’s top-down system of political authority.

Example: The power vertical weakened regional autonomy.

oligarch

IPA: /ˈɒlɪɡɑːk/
Pronunciation: OL-i-gark
French: oligarque

Definition: A very wealthy business figure with political influence.

Example: Russian oligarchs often depend on political connections.

oligarchy

IPA: /ˈɒlɪɡɑːki/
Pronunciation: OL-i-gar-kee
French: oligarchie

Definition: A system where a small wealthy elite holds power.

Example: Post-Soviet privatisation created a powerful oligarchy.

elite capture

IPA: /ɪˌliːt ˈkæptʃə/
Pronunciation: i-LEET KAP-chuh
French: captation par les élites

Definition: Control of institutions or resources by a small elite.

Example: Elite capture undermines accountability.

corruption

IPA: /kəˈrʌpʃən/
Pronunciation: kuh-RUP-shuhn
French: corruption

Definition: Abuse of power for private gain.

Example: Corruption is a major issue in Russian politics and business.

kleptocracy

IPA: /klepˈtɒkrəsi/
Pronunciation: klep-TOK-ruh-see
French: kleptocratie

Definition: A system where leaders use power to steal national wealth.

Example: Critics describe Russia as a kleptocracy.

patronage network

IPA: /ˈpætrənɪdʒ ˌnetwɜːk/
Pronunciation: PAT-ruh-nij NET-work
French: réseau clientéliste

Definition: A system of favours, loyalty and rewards linking powerful actors.

Example: Patronage networks help maintain political loyalty.

security services

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊərəti ˈsɜːvɪsɪz/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR-uh-tee SUR-vis-iz
French: services de sécurité

Definition: Agencies responsible for intelligence, surveillance and internal security.

Example: The security services play a central role in Russia.

FSB

IPA: /ˌef es ˈbiː/
Pronunciation: eff-ess-bee
French: FSB

Definition: Russia’s Federal Security Service, successor to parts of the KGB.

Example: The FSB is central to internal security.

KGB

IPA: /ˌkeɪ dʒiː ˈbiː/
Pronunciation: kay-jee-bee
French: KGB

Definition: The Soviet intelligence and security agency.

Example: Many figures in Putin’s circle have KGB backgrounds.

siloviki

IPA: /ˌsiːləˈviːki/
Pronunciation: see-luh-VEE-kee
French: siloviki

Definition: Powerful Russian officials from security, military or intelligence backgrounds.

Example: The siloviki have gained influence in Russian politics.

state propaganda

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˌprɒpəˈɡændə/
Pronunciation: state prop-uh-GAN-duh
French: propagande d’État

Definition: Government-controlled information designed to influence opinion.

Example: State propaganda shapes public perceptions of the war.

state media

IPA: /ˌsteɪt ˈmiːdiə/
Pronunciation: state MEE-dee-uh
French: médias d’État

Definition: Media controlled or strongly influenced by the government.

Example: Russian state media promotes the Kremlin’s narrative.

censorship

IPA: /ˈsensəʃɪp/
Pronunciation: SEN-suh-ship
French: censure

Definition: Suppression or control of information and expression.

Example: Censorship has increased since the invasion of Ukraine.

dissent

IPA: /dɪˈsent/
Pronunciation: di-SENT
French: dissidence ; contestation

Definition: Public disagreement with official policy or authority.

Example: Dissent is often punished in authoritarian systems.

political opposition

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl ˌɒpəˈzɪʃən/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl op-uh-ZISH-uhn
French: opposition politique

Definition: Parties, movements or figures opposing the government.

Example: The political opposition has been weakened by repression.

crackdown

IPA: /ˈkrækdaʊn/
Pronunciation: KRAK-down
French: répression

Definition: Strong official action to stop dissent or protest.

Example: The crackdown on civil society intensified.

repression

IPA: /rɪˈpreʃən/
Pronunciation: ri-PRESH-uhn
French: répression

Definition: Forceful suppression of opposition, rights or freedoms.

Example: Repression makes independent politics extremely difficult.

political prisoner

IPA: /pəˌlɪtɪkəl ˈprɪzənə/
Pronunciation: puh-LIT-i-kuhl PRIZ-uh-nuh
French: prisonnier politique

Definition: A person imprisoned for political reasons.

Example: Human-rights groups denounce the detention of political prisoners.

civil society

IPA: /ˌsɪvəl səˈsaɪəti/
Pronunciation: SIV-uhl suh-SY-uh-tee
French: société civile

Definition: Independent organisations and groups outside the state and business.

Example: Civil society faces severe restrictions in Russia.

foreign agent law

IPA: /ˌfɒrən ˈeɪdʒənt lɔː/
Pronunciation: FOR-uhn AY-juhnt law
French: loi sur les agents étrangers

Definition: A law labelling some organisations or individuals as foreign agents.

Example: The foreign agent law has been used to pressure NGOs and journalists.

B. Geopolitical worldview and the post-Soviet space

sovereignty

IPA: /ˈsɒvrənti/
Pronunciation: SOV-ruhn-tee
French: souveraineté

Definition: A state’s authority to govern itself and control its territory.

Example: Russia often invokes sovereignty to reject Western criticism.

territorial integrity

IPA: /ˌterɪˈtɔːriəl ɪnˈteɡrəti/
Pronunciation: terr-i-TOR-ee-uhl in-TEG-ruh-tee
French: intégrité territoriale

Definition: The principle that a state’s borders should not be violated.

Example: The invasion violated Ukraine’s territorial integrity.

sphere of influence

IPA: /ˌsfɪər əv ˈɪnfluəns/
Pronunciation: sfeer ov IN-floo-uhns
French: sphère d’influence

Definition: A region where a powerful state seeks dominant influence.

Example: Russia seeks influence over parts of the former Soviet space.

near abroad

IPA: /ˌnɪər əˈbrɔːd/
Pronunciation: near uh-BRAWD
French: étranger proche

Definition: Russian term for former Soviet republics near Russia.

Example: Moscow sees the near abroad as strategically vital.

post-Soviet space

IPA: /ˌpəʊst ˈsəʊviət speɪs/
Pronunciation: post SOH-vee-uht space
French: espace post-soviétique

Definition: The region of former Soviet republics after 1991.

Example: The post-Soviet space remains a zone of geopolitical tension.

Eurasia

IPA: /jʊəˈreɪʒə/
Pronunciation: yoo-RAY-zhuh
French: Eurasie

Definition: The combined landmass of Europe and Asia, often used in geopolitical discourse.

Example: Russia presents itself as a Eurasian power.

Eurasianism

IPA: /jʊəˈreɪʒənɪzəm/
Pronunciation: yoo-RAY-zhuh-niz-uhm
French: eurasisme

Definition: An ideology emphasising Russia’s distinct Eurasian identity and destiny.

Example: Eurasianism rejects Western liberal universalism.

imperial nostalgia

IPA: /ɪmˌpɪəriəl nɒˈstældʒə/
Pronunciation: im-PEER-ee-uhl nos-TAL-juh
French: nostalgie impériale

Definition: Longing for the power or status of a former empire.

Example: Imperial nostalgia shapes some Russian nationalist discourse.

Russian nationalism

IPA: /ˌrʌʃən ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm/
Pronunciation: RUSH-uhn NASH-uh-nuh-liz-uhm
French: nationalisme russe

Definition: Strong identification with Russian nationhood, power and identity.

Example: Russian nationalism has been used to justify foreign policy claims.

great-power status

IPA: /ˌɡreɪt ˈpaʊə ˈsteɪtəs/
Pronunciation: great POW-uh STAY-tuhs
French: statut de grande puissance

Definition: Recognition as a major global power.

Example: Russia seeks to preserve great-power status.

revisionist power

IPA: /rɪˈvɪʒənɪst ˈpaʊə/
Pronunciation: ri-VIZH-uh-nist POW-uh
French: puissance révisionniste

Definition: A state seeking to change the existing international order.

Example: Russia is often described as a revisionist power.

rules-based order

IPA: /ˌruːlz beɪst ˈɔːdə/
Pronunciation: roolz-bayst OR-duh
French: ordre fondé sur des règles

Definition: An international system based on agreed rules rather than force.

Example: Russia is accused of undermining the rules-based order.

multipolar world

IPA: /ˌmʌltiˈpəʊlə wɜːld/
Pronunciation: MUL-tee-POH-luh world
French: monde multipolaire

Definition: An international order with several major centres of power.

Example: Russia calls for a multipolar world to challenge US dominance.

anti-Western rhetoric

IPA: /ˌænti ˈwestən ˈretərɪk/
Pronunciation: AN-tee WES-tuhn RET-uh-rik
French: rhétorique anti-occidentale

Definition: Language attacking Western policies, values or institutions.

Example: Anti-Western rhetoric is common in Russian official discourse.

Western encirclement

IPA: /ˌwestən ɪnˈsɜːkəlmənt/
Pronunciation: WES-tuhn in-SUR-kuhl-muhnt
French: encerclement occidental

Definition: The idea that the West is surrounding or threatening Russia.

Example: The Kremlin uses the narrative of Western encirclement.

NATO enlargement

IPA: /ˌneɪtəʊ ɪnˈlɑːdʒmənt/
Pronunciation: NAY-toh in-LARJ-muhnt
French: élargissement de l’OTAN

Definition: The expansion of NATO through new member states.

Example: Russia has long opposed NATO enlargement.

security dilemma

IPA: /sɪˈkjʊərəti dɪˌlemə/
Pronunciation: si-KYOOR-uh-tee di-LEM-uh
French: dilemme de sécurité

Definition: A situation where one state’s defensive actions make another feel threatened.

Example: NATO and Russia interpret the security dilemma differently.

Ukraine

IPA: /juːˈkreɪn/
Pronunciation: yoo-KRAYN
French: Ukraine

Definition: A sovereign European state invaded by Russia in 2022.

Example: Ukraine is central to Russia-West tensions.

Ukraine war

IPA: /juːˈkreɪn wɔː/
Pronunciation: yoo-KRAYN war
French: guerre en Ukraine

Definition: The war following Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine.

Example: The Ukraine war transformed European security.

full-scale invasion

IPA: /ˌfʊl skeɪl ɪnˈveɪʒən/
Pronunciation: full-scale in-VAY-zhuhn
French: invasion à grande échelle

Definition: A large military invasion involving major forces across multiple fronts.

Example: Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022.

annexation

IPA: /ˌænekˈseɪʃən/
Pronunciation: an-ek-SAY-shuhn
French: annexion

Definition: The incorporation of territory into another state, often by force.

Example: The annexation of Crimea was widely condemned.

Crimea

IPA: /kraɪˈmɪə/
Pronunciation: kry-MEER-uh
French: Crimée

Definition: A peninsula annexed by Russia from Ukraine in 2014.

Example: Crimea remains internationally recognised by many states as part of Ukraine.

Donbas

IPA: /dɒnˈbɑːs/
Pronunciation: don-BASS
French: Donbass

Definition: A region in eastern Ukraine including Donetsk and Luhansk.

Example: The Donbas has been a major battlefield.

C. Ukraine war, hybrid warfare and military power

separatist

IPA: /ˈsepərətɪst/
Pronunciation: SEP-uh-ruh-tist
French: séparatiste

Definition: A person or group seeking separation from a state.

Example: Russia backed separatist forces in eastern Ukraine.

proxy force

IPA: /ˈprɒksi fɔːs/
Pronunciation: PROK-see force
French: force par procuration

Definition: A force supported by another power to fight indirectly.

Example: Proxy forces can hide direct responsibility.

hybrid warfare

IPA: /ˌhaɪbrɪd ˈwɔːfeə/
Pronunciation: HY-brid WAR-fair
French: guerre hybride

Definition: Conflict combining military force, cyberattacks, disinformation and political pressure.

Example: Russia is often associated with hybrid warfare.

grey-zone tactics

IPA: /ˌɡreɪ zəʊn ˈtæktɪks/
Pronunciation: gray-zone TAK-tiks
French: tactiques de zone grise

Definition: Actions below the threshold of open war.

Example: Grey-zone tactics create ambiguity and confusion.

little green men

IPA: /ˌlɪtəl ɡriːn ˈmen/
Pronunciation: LIT-uhl green men
French: petits hommes verts

Definition: Unmarked soldiers associated with Russia’s seizure of Crimea.

Example: The phrase little green men symbolised plausible deniability.

plausible deniability

IPA: /ˌplɔːzəbəl dɪˌnaɪəˈbɪləti/
Pronunciation: PLAW-zuh-buhl di-ny-uh-BIL-uh-tee
French: déni plausible

Definition: The ability to deny responsibility because evidence is ambiguous.

Example: Hybrid operations often rely on plausible deniability.

war of attrition

IPA: /ˌwɔːr əv əˈtrɪʃən/
Pronunciation: war ov uh-TRISH-uhn
French: guerre d’usure

Definition: A conflict where each side tries to exhaust the other over time.

Example: The conflict has become a brutal war of attrition.

mobilisation

IPA: /ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən/
Pronunciation: moh-bi-ly-ZAY-shuhn
French: mobilisation

Definition: Preparing troops, resources or society for war.

Example: Mobilisation revealed tensions inside Russian society.

conscription

IPA: /kənˈskrɪpʃən/
Pronunciation: kuhn-SKRIP-shuhn
French: conscription

Definition: Compulsory military service.

Example: Conscription can be politically sensitive.

mercenary

IPA: /ˈmɜːsənəri/
Pronunciation: MUR-suh-nuh-ree
French: mercenaire

Definition: A fighter hired to fight for money.

Example: Mercenary groups have operated in Russian-linked conflicts.

Wagner Group

IPA: /ˈvæɡnə ɡruːp/
Pronunciation: VAG-nuh group
French: groupe Wagner

Definition: A Russian private military company linked to conflicts abroad.

Example: The Wagner Group became a symbol of Russian shadow warfare.

private military company

IPA: /ˌpraɪvət ˈmɪlɪtəri ˈkʌmpəni/
Pronunciation: PRY-vuht MIL-i-tuh-ree KUM-puh-nee
French: société militaire privée

Definition: A private company providing military or security services.

Example: Private military companies complicate accountability.

war crime

IPA: /ˈwɔː kraɪm/
Pronunciation: war kryme
French: crime de guerre

Definition: A serious violation of the laws of war.

Example: Alleged war crimes require investigation and prosecution.

civilian casualties

IPA: /səˌvɪliən ˈkæʒuəltiz/
Pronunciation: suh-VIL-yuhn KAZH-oo-uhl-teez
French: victimes civiles

Definition: Civilian deaths or injuries caused by conflict.

Example: Civilian casualties have shaped international reactions.

energy superpower

IPA: /ˈenədʒi ˈsuːpəpaʊə/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee SOO-puh-pow-uh
French: superpuissance énergétique

Definition: A country with major influence due to energy resources.

Example: Russia has long been an energy superpower.

oil exporter

IPA: /ˈɔɪl ekˌspɔːtə/
Pronunciation: oil ek-SPORT-uh
French: exportateur de pétrole

Definition: A country that sells oil abroad.

Example: Russia is a major oil exporter.

gas supplier

IPA: /ˈɡæs səˌplaɪə/
Pronunciation: gas suh-PLY-uh
French: fournisseur de gaz

Definition: A country or company providing natural gas.

Example: Russia was a major gas supplier to Europe.

natural gas

IPA: /ˌnætʃərəl ˈɡæs/
Pronunciation: NAT-chuh-ruhl gas
French: gaz naturel

Definition: Gas used as an energy source for heating, electricity and industry.

Example: Natural gas became a geopolitical weapon.

pipeline

IPA: /ˈpaɪplaɪn/
Pronunciation: PIPE-line
French: gazoduc ; oléoduc

Definition: A pipe system transporting oil or gas over long distances.

Example: Pipelines link energy markets and geopolitics.

Nord Stream

IPA: /ˌnɔːd ˈstriːm/
Pronunciation: nord stream
French: Nord Stream

Definition: Gas pipelines connecting Russia and Germany under the Baltic Sea.

Example: Nord Stream became highly controversial after the Ukraine crisis.

energy dependency

IPA: /ˈenədʒi dɪˌpendənsi/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee di-PEN-duhn-see
French: dépendance énergétique

Definition: Reliance on another country for energy supplies.

Example: European energy dependency on Russia became a strategic vulnerability.

energy blackmail

IPA: /ˈenədʒi ˈblækmeɪl/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee BLAK-mayl
French: chantage énergétique

Definition: Using energy supplies as political pressure.

Example: Critics accused Russia of energy blackmail.

weaponisation of energy

IPA: /ˌwepənaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈenədʒi/
Pronunciation: wep-uh-ny-ZAY-shuhn ov EN-uh-jee
French: instrumentalisation de l’énergie

Definition: Use of energy supplies as a geopolitical weapon.

Example: The weaponisation of energy accelerated Europe’s diversification.

energy security

IPA: /ˈenədʒi sɪˌkjʊərəti/
Pronunciation: EN-uh-jee si-KYOOR-uh-tee
French: sécurité énergétique

Definition: Reliable and affordable access to energy.

Example: Energy security became central to European policy.

fossil fuels

IPA: /ˌfɒsəl ˈfjuːəlz/
Pronunciation: FOS-uhl FYOO-uhlz
French: énergies fossiles

Definition: Coal, oil and gas used for energy.

Example: Russia’s economy depends heavily on fossil fuels.

resource curse

IPA: /rɪˈsɔːs kɜːs/
Pronunciation: ri-SORSS curse
French: malédiction des ressources

Definition: The paradox that resource-rich countries may suffer from corruption or poor governance.

Example: The resource curse can weaken institutions.

rentier state

IPA: /ˈrɒntieɪ steɪt/
Pronunciation: RON-tee-ay state
French: État rentier

Definition: A state relying heavily on income from natural resources.

Example: Russia has features of a rentier state.

D. Energy, sanctions, economy and international isolation

sanctions

IPA: /ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions

Definition: Economic or political penalties imposed to change behaviour.

Example: Sanctions targeted Russian banks, firms and officials.

economic sanctions

IPA: /ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: ee-kuh-NOM-ik SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions économiques

Definition: Economic restrictions imposed on a state, company or individual.

Example: Economic sanctions aim to weaken Russia’s war effort.

financial sanctions

IPA: /faɪˌnænʃəl ˈsæŋkʃənz/
Pronunciation: fy-NAN-shuhl SANK-shuhnz
French: sanctions financières

Definition: Sanctions targeting banks, assets or financial transactions.

Example: Financial sanctions limited Russia’s access to global finance.

asset freeze

IPA: /ˈæset friːz/
Pronunciation: AS-et freez
French: gel des avoirs

Definition: A measure preventing someone from using assets.

Example: Several oligarchs faced asset freezes.

travel ban

IPA: /ˈtrævəl bæn/
Pronunciation: TRAV-uhl ban
French: interdiction de voyager

Definition: A restriction preventing someone from travelling.

Example: Officials were placed under travel bans.

export control

IPA: /ˈekspɔːt kənˌtrəʊl/
Pronunciation: EK-sport kuhn-TROHL
French: contrôle des exportations

Definition: Rules restricting exports of sensitive goods or technology.

Example: Export controls targeted advanced technology.

technology embargo

IPA: /tekˈnɒlədʒi emˌbɑːɡəʊ/
Pronunciation: tek-NOL-uh-jee em-BAR-goh
French: embargo technologique

Definition: A ban or restriction on technology exports.

Example: A technology embargo can limit military production.

price cap

IPA: /ˈpraɪs kæp/
Pronunciation: price kap
French: plafonnement des prix

Definition: A limit on the price at which something can be sold.

Example: The oil price cap aimed to reduce Russian revenue.

sanctions evasion

IPA: /ˈsæŋkʃənz ɪˌveɪʒən/
Pronunciation: SANK-shuhnz i-VAY-zhuhn
French: contournement des sanctions

Definition: Avoiding or bypassing sanctions.

Example: Sanctions evasion often uses third countries and shell companies.

shadow fleet

IPA: /ˈʃædəʊ fliːt/
Pronunciation: SHAD-oh fleet
French: flotte fantôme

Definition: Tankers used to transport sanctioned oil outside normal systems.

Example: Russia’s shadow fleet helps bypass oil restrictions.

war economy

IPA: /ˈwɔːr iˌkɒnəmi/
Pronunciation: war i-KON-uh-mee
French: économie de guerre

Definition: An economy reorganised to support war production.

Example: Russia has shifted towards a war economy.

import substitution

IPA: /ˈɪmpɔːt ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən/
Pronunciation: IM-port sub-sti-TYOO-shuhn
French: substitution aux importations

Definition: Replacing imported goods with domestic production.

Example: Import substitution aims to reduce dependence on Western goods.

ruble

IPA: /ˈruːbəl/
Pronunciation: ROO-buhl
French: rouble

Definition: Russia’s currency.

Example: The ruble is affected by sanctions, energy revenue and capital controls.
